Yahuddu – To encourage

For this the words Harada, Hadda, Hatha, Az and Jaram have been used.

1- Harada

Means extremely ill, very weak or near death.

“And they said, By Allah you will not stop remembering Yousuf until you become fatally ill or become of those who perish” 12/85

Harrada means to encourage to do something that if not done will result in a disaster.

“O Prophet, urge the believers to battle. If there are among you twenty [who are] steadfast, they will overcome two hundred” 8/65

2/3- Hatha/Hadda

Means to encourage to accelerate to an action or to encourage towards doing good. Both of these are used for urging. 'Hatha' means to drive animals and for all the rest 'Hadda' is used.

“He covers the night with the day, [another night] chasing it rapidly “ 7/54

'Hadda' is used to encourage for other things.

“Have you seen the one who denies the Recompense? For that is the one who drives away the orphan And does not encourage the feeding of the poor. So woe to those who pray [But] who are heedless of their prayer - Those who make show [of their deeds] And withhold [simple] assistance” 107

4- Az

Means to incite subtly. This is normally used in circumstances that are considered evil.

“Do you not see that We have sent the devils upon the disbelievers, inciting them to [evil] with [constant] incitement? “ 19/83

5- Jarama

It means a major evil or sin and when used as a verb, it means to encourage or boost for some evil.

“And do not let the hatred of a people prevent you from being just ” 5/8

Inference

1- Harada: to encourage to do something which if not done will result in a disaster.

2- Hatha: to swiftly drive the riding animal

3- Hadda: For all good acts apart from driving animals

4- Az: To incite evil in a way that is subtle.

5- Jarama: To encourage or to cheer towards major evil.
