17 GIN-NA [to count]

Synonyms: ADDA, HASABA, AHSAA

17.1 ADDA (عَدََّ): Means to count, to add up, to include. A very famous word. And ADDAD (عدد) means numeral or count. In the Quran

Surah Al-Kahf (The Cave): سورۃ الکھف 18:11

فَضَرَبۡنَا عَلَىٰٓ ءَاذَانِهِمۡ فِى ٱلۡكَهۡفِ سِنِينَ عَدَدً۬ا

So We cast [a cover of sleep] over their ears within the cave for a number of years.

 

17.2 HASABA (حَسَبَ): YAHSABU (یَحْسَبُ), HISAABAN WA HUSBAANAN (حِسَابًا وَ حُسْبَانًا) means to count and to keep the count. Count systematically so that there is no difference in the calculation.

Surah Al-Ana’am (The cattle): سورۃ الانعام 6:96

  وَجَعَلَ ٱلَّيۡلَ سَكَنً۬ا وَٱلشَّمۡسَ وَٱلۡقَمَرَ حُسۡبَانً۬ا‌ۚ

 And He made the night for rest, and the sun and the moon for calculation.

17.3 AHSAA (اَحْصٰی): HASAA (حَصٰی) means a pebble and HASAA YAHSI (حصی یحصی) means to pelt pebbles. The people of Arab use to count on pebbles due to unavailability of a chronologer. Thus this word also refers to counting. HASAA (حَصٰی) refers to two things 1. To count, 2. To keep a record of it or to remember it. It is related to ADDA (عَدََّ). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Ibraheem (Abraham): سورۃ ابراھیم 14:34

 وَإِن تَعُدُّواْ نِعۡمَتَ ٱللَّهِ لَا تُحۡصُوهَآ‌ۗ

And if you count the Blessings of Allah, never will you be able to count them.

And sometimes it is used to refer to having the ability to count. Like in the Quran

Surah Al-Muzammil (The enshrouded one): سورۃ المُزمّل 73:20

 عَلِمَ أَن لَّن تُحۡصُوهُ فَتَابَ عَلَيۡكُمۡ‌ۖ

He knows that you are unable to keep count thereof. So He has turned to you (in mercy):

Summary:

17.1 ADDA: To count

17.2 HASABA: To count systematically so no errors occur.

17.3 AHSAA: To count and keep a record of it.
