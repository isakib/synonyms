3. GARDAN (Neck)

Synonyms: UNUQ, JEED, RAQBAT, WATEEN.

 

3.1 UNUQ(عُنُق): Neck or Throat. Famous/Known part of a human body. It is commonly used. (Plural A’NAAQ اَعنَاق). In the Quran:

Surat Al-'Isrā' (The Night Journey) - سورة الإسراء 17:13

وَكُلَّ إِنسَانٍ أَلْزَمْنَاهُ طَائِرَهُ فِي عُنُقِهِ ۖ

And [for] every person We have imposed his fate upon his neck.

 

3.2 JEED(جِيد): Thin and long neck. A beautiful neck like that of a deer. In the Quran:

Surat Al-Masad (The Palm Fiber, Flame) - سورة المسد 111:4

وَامْرَأَتُهُ حَمَّالَةَ الْحَطَبِْفِي جِيدِهَا حَبْلٌ مِّن مَّسَدٍْ

And his wife [as well] - the carrier of firewood. Around her neck is a rope of [twisted] fiber.

 

3.3 RAQABAT(رَقَبَة): Means neck or the back part of it(nape). And RAQABA(رَقَبَ) means to put a rope or noose around a person’s neck. To guard or supervise. And generally, the Arabs say JAZ’-ASHARAF(جزء اشرف) and refer it as a total. Likewise, RAQBA(رقبة) is referred to a slave because he has a noose of slavery around his neck (Plural RIQAB رقاب).  And TEHREER-RAQBA(تحرير رقبة)  means to free the neck from this loop. Which means to free a slave. In the Quran:

Surat Al-Balad (The City) - سورة البلد 90:13

ْفَكُّ رَقَبَةٍْ وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا الْعَقَبَةُ

And what can make you know what is [breaking through] the difficult pass?.It is the freeing of a slave

3.4 WATEEN (وَتِين): Means the vein of the neck(aorta). The jugular vein which goes from heart to the brain. That side of neck where the jugular vein lies. In the Quran:

Surat Al-Ĥāqqah (The Reality) - سورة الحاقة 69: 45-46

لَأَخَذْنَا مِنْهُ بِالْيَمِينِْثُمَّ لَقَطَعْنَا مِنْهُ الْوَتِينَْ

We would have seized him by the right hand; Then We would have cut from him the aorta.

 

Summary:

1. UNQ: A common word for neck.

2. JEED: Long and beautiful neck

3. RAQABAT: The neck itself and its back part(Nape). Noose of slavery.

4. WATEEN: The front portion of the neck where the jugular vein lies.
