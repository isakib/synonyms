گ (GAAF)

 

1. GAR-NA (To Pitch)

Synonyms: NASABA, ARSA (RASU), DASSA, WAADA

1.1 NASABA(نَصَبَ): Means to pitch or install something such that some part of it is under the ground and most of it is outside in some other direction like pitching in a flagpole or a spear or to erect a building or a stone and it is also used for an idol which is fixed in someplace. In the Quran:

1. Surat Al-Ghāshiyah (The Overwhelming) - سورة الغاشية 88:19

  وَإِلَى الْجِبَالِ كَيْفَ نُصِبَتْ

And at the mountains - how they are erected?

 

2. Surat Al-Mā'idah (The Table Spread) - سورة المائدة 5:3

 وَمَا ذُبِحَ عَلَى النُّصُبِ

And [those animals] killed by strangling

 

1.2 ARSA(اَرسَى): RASU(رسو) means to establish or erect and ARSA(اَرسَى) means to pitch something in the ground so that most part of it is inside the ground and less is outside. RASAL-WAT-DA-FIL-ARDI(رَسَا الوَتدَ فِى الْاَرضِ) means to pitch a rivet in the ground and RASIYA(راسيه) means mountain and its plural is RAWASI(رَوَاسِى). RASIYA(راسية) are those mountains which are spread far away on the ground and their major portion is underground and RAWASI(رَوَاسِى) means mountain range. Both NASABA(نصب) and RASU(رسو) are used for fixing mountains. In the Quran:

1. Surat An-Nāzi`āt (Those who drag forth) - سورة النازعات 79:32

 وَالْجِبَالَ أَرْسَاهَا

And the mountains He set firmly.

 

2. Surat Ar-Ra`d (The Thunder) - سورة الرعد 13:3

هُوَ الَّذِي مَدَّ الْأَرْضَ وَجَعَلَ فِيهَا رَوَاسِيَ وَأَنْهَارًا ۖ

And it is He who spread the earth and placed therein firmly set mountains and rivers

 

1.3 DASSA(دَسَّ): Means to conceal something by burying it in the ground or to enter one thing into another forcefully or to hide something by burying it or sinking down in the soil and DASSA ALAEH(دَسَّ عَلَيه) means to conspire or to plot secretly. In the Quran:

1. Surat Ash-Shams (The Sun) - سورة الشمس 91:10

وَقَدْ خَابَ مَن دَسَّاهَا

And he has failed who instills it [with corruption].

 

2. Surat An-Naĥl (The Bee) - سورة النحل 16:59

 أَيُمْسِكُهُ عَلَىٰ هُونٍ أَمْ يَدُسُّهُ فِي التُّرَابِ ۗ

Should he keep it in humiliation or bury it in the ground

1.4 WA’ADA(وَأَدَ): Burying someone alive under the weight of the earth. To bury alive. It is most particular to DASSA(دَسَّ). In the Quran:

Surat At-Takwīr (The Overthrowing) - سورة التكوير 81:8

بِأَيِّ ذَنبٍ قُتِلَتْ وَإِذَا الْمَوْءُودَةُ سُئِلَتْ

And when the girl [who was] buried alive is asked. For what sin she was killed?

 

Summary:

1. NASABA: To wedge like a flagpole.  A small portion buried in the soil while the rest is erect above the surface.

2. ADSA: Like burying a peg in the ground with most of the part buried in the ground.

3. DASSA: To bury or thrust something under the ground

4. WA’ADA: To bury a living being in the ground.
