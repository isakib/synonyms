19 GEHRA – GEHRAEE [Deep, Depth]

Synonyms: LUJJAHTAN, GAURAN, AMEEQ

19.1 LUJJAHTAN (لُجَّةً): LUJJ (لجّ) means the depth of water, that area of where water reaches its maximum depth. LUJJ-UL-BAHR (لُجُّ الْبَحر) means the utmost depth of a sea.  بَحۡرٍ۬ لُّجِّىٍّ۬  (24:20) means “ vast deep sea” LUJJAH (لُجَّة) means deep waters. Allah says in the Quran

Surah An-Naml (The ant): سورۃ النَّمل 27:44

 فَلَمَّا رَأَتۡهُ حَسِبَتۡهُ لُجَّةً۬ وَكَشَفَتۡ عَن سَاقَيۡهَا‌ۚ

 It was said to her, “Go inside the palace.” And when she saw it, she thought it was a deep pond, and she bared her legs.

19.2 GAURAN (غَوْر): GHAARA (غَارَ) means to move to lower grounds. GHAR (غَار) Meaning cave is a famous word. GHAUR (غَوْر) means a declivity or a low ground, and underground depth. Thus this word refers to depth as well as some place. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Mulk (The Sovereignty): سُوۡرَةُ المُلک67:30

 قُلۡ أَرَءَيۡتُمۡ إِنۡ أَصۡبَحَ مَآؤُكُمۡ غَوۡرً۬ا فَمَن يَأۡتِيكُم بِمَآءٍ۬ مَّعِينِۭ

Say, "Have you considered: if your water was to become sunken [into the earth], then who could bring you flowing water?"

19.3 AMEEQ (عَمِیْق): UMQ (عُمْق) means a depth of something (Opposite IRTAFA’ (ارتفع) (loftiness)) If a distance is towards the horizon then it refers to distance or length, and if the distance is above the ground then the distance will be called IRTAFA’ (ارتفع) or loftiness lastly of distance goes below the level of ground, or is lower than the position where one stands than this distance will be UMQ (عُمْق) or depth. And AMEEQ (عَمِیْق) means deep. In the Quran

Surah Al-Hajj (The Pilgrimage): سُوۡرَةُ الحَجّ 22:27

يَأۡتِينَ مِن كُلِّ فَجٍّ عَمِيقٍ۬

Coming from every remote path,

In the above ayah AMEEQ (عَمِیْق) refers to far off because of FAJH (فَجّ). FAJH (فَجّ) refers to path between two mountains which is a contoured path containing elevations and depressions.

Summary:

19.1 LUJJAHTAN: Deep water, LUJJ means depth of water

19.2 GAURAN: Underground depth, lower ground

19.3 AMEEQ: Depth. Common word.
