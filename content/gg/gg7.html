7 GIRNA – GIRANA [to fall, to cause to fall]

Synonyms: SAKATA, KHARRA, HADAMA, HADDA, INQADDA, HABATA, WAQA’A, HARA, HAWAA, KHAWA, WAJABA, RADAA, SARA’A, TALL, TA’ASA, IDDAARAKA, INHAMARA, SABBA, SAKABA

7.1 SAKATA (سَقَطَ): To throw something down from height. This word is used commonly but it has indirectly contempt in it. For instance “رَجُلٌ سَاقِط” means an ignoble person (dictionary al Manjad). In the Quran

Surah Al-Ana’am (The Cattle): سورۃ الانعام 6:59

 وَمَا تَسۡقُطُ مِن وَرَقَةٍ إِلَّا يَعۡلَمُهَا

 Not a leaf falls but He knows it;

The way this word is used materially, in the same manner it is used semantically. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:49

 أَلَا فِى ٱلۡفِتۡنَةِ سَقَطُواْ‌ۗ

Unquestionably, into trial they have fallen

And SUQITA-FI-YADIHI  (سُقِطَ فِیْ یدِہِ)is used as an idiom, which means to be ashamed and sorry. And only changes due to conscience. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:149

 وَلَمَّا سُقِطَ فِىٓ أَيۡدِيهِمۡ وَرَأَوۡاْ أَنَّهُمۡ قَدۡ ضَلُّواْ

 Then, when they regretted, and realized that they had erred,

And for causing to fall ASQATA  (اَسْقَطَ)is used. In the Quran

Surah Ash-Shura’ (The Poets): صورۃ الشُّعْراء 26:187

 فَأَسۡقِطۡ عَلَيۡنَا كِسَفً۬ا مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ إِن كُنتَ مِنَ ٱلصَّـٰدِقِينَ

So cause to fall upon us fragments of the sky, if you should be of the truthful

7.2 KHARRA (خَرَّ): Falling with perturbation and sound. To be upset or fluster and also creating sound while falling. KHAREER (خریر) means the sound of water falling or dripping. Allah says in the Quran.

Surah Saba (Sheba): سورۃ سَبَإ 34:14

 فَلَمَّا خَرَّ تَبَيَّنَتِ ٱلۡجِنُّ أَن لَّوۡ كَانُواْ يَعۡلَمُونَ ٱلۡغَيۡبَ مَا لَبِثُواْ فِى ٱلۡعَذَابِ ٱلۡمُهِينِ

 

Then, when he fell down, it became clear to the sprites that, had they known the unseen, they would not have remained in the demeaning torment.

7.3 HADAM (ھَدَم): To destroy a building or a construction. HADDAMA (ھَدَّم) means to destroy, level or cause a building to fall. INHIDAAM (اِنْھِدَام) is a famous word, which gives the same meaning as HADAM (ھَدَم) . Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Hajj  (The Pilgrimage): سُوۡرَةُ الحَجّ 22:40

 وَلَوۡلَا دَفۡعُ ٱللَّهِ ٱلنَّاسَ بَعۡضَہُم بِبَعۡضٍ۬ لَّهُدِّمَتۡ صَوَٲمِعُ وَبِيَعٌ۬

Were it not that God repels people by means of others: monasteries, churches, synagogues, and mosques—where the name of God is mentioned much—would have been demolished.

7.4 HADDA (ھَدَّ): Breaking and falling of a building making sound and noise. Allah says in the Quran.

Surah Maryam (Mary): سُوۡرَةُ مَریَم 19:90

 تَڪَادُ ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٲتُ يَتَفَطَّرۡنَ مِنۡهُ وَتَنشَقُّ ٱلۡأَرۡضُ وَتَخِرُّ ٱلۡجِبَالُ هَدًّا

At which the heavens almost rupture, and the earth splits, and the mountains fall and crumble.

 

7.5 INQADDA (اِنْقَضَّ): Cracking of a building or wall, nearing to fall, QADD-AL-HAAIT (قَضَّ الْحَائِط) means to break down a wall, and INQADD-AL-HAAIT (اِنْقَضَّ الحَائِط) means “the wall cracked open and fell”. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Kahf (The Cave): سورۃ الکھف 18:77

 فَوَجَدَا فِيہَا جِدَارً۬ا يُرِيدُ أَن يَنقَضَّ فَأَقَامَهُ ۥ‌ۖ

There they found a wall about to collapse, and he repaired it.

7.6 HABATA (ھَبَطَ): To fall, to get down or descend, to come out. This word refers indirectly to constraint and calamity. HABAT-AS-SAMAN (ھبط الثمن) means price fell. HABAT-AZ-ZAMAAN (ھَبَطَ الزَّمَان) means time made him poor from rich. HABAT-AL-MARD (ھبط المرض) means the sickness made him lean and weak by taking the energies out of him (dictionary al Manjad).Used as both transitive and intransitive verb. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:74

وَإِنَّ مِنۡہَا لَمَا يَہۡبِطُ مِنۡ خَشۡيَةِ ٱللَّهِ‌ۗ

there are some of them that fall down for fear of Allah .

HABATA (ھَبَطَ) also has scorn in it besides constrain and misfortune. In that case it gives the meaning of ‘get out’. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:38

قُلۡنَا ٱهۡبِطُواْ مِنۡہَا جَمِيعً۬ا‌ۖ

We said, "Go down from it, all of you.

In the following ayah

Surah Hud (Hud): سورۃ ھود 11:48

 قِيلَ يَـٰنُوحُ ٱهۡبِطۡ بِسَلَـٰمٍ۬ مِّنَّا

 It was said, “O Noah, disembark with peace from Us;

In the ayah above the word BISALAMIN (بِسَلَـٰمٍ۬) is eliminating the aspects of scorn and constrain making it a divine will.

 

7.7 WAQA’A (وَقَعَ): To fall, to prove, to occur, this word usually represents hate, extremity and turmoil. WAQIA (واقعہ) means an event that is accompanied by hardship. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:171

 وَإِذۡ نَتَقۡنَا ٱلۡجَبَلَ فَوۡقَهُمۡ كَأَنَّهُ ۥ ظُلَّةٌ۬ وَظَنُّوٓاْ أَنَّهُ ۥ وَاقِعُۢ بِہِمۡ

 And when We suspended the mountain over them, as if it was an umbrella, and they thought it would fall on them:

7.8 HARA (ھَارَ): HAWR (ھور) means falling of something in a way that a portion of it falls on the other objects too.  The word INHAAR (اِنْھَارَ) and INHIDAAM (اِنْھِدَام) are similar in meaning. INHIDAAM (اِنْھِدَام) means falling of a building, construction or wall but INHAAR (اِنْھَارَ) covers a wider range of meaning. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:109

 خَيۡرٌ أَم مَّنۡ أَسَّسَ بُنۡيَـٰنَهُ ۥ عَلَىٰ شَفَا جُرُفٍ هَارٍ۬ فَٱنۡہَارَ بِهِۦ فِى نَارِ جَهَنَّمَ‌ۗ

 one who laid the foundation of his building on the edge of a bank about to collapse, so it collapsed with him into the fire of Hell?

7.9 HAWAA (ھوٰی): HAWA (ھَوَا) is a famous word which means the air between the sky and earth, HAWAA (ھوٰی) means to fall from a height on the ground. Used also for a falling or shooting star. Allah says in the Quran

Surah An-Najm (the star): سورۃ النَّجم 53:1

وَٱلنَّجۡمِ إِذَا هَوَىٰ

By the star as it goes down.

In another place in Quran

Surah Al-Hajj  (The Pilgrimage): سُوۡرَةُ الحَجّ 22:31

فَكَأَنَّمَا خَرَّ مِنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ فَتَخۡطَفُهُ ٱلطَّيۡرُ أَوۡ تَهۡوِى بِهِ ٱلرِّيحُ فِى مَكَانٍ۬ سَحِيقٍ۬

it is as though he has fallen from the sky, and is snatched by the birds, or is swept away by the wind to a distant abyss.

7.10 KHAWAA (خوی): KHAWAA (خوی) and HAWAA (ھوٰی) are similar in meaning, HAWAA (ھوٰی) means the air filled empty space between the sky and earth, and KHAWA (خَوٰی) is empty space between any two objects. Like HAW-AN-NAJAM (ھَوَی النَّجْم) is used similarly KHAW-AN-NAJAM (خوی النجم) is used. TAKHWIYAH (تخویة) means to leave space between two things, KHAW-AD-DAAR (خوی الدار) means to demolish or break down a house that is depopulated and deserted. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:259

 أَوۡ كَٱلَّذِى مَرَّ عَلَىٰ قَرۡيَةٍ۬ وَهِىَ خَاوِيَةٌ عَلَىٰ عُرُوشِهَا

Or [consider such an example] as the one who passed by a township which had fallen into ruin.

7.11 WAJABA (وَجَبَ): ALWAJABAH (اَلْوَجَبَة) is to make something fall by a blast (dictionary al Manjad), this word has also the aspect of death in it, like DARABAHU-FAWAJABA (ضَرَبَهُ فَوَجَبَ) means he hit him and he fell down and died. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Hajj  (The Pilgrimage): سُوۡرَةُ الحَجّ 22:36

فَٱذۡكُرُواْ ٱسۡمَ ٱللَّهِ عَلَيۡہَا صَوَآفَّ‌ۖ فَإِذَا وَجَبَتۡ جُنُوبُہَا فَكُلُواْ مِنۡہَا وَأَطۡعِمُواْ ٱلۡقَانِعَ وَٱلۡمُعۡتَرَّ‌ۚ

 So pronounce God’s name upon them as they line up. Then, when they have fallen on their sides, eat of them and feed the contented and the beggar.

7.12 RADAA (ردی): Means to throw some living thing from a height or dash someone in a pit from ground which fatally injures him. In the Quran

Surah Ta-Ha (Ta-ha): سورۃ طٰھٰ  20:16            

 وَٱتَّبَعَ هَوَٮٰهُ فَتَرۡدَىٰ

follows his desire turn you away from it, lest you fall.

ARD-AR-RUJAL (اَرْدَی الرَّجُل) means to throw someone into a well. RADD-AR-RAJUL (رَدَّی الرَّجُل) means to cause a man to fall, to kill. Allah says in the Quran

Surah As-Saaffat (The Aligners) : سُوۡرَةُ الصَّافات37:56

قَالَ تَٱللَّهِ إِن كِدتَّ لَتُرۡدِينِ

He says, "By Allah decidedly you were almost about to topple me down indeed.

TARADDA (تَرَدّٰی) means to fall into a well, MUTARADDIYAH وَٱلۡمُتَرَدِّيَةُ (5:3) is an animal that falls in a well or pit and dies. Allah says in the Quran

Surat Al-Layl (The Night): سورة الليل 92:11

وَمَا يُغۡنِى عَنۡهُ مَالُهُ ۥۤ إِذَا تَرَدَّىٰٓ

Nor will his wealth profit him when he falls headlong (into the Pit).

7.13 SARA’A (صَرَعَ): Means to fall on ground due to perturbation and anxiety, or fall and roll on ground due to a seizure (dictionary al Manjad), SARA’ (صَرَع) refers to epilepsy which causes the patient to fall to ground helpless. SARA’A (صَرَعَ) means to dash an opponent to ground in a brawl and to overcome him. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Haqqah (The reality): سورۃ الحَاقَّۃ 69:7

 فَتَرَى ٱلۡقَوۡمَ فِيہَا صَرۡعَىٰ كَأَنَّہُمۡ أَعۡجَازُ نَخۡلٍ خَاوِيَةٍ۬

You could see the people tossed around, as though they were stumps of hollow palm-trees.

7.14 TALL (تلّ): Means to throw someone upon forehead, Allah says in the Quran

Surah As-Saaffat (The Aligners) : سُوۡرَةُ الصَّافات37:103

فَلَمَّآ أَسۡلَمَا وَتَلَّهُ ۥ لِلۡجَبِينِ

So when they both submitted and he threw him down upon his forehead,

7.15 TA’ASA (تَعَسَ): To stumble and fall, and not recover and stand. Fall down and break, He fell down pronely and died. TA’ASA (تَعَسَ) means to slip and fall head on. AT-TA’SAH (التَّعَسَہ) means fatality (dictionary al Manjad). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Muhammad (Muhammad): سُوۡرَةُ محَمَّد 47:9

وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُواْ فَتَعۡسً۬ا لَّهُمۡ

But as for those who disbelieve, for them is perdition,

7.16 IDDAARAKA (اِدّٰرَك): DARK (دَرْك) means mixing of a thing with another, and then becoming one. DARK (دَرْك) is also the seabed, and also that rope which is combined with another rope to get to the seabed. To achieve an aim through some medium is called ADRAKA (اَدْرَكَ), Like about FIRA’OUN (فرعون) in Quran حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَآ أَدۡرَڪَهُ ٱلۡغَرَقُ (when the (fate of) drowning overtook him) (11:90) means the final destination was that he was drowned. So a chain of causation lead to his drowning. IDDAARAKA (اِدّٰرَك) also gives the same picture. Allah says in the Quran

Surah An-Naml (The ant): سورۃ النَّمل 27:66

 بَلِ ٱدَّٲرَكَ عِلۡمُهُمۡ فِى ٱلۡأَخِرَةِ‌ۚ

In fact, their knowledge of the Hereafter is confused.

Thus even after gathering and composing all knowledge they will fail hopelessly. In another place in the Quran

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:38

 كُلَّمَا دَخَلَتۡ أُمَّةٌ۬ لَّعَنَتۡ أُخۡتَہَا‌ۖ حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَا ٱدَّارَڪُواْ فِيہَا جَمِيعً۬ا

Every time a crowd enters, it will curse its sister-crowd. Until, when they are all in it,

Means the final destination is hell and due to close relationship and connection bringing along one group after another and fall into hell.

7.17 INHAMARA (اِنْھَمَرَ):  HAMAR-AL-MAA’ (ھَمَرَ الْمَاء) means he spilled the water, INHARAM-AL-MAA’ (اِنْھَمَرَ الْمَاء) means the water fell and flowed. Or falling of tears, Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Qamar (The moon): سورۃ القَمَر 54:11

 فَفَتَحۡنَآ أَبۡوَٲبَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ بِمَآءٍ۬ مُّنۡہَمِرٍ۬

 So We opened the floodgates of heaven with water pouring down.

 

7.18 SABBA (صَبَّ): SABB-AL-MAA’ (صَبَّ الماء) means to throw or spill water on someone all at once, SABBA-ALAEH-IL-BALA’ (صَبَّ عَلَیْهِ الْبَلَاء) means to throw someone in trouble (dictionary al Manjad). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Abasa (He frowned): سورۃعَبَسَ 80:24

 فَلۡيَنظُرِ ٱلۡإِنسَـٰنُ إِلَىٰ طَعَامِهِۦۤ أَنَّا صَبَبۡنَا ٱلۡمَآءَ صَبًّ۬ا

Let man consider his food. We pour down water in abundance.

 

7.19 SAKABA (سَکَبَ): SAKAB-AL-MAA’ (سکب الماء) means falling and spilling of water. ASKAB (اسکب) means a continuous rain fall. AL-ASKUB (الاسکوب) means continuous shower. And MAA’IM-MASKOOB (مَاءٍمَّسْکُوْبٍ) means JAARIN-DAAIMAN (جَارٍ دَائِمًا) “continuously flowing water”. Thus SAKABA refers to three things falling of water from a height, flowing of water and continuity. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Waqiah (The Inevitable): سُوۡرَةُ الواقِعَة 56:30

وَظِلٍّ۬ مَّمۡدُودٍ۬  وَمَآءٍ۬ مَّسۡكُوبٍ۬

And extended shade. And outpouring water.

 

Summary:

7.1 SAKATA: Falling from height, a very common word.

7.2 KHARRA: Falling with noise and perturbation.

7.3 HADAMA: Falling of a construction, wall or building.

7.4 HADDA: Sudden falling and collapsing of a building, construction or wall.

7.5 INQADDA: Cracking of a construction, building or wall, about to fall.

7.6 HABATA: To fall due to pain or perturbation, to come out, or get down.

7.7 WAQA’A: Fall accompanied by difficulty,

7.8 HARA: Falling of something on another thing,

7.9 HAWAA: To fall from sky or a great height,

7.10 KHAWA: To fall from space between two things

7.11 WAJABA: to collapse and die, or collapse while dying.

7.12 RADAA: To fall into a pit and die.

7.13 SARA’A: To dash to ground like in brawl or due to death or sickness,

7.14 TALL: To fall head-on, pronely,

7.15 TA’ASA: Stumble and fall head-on,

7.16 IDDAARAKA: To fall after failing and giving up,

7.17 INHAMARA: Falling and flowing of water

7.18 SABBA: Falling, spilling and flowing of water,

7.19 SAKABA: Continuous falling and flowing of water.
