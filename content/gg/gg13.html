13 GUMAN KERNA – KHIAL KERNA [to think, to suppose]

SYNONYMS: ZANNA, ZA’AMA, HASIBA

13.1 ZANNA (ظَنَّ): The signs which lead to some results are called ZANN (ظَنّ). If the signs and suppositions are strong then ZANN (ظَنّ) refers to certainty and surety. In this case this word is preceded by ANNA (اَنَّ), AN (اَنْ). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ 2:46

 ٱلَّذِينَ يَظُنُّونَ أَنَّہُم مُّلَـٰقُواْ رَبِّہِمۡ وَأَنَّهُمۡ إِلَيۡهِ رَٲجِعُونَ

Who are certain that they will meet their Lord and that they will return to Him.

Surah Al-Qiyammah (the resurrection): سورۃ القِیَِامَھ 75:28

 وَظَنَّ أَنَّهُ ٱلۡفِرَاقُ

And the dying one is certain that it is the [time of] separation

Surah Al-Fath (The victory): سورۃ الفَتْح  48:12

 بَلۡ ظَنَنتُمۡ أَن لَّن يَنقَلِبَ ٱلرَّسُولُ

But you thought that the Messenger and the believers would never return to their families,

And when refers to some suspicious or doubt then ZANN (ظَنّ) is preceded by INN (اِنّ) and IN (اِنْ). And the second indication is that the word ZANN (ظَنّ) is accompanied by a context which changes its meaning to assumption or doubt. For instance

Surah An-Najm (the star): سورۃ النَّجم 53:28

إِن يَتَّبِعُونَ إِلَّا ٱلظَّنَّ‌ۖ وَإِنَّ ٱلظَّنَّ لَا يُغۡنِى مِنَ ٱلۡحَقِّ شَيۡـًٔ۬ا

They follow not except assumption, and indeed, assumption avails not against the truth at all.

In this example INNA (اِنَّ)and the word HAQ (حق) (truth), change the meaning of ZANN (ظَنّ) to supposition and doubt.

Surah Al-Jathiya (Crouching): سُوۡرَةُ الجَاثیَة 45:32

 إِن نَّظُنُّ إِلَّا ظَنًّ۬ا وَمَا نَحۡنُ بِمُسۡتَيۡقِنِينَ

 We assume only assumption, and we are not convinced.' "

In this example INN (اِنّ) and the word YAQEEN (یقین) (‘believe’) change ZANN (ظَنّ) into suspicion or superstition.

Surah Al-Fath (The victory): سورۃ الفَتْح  48:6

 ٱلظَّآنِّينَ بِٱللَّهِ ظَنَّ ٱلسَّوۡءِ‌ۚ

Those who harbor evil thoughts about God.

In this example the word ZANN-AS-SU’ (ظَنَّ ٱلسَّوۡءِ‌) particularizes the meaning of ZANN (ظَنّ) to doubt and supposition. Then there are stages of supposition and doubts between belief and suspicion, and ZANN (ظَنّ) is used for every such stage.

13.2 ZA’AMA (زَعَمَ): False supposition; According to Ibn-Al-Faris ‘اَلْقَوْلُ مِنْ غَیْرِ صِحَّةٍ وَّلَا یَقِیْنٍ’ meaning a statement that is untrue and unauthentic. In Quran where ever this word is used, the one who is suspects is derogated or belittled. Allah says in the Quran

Surah At-Taghābun (The Mutual Disillusion): سورة التغابن 64:7

 زَعَمَ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوٓاْ أَن لَّن يُبۡعَثُواْ‌ۚ

Those who disbelieve think that they shall never be raised

13.3 HASIBA (حَسِبَ): HASAB (حَسَب) means to count or to calculate, HASUBA (حَسُبَ) means a noble or honorable. HASIBA (حَسِبَ) means to suppose or think. This word is for that degree or supposition in which a person only secretly knows or wishes for it but does not consider it a truth. A most likely supposition. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:214

 أَمۡ حَسِبۡتُمۡ أَن تَدۡخُلُواْ ٱلۡجَنَّةَ وَلَمَّا يَأۡتِكُم مَّثَلُ ٱلَّذِينَ خَلَوۡاْ مِن قَبۡلِكُم‌ۖ

Or do you think that you will enter Paradise while such [trial] has not yet come to you as came to those who passed on before you?

Summary:

13.1 ZANNA: ZANN (ظَنّ) Refers to larger set of meanings. And all stages of supposition or doubts are covered by it, suspicion,

13.2 ZA’AMA: False supposition

13.3 HASIBA: A most likely supposition.
