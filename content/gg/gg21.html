21 GHAATI [pass, way]

Synonyms: NAJAD, AQABAH

21.1 NAJAD (نَجَد): Means a pass or a raised ground. Clear ground between trees. An elevated path (dictionary al Manjad). And NAJADA (نَجَدَ) means to be lofty and prominent (dictionary al Manjad) and an elevated and hard ground. NAJAD (نَجَد) refers to pass and also those paths which connect to the pass. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Balad (The City): سورة البلد 90:10

 وَهَدَيۡنَـٰهُ ٱلنَّجۡدَيۡنِ

And have shown him the two ways?

 

21.2 AQABAH (عَقَبَةَ): A mountain pass; or a perilous path to climb a mountain. Thus AQABAH (عَقَبَةَ) refers only to that path that leads to a pass or used for climbing a mountain, hence in the ayah below.

Surah Al-Balad (The City): سورة البلد 90:11

 فَلَا ٱقۡتَحَمَ ٱلۡعَقَبَةَ وَمَآ أَدۡرَٮٰكَ مَا ٱلۡعَقَبَةُ

But he has not broken through the difficult pass. And what can make you know what is [breaking through] the difficult pass?

But According to dictionary al Manjad AQABAH (عَقَبَةَ) means a difficult pass, and a perilous path that is used to get on it

Summary:

21.1 NAJAD: Pass

21.2 AQABAH: A perilous pass
