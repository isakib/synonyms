5. GARMI-GARAM KARNA-HONA (Hotness/Heat, To Heat- Be Heated)

Synonyms: SUAIF, HARR, HAMM, HAMI, SAQAR, SAMOOM.

 

5.1 SUAIF(صَيف): Summer season. And SUAI’IF(صَيِّف) means the rainfall of summer and the grass grown due to it. SUAF(صَاف) and SUAI’AF(صَيَّف) means to spend summer season somewhere. In the Quran:

Surat Quraysh (Quraysh) - سورة قريش 106:2

إِيلَافِهِمْ رِحْلَةَ الشِّتَاءِ وَالصَّيْفِ

Their accustomed security [in] the caravan of winter and summer -

 

5.2 HARR(حَرّ): Means temperature or heat. Whether it’s caused by fire or Sun(Sunlight). Like when heat is produced when rubbing two things together. However, this word is usually applied to the virulence of the sun. And HAROOR(حَرُوْر) means radiance/heat. Hot wind(Also look DHOOP).

5.3. HAMMA(حَمّ)

 

5.4. HAMIA(حمى): These two words are so close/similar in meaning that it is difficult  to distinguish between them. That is why Philologists take both their derivatives according to both these root words. There are four means to obtain heat; Fire and sunlight are materialistic means, and heat of emotions/desires and natural heat(of a body). In all these four resources, these two roots are used. If there’s any difference, it’s that HAMMA(حَمّ) is used as a transitive(verb) and HAMIA(حَمِىَ) as intransitive(verb). But when passive voice is used, then they become synonyms. For instance, HAMMAL-MA’(حَمَّ الْمَاء) means to heat-up water. And HUMMA-RRAJUL(حَمَّ الرَّجُل) means a person has got fever. And HUMMA(حُمَّى) means fever. The Philologists have written in subjection to both roots. HAMMAT-TANOOR(حَمَّ التَّنُور) means heated a stove. And HAMIAN-NAAR(حمى النار) means that fire flared up. And HAMEEM(حميم) means hot water and also cold water( LUGHAT AZDAAD لغت اضداد i.e. it has opposite meanings). And it also means a zealous friend or relative. This belongs to emotions/feelings. And HAMMAZ-ZAHIRAT(حَمَّ الظَّهِيرَة) refers to the extreme hotness during midday. And HAMMAT(حَمَّة) means a fountain of hot water. And HUMMAT(حُمَّة) means coal. Everything burned up in fire or ashes. And HAMAAM(حمام) means a place for bath/ablution with hot water. Now inspect their following examples. In the Quran:

1. Surat Al-'An`ām (The Cattle) - سورة الأنعام 6:70

لَهُمْ شَرَابٌ مِّنْ حَمِيمٍ وَعَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ بِمَا كَانُوا يَكْفُرُونَ

For them will be a drink of scalding water and a painful punishment because they used to disbelieve.

 

2. Surat Al-Ma`ārij (The Ascending Stairways) - سورة المعارج 70:10

وَلَا يَسْأَلُ حَمِيمٌ حَمِيمًا

And no friend will ask [anything of] a friend.

 

3. Surat Al-Qāri`ah (The Calamity) - سورة القارعة 101: 10-11

نَارٌ حَامِيَةٌ وَمَا أَدْرَاكَ مَا هِيَهْ

And what can make you know what that is? It is a Fire, intensely hot.

 

4. Surat Al-Fatĥ (The Victory) - سورة الفتح 48:26

إِذْ جَعَلَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا فِي قُلُوبِهِمُ الْحَمِيَّةَ حَمِيَّةَ الْجَاهِلِيَّةِ

When those who disbelieved had put into their hearts chauvinism - the chauvinism of the time of ignorance.

 

5.5. SAQARA(سَقَرَ): Means the scorching of fire or sunlight. SAQARAT-HUSH-SHAMS(سَقَرَتهُ الشَّمْس) means the fire burned/scorched him/her. Then this word has become notorious for hell. In the Quran:

Surat Al-Qamar (The Moon) - سورة القمر 54:48

ذُوقُوا مَسَّ سَقَرَ

Taste the touch of Saqar(Fire).

 

5.6 SAMOOM(سَمُوم): SAMMA(سَمّ) means a narrow hole like the eye of a needle or the hole of nose(nostrils) and ear. Also, SAMMA(سَمّ) means deadly poison. And SAMOOM(سَمُوم) means hot wind which can penetrate through the body from inside like a poison. In the Quran:

Surat Aţ-Ţūr (The Mount) - سورة الطور 52:27

فَمَنَّ اللَّهُ عَلَيْنَا وَوَقَانَا عَذَابَ السَّمُومِ

So Allah conferred favor upon us and protected us from the punishment of the Scorching Fire.

Surah At-Tur (The Mount): سورۃ الطُّور 52:27

 فَمَنَّ ٱللَّهُ عَلَيۡنَا وَوَقَٮٰنَا عَذَابَ ٱلسَّمُومِ

So Allah conferred favor upon us and protected us from the punishment of the Scorching Fire.

 

Summary:

5.1 SAIF: Summer season

5.2 HARR: Heat, heat of sunshine.

5.3 HAMM: Similar to HAMI, used for both heat and cordiality.

5.4 HAMI: Similar to HAMM, used for both heat and cordiality.

5.5 SAQR: Hell, Burning of body due to heat.

5.6 SAMOOM: Hot breeze that penetrates a body.
