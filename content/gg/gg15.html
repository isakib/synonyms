15 GUNEHGAR [sinner]

Synonyms: AATHM, AETHM, KHATI’, MUJRIM, FAASIQ, FAAJIR

AATHM (اٰثِم), AETHM (اَیثَم), KHATI’ (خَطِئ), MUJRIM (مُجْرِم), have been discussed before in GUNAH (sin).

 

15.4 FAASIQ (فَاسِق): FASAQA (فَسَقَ) someone who deviates from the path of virtue, vicious (dictionary al Manjad). To break out of the circle of submission. To be careless on part of licit orders. FASIQ (فَاسِق) word is used for a habitual disobedient. An Immoral person. But a FAASIQ (فَاسِق) does distinguish a sinful act as sin and disobedience. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Hujraat (The Inner Apartments): سُوۡرَةُ الحُجرَات 49:6

 يَـٰٓأَيُّہَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓاْ إِن جَآءَكُمۡ فَاسِقُۢ بِنَبَإٍ۬ فَتَبَيَّنُوٓاْ

O you who have believed, if there comes to you a disobedient one with information, investigate,

15.5 FAAJIR (فَاجِر): (Plural FUJJAR (فُجَّار), Opposite ABRAAR (ابرار)) FAJARA (فَجَرَ) to tear apart something on a wide scale. FAAJIR (فَاجِر) is someone who disobeys religion or betrays religion’s rules. An immoral and vice person who keeps on committing sins and doesn’t repent. FAJOOR (فجور) is someone who is engrossed in committing sins. A debauched person (dictionary al Manjad). FAJARA (فَجَرَ) means to be obstinate or stubborn, display stubbornness. FAAJIR (فاجر) is one who doesn’t consider a sin as bad. A bully or thug. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Muţaffifīn (The Defrauding): سورة المطففين 83:7

كَلَّآ إِنَّ كِتَـٰبَ ٱلۡفُجَّارِ لَفِى سِجِّينٍ۬

Nay! Truly, the Record (writing of the deeds) of the Fujjar (disbelievers, sinners, evil-doers and wicked) is (preserved) in Sijjin.

Summary:

15.1 AATHM: One who sins sometimes, ATHEEM: Who has a vicious nature and is ready to do vice anytime.

15.2 KHATI’: Unintentional sinner

15.3 MUJRIM: He who commits a big or deadly sin.

15.4 FASIQ: Those sinners who consider a sin as bad
