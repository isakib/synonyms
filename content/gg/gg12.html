12 GUM HONA – HATH NA LAGNA [to lose, to miss ]

Synonyms: FATA, FAQADA, ZALLA

12.1 FATA (فَاتَ): Something getting out of hand, FAAT-AL-AMR (فَاتَ الاَمْر) means time to do a task that is lost and would not come back. Allah says in the Quran

 Surat Al-Hadeed (The Iron): سورة الحديد 57:23

لِّكَيۡلَا تَأۡسَوۡاْ عَلَىٰ مَا فَاتَكُمۡ وَلَا تَفۡرَحُواْ بِمَآ ءَاتَٮٰڪُمۡ‌ۗ

You may not be sad over matters that you fail to get, nor rejoice because of that which has been given to you.

12.2 FAQADA (فَقَدَ): To not get something. Not available. Even if is attained later on or to lose something despite of its availability. This word is derived from ADM (عَدَم) ۔In the Quran

Surah Yusuf (Joseph): سورۃ یُوسُف 12:71

 

 قَالُواْ وَأَقۡبَلُواْ عَلَيۡهِم مَّاذَا تَفۡقِدُونَ

 

They said, as they came towards them, “What are you missing?”

And TAFAQQADA (تَفَقَّدَ) means to verify or review if something is missing. According to Imam Raghib to know if something is missing. To find something missing or lost. In the Quran

Surah An-Naml (The ant): سورۃ النَّمل 27:20

 وَتَفَقَّدَ ٱلطَّيۡرَ فَقَالَ مَا لِىَ لَآ أَرَى ٱلۡهُدۡهُدَ

And he reviewed the birds; then he said, "what is it with me that I do not see the hoopoe?

 

12.3 ZALLA (ضَلَّ): Actually means to forget the way and get lost. But it also means to lose one existence and find another in return. In the Quran

Surah As-Sajda (The prostration ): سُوۡرَةُ السَّجدَة 32:10

  وَقَالُوٓاْ أَءِذَا ضَلَلۡنَا فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ أَءِنَّا لَفِى خَلۡقٍ۬ جَدِيدِۭ‌ۚ

 And they say, “When we are lost into the earth, shall we be in a new creation?”

 

Summary:

12.1 FATA: To lose an opportunity, to lose hope of getting something.

12.2 FAQADA: For some time, not able to get something in spite of its availability.

12.3 ZALLA: To convert a thing’s existence into another thing and losing it.
