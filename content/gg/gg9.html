9. GARHA [pit, hole]

Synonyms:  HUFRAH, JUROOF, UKHDOOD, GHAIT

9.1 HUFRAH (حُفْرَۃ): HUFRAH (حُفْرَۃ) and HAFEERAH (حَفِیرَۃ) means a pit. HAFARA (حَفْرَ) means to dig a pit. HAFR (حَفْر) means earth from the pit. MIHFAR (مِحْفَر) is the tool used to dig the pit (dictionary al Manjad).  A famous proverb “مردود فی الحافرۃ” means “dirt landed into where it belonged”. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Ale- Imran (The family of Imran): سورۃ آل عِمْرن 3:103

 وَكُنتُمۡ عَلَىٰ شَفَا حُفۡرَةٍ۬ مِّنَ ٱلنَّارِ فَأَنقَذَكُم مِّنۡہَا‌ۗ

And you were on the brink of a pit of fire, and He saved you from it.

9.2 JUROOF (جُرُف): The coast or a stream or river that is hollowed by the flow of river, wave-cut notch (dictionary al Manjad).  Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:109

 أَم مَّنۡ أَسَّسَ بُنۡيَـٰنَهُ ۥ عَلَىٰ شَفَا جُرُفٍ هَارٍ۬

he who lays his foundation on the edge of a cracking hollowed bank,

9.3 UKHDOOD (اخدود): AL-KADD-WALA-KHADOOD ( الخدّوالاخدود) means a deep and rectangular pit,(plural AKHADEED(اخدید))  AL-KHADD (اَلْخَدّ) means a stream of water, a long pit. AL-KHADDAH-WAL-UKHDOOD ( اَلْخَدَّۃ والْاُخْدُوْد) means a pit (dictionary al Manjad).  UKHDOOD (اخدود) is that deep and long hole that is dug in rectangular shape. Ditch or pit. ALLAH says in the Quran

Surat Al-Burūj (The Mansions of the Stars): سورة البروج 85:4

قُتِلَ أَصۡحَـٰبُ ٱلۡأُخۡدُودِ

Cursed be the makers of the pit,

9.4 GHAIT (غَائِط): GHOAUT (غَوْط) means a depression in ground. GHAAT-AL-HUFRAH (غَاط الْحُفْرَة) means to dig a hole. GHAWWAT-AL-BI’R (غَوَّطَ الْبِئْر) means to dig deeper into a well. AL-GHAIT (الغَائِط) means a low land, a place for defecation, toilet (dictionary al Manjad).  It’s a metaphor that a noble and a shy person would try to find an isolated and deep place for urination. Allah says in the Quran

Surah An-Nisa (The women): سورۃ النِّسا 4:43 and

Surah Al-Maeda (The table spread): سورۃ المَائدہ 5:6

 أَوۡ جَآءَ أَحَدٌ۬ مِّنكُم مِّنَ ٱلۡغَآٮِٕطِ

or one of you comes from the toilet,

Summary :

9.1 HUFRAH: Pit, a common word.

9.2 JUROOF: Wave-cut notch at the bank of river or stream.

9.3 UKHDOOD: Ditch or trench, a self dug deep rectangular pit.

9.4 GHAIT: Depressed ground, metaphorically a place for defecation.
