Al Aadiyaat (100)

Ayah1:

Horse(taken in the context of running),those who run
Ghoora (horse), [p.754]
http://qurandev.github.com/widgets/book.html?pageno=754

1.Khail() –it’s a common gender that can be used for both male and female horse.It is also used to represent a herd. For example,Ganum(), a herd of goats.It can be used to describe a horse rider. Ref(16:8),where it is used as a common gender. Ref(17:64) where it is used to represent a herd and a horse rider.
The literal meaning of Khail is to have different kinds of imaginations that come in one’s mind and the word of Khayal(thought) has been derived from it. Alkhaila() means to have a feeling of pride and superiority that a horse riders feels as compared to a pedestrian. Takhayul() is also derived from it which means to have a deep insight.

2.Safinaat()-Plural of Safin(),Safana means that the horse is so active to be able to stand on three legs while standing on only the heal of the fourth leg.

3.Jiyaad() – Plural of Jiyyad(),Jod() means anything that is vast. Jawwad() refers to the generous man and woman.Ja’adul Furs() means that the horse that is very smart and fast runner.Jiyyad also refers to the sophistication of anything. According to lexicographers,Jiyaad means the horse that is a fast runner and from superior breed.
Ref(38:31)

4.Aadiyaat()-Plural of Aadiyat() and Aadiyat is the feminine of Aadi(). Aadi() means a specific group that is ready to die and to put others to death.In another context,it refers to horses that are extremely fast and furious. Aadiul Furs() means a horse starts running fast.Tadal Qaum() means people tried to compete in marathon.Therefore,Aadiyat means those warrior horses that used to participate in the race.Ref(100:1).


Summary:
1. Khail- Common gender used for horse,horse rider and group of each.
2. Safinaat- Active,sturdy horse capable of standing on three legs.
3. Jiyaad – Good runner and superior breed horse.
4. Aadiyaat- warrior horses that are also used in race.
