14 GUNNAH [sin]

Synonyms: ZAMB, KHATA, HUUB, HINTH, ITHM, IJRAAM,JUNAH, LAMAM

14.1ZAMB (ذَنْۢب): Any act whose outcome is bad, and MA-YATBA’-ZAMM (ما یتبع الذم) and its usage is so common that it can be used for small mistakes to bigger sins. (Plural ZUNOOB (ذنوب))

Surah Al-Fath (The victory): سورۃ الفَتْح  48:1

إِنَّا فَتَحۡنَا لَكَ فَتۡحً۬ا مُّبِينً۬ا لِّيَغۡفِرَ لَكَ ٱللَّهُ مَا تَقَدَّمَ مِن ذَنۢبِكَ وَمَا تَأَخَّرَ

We have granted you a conspicuous victory. That God may forgive you your sin, past and to come,

These sins can only be some very small mistakes on part of Prophet Mohammed (S.A.A.W) because no Muslim can even think of Prophet Mohammed (S.A.A.W) doing even of the smallest sins. For instance in another place in the Quran

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:43

عَفَا ٱللَّهُ عَنكَ لِمَ أَذِنتَ لَهُمۡ

May God pardon you! Why did you give them permission

And one of the biggest sins is an unjust murder. And even for that the word ZAMB (ذَنْۢب) is used. In the Quran

Surah Ash-Shura’ (The Poets): صورۃ الشُّعْراء 26:14

 وَلَهُمۡ عَلَىَّ ذَنۢبٌ۬ فَأَخَافُ أَن يَقۡتُلُونِ

And they have upon me a [claim due to] sin, so I fear that they will kill me."

14.2 KHATA (خَطَا): A sin that is not done intentionally by any human and it happens accidently. For instance a hunter while hunting a prey kills a human. And if this mistake can be rectified by some offering then its punishment can be reduced and it is called KHITA’ (خِطْأ) KHAT’A (خَطْأ). And if of some other kind of sin, Allah can forgive it if begged for mercy and for good deeds, and this is known as KHATAA’ (خَطَأ) (Plural KHATAAYA (خطایا)). And KHATIA (خَطِئَ) (Plural KHATIA’AT (خطئات)) also comes in the same meaning. Allah says in the Quran

Surah An-Nisa (The women): سورۃ النِّسا 4:92

وَمَن قَتَلَ مُؤۡمِنًا خَطَـًٔ۬ا فَتَحۡرِيرُ رَقَبَةٍ۬ مُّؤۡمِنَةٍ۬ وَدِيَةٌ۬ مُّسَلَّمَةٌ إِلَىٰٓ أَهۡلِهِۦۤ إِلَّآ أَن يَصَّدَّقُواْ‌ۚ

 Anyone who kills a believer by error must set free a believing slave, and pay compensation to the victim’s family, unless they remit it as charity.

In another place in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:58

 وَقُولُواْ حِطَّةٌ۬ نَّغۡفِرۡ لَكُمۡ خَطَـٰيَـٰكُمۡ‌ۚ

Say, 'Pardon.' We will forgive your sins,

14.3 HUUB (حُوْب): ALHUUBAH (اَلْحُوْبَة) refers to parents, sister or daughter, the phrase ‘اِنَّ لِیْ حُوْبَةٌ اَعْوَلُھَا’ means my family who are under my guardianship (dictionary al Manjad). HAUB (حَوْب) means to disobey parents or parents delinquency in upbringing their child. According to Imam Raghib those act that are strictly forbidden, their perpetration is called HUUB (حُوْب).  HUUB (حُوْب) basically means scolding or castigation. HUUB (حُوْب) is any such sin which when performed must be punished by castigation.

Surah An-Nisa (The women): سورۃ النِّسا 4:2

 وَلَا تَأۡكُلُوٓاْ أَمۡوَٲلَهُمۡ إِلَىٰٓ أَمۡوَٲلِكُمۡ‌ۚ إِنَّهُ ۥ كَانَ حُوبً۬ا كَبِيرً۬ا

And do not consume their properties by combining them with yours, for that would be a serious sin.

14.4 HINTH (حِنْث):  Means a false or fake oath, sin or disobedience. HANATH-FIL-YAMEEN (حَنَثَ فِی الْیَمِیْن) means to disregard an oath. And HANITHA (حَنِثَ) means to sway towards lie and sins, to get involved in them (dictionary al Manjad). Thus HINTH (حِنْث) is a type of sin related to breaking of an oath or pledge. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Waqiah (The Inevitable): سُوۡرَةُ الواقِعَة 56:46

وَكَانُواْ يُصِرُّونَ عَلَى ٱلۡحِنثِ ٱلۡعَظِيمِ

And were persisting in great sin

 

14.5 ITHM (اِثْم): Any such act that stops from good deeds, (Plural ATHAAM (اٰثام)) (Opposite BIRR (بِرّ) ). ITHM (اِثْم) means culpability according to dictionary. According to Prophet Mohammed (S.A.A.W) “ ‏اَلْبِرُّ مَا اطْمَأَنَّتْ إِلَيْهِ النَّفْسُ وَالْإِثْمُ مَا ‏ ‏حَاكَ فِی صَدْرِكَ”  A virtue is something which brings content to your heart, and vice is that which brings discontent.

Thus ITHM (اِثْم) is that condition of heart that stops from good deeds and encourages vice and sinful activities and when time comes a person cannot resist from sin. ITHM (اِثْم) is related more to heart than to act and such a person is called ATHEEM (اِثِیْم) (Opposite SALEEM (سَلِیْم)). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Maeda (The table spread): سورۃ المَائدہ 5:2

 وَتَعَاوَنُواْ عَلَى ٱلۡبِرِّ وَٱلتَّقۡوَىٰ‌ۖ وَلَا تَعَاوَنُواْ عَلَى ٱلۡإِثۡمِ وَٱلۡعُدۡوَٲنِ‌ۚ

 And cooperate with one another in virtuous conduct and conscience, and do not cooperate with one another in sin and hostility.

In another place in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:283

 وَلَا تَكۡتُمُواْ ٱلشَّهَـٰدَةَ‌ۚ وَمَن يَڪۡتُمۡهَا فَإِنَّهُ ۥۤ ءَاثِمٌ۬ قَلۡبُهُ ۥ‌ۗ

And do not conceal testimony. Whoever conceals it is sinner at heart.

14.6 IJRAAM (اِجْرَام): JURM (جُرْم) is an action that is punishable by law. But that type of action is not right by both Quran and dictionary as well. JURM (جُرْم) and JAREEMAH (جَرِیْمَۃ) means someone who commits huge sins. AJRAMA (اَجْرَمَ) means the misfortune that comes from a sin (dictionary al Manjad). JURM (جُرْم) is every such act which is not legal and not as a result of some obligatory or worthy action. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Hud (Hud): سورۃ ھود 11:35

 قُلۡ إِنِ ٱفۡتَرَيۡتُهُ ۥ فَعَلَىَّ إِجۡرَامِى وَأَنَا۟ بَرِىٓءٌ۬ مِّمَّا تُجۡرِمُونَ

 Say, “If I made it up, upon me falls my crime, and I am innocent of the crimes you commit.”

14.7 JUNAH (جَنَاح): Doesn’t actually mean sin but to sway towards a sin, to incline to sinful activities, JANAHA (جَنَحَ) means to incline or lean towards (dictionary al Manjad). In the Quran it’s a common phrase “لَا جُنَاحَ عَلَیْکُمْ” or “لَیْسَ عَلَیْکُمْ جُنَاحٌ” meaning there is no encumbrance, or not a punishable or convictable thing. Like in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:236

 لَّا جُنَاحَ عَلَيۡكُمۡ إِن طَلَّقۡتُمُ ٱلنِّسَآءَ مَا لَمۡ تَمَسُّوهُنَّ

There is no sin on you, if you divorce women while yet you have not touched

And JANAHA (جَنَحَ) (Plural MAFTUHA (مفتوحہ) means arm, wing or side ) is used for inclination towards virtue. Like in the Quran

Surah Al-Isra (The Night Journey): سُوۡرَةُ الإسرَاء 17:24

 وَٱخۡفِضۡ لَهُمَا جَنَاحَ ٱلذُّلِّ مِنَ ٱلرَّحۡمَةِ

 And lower to them the wing of humility, out of mercy

In another place in the Quran

Surah Ash-Shura’ (The Poets): صورۃ الشُّعْراء 26:215

وَٱخۡفِضۡ جَنَاحَكَ لِمَنِ ٱتَّبَعَكَ مِنَ ٱلۡمُؤۡمِنِينَ

And lower your wing to those of the believers who follow you.

14.8 LAMAM (لَمَمْ): Those tiny mistakes and sins that lead to committing bigger sins. And ALAMA (اَلَمَ) means to commit small vices or sins (dictionary al Manjad). For instance before adultery visiting a stranger woman, to talk freely with her. Or opinions and advices related to theft before actually committing a theft. Allah says in the Quran

Surah An-Najm (the star): سورۃ النَّجم 53:32

 ٱلَّذِينَ يَجۡتَنِبُونَ كَبَـٰٓٮِٕرَ ٱلۡإِثۡمِ وَٱلۡفَوَٲحِشَ إِلَّا ٱللَّمَمَ‌ۚ إِنَّ رَبَّكَ وَٲسِعُ ٱلۡمَغۡفِرَةِ‌ۚ

Those who avoid gross sins and indecencies—except for minor lapses—your Lord is of Vast Forgiveness.

Summary:

14.1 ZAMB: Common word for any level or kind of sin

14.2 KHATA: A sin committed unintentionally

14.3 HUUB: The bigger sins that are related to family, Sin for which castigation should be opted.

14.4 HINTH: To break an oath or vow, sins related to oaths and vows

14.5 ITHM: Constant inclination towards sin, and not refraining from committing a sin

14.6 IJRAAM: The crime or burden or greater sins,

14.7 JUNAH: Inclination towards sins,

14.8 LAMAM: The small sins that lead to bigger sins.
