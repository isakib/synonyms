9- FARYAAD KARNA [To Call For Aid]

Quran has referred to it by the words ISTIGHAATH (استغاث) and ISTISRAKH (استصرخ).

 

 

9.1    ISTIGHAATHA (استغاث):  GHATHA (غاث ) means to help or support somebody in harsh times and GHAYTH (غيث ) is the rain which is on-time and according to the needs as well. ISTIGHAATH (استغاث ) means to call Allah for aid as well as call a ruler for help and to seek assistance in hard times. ISTIGHAATHAH (استغاثة ) is a well-known word meaning to call for  aid from a ruler or court (regarding a problem affecting him). Allah says:

Surat Al-'Anfāl (The Spoils of War) - سورة الأنفال 8:9

 اِذْ تَسْتَغِيْثُوْنَ رَبَّكُمْ فَاسْتَجَابَ لَكُمْ

 [Remember] when you asked help of your Lord, and He answered you.

 

9.2   ISTISRAKH (استصرخ):  means to gain people’s attention by screaming and shouting for help. (Description is explained above). It is in Qur’an:

Surat Al-Qaşaş (The Stories) - سورة القصص 28:18

 فَاِذَا الَّذِي اسْتَـنْصَرَهٗ بِالْاَمْسِ يَسْتَــصْرِخُهٗ

 When suddenly the one who sought his help the previous day cried out to him [once again].

 

Summary:

 

If help is asked before Allah or authorised ruler then the word ISTIGHAATH (استغاث) will be used and if the people are called by howling and screaming then  ISTISRAKH (استصرخ) will be used.
