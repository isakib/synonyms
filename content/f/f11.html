11- FAZOOL BATAIN KARNA [Involved in idle talk]

Qur’an has referred to it the words ALGHA (الغی) KHAAD (خاض) FIQA (فکہ) SAMARA (سمر) AND HAJR (ھجر).

 

 

11.1  ALGHA (الغی): LAGHA (لغا ) means to talk without thinking, malicious talk, and means the chirping of birds and similarly, anything mentioned repeatedly. And ALGHA (الغی) means to blabber or babble in such a way that the voice of the addressed person is drowned from its noise so that other people also cannot hear it. It is in Qur’an:

(Surat-Ham-m-Sajda) (H-m Sajda) سورة حم السجدہ 41:26

 وَقَالَ الَّذِيْنَ كَفَرُوْا لَا تَسْمَعُوْا لِھٰذَا الْقُرْاٰنِ وَالْـغَوْا فِيْهِ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَغْلِبُوْنَ

 And those who disbelieve say, "Do not listen to this Qur'an and speak noisily during [the recitation of] it that perhaps you will overcome."

11.2  KHAAD (خاض): means to impact. KHAADA FI AL-MAA’ (خاض فی الماء) means to impact water. KHAAD FIL-HADEETH (خاض فی الحدیث ) to engage in talks and interfere in the middle of something. Means to be fully involved in talking or working. It is mostly used in the Qur’an for doing idle work or talk. Allah says:

Surat Al-'An`ām (The Cattle) - سورة الأنعام 6:68

 وَاِذَا رَاَيْتَ الَّذِيْنَ يَخُوْضُوْنَ فِيْٓ اٰيٰتِنَا فَاَعْرِضْ عَنْهُمْ حَتّٰي يَخُوْضُوْا فِيْ حَدِيْثٍ غَيْرِهٖ

 And when you see those who engage in [offensive] discourse concerning Our verses, then turn away from them until they enter into another conversion.

11.3  FIQA (فکہ): means to engage in laughter, laugh or to make laugh. And FIQAHA (فکاھ ) means jesting and TAFKAHA BA’RAD FULAAN (تفکھ بعرض فلان) means to enjoy backbiting someone. To get pleasure from talking. It is in the Qur’an:

Surat Al-Muţaffifīn (The Defrauding) - سورة المطففين 83:31

 وَاِذَا انْقَلَبُوْٓا اِلٰٓى اَهْلِهِمُ انْقَلَبُوْا فَكِهِيْنَ  

 And when they returned to their people, they would return jesting.

11.4  SAMARA (سمر): To describe tales and stories before going to sleep at night. The talks before going to sleep. The story telling.  

11.5 HAJAR (ھجر): YAHJARA (یهجر ) to do malicious talks, to talk rubbish. HAJAR (ھجر ) means inappropriate talk, bad conversation, talking nonsense, rave in sleep or due to disease. Allah Said:

         Surat Al-Mu'minūn (The Believers) - سورة المؤمنون 23:67

 مُسْتَكْبِرِيْنَ ڰ بِهٖ سٰمِرًا تَهْجُرُوْنَ  

         In arrogance regarding it, conversing by night, speaking evil.

 

 

Summary:

11.1 ALGHA: To blabber in such a way that the voice of others cannot be heard.

11.2 KHAADA: To be engaged in some malicious talk.

11.3 FIQA: Such type of laughter and jesting that gives pleasure. Jesting.

11.4 SAMARA: To describe tales and stories before going to sleep at night.

11.5 HAJAR: To rave in sleep or in disease. To talk rubbish.
