14- FAISLA KERNA [To Decide]

 

The Qur’an has to it by the words FATAHA (فتح) FASALA (فصل) HAKAMA (حکم) QADA (قضی) HATAMA (حتم).

 

 

14.1 FATAHA (فتح): means to remove the closure or complication from something. Antonym is IGHLAAQ (اغلاق) meaning to give victory and to decide. Those decision that differentiates between right and wrong and where the complication is removed and no party has any doubts. Allah Said:

Surat Al-'A`rāf (The Heights) - سورة الأعراف 7:89

 رَبَّنَا افْتَحْ بَيْنَنَا وَبَيْنَ قَوْمِنَا بِالْحَقِّ  

 Our Lord, decide between us and our people in truth.

14.2 FASALA (فصل): means to differentiate one thing from the other and the act of moving away from it. Hence FASAL (فصل) is AKHAS (اخص ) in comparison with FATAHA (فتح). Only distinction is found in FATAHA (فتح) but ‘distinction’ and ‘separation’ are both found (in FASALA).This distinction and separation can be found in the same thing as well, such as a book. Similarly at times, FATAHA (فتح) is related to one thing and sometimes, with many things.

  Allah Said:

Surat Al-Mursalāt (The Emissaries) - سورة المرسلات 77:38

 هٰذَا يَوْمُ الْفَصْلِ ۚ جَمَعْنٰكُمْ وَالْاَوَّلِيْنَ

This is the Day of Judgement; We will have assembled you and the former peoples.

14.3 HAKAMA (حکم): means MANA’ ‘AN-ADHLAM( منع عن اظلم), a decision in which injustice is prevented. And means a command to stop fighting and quarrelling.  It is in Qur’an:

Surat Al-'Anbyā' (The Prophets) - سورة الأنبياء 21:78

   وَدَاوٗدَ وَسُلَيْمٰنَ اِذْ يَحْكُمٰنِ فِي الْحَرْثِ

 And [mention] David and Solomon, when they judged concerning the field.

14.4 QADA (قضی): YAQDI (یقضی) QADYA (قضیا) WAQADAA (وقضا) and WAQADI’ (وقضئ) means to: 1) do and complete some work, and: 2) give a final (lasting) ruling among parties, and QADA (قضی) is be used in judgement when power is available to impose that ruling. QADHI (قاضی) is a renowned word. Allah Said:

 

Surat An-Nisā' (The Women) - سورة النساء 4:65

فَلَا وَرَبِّكَ لَا يُؤْمِنُوْنَ حَتّٰي يُحَكِّمُوْكَ فِيْمَا شَجَــرَ بَيْنَھُمْ ثُمَّ لَا يَجِدُوْا فِيْٓ اَنْفُسِهِمْ حَرَجًا مِّمَّا قَضَيْتَ           

But no, by your Lord, they will not [truly] believe until they make you, [O Muhammad], judge concerning that over which they dispute among themselves and then find within themselves no discomfort from what you have judged.

14.5 HATAMA (حتم): means to decide, to make strong, to give ruling about something, to make obligatory. And HAATIM (حاتم) means ruler. It is said HATAM AL-HAATIM KADHA (حتم الحاتم کذا) i.e. the ruler has given a ruling for this. And HADHA WALAD HATAM (ھاذا ولد حتم) means this is a boy and there is no doubt of his origin (i.e. lineage). AND HATAMA (حتم) means QADHAA WA QADR (قضا و قدر). Hence HATAM (حتم) is a decision that is lasting and irrevocable. It is in Qur’an:

Surat Maryam (Mary) - سورة مريم 19:71

  وَاِنْ مِّنْكُمْ اِلَّا وَارِدُهَا  ۚ كَانَ عَلٰي رَبِّكَ حَتْمًا مَّقْضِيًّا    

And there is none of you except he will come to it. This is upon your Lord an inevitability decreed.

 

Summary:

14.1       FATAH: To make AGHLAAQ away and give a clear decision about a thing.

14.2       FASAL:  (Making) distinction and separation.

14.3       HAKAM: Such a ruling that prevents oppression.

14.4           QADAA: A final ruling given from a person who has the power to impose it.

14.5           HATAM: Absolute and irrevocable ruling. The decision by Allah.
