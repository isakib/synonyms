8- FARYAAD RASI [To Complaint]

 

Qur’an has referred to it by the words ISTIJAAB (استجاب), JOOB (جوب) and ASRAKHA (اصرخ).

 

8.1  ISTIJAAB (استجاب):  JAABA (جاب) means stone-cutting. And ‘AJAABA (أجاب ) means MARAJA’T AL-KALAAM (مراجعتہ الکلام ) means to answer question. And SALA (سل ) also comes with two meanings: 1) To inquire about a thing, and: 2) To ask for a thing or to demand. Hence ‘AJAABA (أجاب ) also comes with those two meanings, to respond to someone's query. Like Allah Said:

Surat Al-Mā'idah (The Table Spread) - سورة المائدة 5:109

 يَوْمَ يَجْمَعُ اللّٰهُ الرُّسُلَ فَيَقُوْلُ مَاذَآ اُجِبْتُمْ  

[Be warned of] the Day when Allah will assemble the messengers and say, "What was the response you received?".

And to accept its meaning in response to a demand for something. Like it is Said:

 

Surat An-Naml (The Ant) - سورة النمل 27:62

 اَمَّنْ يُّجِيْبُ الْمُضْطَرَّ اِذَا دَعَاهُ

Is He [not best] who responds to the desperate one when he calls upon Him.

And ISTIJAAB (استجاب) means accepting the prayer, or to honour or accept the demands of a caller especially when call is exercised. Allah Said:

 

Surat Al-'Anbyā' (The Prophets) - سورة الأنبياء 21:76

 وَنُوْحًا اِذْ نَادٰي مِنْ قَبْلُ فَاسْتَجَبْنَا لَهٗ فَنَجَّيْنٰهُ وَاَهْلَهٗ مِنَ الْكَرْبِ الْعَظِيْمِ

                        And [mention] Noah, when he called [to Allah ] before [that time], so We responded to him and saved him and his family from the great flood.

8.2 ASRAKHA (اصرخ): SARAKHA (صراخ ) means to howl or scream so that someone comes (to help). And SAREEKH (صريخ ) means to come to the aid and the meaning of ASRAKHA (اصرخ) is same. It is in the Qur’an:

Surat 'Ibrāhīm (Abrahim) - سورة ابراهيم 14:22

 فَلَا تَلُوْمُوْنِيْ وَلُوْمُوْٓا اَنْفُسَكُمْ ۭ مَآ اَنَا بِمُصْرِخِكُمْ وَمَآ اَنْتُمْ بِمُصْرِخِيَّ

So do not blame me; but blame yourselves. I cannot be called to your aid, nor can you be called to my aid.

Summary:

 

8.1   If a prayer or call is offered to Allah for aid and He accept it then it is called ISTIHAAB (استحاب ) while in case of ASRAKHA (اصرخ), anybody who listens to the shouting can come to the aid.
