46. Peechhaa Kerna پیچھا کرنا (To follow)

Also known as ibti-ghaa اِبتِغَاء , qassaa قَصَّ  and atba’aa اَتبَعَ .

1.      Ibti-ghaa اِبتِغَاء : Baghaa بَغَى  means to search, to look for, to crave. Ibti-ghaa اِبتِغَاء  means to search for a required object and then pursue it.

 

Allah Taa’la says:

 

Surah An Nisa (The Women) سُوۡرَةُ النِّسَاء

 

(4:104)

 

وَلَا تَهِنُواْ فِى ٱبۡتِغَآءِ ٱلۡقَوۡمِ‌ۖ

“And do not weaken in pursuit of the enemy. If you should be suffering - so are they suffering as you are suffering, but you expect from Allah that which they expect not. And Allah is ever Knowing and Wise.”

 

2.      Qassaa قَصَّ : It means to follow by foot, or to continuously follow a sign or a landmark.

In the Holy Quran,

Surah Al Qasas (The Stories) سُوۡرَةُ القَصَص

(28:11)

وَقَالَتۡ لِأُخۡتِهِۦ قُصِّيهِ‌ۖ

“And she said to his sister, "Follow him"; so she watched him from a distance while they perceived not.”

3.      Atba’aa اَتبَعَ : It means to follow someone, pursue them and reach them.

In the Holy Quran,

Surah Al Shuara (The Poets) سُوۡرَةُ الشُّعَرَاء

(26:60-61)

فَأَتۡبَعُوهُم مُّشۡرِقِينَ (﻿٦٠﻿) فَلَمَّا تَرَٲٓءَا ٱلۡجَمۡعَانِ قَالَ أَصۡحَـٰبُ مُوسَىٰٓ إِنَّا لَمُدۡرَكُونَ

“So they pursued them at sunrise. And when the two companies saw one another, the companions of Moses said, "Indeed, we are to be overtaken!"

 

Conclusion:

(1)   Ibti-ghaa اِبتِغَاء : To search for a particular required object and then to pursue it.

(2)   Qassaa قَصَّ  : To follow something with the guidance of a sign or a landmark.

(3)   Atba’aa اَتبَعَ : To follow someone and reach them.
