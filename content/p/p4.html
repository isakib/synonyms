4. Paana پانا (To get/ to find)

Also known as ‘Wajad’ وَجَد, ‘thaqaf’ ثَقَفَ, ‘alfaa’ اَلفَى and ‘Adrak’ اَدرَك

1.      Wajad وَجَد: To find the presence of something. This is a commonly used word and can be used anywhere.

In the Holy Quran,

Surah Al-e-Imran (Family of Imran) سُوۡرَةُ آل عِمرَان

(3:37)

كُلَّمَا دَخَلَ عَلَيۡهَا زَكَرِيَّا ٱلۡمِحۡرَابَ وَجَدَ عِندَهَا رِزۡقً۬ا‌ۖ

“So her Lord accepted her with good acceptance and caused her to grow in a good manner and put her in the care of Zechariah. Every time Zechariah entered upon her in the prayer chamber, he found with her provision. He said, "O Mary, from where is this [coming] to you?" She said, "It is from Allah. Indeed, Allah provides for whom He wills without account."

2.      Thaqaf ثَقَفَ: It means for one to be meticulous and skillful while in the process of acquiring something. ‘Thaqaf fin’ ثقف فِن is known to be a skillful master of war and combat. ‘Thaqeef’ ثقِيف means wise and clever. ‘Thaqafat’ ثقَافت means wisdom and skillfulness. ‘Thaqaf’ ثَقَفَ means to get or find someone, dress down and rebuke and then conquer them.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Baqarah (The Cow) سُوۡرَةُ البَقَرَة

(2:191)

وَٱقۡتُلُوهُمۡ حَيۡثُ ثَقِفۡتُمُوهُمۡ وَأَخۡرِجُوهُم مِّنۡ حَيۡثُ أَخۡرَجُوكُمۡ‌ۚ

“And kill them wherever you overtake them and expel them from wherever they have expelled you, and fitnah is worse than killing. And do not fight them at al-Masjid al- Haram until they fight you there. But if they fight you, then kill them. Such is the recompense of the disbelievers.”

In another location, for a similar situation the word ‘wajd’ وَجَد has been used instead of ‘thaqaf’ ثَقَفَ, which implies the generality of ‘wajd’ وَجَد.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah An Nisa (The Women) سُوۡرَةُ النِّسَاء

(4:89)

فَخُذُوهُمۡ وَٱقۡتُلُوهُمۡ حَيۡثُ وَجَدتُّمُوهُمۡ‌ۖ

“They wish you would disbelieve as they disbelieved so you would be alike. So do not take from among them allies until they emigrate for the cause of Allah . But if they turn away, then seize them and kill them wherever you find them and take not from among them any ally or helper.”

3.      Alfaa اَلفَى: ‘Lafu’ لفو  means for something to uncover by losing its veil/cover. ‘Alfaa’ اَلفَى means for something to disclose/come into knowledge or reveal itself.

In the Holy Quran:

Surah Al Yusuf (Joseph) سُوۡرَةُ یُوسُف

(12:25)

وَٱسۡتَبَقَا ٱلۡبَابَ وَقَدَّتۡ قَمِيصَهُ ۥ مِن دُبُرٍ۬ وَأَلۡفَيَا سَيِّدَهَا لَدَا ٱلۡبَابِ‌ۚ

“And they both raced to the door, and she tore his shirt from the back, and they found her husband at the door. She said, "What is the recompense of one who intended evil for your wife but that he be imprisoned or a painful punishment?"

In another location in the Holy Quran,

Surah Al Baqarah (The Cow) سُوۡرَةُ البَقَرَة

(2:170)

قَالُواْ بَلۡ نَتَّبِعُ مَآ أَلۡفَيۡنَا عَلَيۡهِ ءَابَآءَنَآ‌ۗ

“And when it is said to them, "Follow what Allah has revealed," they say, "Rather, we will follow that which we found our fathers doing." Even though their fathers understood nothing, nor were they guided?”

In the above ayah, the use of the word ‘alfaa’ اَلفَى has been in a context that something has been handed down to them by their ancestors. In a different location, but a similar situation, the words ‘wajd’ وَجَد has been used instead of ‘alfaa’ اَلفَى proving the generality of the word ‘wajd’ وَجَد.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Maidah (The Table Spread) سُوۡرَةُ المَائدة

(5:104)

 قَالُواْ حَسۡبُنَا مَا وَجَدۡنَا عَلَيۡهِ ءَابَآءَنَآ‌ۚ

“And when it is said to them, "Come to what Allah has revealed and to the Messenger," they say, "Sufficient for us is that upon which we found our fathers." Even though their fathers knew nothing, nor were they guided?”

4.      Adrak اَدرَك: ‘Darak’ درَك and ‘Darj’ درج are two words meaning the same thing. While climbing up the stairs, it is called ‘darj’ درج or ‘darjah’ درجَة, and while climbing down the stairs it is called ‘darak’ درَك. It is because of this the phrases ‘darjat-ul-jannah’ درجَتُ الجَنَّة and ‘darkat-un-naar’ دركَاتُ النَار are used.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah An Nisa (The Women) سُوۡرَةُ النِّسَاء

(4:145)

إِنَّ ٱلۡمُنَـٰفِقِينَ فِى ٱلدَّرۡكِ ٱلۡأَسۡفَلِ مِنَ ٱلنَّارِ

“Indeed, the hypocrites will be in the lowest depths of the Fire - and never will you find for them a helper -”

‘Adrak’ اَدرَك means for something to, step by step, reach its limit or extremity. ‘Adrak uth-thamar’ اَدرَكَ الثّمَرُ would mean a fruit that reaches its fruition and ‘adrak al-wald’ اَدرَكَ الوَلد would mean for a boy to reach maturity/puberty. According to Ibn-e-Faris, it means for one thing to grasp or merge with another. (to catch it, grab it or occupy or take over). For more details, see ‘Girana’ گرانا (to demolish, spill, bring down)

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Anaam (The Cattle) سُوۡرَةُ الاٴنعَام

(6:103)

لَّا تُدۡرِڪُهُ ٱلۡأَبۡصَـٰرُ وَهُوَ يُدۡرِكُ ٱلۡأَبۡصَـٰرَ‌ۖ

“Vision perceives Him not, but He perceives [all] vision; and He is the Subtle, the Acquainted”

Conclusion:

(1)   Wajd وَجَد: To find the presence of something. It is used commonly.

(2)   Thaqaf ثَقَفَ: To be meticulous and skillful while achieving or acquiring something. To get hold of required individuals during a war.

(3)   Alfaa اَلفَى: To find something coincidentally or by chance. To discover something as it uncovers itself or loses its veil. For one to find out/discover something by themselves.

(4)   Adrak اَدرَك: To gain something by reaching to its limits.
