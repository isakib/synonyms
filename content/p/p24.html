Topic: 24

 

POORA KARNA – HONA

To complete-To be completed

 

Synonyms: Tamma , Atamma,Akmal,Aoufa,Qada, and Asbaga

 

24.1: Atamma: tama or tama us-shai means that some thing reaches to that level where is no need of any external factor and its opposite is Naqasa (-----) Allah (SWT) said:

 

Surat Al-'An`ām (The Cattle)

6:115

 

وَتَمَّتْ كَلِمَتُ رَبِّكَ صِدْقًا وَعَدْلًا

And the Word of your Lord has been fulfilled in truth and in justice

 

And Atamma means to make any inexact or incomplete thing to reach at the level of complete, and it is usually used for counting, quantity or to complete the duration. Allah (SWT) said:

 

Surat Al-Qaşaş (The Stories):

 28:27

فَإِنْ أَتْمَمْتَ عَشْرًا فَمِنْ عِنْدِكَ

but if you complete ten years, it will be (a favour) from you

 

a)  For counting:

Surat Al-Mā'idah (The Table Spread)

5:3

وَأَتْمَمْتُ عَلَيْكُمْ نِعْمَتِي

Completed My Favour upon you

b): For quantity:

Surat-Al-Baqarah (The Cow)

2:187

ثُمَّ أَتِمُّوا الصِّيَامَ إِلَى اللَّيْلِ

then complete your fast till the night appears

 

24.2: Akmala: (kamil its opposite is also naqis) means to complete a started work. means to achieve the purpose for which it came into existence. Allah (SWT) said:

 

Surat Al-Mā'idah (The Table Spread)

5:3

الْيَوْمَ أَكْمَلْتُ لَكُمْ دِينَكُمْ وَأَتْمَمْتُ عَلَيْكُمْ نِعْمَتِي

 

This day, I have perfected your religion for you, completed My Favour upon you

 

Lets see the second example to differentiate between Tamamm(all) and Kamal (complete).

 

Surat-Al-Baqarah (The Cow)

 2:233

 

وَالْوَالِدَاتُ يُرْضِعْنَ أَوْلَادَهُنَّ حَوْلَيْنِ كَامِلَيْنِ ۖ لِمَنْ أَرَادَ أَنْ يُتِمَّ الرَّضَاعَة

 

َThe mothers shall give suck to their children for two whole years, (that is) for those (parents) who desire to complete the term of suckling,

 

In this verse word Tamam is used to complete the term (duration) of suckling and Kamil is used to complete the need of term of suckling.

 

24.3: Awufa: wafa this word is used for fulfilling the promise, measurement, life or conditions and the reward, and its opposite is (Gadar) to betrayal. Let’s have look on the examples:

 

a) For Promise:

Surat-Al-Baqarah (The Cow)

2:40

وَأَوْفُوا بِعَهْدِي أُوفِ بِعَهْدِكُمْ

 

and fulfill (your obligations to) My Covenant (with you) so that I fulfill (My Obligations to) your covenant (with Me),

b) For measurement:

Surat Al-'A`rāf (The Heights)

7:85

 

فَأَوْفُوا الْكَيْلَ وَالْمِيزَانَ وَلَا تَبْخَسُوا النَّاسَ أَشْيَاءَهُمْ

so give full measure and full weight and wrong not men in their things,

 

For vows:

c) Surat Al-Ĥaj (The Pilgrimage)

22:29

وَلْيُوفُوا نُذُورَهُمْ

and perform their vows

 

d) For reward or for Paying rewards and it will come from waffa:

 

Allah (SWT) said:

Surat 'Āli `Imrān (Family of Imran)

3:57

 

وَأَمَّا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ فَيُوَفِّيهِمْ أُجُورَهُمْ ۗ

And as for those who believe (in the Oneness of Allâh) and do righteous good deeds, Allâh will pay them their reward in full.

 

24.4: Qada: When it is related to human being then it means to fulfill the responsibility and to free from it. Allah (SWT) said:

 

Surat-Al-Baqarah (The Cow)

2:200

 

فَإِذَا قَضَيْتُمْ مَنَاسِكَكُمْ فَاذْكُرُوا اللَّهَ كَذِكْرِكُمْ آبَاءَكُمْ

 

So when you have accomplished your Manasik remember Allâh as you remember your forefathers

Like this it comes to another place:

 

Surat Al-'Aĥzāb (The Combined Forces)

33:23

 

فَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ قَضَىٰ نَحْبَهُ وَمِنْهُمْ مَنْ يَنْتَظِرُ

of them some have completed their vow (to the extreme) and some (still) wait

 

24.5: Asbagh:----------- -------  ,and according to Imam Raghib its meaning is wide and can be easily understood. Sabigh is wide (zirrah) and Asbagh means to complete the work in such way that there is no shortcoming and purpose is also fulfilled. Usually this word is used for ablution and to fulfill the blessings. Allah (SWT) said:

 

Surat Luqmān (Luqman

31:20

وَأَسْبَغَ عَلَيْكُمْ نِعَمَهُ ظَاهِرَةً وَبَاطِنَةً

and has made His bounties flow to you in exceeding measure, (both) seen and unseen?

 

Summary:

 

Atamma: To complete some thing in such way that there is not remaining any fault.

Akmala: To complete some thing in such way that the purpose is gained.

Aw’fa: it is used for, Promise, measurement, reward, value and life.

Qada: when it related to mankind it means to fulfill the responsibility and to free from it.

Asbagha: To complete some thing in such way that there is not remaining any fault and need is also fulfilled in its best way.
