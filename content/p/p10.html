اخر, خلف are the two words that Quran uses for previous/back/passed.

1.اخر is antonym of first and means that comes after. It’ used for adjectives of time. Ref (56:40)

Page274

2.خلف: is the two back to back or following nations or to be descendent of someone. خلف also comes in negative or bad context i.e. Illegible or incapable descendent. Ref (19:59). And the opposite of خلف is بِين يدِي and both of them come as adjectives of time and place. If it is used as adjective of time then obviously it’s used for ancestors. Ref (2:66). But if خلفhas been brought as compare to بِين يدِي then it gives meaning of being at back. Ref ( 13:11)

Summary:

اخر just comes as adjective of time and it’s general. خلف can be used in both cases i.e. adjective of time or place. As adjective of time it shows previous but there should be an ancestral/ patrimonial relationship between the prior and former.
