to make someone follow (peechay laagana) or to pursue – 52

Synonyms are:

1) اَتْبَعَ

2) قَفٰي (قَفَّي) and (قَفُّو)

3) اَغْرٰ (غرَى)

1)Itbaa اَتْبَعَ: can be used in both ways as Transitive and intransitive verbs if it is intransitive verb then it means behind someone or to follow sum one for example:

فَأَتْبَعُوهُم مُّشْرِقِينَ

26:60 (surah 26 as shuaaraa,verse 60)

Translation: So they pursued them at sunrise.

and if it is transitive verb then it means to put something on follow.

Example : وَأَتْبَعْنَاهُمْ فِي هَٰذِهِ الدُّنْيَا لَعْنَةً

28:42 (Surah 28 al qasas verse 42)

Transaltion: in this world we made a curse to follow them.

2)kafa and qaffa قَفٰي (قَفَّي): al qafaa القفَاء means nape and qafa ,yakfoo قفا يَقفُوْا means to hit someone on nape and to follow sumone or behind sumone Mufraddat - مفرداتby Imam Raghib - (Arabic to Urdu) =(مف).(to follow sumthing ,to purse in a way that is irritating,peechay paar jaanaa)

Allah says in quran :

وَلَا تَقْفُ مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِ عِلْمٌ

17:36 (surah 17 al israa,verse 36)

Transaltion: (O man), follow not that whereof thou hast no knowledge

And qaffaa قَفَّي means to put sumthing behind other thing continually from this word the word qafia قافيه originated. It means to follow consecutively.

Allah says

وَقَفَّيْنَا مِن بَعْدِهِ بِالرُّسُلِ

2:87 (surah 2 al baqarah verse 87)

Translation: and followed him up with a succession of messengers.

3)aghraa اَغْرٰ means to provoke dog or any other animal used for hunting behind its prey Allah says :

فَأَغْرَيْنَا بَيْنَهُمُ الْعَدَاوَةَ وَالْبَغْضَاءَ

5:14 (surah 5 al maiodah,verse 14)

Translation: so we estranged them, with enmity and hatred between the one and the other.

Summary

1) aatbaa: is usual word comes in both senses transitive and intransitive verb. it means to follow or to put someone/sumthing on following

2) qafaa: means to pursue someone and qaffaa means to put something behind other continually .

3)aghraa :to provoke and make one thing to purse the other.
