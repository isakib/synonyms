32: PHAARNA [TO RIP]

Synonyms: KHARAQA, QADDA, FATARA, FAJARA, MAKHARA, SHAQQA, FALAQA, FARAQA, MAZZAQA

32.1 KHARAQA (خَرَقَ): To rip something and ruin it. To break something in order to destroy it. Kharaqah (خَرَقَه) refers to a piece of tattered cloth and khariq (خارق) means something non-typical. For example

Surah Al-Kahf 18:71

فَانطَلَقَا حَتَّىٰ إِذَا رَكِبَا فِي السَّفِينَةِ خَرَقَهَا ۖ قَالَ أَخَرَقْتَهَا لِتُغْرِقَ أَهْلَهَا لَقَدْ جِئْتَ شَيْئًا إِمْرًا

So they set out, until when they had embarked on the ship, al-Khidhr tore it open. [Moses] said, "Have you torn it open to drown its people? You have certainly done a grave thing."   

32.2 QADDA (قَدَّ): To rip something lengthwise. Also refers to a person’s height. Qadeed (قديد) refers to long slices of dried meat. For example

Surah Yusuf 12:25

وَاسْتَبَقَا الْبَابَ وَقَدَّتْ قَمِيصَهُ مِن دُبُرٍ

 “And they both raced to the door, and she tore his shirt from the back”

32.3 FATARA (فَطَرَ): To rip something lengthwise in order to crack or break it. To scratch. In this way, it can also mean to create or to give birth to. Futoor (فُطُوْر)  refers to a crack. The Quran says:

Surah Al-Mulk 27:3

فَارْجِعِ الْبَصَرَ هَلْ تَرَىٰ مِن فُطُورٍ

“So return [your] vision [to the sky]; do you see any breaks?”  

At another place, it says

Surah Ar-Rum 30:30

فِطْرَتَ اللَّهِ الَّتِي فَطَرَ النَّاسَ عَلَيْهَا

 “[Adhere to] the fitrah of Allah upon which He has created [all] people”

32.4 FAJARA (فَجَرَ): To break or rip something on a vast scale. Hence the time of breaking dawn is called Fajr (فَجَر) as it appears on the vast horizon. Fajoor (فجور) refers to blatant disregard for religious tenets. Fajir (فاجر) and Fajjar (فجّار) also rise from the same root. Fajara (فَجَرَ) and fajjara (فَجَّرَ) both refer to making water flow over a vast area. For example

Surah Al-Isra’ 17:90

وَقَالُوا لَن نُّؤْمِنَ لَكَ حَتَّىٰ تَفْجُرَ لَنَا مِنَ الْأَرْضِ يَنبُوعًا

‘And they say, "We will not believe you until you break open for us from the ground a spring.’

Fajjara (فَجَّرَ ) specifically refers to opening up a long waterway. For example

Surah Al-Kahf 18:33

وَفَجَّرْنَا خِلَالَهُمَا نَهَرًا

 “And We caused to gush forth within them a river.”

32.5 MAKHARA (مَخَرَ): Refers to erosion caused by water. Makhar-ul-maa-ul-arda (مَخَرُالْمَاءُالْاَرْضَ) refers to water eroding the earth and swirling in it. Makhar us safeena (مخرالسفينه) refers to a boat plowing through the water and making noise. Makhir (ماخِر) [plural: Mawakhir (مَوَاخِر)] is a boat which plows through the water and makes noise.

Surah An-Nahl 16:14

وَتَرَى الْفُلْكَ مَوَاخِرَ فِيهِ

 “And you see the ships plowing through it”

32.6 SHAQQA (شَقَّ): To cause something hard to burst or break. The Quran uses it to describe the cracking or bursting of such hard things like the sky, earth, moon and rocks. For example

Surah ‘Abasa 80:26

ثُمَّ شَقَقْنَا الْأَرْضَ شَقًّا

“Then We broke open the earth, splitting it”

 32.7 FALAQA and FARAQA (فَلَقَ, فَرَقَ): Both words are very similar in meaning and both contain two basic steps:  a. to burst or crack and then b. to separate. Parting the hair is referred to by using either  farq (فَرق) or filq (فِلْق). Therefore, the Quran uses both these words for the same meaning. For example

Surah Al-Baqarah 2:50

وَإِذْ فَرَقْنَا بِكُمُ الْبَحْرَ فَأَنجَيْنَاكُمْ وَأَغْرَقْنَا آلَ فِرْعَوْنَ وَأَنتُمْ تَنظُرُونَ

“And [recall] when We parted the sea for you and saved you and drowned the people of Pharaoh while you were looking on.”

At another point, the Quran says

Surah Ah-Shu’ara 26:63

فَأَوْحَيْنَا إِلَىٰ مُوسَىٰ أَنِ اضْرِب بِّعَصَاكَ الْبَحْرَ ۖ فَانفَلَقَ فَكَانَ كُلُّ فِرْقٍ كَالطَّوْدِ الْعَظِيم

 ‘Then We inspired to Moses, "Strike with your staff the sea," and it parted, and each portion was like a great towering mountain.’

However, there is a slight difference in the meaning. Falaqa is often used when something breaks into two approximately equal parts. Falaqa or filq refer to a broken part of the original thing. Faraqa is more generic in its use and can refer to two or more than two parts.  The second difference is that falaqa is usually used for non-living things whereas faraqa can be used for both non-living and living thing especially humans. A separated part of a class is called firq and if this separated part is big enough, it is called faraqah. In a dispute, one side is called fareeq (فريق). For example

Surah Al-An’am 6:95

إِنَّ اللَّهَ فَالِقُ الْحَبِّ وَالنَّوَىٰ

“Indeed, Allah is the cleaver of grain and date seeds”

Similarly, in Surah Al-An’am 6:96, falaqa is used because the sunlight breaks the sky in a clear line.

فَالِقُ الْإِصْبَاحِ  

 “[He is] the cleaver of daybreak”

32.9 MAZZAQA (مَزَّقَ): To rip, to disperse, to destroy. Includes the intensity if action. For example

Surah Saba’ 34:19

فَجَعَلْنَاهُمْ أَحَادِيثَ وَمَزَّقْنَاهُمْ كُلَّ مُمَزَّقٍ

“so We made them narrations and dispersed them in total dispersion”  

Summary:

32.1 KHARAQA: To rip something and ruin it

32.2 QADDA: To rip something lengthwise

32.3 FATARA: To rip, to scratch

32.4 FAJARA: To break something on a vast scale

32.5 MAKHARA: A boat ripping through the water and making noise

32.6 SHAQQA: To rip or break something hard

32.7 FALAQA: To break a non-living thing into two separate parts

32.8 FARAQA: To break something into two or more separate pieces

32.9 MAZZAQA: To destroy by breaking into small scattered pieces
