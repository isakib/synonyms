Subhan (p.261)

According to Ibn-e-Faaris, two types of meanings may originate from the word 'Sabaha'.

i) Asort of Ibadah

ii)A sort of Running (meem-laam)

And Imam Raaghib wrote 'floating' or 'gliding' or 'quick passing of an object through water or air' as the meanings of 'Sabaha'.(muff)

'Sabbaah' means a Natator or a Glider. and 'Farasun Sabooh' refers to a horse which does not move to either side due it's fast speed.(munjad)

Allah(swt) says:

وَهُوَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ اللَّيْلَ وَالنَّهَارَ وَالشَّمْسَ وَالْقَمَرَ ۖ كُلٌّ فِي فَلَكٍ يَسْبَحُونَ

"And He it is Who has created the night and the day, and the sun and the moon, each in an orbit floating." [21:33]

In another Verse:

وَالسَّابِحَاتِ سَبْحًا

"And by those(angels) that swim along." [79:3]

And later on, they started using this word in the meaning of doing something quickly. As Allah(swt) says:

إِنَّ لَكَ فِي النَّهَارِ سَبْحًا طَوِيلًا

"Verily, there is for you by day prolonged occupation with ordinary duties." [73:7]

Subhan is a verbal noun which stems from 'Sabaha' like 'Ghufran' from 'ghafara'.

There are millionsand billions of planets in the space revolving in their orbits and the never palpitate or swing neither do the bump or collide with one another. So, it is obvious that their Controlling Entity could only be the One who is sound and deviceful in His contrivance and management, free of any miscue or imperfection and that is exactly what Subhan means.

And furthermore; such a wise planner who is contriving the affairs of this universe with His excellent contrivance must be the Only Super Power free of any partners. Because the interference of any second party may infest and thus embroil the discipline of this Universe. Hence 'Subhan' refers to that Being who is free of any imperfections having absolute authority over all the matters and is montioring the movements of all the objects that comprise this universe.

Allah(swt) says:

وَقَالُوا اتَّخَذَ اللَّهُ وَلَدًا ۗ سُبْحَانَهُ ۖ بَلْ لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ ۖ كُلٌّ لَهُ قَانِتُونَ

"And they (Jews, Christians and pagans) say: Allâh has begotten a son (children or offspring). Glory be to Him (Exalted be He above all that they associate with Him). Nay, to Him belongs all that is in the heavens and on earth, and all surrender with obedience (in worship) to Him."[2:116]

And these movements or actions of these objects as in accordance with the laws prescribed by their Wise Contriver are named as 'Their Tasbeeh (Proclaimation of the perfection and glorification of ALLAH(SWT) in the way He taught), or Obedience(Submission) or Worship.

So, all the things ,this universe contains, confirm silently through their actions that the Entity Who is contriving them is free of any imperfections and having any partneres.

Allah(swt) says:

سَبَّحَ لِلَّهِ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۖ وَهُوَ الْعَزِيزُ الْحَكِيمُ

"Whatsoever is in the heavens and whatsoever is on the earth glorifies Allâh. And He is the All-Mighty, the All-Wise."[61:1] 

Another aspect of this universal management system is that man gets deeply surprised to see it's perfect discipline in which there is not even a moment's delays neither it's get stuck nor clash. So, his tongue cannot help proclaiming the greatness and glorification of the One managing this universe. So, the word 'Subhan' can also be used in those moments of amazement when one is taken aback by the outstanding,artistic pieces of work by The Creator of the universe. Therefore; it is a word which can be used to express amazement as well as the transcedence.

Allah(swt) says:

سُبْحَانَ الَّذِي أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِ لَيْلًا مِنَ الْمَسْجِدِ الْحَرَامِ إِلَى الْمَسْجِدِ الْأَقْصَى

"Glorified (and Exalted) is He (Allâh) [above all that (evil) they associate with Him]. Who took His slave (Muhammad SAW) for a journey by night from Al-Masjid-al-Harâm (at Makkah) to Al-Masjid-al-Aqsa (in Jerusalem)." [17:1]

Here, the usage of word 'Subhan' marks the fact that this journey was physical and not a spiritual one.

Sabaha (p.344)

To do Tasbeeh(to remeber ALLAH with a certain profound remembrance that ALLAH(SWT) Himself has taught).

To proclaim Subhaan ALLAH or to do dhikr of ALLAH (i.e remembring ALLAH with the tongue with the involvement of the heart by certain words taught by Himself).

To declare the perfection of the One Who is free of any flaws or imperfections. To remember His attributes positively and praise Him out of love for Him.

End of QATADA post

2 QADDASA(قَدَّسَ):QUDDOS(قَُدَّوس) ,ALLAH's name,meaning entity that is pure from counterparts and partners and QADDAS(قدّس) meaning to remember/invoke/pronounce the purity of such an entity,and to negate/repudiate expressions which are not befitting to the majesty of ALLAH

Surat Al-Baqarah [2:30] - The Noble Qur'an

while we declare Your praise and sanctify You?

3 HASHA(حَاشَ): expression of exclamation. and HASHA ALLAH (حَاشَ الّله)same as SUBHAN ALLAH(سبحان الّله), but is very limited inmeaning.it's an expression of negation and exclusion.it is not used even as expression of exclamation

Surat Yusuf [12:31] - The Noble Qur'an

فَلَمَّا رَأَيْنَهُ أَكْبَرْنَهُ وَقَطَّعْنَ أَيْدِيَهُنَّ وَقُلْنَ حَاشَ لِلَّهِ مَا هَٰذَا بَشَرًا إِنْ هَٰذَا إِلَّا مَلَكٌ كَرِيمٌ

قَالَ مَا خَطْبُكُنَّ إِذْ رَاوَدتُّنَّ يُوسُفَ عَن نَّفْسِهِ ۚ قُلْنَ حَاشَ لِلَّهِ مَا عَلِمْنَا عَلَيْهِ مِن سُوءٍ ۚ

and KALLA(كلّا) is sometimes used with HASHA ,HASHA WA KALLA meaning to strongly deny allegations against oneself or others.this is not used in the Quran.so HASHA includes the connotation of strong denial of allegation

2 QADDAS:to reject and deny,bad and negative attribute of an entity or thing while remembering that entity or thing

3 HASHA:used in meaning of SUBHAn and comes for denial of allegations

4 .Sabaha (p.808) 

To float; According to Imam Raaghib, this word refers to something floating or gliding or it's quick passing through water or air. (muff)

Allah(swt) says:

وَكُلٌّ فِي فَلَكٍ يَسْبَحُونَ

"They all float, each in an orbit." [36:40]

And then later on, they started using this word in the meaning of 'quickly doing something'.

ALLAH(SWT) says in Qura'n:

إِنَّ لَكَ فِي النَّهَارِ سَبْحًا طَوِيلًا

"Verily, there is for you by day prolonged occupation with ordinary duties." [73:7] 

Subhaan: such a state of busyness in which speediness is dominent due to heavy work load.

END OF QATADA POST

Summary:

1 SHUGL: general,meaning to get occupied in some work

2 KHADA: to be involved with full attention,used in bad meaning

3 AFADA:such an involvment in some work which makes one forgetful of oneself

4 SABHAN:such an occupation/engagement in which swiftness is prominent owing to work load
