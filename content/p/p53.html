Other derivatives of behind -53

Furthermore some derivatives of behind for which Quran uses only one word they are mentioned below:

For example :

1)to walk behind the word used is itbaa اِتَّبَعَ

And to look behind the word is altafaat اِلْتَفَتَ:lafata لَفَتَ means to turn sumthing towards left or right Maqaayeesul-lughat - مقائيس اللغة(Arabic to Arabic) = (م-ل)

and altafat التَفت means to turn and watch. Giving attention and looking behind.altafaat التَفاتis a common word.

ALLAh says :

فَأَسْرِ بِأَهْلِكَ بِقِطْعٍ مِّنَ اللَّيْلِ وَاتَّبِعْ أَدْبَارَهُمْ وَلَا يَلْتَفِتْ مِنكُمْ أَحَدٌ

15:65 (surah 15 al hijr verse no 65)

Translation: Then travel by night with thy household, when a portion of the night (yet remains), and do thou bring up the rear: let no one amongst you look back

3)moving behind for this khunaasa خنَّسَ is used:it means moving behind and shrinking Munjid - منجد - Dictionary - (Arabic to Urdu) and also means walking staright and taking one step backMufraddat - مفرداتby Imam Raghib - (Arabic to Urdu) =(مف).to hide and move back .and khanis خانِس means the one who moves behind and its plural is khunas خُنَّس.

Allah says :

فَلَا أُقْسِمُ بِالْخُنَّسِ

81:15 (surah 81 at takwir,verse number 15)

Transaltion: Those that run [their courses] and disappear.

And from khanis خانِس derives the word khanaas خَنّاس (super lative noun) means the one who has made it a routine of stepping back again and again.

Ism e mubalgha ( an ism or noun that is used to show force or strength ) a grammatical term in Arabic for superlative noun .

Allah says

مِن شَرِّ الْوَسْوَاسِ الْخَنَّاسِ

114:4 (surah 114 surah an naas,verse number 4)

Translation: From the mischief of the Whisperer (of Evil), who withdraws (after his whisper).
