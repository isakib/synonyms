5.Paani aur uski Aqsaam پانی اور اسکی اقسام (Water and its Types)

A commonly used word for water is ‘Maa-un’ مَاءٌ, whether the described water is muddy or clean, sweet or has alkalinity. The following examples of water are discussed in the Holy Quran:

Hameem حَمِيم, Ghassaaq غَسَّاق, Aanin اَنٍ and Aaniyatin اَنِيَةٍ, Ghaor غَور, Ma’een مَعِين, Azb عَزب, Furaat فُرَات, Milh مِلحٌ, Ujaaj اُجَاج.

1.      Hameem حَمِيم: ‘Hamma’ حَمَّ means to be hot. ‘Hammaa’ حَمَّى means temperature or fever. ‘Hameem’ حميم is known to be the water that becomes scalding and often times reeks due to its presence in the sun. The scholars of linguistics write its meaning as ‘scalding hot water’.

 

2.      Ghassaaq غَسَّاق: It is normally translated as pus or oozing pus or purulence. However, the scholars of linguistics and scholars of Arabic literature unanimously state its meaning to be cold and smelly water. The Holy Quran has also verified the same meaning, since both words ‘Hameem’ حَمِيم and ‘Ghassaaq’ غَسَّاق have been used in the Holy Quran in contrast. For example:

 

(i)                 Surah An Naba (The Tidings) سُوۡرَةُ النّبَإِ

 

(78:24-25)

 

لَّا يَذُوقُونَ فِيہَا بَرۡدً۬ا وَلَا شَرَابًا (﻿٢٤﻿) إِلَّا حَمِيمً۬ا وَغَسَّاقً۬ا

 

“They will not taste therein [any] coolness or drink - Except scalding water and [foul] purulence”

 

(ii)               Surah As Sad (The Letter Saad) سُوۡرَةُ صٓ

(38:56-57)

جَهَنَّمَ يَصۡلَوۡنَہَا فَبِئۡسَ ٱلۡمِهَادُ (﻿٥٦﻿) هَـٰذَا فَلۡيَذُوقُوهُ حَمِيمٌ۬ وَغَسَّاقٌ۬

“Hell, which they will [enter to] burn, and wretched is the resting place. This - so let them taste it - is scalding water and [foul] purulence.”

 

3.      Aanin اَنٍ –Aaniyatin اَنِيَةٍ: ‘Anaa’ اَنَى means for the water to boil or its boiling time to be near. ‘Aanin’ اَنٍ means such hot water that it starts scorching and scalding.

Allah Taa’la says:

 

Surah Ar Rahman (The Beneficent) سُوۡرَةُ الرَّحمٰن

 

(55:44)

 

يَطُوفُونَ بَيۡنَہَا وَبَيۡنَ حَمِيمٍ ءَانٍ۬

“They will go around between it and scalding water, heated [to the utmost degree].”

 

In another location, Allah Taa’la says:

 

Surah Al Ghashiyah (The Overwhelming) سُوۡرَةُ الغَاشِیَة

 

(88:4-5)

 

تَصۡلَىٰ نَارًا حَامِيَةً۬ (﻿٤﻿) تُسۡقَىٰ مِنۡ عَيۡنٍ ءَانِيَةٍ۬

 

“They will [enter to] burn in an intensely hot Fire. They will be given drink from a boiling spring.”

 

4.      Ghaur غَور: Water that is underground; in a depth. The Arabic scholars state its meaning as water that tends to absorb in the ground.

 

5.      Ma’een مَعِين: ‘Ma’ana’ مَعَنَ means water that flows smoothly. ‘Ma’een’ مَعِين is described as water that runs on the surface of the ground. Unlike a gushing flood, it is flowing rather smoothly, peacefully and serenely.

Therefore, Allah Taa’la says:

 

Surah Al Mulk (The Sovereignty) سُوۡرَةُ المُلک

 

(67:30)

 

قُلۡ أَرَءَيۡتُمۡ إِنۡ أَصۡبَحَ مَآؤُكُمۡ غَوۡرً۬ا فَمَن يَأۡتِيكُم بِمَآءٍ۬ مَّعِينِۭ

 

“Say, "Have you considered: if your water was to become sunken [into the earth], then who could bring you flowing water?"

 

In terms of taste:

 

6.      Azb عَزب: Water that is sweet and freshly pleasing to taste; such water that one would desire to drink without a reason.

 

7.      Furaat فُرَات: ‘Furaat’ فُرَات means sea. It is also the name of a great river that falls into the Persian Gulf. ‘Furataan’ فُرَاتَان is known to be ‘furaat’ فُرَات and the river Daljh دلجہ. ‘Furaat-il-maa’ فُرَاتِ المَاءِ means for the water to be pleasant and sweet in taste as though ‘furaat’ فُرَات carries the qualities of ‘azb’ عَزب, yet be great in quantity.

 

8.      Milh مِلحٌ: Alkaline/saltwater/brine.
