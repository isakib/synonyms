21. Shelter

For shelter, (i) خيَام, (ii) ظُلَّة and (iii) سُرَادِق

(i) خيَام

It is the plural of خیمہ. It refers to a tent that is made out of thick cloth or mat to keep shelter from rain or sun. These have a slope instead of a proper rooftop. Followers of Munjid refer it to as any tent which is not made up of bricks, stones or clay. خیمہ is also a camel's seat or saddle. Allah says in the Quran:

حُورٌ مَقْصُورَاتٌ فِي الْخِيَامِ

Houris (beautiful, fair females) restrained in pavilions (55:72)

(ii) ظُلَّة

This is a shelter which has a rooftop but no walls. This word is also used for an umbrella. ظلِيل is a tree with big canopy (Munjid). ظِلّ means shadow and ظِلّاً ظلِيلَاً refers to as long and thick shadows. Allah says in the Quran:

وَإِذْ نَتَقْنَا الْجَبَلَ فَوْقَهُمْ كَأَنَّهُ ظُلَّةٌ

And (remember) when We raised the mountain over them as if it had been a canopy, (7:171)

(iii) سُرَادِق

This is a plural of سردق and this word is derived from Persian. It is a tent, normally used at weddings, which has only walls, but no roof. Allah says in the Quran:

إِنَّا أَعْتَدْنَا لِلظَّالِمِينَ نَارًا أَحَاطَ بِهِمْ سُرَادِقُهَا

Verily, We have prepared for the Zalimun (polytheists and wrong-doers, etc.), a Fire whose walls will be surrounding them (disbelievers in the Oneness of Allah). (18:29)

22. To enter:

The words used for entering are (i) دَخَلَ and اَدخَلَ, (ii) وَلَجَ and اَولَجَ, (iii) صَلى and صَلّى, and (iv) سَلَكَ.

(i) دَخَلَ

This is a commonly used word, meaning to enter. Its opposite is خَرَجَ, meaning to go out. Allah says in the Quran:

وَدَخَلَ مَعَهُ السِّجْنَ فَتَيَانِ

And there entered with him two young men in the prison. (12:36)

Another word used for entering (passive) is اَدخَلَ. Allah says in the Quran:

يُّدْخِلُ مَنْ يَّشَاءُ فِيْ رَحْمَتِه

He will admit to His Mercy whom He will (76:31)

(ii) وَلَجَ

الولولج is the word used when you enter a narrow or confined space (M.F.) or to enter with difficulty. Allah says in the Quran:

وَلَا يَدْخُلُوْنَ الْجَنَّةَ حَتّٰي يَلِجَ الْجَمَلُ فِيْ سَمِّ الْخِيَاطِ

and they will not enter Paradise until the camel goes through the eye of the needle (which is impossible) (7:41)

اَولَجَ is used to make someone enter with difficulty (used passively) Allah says in the Quran:

يُولِجُ اللَّيْلَ فِي النَّهَارِ وَيُولِجُ النَّهَارَ فِي اللَّيْلِ

That is because Allah merges the night into the day, and He merges the day into the night. And verily, Allah is All-Hearer, All-Seer. (22:61)

(iii) صَلى

This word is used specifically for fire, to fall into fire and burn (M.F.). Allah says in the Quran:

جَهَنَّمَ يَصْلَوْنَهَا ۖ وَبِئْسَ الْقَرَارُ

Hell, in which they will burn, - and what an evil place to settle in! (14:29)

Similarly, صَلّى is used for throwing into fire.

Allah says in the Quran:

خُذُوهُ فَغُلُّوهُثُمَّ الْجَحِيمَ صَلُّوهُ

(It will be said): "Seize him and fetter him, then throw him in the blazing Fire. (69:30-31)

(iv) سَلَكَ

This word has multiple meanings, both in imperative and optional meanings, all of which mean'to enter'.

1. It means to insert, like inserting thread into a bead. Allah says in the Quran:

كَذَٰلِكَ سَلَكْنَاهُ فِي قُلُوبِ الْمُجْرِمِينَ

Thus have We caused it (the denial of the Qur'an) to enter the hearts of the Mujrimun (criminals, polytheists, sinners, etc.). (26:200)

2. To enter as in the famous urdu idiomگریبان میں ہاتھ ڈالنا (putting one's hand in one's bosom). Allah says in the Quran:

اسْلُكْ يَدَكَ فِي جَيْبِكَ تَخْرُجْ بَيْضَاءَ مِنْ غَيْرِ سُوءٍ

Put your hand in your bosom, it will come forth white without a disease (28:32)

A similar ayah is used with the word وَأَدْخِلْ at another place:

وَأَدْخِلْ يَدَكَ فِي جَيْبِكَ تَخْرُجْ بَيْضَاءَ مِنْ غَيْرِ سُوءٍ

And put your hand into your bosom, it will come forth white without hurt. (27:12)

3. To go along with the way. Allah addresses honey bee in the Quran:

فَاسْلُكِي سُبُلَ رَبِّكِ ذُلُلًا ۚ

follow the ways of your Lord made easy (for you). (16:69)

Allah also says in the Quran:

مَا سَلَكَكُمْ فِي سَقَرَ

"What has caused you to enter Hell?" (74:42)

Followers of Muntahi Al-arb translate سَلَكَ as pulling one thing from between the second. Followers of Munjid translate it as keeping one thing between the other. Followers of Maqaayeesul-lughat give the following two meanings: 1) to go along the way and 2) to insert.

Conclusion:

(i) دَخَلَ is commonly used for entering

(ii) وَلَجَ is to enter into a confined space

(iii) صَلى to enter the fire

(iv) سَلَكَ to make one thing enter through the second.
