20- Breech of trust

For breech of trust, the words kha-na and ghalla have been used

1. Kha-na:

It means breech of trust and its antonym is to deposit. Breech of trust can be of promises as well as in embezzlement of deposited money, if all or some of it is stolen by fraud or confiscation. Similarly,it is used for breaking promises. To deceive.

These can be clarified from the following ayah:

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا تَخُونُوا اللَّـهَ وَالرَّسُولَ وَتَخُونُوا أَمَانَاتِكُمْ

O you who believe! Betray not Allah and His Messenger, nor betray knowingly your Amanat (things entrusted to you, and all the duties which Allah has ordained for you). (8:27)

also see "to cheat"

2. Ghalla:

This means to hide a stolen property among your things or to pack it in a manner that it can not be seen (Munjad, M.L.)

and غلول is used in the context of breaking trust, closing, and miserliness. Therefore, there is an element of burglary along with breech of trust in غَلَّ or the means of breech of trust were burglary.

وَمَا كَانَ لِنَبِيٍّ أَنْ يَغُلَّ ۚ وَمَنْ يَغْلُلْ يَأْتِ بِمَا غَلَّ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ

It is not for any Prophet to take illegally a part of booty (Ghulul), and whosoever deceives his companions as regards the booty, he shall bring forth on the Day of Resurrection that which he took (illegally). (3:161)

Conclusion:

خََان is used in the breaking of promises or embezzlement of money by deception or fraud whereas غَلَّ has an element of burglary in the breech of trust.
