1 HAAJAT

   [to want]

                   

SYNONYMS: HAAJAT, MAARIB, AND WATAR.

 

1.1  HAAJAT (حَاجَة) : الحوج means a need and worry. A need or desire which, if not fulfilled causes the heart to feel distress and suffocation.

Refer to 12:68 and 59:9

_________________________________

1.2  MAARIB (مَاٰرِب) : اَرب and اِرب refers to a need without which there is no option and you have to make an effort to gain it.

Refer to 24:31

And مَاٰرب is the plural of ماربة which means a need or wish which is fulfilled.

Refer to 20:18

__________________________________

1.3  WATAR (وَطَر) : means 1) a desire which is sought for. 2) to have intercourse, eroticism. Its plural is اوطار . In the Quran it is only used in the second meaning.

Refer to 33:37

__________________________________

SUMMARY

HAAJATA: a need which when not fulfilled causes distress.

MAARIB: needs which are fulfilled

WATAR: it has been used in the Quran for needs in plural but there are extensions in its meaning.
