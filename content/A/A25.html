25 AANA [COMING ]
Synonyms: JAA-(JEE-),ATAA,HYTA,HALOMMA,TA3AL(3ALO)

25.1 JAA-(JEE-)((جَاء(جِئ): It is generally used for 'coming' . but it is necessary that the act of coming had taken place. ALLAH said

Surat Yūsuf (Joseph) - سورة يوسف
12:16
وَجَاءُوا أَبَاهُمْ عِشَاءً يَبْكُونَ
And they came to their father at night, weeping.


25.2 ATAA( اَتَي): meaning to come as result of some event/process or come with ease like churning milk results in butter ATUWWA( اَتُوّة) and AATI(إِتِي) is flood
the difference between JAA- and ATAA is that in ATAA it is not necessary that the act of coming had taken place. as in the ayah below

Surat An-Naĥl (The Bee) - سورة النحل
16:1
أَتَىٰ أَمْرُ اللَّهِ فَلَا تَسْتَعْجِلُوهُ ۚ سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ عَمَّا يُشْرِكُونَ
The command of Allah is coming, so be not impatient for it. Exalted is He and high above what they associate with Him.

the command of Allah in above ayah refers to Judgement day which is yet to happen

25.3 HYTA(هَيْتَ):This word is used as 2nd person command of AATA which changes with the attached pronoun HYTA LAKA(هَيْتَ لَكَ)
HYTA LAKUMA(هَيْتَ لَكُمَا)
HYTA LAKUM(هَيْتَ لَكُمْ).
Ibne Faris said that HYTA refers to shouting. some said that it means to come here,come there,come quickly. In the Quran

Surat Yūsuf (Joseph) - سورة يوسف
12:23
وَرَاوَدَتْهُ الَّتِي هُوَ فِي بَيْتِهَا عَن نَّفْسِهِ وَغَلَّقَتِ الْأَبْوَابَ وَقَالَتْ هَيْتَ لَكَ ۚ قَالَ مَعَاذَ اللَّهِ ۖ إِنَّهُ رَبِّي أَحْسَنَ مَثْوَايَ ۖ إِنَّهُ لَا يُفْلِحُ الظَّالِمُونَ
And she, in whose house he was, sought to seduce him. She closed the doors and said, "Come, you." He said, "[I seek] the refuge of Allah . Indeed, he is my master, who has made good my residence. Indeed, wrongdoers will not succeed."

25.4 HALOMMA(هَلُمَّ): It's from Verbal Noun. HALOMMA means to call out someone by keep on calling .this is very rarely used. In the Quran

Surat Al-'Aĥzāb (The Combined Forces) - سورة الأحزاب
33:18
قَدْ يَعْلَمُ اللَّهُ الْمُعَوِّقِينَ مِنكُمْ وَالْقَائِلِينَ لِإِخْوَانِهِمْ هَلُمَّ إِلَيْنَا ۖ وَلَا يَأْتُونَ الْبَأْسَ إِلَّا قَلِيلًا
Already Allah knows the hinderers among you and those [hypocrites] who say to their brothers, "Come to us," and do not go to battle, except for a few,


25.5 TA3AL(3ALO)((تعََال (علو)): This 3ALO meaning height and TA3AL meaning to invite someone to a higher place or a higher cause, to call with respect only used as 2nd person command

Surat 'Āli `Imrān (Family of Imran) - سورة آل عمران
3:64
قُلْ يَا أَهْلَ الْكِتَابِ تَعَالَوْا إِلَىٰ كَلِمَةٍ سَوَاءٍ بَيْنَنَا وَبَيْنَكُمْ أَلَّا نَعْبُدَ إِلَّا اللَّهَ وَلَا نُشْرِكَ بِهِ شَيْئًا وَلَا يَتَّخِذَ بَعْضُنَا بَعْضًا أَرْبَابًا مِّن دُونِ اللَّهِ ۚ فَإِن تَوَلَّوْا فَقُولُوا اشْهَدُوا بِأَنَّا مُسْلِمُونَ
Say, "O People of the Scripture, come to a word that is equitable between us and you - that we will not worship except Allah and not associate anything with Him and not take one another as lords instead of Allah ." But if they turn away, then say, "Bear witness that we are Muslims [submitting to Him]."

Summary:
25.1JAA-:generally used and for something thing that that already happened
25.2 AATA:come as a result of some event/process or come with ease. It may be used for events yet to happen
25.3 HYTA: to call someone to the caller by shouting
25.4 HALUMMA: to call out repeatedly
25.5 TA3AL: to call/invite to higher cause/purpose or to call/invite with respect
