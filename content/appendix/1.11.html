11 3BADAAT AUR SHAR3Y ISTALAHAT

[ Acts of Devotion and Religious Terminology ]

There are five pillars of Islam. among them the first is shahadah by which a person enters Islam. the remaining four pillars are comprised of acts of devotion.All of them and their brief descriptions are mentioned in the Holy Quran. and they are SALAH( صَلوة), ZAKAH(زَكوة),SAUWM(صَوْم) and HAJJ(حجّ). and these words are used in the Quran as Religious Terminology. Which means that complete meaning and understanding of these words can only be obtained from the Sunnah(sayings and teachings,actions ) of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). Lexicon will be unable to solve this knot.

1 SALAH: people of lexicon gives its meaning as to pray for someone and to applause and bless. and SALLA(صَلّي) meaning to offer prayer. In the Holy Quran , its command came nearly 700 times yet it neither mentions the count of daily prayers nor the units of prayers and nor the method of prayer and nor the details of the timing of prayers. From Quran ,one knows of only 3 prayers and their times. at forenoon, in the evening and at the time of dawn. Also mentioned the middle prayer was mentioned but it is not explained as which one is it. though mention of congregational prayer is in abundance. for this the term AQAMAT ASSALAH(اقامت الصّلوة) is used. before the mention of offering prayer was mentioned the mandatory acts of Purification and Ablution. and of pillars of Prayer i.e Bowing,Standing, Prostration.

RAKA3(ركع) literally means to bow, but in terminology it means to bow the back in a peculiar and known form in prayer which also includes the element of humbless and humility. from RAKA3 is derived the word RAKA3AT(ركعت) the unit of prayer. which means that this is the most important of pillar of prayer. furthermore this word is used in the Quran for complete prayer. ALLAH said

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - سورة البقرة

2:43

وَأَقِيمُوا الصَّلَاةَ وَآتُوا الزَّكَاةَ وَارْكَعُوا مَعَ الرَّاكِعِينَ

And establish prayer and give zakah and bow with those who bow [in worship and obedience].

SAJADA(سَجَدَ): literally means to show humility and humbleness.and in that meaning every thing of this universe prostrate in front of ALLAH. but in Religious terminology it means a peculiar and known form of sitting and placing forehead and nose on the ground. like bowing ,prostration is also an important part of prayer . 2 ZAKAH:(alms giving)literally means to grow, to increase, to nurture. and in religious terminology it means that part of one's excess of wealth that is made mandatory by religion to give charity in the way of ALLAH . ZAKAH was mentioned nearly 70 times in the Quran but details of Wealth on which Zakah is applicable,Measure/Qualification of Zakah and Explaination of Zakah is not mentioned. this is only found in the Sunnah of Prophet (PBUH). Zakah is also called SADQAH(صدقة) in the Quran. and its the responsibility of an Islamic state that it receives Zakah from the qualifying people to where it must be spend in the permissble usage of Zakah.

ALLAH said

Surat At-Tawbah (The Repentance) - سورة التوبة

9:103

Take, [O, Muhammad], from their wealth a charity by which you purify them and cause them increase, and invoke [ Allah 's blessings] upon them. Indeed, your invocations are reassurance for them. And Allah is Hearing and Knowing.

SAQDAH can be voluntarily or mandatory. Zakah is the other name of mandatory charity. In this ayah خُذْ implies this charity to be mandatory i.e Zakah. also proven from this word is that receiving Zakah and establishing a system of Zakah is responsibility of Isamic Government.

Zakah is an act of devotion by wealth and is the third most important pillar of Islam after prayer. yet in act of devotion by wealth, also includes voluntary charity,and penalty for some sins, sacrifice and votive offering. votive offering is that SADQAH which a person conditioned on oneself to be mandatory(Vow) then it becomes mandatory to fullfill it. and sacrifice (especially on the occassion of Eid Ul Azha) is mandatory on all who are capable.

Then complete the fast until the sunset. And do not have relations with them as long as you are staying for worship in the mosques. These are the limits [set by] Allah , so do not approach them. Thus does Allah make clear His ordinances to the people that they may become righteous.

and in religious terminology AL3KWF(العكوف) means to concentrate on something out of respect and to associate with it and A3TAKAF means to stay in the mosque with the intention of worship and not to go out.

There is a deep relation between fasting and A3TAKAF. A3TAKAF is FARD KIFAYA(الفرض الكفاية) by Shariah in every mosque in the month of fasting's last 10 days. and ALLAH has also mentioned A3TAKAF along with Fasting in the aforementioned ayah. yet fasting and A3TAKAF are not complementary to each other. A3TAKAF can be done without fasting and also in other times apart from the month of fasting. and like from point of view of purification of soul , A3TAKAF has a special relation with fasting. likewise from point of view of mosque , A3TAKAF has a special relation with the House of ALLAH (كعبه), ALLAH said
