14 ISM ISHARA [Demonstrative Noun/Pointing Noun]

In reality demonstrative nouns are ZA(ذَا) and ZANI( ذَانِ) etc.

these words are prefixed with HA(ها) and suffixed with K(ك) or LK(لك) transformed in the ISM ISHARA QAREEB(اسم اشاره قريب)[NEAR POINTING NOUNS] and ISM ISHARA BA3YD(اسم اشاره بعيد)[FAR POINTING NOUNS].for near pointing HAZA(هَذَا) is used. for Dual HAZAAN(هَذَانِ)and HAZYN(هَذَين) and for plural HAUWLAA-(هَؤُلَََاءِ) .see the examples

1- ISHARA QAREEB:

Singular Masculine

Surat Al-'Isrā' (The Night Journey) - سورة الإسراء

17:62

قَالَ أَرَأَيْتَكَ هَٰذَا الَّذِي كَرَّمْتَ عَلَيَّ لَئِنْ أَخَّرْتَنِ إِلَىٰ يَوْمِ الْقِيَامَةِ لَأَحْتَنِكَنَّ ذُرِّيَّتَهُ إِلَّا قَلِيلًا

[Iblees] said, "Do You see this one whom You have honored above me? If You delay me until the Day of Resurrection, I will surely destroy his descendants, except for a few."

4 HAZA and ZALIKA are also used with the addition of K(ك) of like, for e.g HAKAZA(هَكَذَا) meaning 'like this' and KAZALIKA(كَذَالِكَ) meaning 'like that'but in urdu translation all these demonstrative nouns are translated as ISHARA QAREEB because in urdu in pointing there is no difference relative to dual,plural,masculine,feminine. HAZA ZALIKA HAZIHI TILKA HAUWLAA- all got translated as "this". and KAZALIKA as "like this" or "as this".

5 HUNA(هُنا) :(meaning here)used to point the time and place.yet mostly used for pointing place. and like ZA ZAKA and ZALIKA is HUNA HUNAKA(هُنَاكَ) and HUNALIKA(هُنَالِكَ) are used .ALLAH said
