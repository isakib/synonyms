6 MANGNA [to ask]

Synonyms: TALABA, SA’ALA, IDDAA’, HAFA, AE’TARRA

6.1 TALABA (طَلَبَ): TALAB-ASH-SHAI (طَلَبَ الشَّیْ) means to ask for something, to make efforts for something. TILB (طِلْب) and TILBAH (طِلْبَۃ), a thing that is lent from someone or to make efforts in order to get something. This word is used for asking, wanting or finding. And is the initial state in case of asking for something. In the Quran

Surah Al-Hajj  (The Pilgrimage): سُوۡرَةُ الحَجّ 22:73

ضَعُفَ الطَّالِبُ وَالْمَطْلُوبُ

 Weak (indeed) are the seeker and the sought!

6.2 SA’ALA (سَاَلَ): Refers to two things 1. To inquire about something in order to get an answer for it. 2. To ask for something necessary, to plead for wealth or anything necessary and SUA’L (سُؤْل) is that necessity which the superego covets and one also declares it.

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:61

ۚ اهْبِطُوا مِصْرًا فَإِنَّ لَكُم مَّا سَأَلْتُمْ

Go down to Egypt, where you will have what you asked for.

6.3 IDDAA’ (اِدَّعَ): DAA’ (دَعَا) means to ask for, to call for, to pray for. And IDDAA’ (اِدَّعَ) has exaggeration in it. To call loudly while asking for something. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Yaseen (Yaseen): سورۃ ٰیٰس 36:57

لَهُمْ فِيهَا فَاكِهَةٌ وَلَهُم مَّا يَدَّعُونَ

They will have therein fruits (of all kinds) and all that they ask for.

6.4 HAFA (حَفَا): To exaggerate while asking for something, to put in extreme efforts. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Muhammad (Muhammad): سُوۡرَةُ محَمَّد 47:37

إِن يَسْأَلْكُمُوهَا فَيُحْفِكُمْ تَبْخَلُوا

 Were He to ask you for it, and press you, you would become tightfisted

In Another place in Holy Quran

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:187

يَسْأَلُونَكَ كَأَنَّكَ حَفِيٌّ عَنْهَا

They ask you as if you are responsible for it.

6.5 AE’TARRA (اِعْتَرَّ): ARR (عَرّ) and AETARR (اِعْتَرّ) both refer to the same meaning i.e. to ask for forgiveness and to confront someone for that. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Hajj (The Pilgrimage): سُوۡرَةُ الحَجّ 22:36

وَأَطْعِمُوا الْقَانِعَ وَالْمُعْتَرَّ

Eat thereof, and feed the beggar who does not ask (men), and the beggar who asks (men).

Summary:

6.1 TALABA: A necessity which the human superego covets and make efforts for it.

6.2 SA’ALA: A necessity which the superego covets and tell it to someone.

6.3 IDDAA’: A necessity which the superego covets and to ask for it loudly.

6.4 HAFA: A necessity which the superego covets and insists on it.

6.5 AE’TARRA: To face people for forgiveness.
