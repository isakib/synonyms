43 MEHRBAAN [Kind, Compassionate]

Synonyms: REHMAN, RAHEEM, RAU’OOF, LATEEF, HANAN

43.1 REHMAN (رحمٰن), 43.2 RAHEEM (رَاحِیْم): are exaggerated nouns of kindness.  Means extremely kind. REHM (رحم) has compassion, kindness and softness in it. The word RAHMAN (رحمان) is more amplified. Apart from all the other qualities it has a greater portion of kindness in it. REHMAN (رحمٰن) means someone who bestows his favors equally upon all the creation. And by favors we mean the things from which all the creations can equally get benefit from like sun, moon, stars, air, water, light and earth. These all favor can only be from Allah that is why Allah can only be referred as REHMAN (رحمان) no other being can be REHMAN (رحمان) but RAHEEM (رَاحِیْم) can be another human. This word is used for Prophet Muhammad (S.A.A.W) and also for his companions. When Allah is referred as RAHEEM (رَاحِیْم) then it means ‘the one who is kind to his virtuous people’. But this quality does not possess much because Allah listens to the call of all the poor and needy, and bestows his kindness upon them and lessens their tribulations irrespective of them being believers or infidels. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Fatiha (The Opening): سُوۡرَةُ الفَاتِحَة 1:1

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

In the Name of Allah, the Most Beneficent, the Most Merciful.

43.3 RAU’OOF (رءوف): RAA’FAH (رَأفَة) means to feel compassion to see someone in agony. To fill pity for and touched by it. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Noor (The light): سورۃ النور 24:2

الزَّانِيَةُ وَالزَّانِي فَاجْلِدُوا كُلَّ وَاحِدٍ مِّنْهُمَا مِائَةَ جَلْدَةٍ ۖ وَلَا تَأْخُذْكُم بِهِمَا رَأْفَةٌ فِي دِينِ

The adulteress and the adulterer—whip each one of them a hundred lashes, and let no pity towards them overcome you regarding God’s Law,

And RAU’OOF (رءوف) means soft hearted, who feels compassion seeing the agony of someone and buries himself in the worries of getting him out of agony. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:128

لَقَدْ جَاءَكُمْ رَسُولٌ مِّنْ أَنفُسِكُمْ عَزِيزٌ عَلَيْهِ مَا عَنِتُّمْ حَرِيصٌ عَلَيْكُم بِالْمُؤْمِنِينَ رَءُوفٌ رَّحِيمٌ

 There has come to you a messenger from among yourselves, concerned over your suffering, anxious over you. Towards the believers, he is compassionate and merciful.

43.4 LATEEF (لطیف): LUTF (لُطْف) consists of two main things 1. Subtlety, 2. Softness. And being fine and soft, or tiny, and LATEEF (لطیف) also refers to only a single thing, either subtlety or refinement, or knowledge of secret or tiny detail in a matter. For instance in the Quran

Surah Al-Ana’am (The Cattle): سورۃ الانعام 6:103

لَّا تُدْرِكُهُ الْأَبْصَارُ وَهُوَ يُدْرِكُ الْأَبْصَارَ ۖ وَهُوَ اللَّطِيفُ الْخَبِيرُ

No vision can grasp Him, but His grasp is over all vision. He is the Subtle, the Expert.

Or sometimes both the meaning are found in it, for instance to have knowledge of small and tinniest needs of a creature and out of kindness taking care of these needs. In this case LATEEF (لطیف) refers to ‘Kind’. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Ash-Shuraa (The Consultation) - سورة الشورى 42:19

اللَّهُ لَطِيفٌ بِعِبَادِهِ يَرْزُقُ مَن يَشَاءُ

Allah is Ever-Kind to His bondmen; He provides whomever He decides;

43.5 HANAN (حَنَان): ALHANEEN (اَلْحَنِیْن) to get attracted towards something due to affection. ‘حَنَتِ المَرْاَۃ اَوِ النَّاقَةُ لِوَلَدِھَا’ means a woman or she-camel was attracted towards her baby or being eager for it. HANNA (حَنَّ) means kind and compassionate, HANAN (حَنَان) means kindness and graciousness. HANNAN (حَنَّان)means a one who is compassionate. Also one of the beautiful names of Allah (dictionary al Manjad). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Maryam (Mary): سُوۡرَةُ مَریَم 19:13

وَحَنَانًا مِّن لَّدُنَّا وَزَكَاةً ۖ وَكَانَ تَقِيًّا

And (made him) sympathetic to men as a mercy (or a grant) from Us, and pure from sins [i.e. Yahya (John)]

Summary:

43.1 REHMAN: Very compassionate only Allah can be REHMAN.

43.2 RAHEEM: Very kind, this quality is second to REHMAN

43.3 RAU’OOF: One who feels compassion when seeing someone in agony.

43.4 LATEEF: Someone who knows and takes care of small things.

43.5 HANAN: The compassion and affection like that of a mother for her child.
