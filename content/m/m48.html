48 MAIKH [nail, peg]

Synonyms: DUSUR, AWTAAD

48.1 DUSUR (دُسُر): (Singular DISAAR (دِسَار)) DASARA (دَسَر) means to stab with a spear or drive a nail. DISAAR (دِسَار) is the old rope used to close the cracks in a plane. A nail (dictionary al Manjad). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Qamar (The moon): سورۃ القَمَر 54:13

وَحَمَلْنَاهُ عَلَىٰ ذَاتِ أَلْوَاحٍ وَدُسُرٍ

 

And We carried him on a [construction of] planks and nails,

48.2 AWTAAD (اَوْتَاد): (singular WATAD (وَتد)) WATAD (وتد) means a peg or a big nail. Can be either  wood or metal. WATDAAN (وتدان) are the bulged portions of shoulders. Thus WATD (وْتد) is anything that has some of the portion protruding out. WATADA (وَتَدَ) means he drove in a strong peg. WATD (وَتد) means a wooden nail. AWTAAD-UL-ARD (اَوْتَادُ الاَرْضِ) means a mountain. Allah says in the Quran

Surah An-Naba (The Tidings) : سورۃ النّبَإ 78:6

أَلَمْ نَجْعَلِ الْأَرْضَ مِهَادًا وَالْجِبَالَ أَوْتَادًا

Have We not made the earth as a bed, And the mountains as pegs?

Page 833

Summary:

48.1 DUSUR: DISAAR is a small nail that is driven to close a fissure or firmly close an opening.

48.2 AWTAAD: WATD is a large nail or peg, a portion of which is outside, and a greater area is in earth.
