32 MAZBOOT BANANA – KARNA [to strengthen, to make firm]

SYNONYMS: THABBATA, AHKAMA, AWTHAQA, SHADD, ATHQANA, RABATA, AZARA, AQQDA, WAKKADA, SANNADA, RASSA, SHAYYAD

32.1 THABBATA (ثَبَّتَ): Means to solidify something in its place, to strengthen, to make it stand firm. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Ibraheem (Abraham): سورۃ ابراھیم 14:27

يُثَبِّتُ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا بِالْقَوْلِ الثَّابِتِ فِي الْحَيَاةِ الدُّنْيَا وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ

Allah will establish in strength those who believe, with the word that stands firm, in this world and in the Hereafter;

32.2 AHKAMA (اَحْکم): Wisdom, to strengthen something with experience and profundity. And HAKEEM (حکیم) means a scholar of Laws and affairs. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Hajj (The Pilgrimage): سُوۡرَةُ الحَجّ 22:52

ثُمَّ يُحْكِمُ اللَّهُ آيَاتِهِ

 God affirms His revelations.

 

AYAT-E-MUHKMAAT (ایا ت محکمٰت) are those that have no ambiguity in meaning and words.

Page 814

32.3 AWTHAQA (اَوْثَقَ): Means to make an oath strong and reliable. Or bind someone firmly with ropes. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Fajr (The Dawn): سُوۡرَةُ الفَجر 89:26

وَلَا يُوثِقُ وَثَاقَهُ أَحَدٌ

And none will bind [as severely] as His binding [of the evildoers].

 

In another place in the Quran

Surah Al-Maeda (The table spread): سورۃ المَائدہ 5:7

وَاذْكُرُوا نِعْمَةَ اللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ وَمِيثَاقَهُ الَّذِي وَاثَقَكُم بِهِ

And remember the favor of Allah on you and His covenant with which He bound you firmly,

32.4 SHADD (شدّ): Something being strong and robust individually. Used as both transitive and intransitive. And SHADEED (شدید) means extreme, ASHUDD (اشُدّ) means that part of youth when strength is at its peak. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Insan (The Man) - سورة الانسان 76:28

نَّحْنُ خَلَقْنَاهُمْ وَشَدَدْنَا أَسْرَهُمْ

We have created them and strengthened their forms,

32.5 ATHQAN-AL-AMR (اَثْقَنَ الْاَمْر): Means to make a task firmly. And THAQQAN-AL-ARD (ثَقَّنَ الْاَرْضَ) means to strengthen and enriching earth by irrigating it with muddy and rich water. RAJULUN-THAQINUN (رَجُلٌ ثَقِنٌ) means someone who performs a task reliably. Expert in a task (dictionary al Manjad). Intelligent in work, and someone who performs a task correctly. This ATHQAN (اَثْقَنَ) means to strengthen something with expertise. Allah says in the Quran

Surah An-Naml (The ant): سورۃ النَّمل 27:88

صُنْعَ اللَّهِ الَّذِي أَتْقَنَ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ

The Work of Allah, Who perfected all things,

32.6 RABATA (رَبَطَ): Means to tie up firmly, an idiom. RABT ALLAHU ALA QALBHI (ربط اللہُ علٰی قلبه) means Allah bestowed his heart with strength and patience. And RIBAAT (رِبَاط) means something which other things are tied to. RABITA (رَابِطہ) means bonds and attachments (dictionary al Manjad). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al Qasas (The stories): سورۃ القَصَص 28:10

وَأَصْبَحَ فُؤَادُ أُمِّ مُوسَىٰ فَارِغًا ۖ إِن كَادَتْ لَتُبْدِي بِهِ لَوْلَا أَن رَّبَطْنَا عَلَىٰ قَلْبِهَا

And the heart of the mother of Musa (Moses) became empty [from every thought, except the thought of Musa (Moses)]. She was very near to disclose his (case, i.e. the child is her son), had We not strengthened her heart (with Faith)

 

32.7 AZARA (اٰزَرَ): AL-IZR (اَلْاِزْر) means root or TEHBAND (a cloth around the waist and passing between the legs); AL-AZAAR (الازار) means a sheet of cloth, TEHBAND, curtains, support or a wall. AAZAR-AN-NABATAT (اٰزَرَ النبات) means entwining of plants. AZZARAH (اَزَّرَہ) means to give strength, to empower (dictionary al Manjad). Thus AAZARA (اٰزَرَ) means to strengthen someone gradually by giving it power. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Fath (The victory): سورۃ الفَتْح  48:29

كَزَرْعٍ أَخْرَجَ شَطْأَهُ فَآزَرَهُ

 Like a plant that sprouts, becomes strong, grows thick, and rests on its stem,

Page 815

32.8 WAKKADA (وَکَّدَ): WIKAAD (وکاد) is that rope with which the legs of cows and other animals are tied together while milking them. WAKKAD (وَکَّد) or AKKAD (اَکَّدَ), or AWKAD-AS-SARJ-AWIL-AHAD (اَوْکَدَ السَّرج اَوِالْعَھْد) means to tie up firmly or strengthen an accord.

Surah An-Nahl (The Bee): سورۃ النّحل16:91

وَلَا تَنقُضُوا الْأَيْمَانَ بَعْدَ تَوْكِيدِهَا  

And do not break oaths after their confirmation

32.9 AQQDA (عَقَّدَ): AQADA (عَقَدَ) means to tie a knot; AQDAH (عقدۃ) means a knot, tangle, or a difficult matter. AQQAD-AL-BAE’-WAL-YAMEEN (عَقَّد البیع و الیمین) to an oath or (dictionary al Manjad). AQD (عَقْد) means an oath or a covenant; Declaration. (Plural AQOOD (عقود)) Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Maeda (The table spread): سورۃ المَائدہ 5:89

لَا يُؤَاخِذُكُمُ اللَّهُ بِاللَّغْوِ فِي أَيْمَانِكُمْ وَلَٰكِن يُؤَاخِذُكُم بِمَا عَقَّدتُّمُ الْأَيْمَانَ

Allah will not call you to account for what is futile in your oaths, but He will call you to account for your deliberate oaths:

32.10 SANNADA (سَنَّدَ): SANADA (سَنَدَ) to trust, to rely, to get support from, SANNADA (سَنَّدَ) means to give support and strengthen (dictionary al Manjad). For instance when the wood supporting the roof gets weak, then another wood log is place under them to support and strengthen the roof. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Munafiqoon (The Hypocrites) - سورة المنافقون 63:4

كَأَنَّهُمْ خُشُبٌ مُّسَنَّدَةٌ ۖ يَحْسَبُونَ كُلَّ صَيْحَةٍ عَلَيْهِمْ

[They are] as if they were pieces of wood propped up - they think that every shout is against them.

32.11 RASSA (رَصَّ): Means to connect something, to join, to sinter RASAS (رصاص) also means lead. Thus RASSAH (رَصَّ) means to strengthen something by adding molten lead to it. Allah says in the Quran

Surah As-Saf (The Ranks) - سورة الصف 61:4

إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ الَّذِينَ يُقَاتِلُونَ فِي سَبِيلِهِ صَفًّا كَأَنَّهُم بُنْيَانٌ مَّرْصُوصٌ

Indeed, Allah loves those who fight in His cause in a row as though they are a [single] structure joined firmly.

32.12 SHAYYAD (شَیَّدَ): SHAAD-AL-HAIT (شَادَ الحَائِط) means to plaster a wall with lime (dictionary al Manjad). And SHAYYAD (شَیَّدَ) is to plaster over a wall with lime or other cement in order to fortify a building. Allah says in the Quran

Surah An-Nisa (The women): سورۃ النِّسا 4:78

أَيْنَمَا تَكُونُوا يُدْرِككُّمُ الْمَوْتُ وَلَوْ كُنتُمْ فِي بُرُوجٍ مُّشَيَّدَةٍ

Wherever you may be, death will catch up with you, even if you were in fortified towers.

Summary:

32.1 THABBATA: To keep something firm and strong in its place.

32.2 AHKAMA: To remove ambiguity in words using wisdom and experience.

32.3 AWTHAQA:to make something strong and reliable with some other thing

32.4 SHADDA:to strengthen something in it of itself by reinforcing it.

32.5 ATHQANA:to strengthen something by means of mastery of skill

32.6 RABATA ALLAH 3LA ALQALB(ربط اللّه علي القلب):ALLAH strengthening heart by bestowing patience upon it.

32.7 AZARA:to strengthen gradually by reinforcing  

32.8 AKKAD:to strengthen vows,pledges and oaths

32.9 AQQDA:for strengthening transactions/trade,nuptial contract,and every other type of vows and promises.and is general/common than AKKAD

32.10 SANNADA:to strengthen by giving support

32.11 RASSA: to strengthen something by means of 'moltenlead'[figurative usage:implies intensely firm]

32.12 SHAYYAD:to strengthen building by plastering with lime etc.
