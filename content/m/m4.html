4. MAALIK [Master]

Synonyms: MAALIK, RABB, AHL

4.1 MAALIK (مَالِك): MILK (مِلْك) is anything under the possession of someone, and nobody other than him is allowed to use it. MAALIK (مَالِك) means an owner of something; master. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Fatiha (The Opening): سُوۡرَةُ الفَاتِحَة 1:4

مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ

Master of the Day of Judgment,

4.2 RABB (رَبّ): Means a lord or master. Mostly this word is used as a verbal noun, but actually a gerund which means to nurture someone to its excellence and take care of its necessities. Though RABB (رَبّ) is only Allah but it can be used to refer to a person who is an owner and also the nurturer. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Yusuf (Joseph): سورۃ یُوسُف 12:41

يَا صَاحِبَيِ السِّجْنِ أَمَّا أَحَدُكُمَا فَيَسْقِي رَبَّهُ خَمْرًا

O two companions of prison, as for one of you, he will give drink to his master of wine;

 

4.3 AHL (اَھْل): Means kinsmen, and family, relative, wife and kids.  AHL-AR-RUJAL (اَھْلَ الرَّجُل) means wife, and AHAL-AR-RUJAL (اَھَلَ الرَّجُل) means to marry. AHL also means people of same lineage or same religion and AHL (اَھْل) also means people of same household, and house (see GHAR [home]). In the following ayah in Quran the word AHL (اَھْل) is used for referring the Master of house. Allah says in the Quran

Surah An-Nisa (The women): سورۃ النِّسا 4:25

فَانكِحُوهُنَّ بِإِذْنِ أَهْلِهِنَّ

So marry them with the permission of their masters,

Summary:

4.1 MAALIK: Owner and master,

4.2 RABB: Who is also the master and nurturer,

4.3 AHL: People of household, house and its master
