27 MASHGHOOL HONA

[to become absorbed, engaged]

Synonyms: SHAGHALA(شغل), KHAAZA(خاض), AFAAZ(أفاض), SABAHA(سبح)

27.1 shaghala(شغل): that kind of engagement due to which a person is not able to give attention to other tasks. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Al-Fath (Victory) - سُوۡرَةُ الفَتْح

48:11

سَيَقُولُ لَكَ ٱلۡمُخَلَّفُونَ مِنَ ٱلۡأَعۡرَابِ شَغَلَتۡنَآ أَمۡوَٲلُنَا وَأَهۡلُونَا

Those of the wandering Arabs who were left behind will tell thee: Our possessions and our households occupied us, so ask forgiveness for us! They speak with their tongues that which is not in their hearts. Say: Who can avail you aught against Allah, if He intend you hurt or intend you profit? Nay, but Allah is ever Aware of what ye do.

________________

27.2 khaaza(خاض): means to penetrate and enter till the middle of something. Khaaz fil maa(خاض فى ألما) meaning to penetrate in water and khaaz fil hadees(خاض فى حديث) meaning to be engaged in talk. It means to be absorbed in some work or thing with full concentration. It is usually used in the Quran in the negative sense i.e. to be absorbed in rubbish talk and tasks. 27.3 afaaza(أفاض): faaz(فاض), faiz(فيض) meaning to continue something easily or the flow of water after rising from some place, to have something in abundance. It is said that faaz as-sail(فاض السيل) i.e. to have abundant water and having it flow from the banks of canals and rivers . faazat a’ainah(فاضت عينه) means that tears started dropping from his eyes. Afaaza ina-a’(أفاض اناءه) meaning that he poured his vessel to such an extent that water started to flow through the edges. It is stated in the Quran:

Summary:

1. SHAGHALA(شغل): it is common, to be engaged in some work.

2. KHAAZA(خاض): to be engaged with concentration, used in negative sense.

3. AFAAZA(أفاض): such kind of engagement which makes one forget himself.

4. SABAHA(سبح): that kind of engagement in which the aspect of hurry is prominent because of abundance of work.
