ڈرنا- to fear

These words     حذر  راع  اوجس وجف رهب  وجل  رعب  اشفق   خاف   خشي   خشع   اتقى (وقى)     have been used in the Quran

1.     خاف – to fear of a forthcoming calamity due to presumptions (MF) the opposite of خوف is امن – peace, meaning the connection of خاف is with the future. The wordings of Abu Hilaal to the meaning of خوف is توقع الضرر المشكوك   - The expectation of a doubtful damage/harm. (I have translated it plz get it checked) (FQ, L 199)

 

In Surah Hud: (11:70):

 

قَالُوا لَا تَخَفْ إِنَّا أُرْسِلْنَا إِلَىٰ قَوْمِ لُوطٍ

 

They said, "Fear not. We have been sent to the people of Lot."

 

2.    خشي – fear which appears in the heart due to the enormity of the matter (MF).

 

In Surah Al-Israa: (17:31):

 

وَلَا تَقْتُلُوا أَوْلَادَكُمْ خَشْيَةَ إِمْلَاقٍ

 

And do not kill your children for fear of poverty.

 

3.    خشع – such a fear which, besides the heart, shows other visible signs, the softening of the heart (MF) that’s why this word has been used for جوارح body parts, such as:

 

 In Surah Maa-aarij: (70:44):

 

خَاشِعَةً أَبْصَارُهُمْ 

 

Their eyes humbled (sahih),  meaning their gaze will be lowered due to fear.

 

 In Surah Gashiyah:  (88:2):

 

وُجُوهٌ يَوْمَئِذٍ خَاشِعَةٌ

[Some] faces, that Day, will be humbled  (Sahih), Many faces will be lowered cause of fear.

 

And in Surah Tahaa: (20:108):

 

وَخَشَعَتِ الْأَصْوَاتُ 

 

and [all] voices will be stilled (Sahih), voices will be surpressed, quelled due to fear.

 

In Surah Hadid: (57:16):

 

أَلَمْ يَأْنِ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا أَن تَخْشَعَ قُلُوبُهُمْ لِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ

 

Has the time not come for those who have believed that their hearts should become humbly submissive at the remembrance of Allah

 

4.   اتقى (وقى)   - تقوى  - to fear from the outcome of deeds, a natural inclination towards good and leaving the bad (MF), to bear in mind the commands and prohibitions of Allah due to His fear (MJD), (opposite of عدوان) and اتقى means to try to stay away from sins because of their consequences.   To choose righteousness.

 

In Surah Baqarah:   (2:187):

 

كَذَٰلِكَ يُبَيِّنُ اللَّهُ آيَاتِهِ لِلنَّاسِ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَتَّقُونَ

 

Thus does Allah make clear His ordinances to the people that they may become righteous.

 

 

5.    حذر  - to prevent or stay away from a fearful thing (MF), to be alert of any danger, to prevent (MJD) , In the word حذر the danger is sometimes definite sometimes indefinite, yet by taking precautions you can prevent yourself from that danger, (FQ,L200) like the transmitter of electricity or a frayed electricity wire.

 

In Surah Baqarah:   (2:19):

 

يَجْعَلُونَ أَصَابِعَهُمْ فِي آذَانِهِم مِّنَ الصَّوَاعِقِ حَذَرَ الْمَوْتِ

 

They put their fingers in their ears against the thunderclaps in dread of death.

 

And حذر   is also used for safety equipment , as:

 

In Surah Nisaa: (4:71):

 

خُذُوا حِذْرَكُمْ 

 

take your precaution (Sahih) take control of your weapons, or consider your safety by any other means.

 

6.   راع   - to perplex and to astonish someone (MJD) روع – heart , رعته (raa tuhu) to frighten , perplex someone and ناقة روعاء means cowardly camel (MF), and الروع means such a state in which man is amazed by something but also feel frightened due to some imaginary danger.

 

In Surah Hud: (11:74):

 

فَلَمَّا ذَهَبَ عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ الرَّوْعُ 

 

And when the fright had left Abraham.

 

7.    اوجس- وجس to be hidden, to hear the footfall, to fear from something you have heard (MJD), and اوجس means to try and hide the fear and not to show the signs/effects of fear on the face.

 

In Surah Hud: (11:70):

 

فَلَمَّا رَأَىٰ أَيْدِيَهُمْ لَا تَصِلُ إِلَيْهِ نَكِرَهُمْ وَأَوْجَسَ مِنْهُمْ خِيفَةً ۚ 

 

But when he saw their hands not reaching for it, he distrusted them and felt from them apprehension.

 

 

8.   وجف – to be agitated, palpitations of the heart, and to fall from fear (MJD), two things are present in وجف anxiousness and fear, and with these the heart starts to beat faster.

 

In Surah An-Naziaat: (79:8):

 

قُلُوبٌ يَوْمَئِذٍ وَاجِفَةٌ        

 

Hearts, that Day, will tremble.

 

9.   وجل – to fear in the heart (MF), to get goose bumps.  (MQ) to increase in fear, to become old. To fear, due to past mistakes and to be restless (FQ, L 202).

 

In Surah Anfaal: (8:2):

 

إِنَّمَا الْمُؤْمِنُونَ الَّذِينَ إِذَا ذُكِرَ اللَّهُ وَجِلَتْ قُلُوبُهُمْ

 

The believers are only those who, when Allah is mentioned, their hearts become fearful.

 

10.                      رهب  - such a fear which comprises of anxiousness and caution (opposite of رغب ) and راهب  means people who fear from Allah, a person who sits in a secluded corner and worshipper, from it the word رهبانية is derived, which means to leave behind all the worldly desires(MJD) , and طول الخوف واستمراره (FQ, L200) means to be always fearful.

 

In Surah Al-ambiya: (21:90):

 

إِنَّهُمْ كَانُوا يُسَارِعُونَ فِي الْخَيْرَاتِ وَيَدْعُونَنَا رَغَبًا وَرَهَبًا ۖ 

 

Indeed, they used to hasten to good deeds and supplicate Us in hope and fear.

 

11.رعب  - the threat,  terror of fear and رعب (ra a ba) means to fill up with fear (MF) to be overawed by fear, flinch with fear.

 

In Surah Kahf: (18:18):

لَوِ اطَّلَعْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ لَوَلَّيْتَ مِنْهُمْ فِرَارًا وَلَمُلِئْتَ مِنْهُمْ رُعْبًا

 

If you had looked at them, you would have turned from them in flight and been filled by them with terror.

 

12.                       اشفق – شفق على  - to wish someone well, and اشفق means thinking  of someone’s good welfare whilst fearing that  no danger befalls them(MF) to be worried about welfare and reform, to have mercy, to be merciful and شفقة means mercy compassion, compassion with fear, and اشفق منه means to be scared, to be greedy, (MJD).

 

In Surah  Kahf:  (18:49):

 

وَوُضِعَ الْكِتَابُ فَتَرَى الْمُجْرِمِينَ مُشْفِقِينَ مِمَّا فِيهِ 

 

And the record [of deeds] will be placed [open], and you will see the criminals fearful of that within it,

 

Summary:

1.    خاف – fear of coming danger

2. خشي – to fear due to the enormity of the matter

3.  خشع – fear which has a visible effect on body.

4. اتقى (وقى)   - to refrain from sins due its consequences

5. حذر   - to stay away from danger, to be scared, to be alert

6. راع   - to be amazed whilst fearing an imaginary danger

7. اوجس – to be fearful in the heart and try to conceal it

8. وجف- the palpitating of the heart with fear and restlessness

9. وجل – to be anxious and to fear someone due to previous error

10. رهب- fear which combines caution with anxiousness, and it is lengthy

11. رعب- terror, overawed by fear

12. اشفق- wishing well for someone whilst fearing for them aswell,   mixed feelings of hope and fear.

3.11  Ra’aba (رَعَبَ ) means intense fear, terror or to be filled with fear or to lie low because of fear of (being spotted by) someone. It is mentioned in Qur’an 

Surat Al-Kahf (The Cave) - سورة الكهف 18:18

 

 للَوِ اطَّلَعْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ لَوَلَّيْتَ مِنْهُمْ فِرَارًا وَّلَمُلِئْتَ مِنْهُمْ رُعْبًا        

If you had looked at them, you would have turned from them in flight and been filled by them with terror. 

3.12  Ashfaqa (اَشْفَقَ):  SHAFQA-A’ALA ( شفق أعلى) means to seek after someone's welfare. ASHFAQ (اشفق) means in addition to someone's welfare, to be afraid if trouble comes to him. Moreover, to be worried about welfare and reform. To show mercy, to be kind. And SHAFQATAH (شفقته) means kindness, mercy, kindness with fear. And ASHFAQA MINHU ( اشفق منه ) means to be frightened, greedy. It is mentioned in Qur’an:

 

Surat Al-Kahf (The Cave) - سورة الكهف 18:49

 وَوُضِـعَ الْكِتٰبُ فَتَرَى الْمُجْرِمِيْنَ مُشْفِقِيْنَ مِمَّا فِيْهِ

And the record [of deeds] will be placed [open], and you will see the criminals fearful of that within it.

 

Summary:

 

3.1 KHAFA: The apprehension of danger.

3.2 KHASHIA: Fear because of the greatness/dignity of some Amer.

3.3 KHASHAA:  A fear that affect organs and soul as well.

3.4 ITTAQA: To avoid sins due to the fear of consequences.

3.5 RAAA: To wonder and the fear of imaginative danger.

3.6 HAZERA: To refrain from something dangerous. To be alert.

3.7 AOOJASA: To be frightened in heart and try to hide it.

3.8 WAJAFA: Beating of heart due to fear. Fear and anxiety.

3.9 WAJILA: To be afraid from someone and apprehensive due to personal negligence.

3.10 RAHABA: A fear in which caution and anxiety both are present and it is long.

3.11 RA’ABA: Intensity of fear. Terror.

3.12 ASHFAQA: in addition to someone's welfare, to be afraid if trouble comes to him. Mixed feelings of fear and mercy.
