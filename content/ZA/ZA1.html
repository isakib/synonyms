To become evident (Zahir hona) ظ – ظاہر ہونا [p631-633]

Synonyms:

ظَهَر, جَهْرً, بَدَا, عُثِرَ, تَّبَيَّن,حَصْحَصَ, تَجَلَّى, شُرَّعًا

1. ظَهَرَ : back, rear, external, outward, apparent. Opposite of بطن which means stomach, internal.

Example: ظَهَرَ الْفَسَادُ فِي الْبَرِّ وَالْبَحْرِ بِمَا كَسَبَتْ أَيْدِي النَّاسِ

Corruption has appeared throughout the land and sea by [reason of] what the hands of people have earned. [Ar-Rum 30:41]

ظَهَر is also used in literal meanings. Example: قُلْ إِنَّمَا حَرَّمَ رَبِّيَ الْفَوَاحِشَ مَا ظَهَرَ مِنْهَا وَمَا بَطَنَ

Say, "My Lord has only forbidden immoralities - what is apparent of them and what is concealed. [Al-Araf 7:33]

2. جَهْرً : openly, visibly. Example: وَإِذْ قُلْتُمْ يَا مُوسَىٰ لَن نُّؤْمِنَ لَكَ حَتَّىٰ نَرَى اللَّهَ جَهْرَةً

And [recall] when you said, "O Moses, we will never believe you until we see Allah outright" [Al-Baqarah 2:55]

جَهْرً is usually used to express a loud voice. جَهْری salah is when the imam recites in a loud voice, in Fajr, Maghrib and Isha. It’s opposite is سرّی, where the imam recites in a low voice.

Example: لَّا يُحِبُّ اللَّهُ الْجَهْرَ بِالسُّوءِ مِنَ الْقَوْلِ إِلَّا مَن ظُلِمَ

Allah does not like the public mention of evil except by one who has been wronged. [An-Nisa 4:148]

3. بَدَا : To become apparent without the consent of anyone.

Example: بَلْ بَدَا لَهُم مَّا كَانُوا يُخْفُونَ مِن قَبْلُ

But what they concealed before has [now] appeared to them. [Al-An’am: 6:28]

Example 2: بَدَتْ لَهُمَا سَوْآتُهُمَا وَطَفِقَا يَخْصِفَانِ عَلَيْهِمَا مِن وَرَقِ الْجَنَّةِ

“…their private parts became apparent to them, and they began to fasten together over themselves from the leaves of Paradise.” [Al-Ar’af 7:22]

بَدَا is also used in literal meanings. Example: ثُمَّ بَدَا لَهُم مِّن بَعْدِ مَا رَأَوُا الْآيَاتِ لَيَسْجُنُنَّهُ حَتَّىٰ حِينٍ

Then it appeared to them after they had seen the signs that al-'Azeez should surely imprison him for a time. [Yusuf 12:35]

4. عُثِرَ: To slip and fall. عُثِرَ shall mean “to become visible” when someone is lying and tells the truth by mistake, and therefore the truth becomes ‘visible’.

Example: فَإِنْ عُثِرَ عَلَىٰ أَنَّهُمَا اسْتَحَقَّا إِثْمًا

But if it is found that those two were guilty of perjury… [Al-Maidah 5:107]

5. تَّبَيَّن and استبان : بان means to become visible in comparison to another thing. تَّبَيَّن is used in the same meaning.

Example: قَد تَّبَيَّنَ الرُّشْدُ مِنَ الْغَيِّ

The right course has become clear from the wrong. [Al-Baqarah 2:256]

Example 2: وَلِتَسْتَبِينَ سَبِيلُ الْمُجْرِمِينَ

and [thus] the way of the criminals will become evident. [Al-An’am 6:56]

6. حَصْحَصَ : to become clear, when the truth gets out.

Example: قَالَتِ امْرَأَتُ الْعَزِيزِ الْآنَ حَصْحَصَ الْحَقُّ أَنَا رَاوَدتُّهُ عَن نَّفْسِهِ

The wife of al-'Azeez said, "Now the truth has become evident. It was I who sought to seduce him [Yusuf 12:51]

7. تَجَلَّىٰ : A bright and beautiful thing becoming evident.

Example: فَلَمَّا تَجَلَّىٰ رَبُّهُ لِلْجَبَلِ جَعَلَهُ دَكًّا

But when his Lord appeared to the mountain, He rendered it level [Al-Ar’af 7:143]

8. شُرَّعًا : شرع means to become visible openly. It is phrased شرع البعیر عنقھ, which means that the camel raised his neck visibly.

Example: إِذْ تَأْتِيهِمْ حِيتَانُهُمْ يَوْمَ سَبْتِهِمْ شُرَّعًا

…when their fish came to them openly on their sabbath day.. [Al-Ar’af 7:163]

Summary: ظَهَر, جَهْرً, بَدَا, عُثِرَ, تَّبَيَّن,حَصْحَصَ, تَجَلَّى, شُرَّعًا

1. ظَهَر : to become visibly apparent.

2. جَهْرً : To speak loudly or announce. Usually used to express sound.

3. بَدَا : To become apparent without the consent of anyone.

4. عُثِرَ: truth coming out mistakenly.

5. تَّبَيَّن : When separation, distance and clearness are present.

6. حَصْحَصَ : truth becoming evident after not being compelled to hide the truth anymore.

7. تَجَلَّى : Something bright and beautiful becoming evident.

8. شُرَّعًا : to become visible openly.
