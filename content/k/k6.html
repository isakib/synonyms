6 KAAM KERNA [to do something]

Synonyms: FA’A’LA, A’MILA, SANAA’, SADAA’, JARAHA, IJTARAH’, TAM’MAD, AMR, SHA’N

 

6.1 FA’A’LA (فَعَلَ): FA’EL (فَعْل) is something which comes as an effect by the doer, despite the one who does have or haven’t planned it or may or may not have material nature, and all is applied on him (doer) (plural AFA’AL (افعال) ). And FA’LAH (فَعْلَۃً) to do a something once. In the Quran

Surah Ash-Shura’ (The Poets): صورۃ الشُّعْراء 26:19

 وَفَعَلۡتَ فَعۡلَتَكَ ٱلَّتِى فَعَلۡتَ وَأَنتَ مِنَ ٱلۡكَـٰفِرِينَ

And you committed that deed you committed, and you were ungrateful.”

 

This Ayah relates the murder that was done by Moses (Hazrat Musa (A.S)) unintentionally. The example for something done intentionally is following

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:68

 فَٱفۡعَلُواْ مَا تُؤۡمَرُونَ

So do what you are commanded.

6.2 A’MILA (عَمِلَ):  A’ML (عَمْل) means anything done by a living being intentionally which may be good or bad (plural A’MAAL(اعمال)). AMAL (عَمِل) refers to hard work and labor. In the Quran

Surah Al-Kahf (The Cave): سورۃ الکھف 18:79

أَمَّا ٱلسَّفِينَةُ فَكَانَتۡ لِمَسَـٰكِينَ يَعۡمَلُونَ فِى ٱلۡبَحۡرِ

 As for the boat, it belonged to paupers working at sea.

 

 6.3 SANAA’ (صَنَعَ): To perform a task with perfection. This word is from AMALA (عَمَلَ) and not used when referring animals. SANNA’ (صَنَعَ) means a craftsman or an expert in his domain. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Hud (Hud): سورۃ ھود 11:37

وَٱصۡنَعِ ٱلۡفُلۡكَ بِأَعۡيُنِنَا وَوَحۡيِنَا

And build the Ark, under Our eyes, and with Our inspiration

Page 694

6.4 SADAA’ (صَدَعَ): Means to finish a work (dictionary Al Manjad) despite of any difficulties, completing the task. SADA’-BIL- HAQQ (صَدَعَ بِالْحَقّ) means to speak truth openly. SADA’-ASH-SHAI (صَدَعَ الشَّئ) means to tear something without separating it (dictionary Al Manjad). Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Hijr (The Rock): سورۃ الحِجر 15:94

 فَٱصۡدَعۡ بِمَا تُؤۡمَرُ وَأَعۡرِضۡ عَنِ ٱلۡمُشۡرِكِينَ

So proclaim openly what you are commanded, and turn away from the polytheists.

 

6.5 JARAHA (جَرَحَ): JRAH (جرح) means a wound (plural wound) JARAHA (جَرَحَ) means to inflict a wound, JARAH-BIL-LISAAN (جَرَحَ بِاللِّسَان) to hurt someone with words, or torture verbally. To narrate someone’s imperfections. JARAH-ASH-SHAHADAH (جَرَحَ الشَھَادَۃ) means to falsify a testimony (dictionary Al Manjad). When this word is used to refer to doing something then usually it is taken in a negative manner. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Ana’am (The cattle): سورۃ الانعام 6:60

وَهُوَ ٱلَّذِى يَتَوَفَّٮٰڪُم بِٱلَّيۡلِ وَيَعۡلَمُ مَا جَرَحۡتُم بِٱلنَّہَارِ

 It is He Who takes you by night, and He knows what you earn by day

And IJTARAH’ (اِجْتَرَح) means to commit something (dictionary Al Manjad). IJTARAH-AL-ISM (اِجْتَرَح الِاثْم) means he committed a sin. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Jathiya (Crouching): سُوۡرَةُ الجَاثیَة 45:21

 أَمۡ حَسِبَ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱجۡتَرَحُواْ ٱلسَّيِّـَٔاتِ أَن نَّجۡعَلَهُمۡ كَٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ وَعَمِلُواْ ٱلصَّـٰلِحَـٰتِ

Do those who perpetrate the evil deeds assume that We will regard them as equal to those who believe and do righteous deeds

 

6.6 TA’AM’MAD (تَعَمَّدَ): AMADA (عَمَدَ) means to aim to do something, or vow to do something. AMAD (عَمَد) means a pillar (plural I’MAAD (عِمَاد)). And AMOOD (عَمُوْد) is that wooden pillar that supports the tent. TA’AM’MAD (تَعَمَّدَ) means to do something deliberately, to do something despite of knowing it is bad (opposite SAHW). In the Quran

Surah An-Nisa (The women): سورۃ النِّسا 4:93

 وَمَن يَقۡتُلۡ مُؤۡمِنً۬ا مُّتَعَمِّدً۬ا فَجَزَآؤُهُ ۥ جَهَنَّمُ خَـٰلِدً۬ا فِيہَا

Whoever kills a believer deliberately, the penalty for him is Hell, where he will remain forever

6.7 AMR (اَمْر): Refers to an action, state, matter that may be verbal or in act. (Plural AMOOR (امور)) This word is more popular than FA’EL (فَعْل) because it not only refers to actions but also to words. Moreover it is also used for words or acts related to will of Allah. In the Quran

Surah An-Naml (The ant): سورۃ النَّمل 27:32

 مَا ڪُنتُ قَاطِعَةً أَمۡرًا حَتَّىٰ تَشۡہَدُونِ

 I never make a decision unless you are present.”

 Page 695

In this ayah the word AMR (اَمْر) is giving the meaning of act, matter and words. And the work is referring to neither act nor words. AMR (اَمْر) also means an order. In that case its plural will be AWAAMIR (اَوَامِْر)  (opposite NAWAHI(نواھی)). When denotes an action then it won’t mean to do something, rather to abide by an order.

6.8 SHA’N (شاْن): (plural SHU’OON(شؤون)) means huge affairs, matters or conditions. MIN-SHANIHI-KAZA ( مِنْ شَانِہِ کَذَا) means to do this thing is in his nature (dictionary Al Manjad). Thus SHA’N (شاْن) means to do something befitting for someone. Allah says in Quran

Surah Ar-Rahman (The Beneficent): سُوۡرَةُ الرَّحمٰن 55:29

كُلَّ يَوۡمٍ هُوَ فِى شَأۡنٍ۬

Every day He is managing

In another reference

Surah Al-Noor (The light): سورۃ النور 24:62

ۦ‌ۚ فَإِذَا ٱسۡتَـٔۡذَنُوكَ لِبَعۡضِ شَأۡنِهِمۡ فَأۡذَن لِّمَن شِئۡتَ مِنۡهُمۡ

 So when they ask your permission to attend to some affair of theirs, give permission to any of them you wish,

Summary

6.1 FA’A’LA:  Something done with or without intention

6.2 A’ML: Action performed intentionally due to some interest

6.3 SANAA’: To do something skillfully

6.4 SADAA’: To complete a task despite of tribulations

6.5 JARAHA: Usually refers to ill actions

6.6, TAM’MAD: To perform an ill action intentionally

6.7 AMR: Related to actions, words or matters

6.9 SHA’N: To do a befitting work.
