7. KAMYAAB HONA, MURAD PAANA [To be successful, to obtain one’s wish]

Synonyms: AFLAHA, FAAZ

7.1. AFLAHA (افلح): There are three meanings in FALAHA (فلح): a) to rip or tear apart b) success c) imperishability. FALLAH (فلاح) means a farmer who tears open the land to sow seeds, achieves success when his crops are ready to be harvested and obtains his wish when he benefits from it. AFLAHA (افلح) means to be successful, fulfilment of one’s wish by working hard for it.

Allah (swt) said:

Surah al-Muminun 23:1-2

Certainly will the believers have succeeded: they who are humbly submissive during their prayer.

7.2. FAAZ (فاز): It means to be saved from some trouble and achieve something which is dear. It also means to receive security as well as blessing.

Allah (swt) said:

Surah Al-e-Imran 3:185

فَازَ فَقَدْ الْجَنَّةَ وَأُدْخِلَ النَّارِ عَنِ زُحْزِحَ فَمَن

So he who is drawn away from the Fire and admitted to Paradise has attained [his desire].

The word FAAZ (فاز) is from the dictionary of opposites. It means to be successful and obtain emancipation and it also means to perish or die. FAWWAZ ARAJUL (الرجل فوز) means a man died. MAFAAZATUN (مفازۃ, 3:188) means the cause or place of success. It also means the cause or place of annihilation as it is used in this meaning in the aforementioned verse. However, this word usually means to be saved from some trouble and to achieve success or fulfil one’s wish by attaining something desirable.

It is mentioned in the Quran:

Surah an-Nisa 4:73

عَظِيمًافَوْزًا فَأَفُوزَ مَعَهُمْ كُنتُ لَيتَنِي يَا

(Hypocrite will say :) "Oh, I wish I had been with them so I could have attained a great attainment."

CONCLUSION:

1) AFLAHA is used for achieving success by gaining the rightful fruit of hard work and 2) FAAZ refers to an act which, if it has shortcomings, would lead to destruction, but if it is completed successfully , then it would result in the attainment of a lot of rewards.
