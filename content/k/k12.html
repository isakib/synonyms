12. KITNAY? [How much?]

Synonyms: KAM, KA’AYYIN

12.1 KAM (کَمْ): To inquire for an amount, meaning how much or how many, and is used as a question. In the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:259

 قَالَ ڪَمۡ لَبِثۡتَ‌ۖ

He said, “For how long have you tarried?”

 

12.1 KA’AYYIN (کَاَیٍّ): KAM (کَمْ) and KA’YYIN (کَاَیٍّ) almost means the same. The difference is only that KA’AYYIN (کَاَیِّنْ) has excess in it too. A lot or number of or how many and not taken as a question. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Yusuf (Joseph): سورۃ یُوسُف 12:105

وَڪَأَيِّن مِّنۡ ءَايَةٍ۬ فِى ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٲتِ وَٱلۡأَرۡضِ يَمُرُّونَ عَلَيۡہَا

How many a sign in the heavens and the earth do they pass by,
