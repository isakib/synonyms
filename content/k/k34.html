Ta'aam - Food

Ta'aam means food and the words Akala, Rat'aa and Ta'ama have been used.

1- Akala

This is the common word for food and has been used for every food whether it be bread, fruit, sweets or anything athat is edible. It is used for all purrposes, for both humans and non-humans.

“And those from which a wild animal has eaten , except what you are able to slaughter” 5/3

2- Ta'ama

It is used in limited situation as compared to Akala and means having a meal, eating from bread and everything that is eaten as food. This word is usually used for humans. 'Ta'ami' means one who sells bread and 'Mat'am' means restaurant.

“And when you have eaten, disperse without seeking to remain for coversation” 33/53

3- Rat'aa

It basically means the feeding of animals and it is also used to point out people eating to their fills, meaning eating a lot.

“Send him with us tomorrow that he may eat well and play” 12/12

Inference

1- Akala: Used in wider sense, meaning eating in general.

2- Ta'ama: Used when people eat.

3- Rat'aa: Means eating well or animals eating.
