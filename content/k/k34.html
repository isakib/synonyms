34 KHANA [eat]

Synonyms:  AKALA, TA’AMA, RATTAA’

34.1 AKALA (اَکَلَ): A very common word and is used for any type of eatable, food, fruits, sweets, bread etc. Used for both human and other non human beings. The idiom “اَکَلَتِ النَّارُ الْحَطَبَ” means the fire ate the wood. In the Quran

Surah Al-Maeda (The table spread): سورۃ المَائدہ 5:3

 وَمَآ أَكَلَ ٱلسَّبُعُ إِلَّا مَا ذَكَّيۡتُمۡ

that which have been (partly) eaten by a wild animal; unless you are able to slaughter it (in due form);

34.2 TA’AMA (طَعَمَ): Has a very limited use then AKALA (اَکَلَ), means to eat food, to eat bread, and anything that is eaten as nutrition. This word is applied to humans mostly. TA’AMMI (طَعَامِّی) means a person who sells bread. MAT’AM (مطْعَم) means a hotel. Allah says in the Quran

Suran Al-Ahzaab (The clans): سورۃ الأحزَاب 33:53

 فَإِذَا طَعِمۡتُمۡ فَٱنتَشِرُواْ وَلَا مُسۡتَـٔۡنِسِينَ لِحَدِيثٍۚ

And when you have eaten, disperse, without lingering for conversation.

34.3 RATTAA’ (رَتَعَ): Usually means grazing and eating of animals. When used for humans metaphorically it refers to a person eat to his fill. Or eating too much like animals. In the Quran

Surah Yusuf (Joseph): سورۃ یُوسُف 12:12

أَرۡسِلۡهُ مَعَنَا غَدً۬ا يَرۡتَعۡ وَيَلۡعَبۡ

Send him with us tomorrow that he may eat well and play.

Summary:

34.1 AKALA: Anything eating anything, used for a wide range of meanings.

34.2 TA’AMA: Humans eating food.

34.3 RATTAA’: Eating to fill. Animals grazing and eating.
