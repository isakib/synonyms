13 KURAIDNA [to scrape, to dig]

Synonyms: BAHATHA, BA’THARA

13.1 BAHATHA (بَحَثَ): To dig up, to scrape, to find explore in under the earth. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Maeda (The table spread): سورۃ المَائدہ 5:31

 فَبَعَثَ ٱللَّهُ غُرَابً۬ا يَبۡحَثُ فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ لِيُرِيَهُ ۥ كَيۡفَ يُوَٲرِى سَوۡءَةَ أَخِيهِ‌ۚ

Then God sent a raven digging the ground, to show him how to cover his brother's corpse.

 

13.2 BA’THARA (بَعْثَرَ): According to Imam Raghib BU’THARA (بَعْثَرَ) is made from two words BU’ITH (بُعِثَ) and UTHEER (اُثِیْر) means to dig up a grave or exhume a body. BA’THAR-AL-MATA’ (بَعْثَرَ المَتَاع) means to turn over or scatter luggage (dictionary Al Manjad). BA’THARA (بَعْثَرَ) to dig up, bring up, or investigate. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Adiyat (The Chargers): سُوۡرَةُ العَادیَات 100:9

أَفَلَا يَعۡلَمُ إِذَا بُعۡثِرَ مَا فِى ٱلۡقُبُورِ

Does he not know? When the contents of the graves are scattered around.

13.2BAHATHA: To dig up beneath the earth

13.2 BA’THARA: Scrape and bring up or reveal.
