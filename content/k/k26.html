26 KOSHISH KERNA [to try, to strive, to struggle]

Synonyms: SAA’, JAHADA, KADAHA

26.1 SAA’ (سعٰی): To pace or walk briskly, to jog. To make efforts for the completion of a good or bad matter to strive. In the Quran

Surah An-Najm (the star): سورۃ النَّجم 53:39

 وَأَن لَّيۡسَ لِلۡإِنسَـٰنِ إِلَّا مَا سَعَىٰ

And that the human being attains only what he strives for.

26.2 JAHADA (جَھَدَ): To put in the best effort to complete a task. Extreme effort. According to ibn al Faris “اَصْلُهُ الْمُشَقَّۃ وَمَا یُقَارِبُهُ”. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Ankaboot  (The Spider): سُوۡرَةُ العَنکبوت 29:69

 وَٱلَّذِينَ جَـٰهَدُواْ فِينَا لَنَہۡدِيَنَّہُمۡ سُبُلَنَا‌ۚ

As for those who strive for Us —We will guide them in Our ways

26.3 KADAHA (کَدَحَ): AL-KADAH (اَلْکَدْح) means a scratch. KADAHA (کَدَحَ) means to work very hard. Or bear pain and peril while doing work. In the Quran

Surah Al-Inshiqaq (The Splitting Open):  سُوۡرَةُ الانشقاق84:6

 يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلۡإِنسَـٰنُ إِنَّكَ كَادِحٌ إِلَىٰ رَبِّكَ كَدۡحً۬ا فَمُلَـٰقِيهِ

O man! You are laboring towards your Lord, and you will meet Him.

Summary:

26.1 SAA’: To make efforts for a work.

26.2 JAHADA: To put in all the efforts for a task

26.3 KADAHA: To work while bearing pain
