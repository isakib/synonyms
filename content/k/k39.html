Surat Ţāhā (Ta-Ha) - سورة طه 20:27

يَفْقَهُوا قَوْلِي وَاحْلُلْ عُقْدَةً مِّن لِّسَانِي

And untie the knot from my tongue. That they may understand my speech.

 

39.4 SHARAHA(شَرَحَ): Means to open the meanings of difficult speech/conversations  and according to Ibn-un-Faris, there are basically two things in SHARAH(شرح).

1. To open victory

2. Statement (Wazahat) or explanation and SHARAH SADAR(شرح صدر) means to expand the chest. Means to understand a difficult or complicated matter. In the Quran:

Surat Ţāhā (Ta-Ha) - سورة طه 20:25

وَيَسِّرْ لِي أَمْرِي رَبِّ اشْرَحْ لِي صَدْرِي

My Lord, expand for me my breast [with assurance]. And ease for me my task.

 

39.5 KASHAFA(كَشَفَ): To expand and move off. To unveil. To remove one thing from another and revealing and expressing the first one. In the Quran:

Surat An-Naml (The Ant) - سورة النمل 27:44

فَلَمَّا رَأَتْهُ حَسِبَتْهُ لُجَّةً وَكَشَفَتْ عَن سَاقَيْهَا ۚ

But when she(Princess SABA) saw it, she thought it was a body of water and uncovered her shins [to wade through].

 

39.6 NASHATA (نَشَطَ): This word belongs to the Lughat Azdad لغت اضداد. Which means that it has opposing meanings. NASHAT-AL-HABLA(نَشَطَ الحَبلَ) means tied a knot. And NASHAT-AL-UQDAT(نَشَطَ العُقدَة) means tightening/tightened the knot. And ALNASHOOT(النشوط) means a knot which can be easily opened/undone. And NASHATA(نَشَطَ) means to undo/open a knot. In that context, this word is in particular to HALLA(حَلَّ). Which means that HALL-AL-UQDAT(حل العقدة) and NASHATA(نَشَطَ), both have the same meanings. Means opening a knot. To loosen a close linkage or constraint. To loosen a knot etc. In the Quran:

Surat An-Nāzi`āt (Those who drag forth) - سورة النازعات 79:2

ۚ وَالنَّاشِطَاتِ نَشْطًا وَالنَّازِعَاتِ غَرْقًا

By those [angels] who extract with violence. And [by] those who remove with ease.

 

39.7 FARAJA(فَرَجَ):  A concept of openness is found in FARJ (فَرْج). FARAJA(فَرَجَ) means to open, to widen, expand etc. (Refer to SHAGAAF for further details). In the Quran:

Surat Al-Mursalāt (The Emissaries) - سورة المرسلات 77:9

وَإِذَا السَّمَاءُ فُرِجَتْ

And when the heaven is opened.

 

Summary:

1. BAST: To open and spread.

2. FATAH: To open a complicated matter or a closed stuff/equipment.

3. HALLA: (ALUQDAT) To open/undo a knot.

4. SHARAHA: To open the meanings of a difficult talk/speech.

5. KASHAFA: To remove one thing and then unveil another.

6. NASHATA: To loosen a knot, constraint or linkage.

7. FARAJA: To open and expand. To widen.
