41. KHENCHNA (To pull / To stretch)

Synonyms: MADDA, JARRA, IZTARRA, SALAKHA, NAZA’A, SAFA’A.

41.1 MADDA(مَدَّ): Means to make something tall/long by stretching it and to spread it. In a way that the connection/linkage remains established. MUDDAT(مدّت) means the stretches of time. In the Quran:

Surat Al-'A`rāf (The Heights) - سورة الأعراف 7:202

وَإِخْوَانُهُمْ يَمُدُّونَهُمْ فِي الْغَيِّ ثُمَّ لَا يُقْصِرُونَ

But their brothers - the devils increase them in error; then they do not stop short.

 

And MADDADA(مَدَّدَ) is used as more emphasis. In the Quran:

Surat Al-Humazah (The Traducer) - سورة الهمزة 104:9

فِي عَمَدٍ مُّمَدَّدَةٍ

In extended columns.

41.2 JARRA(جَرَّ): Means to pull/stretch and drag. To drag on the floor. And JAISHUN-JARRARUN(جَيشٌ جَرَّارٌ) refers to a big army spread in a vast area and advances forward like as if it’s stretching in numbers. In the Quran:

Surat Al-'A`rāf (The Heights) - سورة الأعراف 7:150

وَأَخَذَ بِرَأْسِ أَخِيهِ يَجُرُّهُ إِلَيْهِ ۚ

And he (Musa A.S) seized his brother by [the hair of] his head, pulling him toward him.

 

41.3 IZTARRA(اِضطَرَّ): To forcibly pull someone to work. To make someone work by turning him helpless and powerless. In the Quran:

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - سورة البقرة 2:126

وَمَن كَفَرَ فَأُمَتِّعُهُ قَلِيلًا ثُمَّ أَضْطَرُّهُ إِلَىٰ عَذَابِ النَّارِ ۖ

And whoever disbelieves - I will grant him enjoyment for a little; then I will force him to the punishment of the Fire, and wretched is the destination.

41.4 SALAKHA(سَلَخَ): To remove the skin of a living being by pulling it. Casting-off of a snake’s exuviae. And according to Ibn-ul-Faris, it means to remove something from the skin. And SALAKHATISHAHR(سَلَخَتِ الشَّهْر) means the passing of the last day of a month. In the Quran:

Surat Yā-Sīn (Ya Sin) - سورة يس 36:37

وَآيَةٌ لَّهُمُ اللَّيْلُ نَسْلَخُ مِنْهُ النَّهَارَ فَإِذَا هُم مُّظْلِمُونَ

And a sign for them is the night. We remove from it [the light of] day, so they are [left] in darkness.

 

41.5 NAZA’A(نَزَعَ): Means to draw out/extort something from its place/habitat. Like drawing out a soul from a body, to draw out something by pulling it. In the Quran:

Surat Al-'A`rāf (The Heights) - سورة الأعراف 7:43

وَنَزَعْنَا مَا فِي صُدُورِهِم مِّنْ غِلٍّ

And We will have removed whatever is within their breasts of resentment

 

41.6 SAFA’A(سَفَعَ): Means to hold and pull the forelock of a horse. In the Quran:

Surat Al-`Alaq (The Clot) - سورة العلق 96:15

لَئِن لَّمْ يَنتَهِ لَنَسْفَعًا بِالنَّاصِيَةِ

If he does not desist, We will surely drag him by the forelock.

 

Summary:

1. MADDA: Means to stretch and spread. To elongate such that the substance remains intact/linked.

2. JARRA: To pull or drag someone on the ground.

3. IZTARRA: To pull someone to work, forcibly and violently.

4. SALAKHA: To pull skin from the body.

5. NAZA’A: To extort something from its place or habitat.

6. SAFA’A: To pull by the forelocks.
