18. KAL (Yesterday)

Synonyms: AMS, GHAD.

 

18.1 AMS(اَمْس): One day past  today. Deroz(ديروز) (Yesterday) or if it occurs in vowel points, then AL’AMS(اَلاَمْس) means any day from the past periods. Also, the word AMS(اَمْس) idiomatically refers to the time that is passed recently. In the Quran:

Surat Al-Qaşaş (The Stories) - سورة القصص 28:18

فَإِذَا الَّذِي اسْتَنصَرَهُ بِالْأَمْسِ يَسْتَصْرِخُهُ

When suddenly the one who sought his (Musa A.S) help the previous day cried out to him [once again].

 

In this Aayah, AMS( اَمْس) refers to yesterday. While in the following Aayah, it refers to the time that has passed recently. In the Quran:

Surat Yūnus (Jonah) - سورة يونس 10:24

فَجَعَلْنَاهَا حَصِيدًا كَأَن لَّمْ تَغْنَ بِالْأَمْسِ

We make it (town) as a harvest, as if it had not flourished yesterday.

18.2 GHAD(غَد): Means the following day. FARDA(فردا) (Tomorrow). Like AMS(اَمْس), this word also gives the meaning of both, the coming day and the near future. For instance, in the following  Aayah it refers to the coming day.

Surat Yūsuf (Joseph) - سورة يوسف 12:12

أَرْسِلْهُ مَعَنَا غَدًا يَرْتَعْ وَيَلْعَبْ وَإِنَّا لَهُ لَحَافِظُونَ

Send him with us tomorrow that he may eat well and play.

 

And in the below mentioned Aayah, the word GHAD(غَد) is not only referring to near future, but is also giving the meaning of the life hereafter which is close to the accordance of Quran.
