40. KHETI BAARI KARNA (To cultivate/To Farm)

Synonyms: HARASA, ZARA’A

 

Page 722--

40.1 HARASA(حَرَثَ):  Means to plow the land and sow seeds in it and to prepare it for cultivation. And MEHRAS (مِحرَث) (plural MAHARIS محارث) means the tools for plowing land/plowing tools etc. But sometimes the cultivation/harvest is also referred as HARS(حَرْث). In the Quran:

Surat Al-Qalam (The Pen) - سورة القلم 68:22

أَنِ اغْدُوا عَلَىٰ حَرْثِكُمْ إِن كُنتُمْ صَارِمِينَ

Go early to your crop if you would cut the fruit.

 

40.2 ZARA’A(زَرَعَ):  Its real meaning is INBAAT (اِنبَات) (germination) or to grow. And to sow seeds and irrigate it (land) with water, and to take care of it appropriately. It’s growth is virtually linked to a human being because he is the one who fulfills the duty for taking care of it otherwise, the fact is that it is Allah’s job to grow the farm. In the Quran:

Surat Al-Wāqi`ah (The Inevitable) - سورة الواقعة 56: 63-64

أَأَنتُمْ تَزْرَعُونَهُ أَمْ نَحْنُ الزَّارِعُونَ أَفَرَأَيْتُم مَّا تَحْرُثُونَ

And have you seen that [seed] which you sow? Is it you who makes it grow, or are We the grower?

 

Summary:

1. HARASA means to plow a land and sow seeds.

2. ZARA’A to sow seed and irrigate it afterwards and to take appropriate care of it.
