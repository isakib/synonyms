1. KATNA [to cut]

Synonyms:  HASADA, SARAMA, QATA’A, QATTA’A, BATARA, BATAKA, ADDA, KHASADA, JAZZA, AQARA

1.1 HASADA (حَصَدَ): To cut or harvest a fully ready crop. HASEED (حَصِیْد) means harvested crop. MAHSAD (محصد) means a sickle and HASEEDAH (حصیدۃ) is the portion of crop left after harvest, which cannot be reached by the sickle. Thus the word HASADA (حَصَدَ) is used for cutting a crop with a sickle. In the Quran

Surah Yusuf (Joseph): سورۃ یُوسُف 12:47

فَمَا حَصَدتُّمۡ فَذَرُوهُ فِى سُنۢبُلِهِۦۤ

 But whatever you harvest, leave it in its spikes,

 

1.2 SARAMA (صَرَمَ): To cut down a tree with a sharp tool. SAARIM (صَارِم) means a sword and SARUMA (صَرُمَ) means to cut with a sword. And SAROOM (صَرُوُم) a cutting sword (dictionary Al Manjad). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Qalam (The Pen):سورۃ القلم  68:17

 إِنَّا بَلَوۡنَـٰهُمۡ كَمَا بَلَوۡنَآ أَصۡحَـٰبَ ٱلۡجَنَّةِ إِذۡ أَقۡسَمُواْ لَيَصۡرِمُنَّہَا مُصۡبِحِينَ

 We tested them, as We tested the owners of the garden, when they vowed to harvest it in the morning.

 

1.3 QATA’A (قَطَعَ): To slice of a part of something. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al Hashr (The exile): سورۃ الحَشر 59:5

مَا قَطَعۡتُم مِّن لِّينَةٍ أَوۡ تَرَڪۡتُمُوهَا قَآٮِٕمَةً عَلَىٰٓ أُصُولِهَا فَبِإِذۡنِ ٱللَّهِ

Whether you cut down a tree, or leave it standing on its trunk, it is by God’s will.

 

1.4 QATTA’A (قَطَّعَ): To cut something into pieces. To chop into small pieces. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Muhammad (Muhammad): سُوۡرَةُ محَمَّد 47:15

وَسُقُواْ مَآءً حَمِيمً۬ا فَقَطَّعَ أَمۡعَآءَهُمۡ

 and are given to drink boiling water, that cuts-up their bowels?

1.5 BATARA (بَتَرَ): This word is used to refer to cutting the tail of an animal. It also means to clip off generations, childless or someone who won’t be remembered in good terms. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Kauther (Abundance): سُوۡرَةُ الکَوثَر108:3

 إِنَّ شَانِئَكَ هُوَ ٱلۡأَبۡتَرُ

 For he who hated you, he will be cut off (from Future Hope).

 

1.6 BATAKA (بتك): According to Khalil this word means to cut the ear of. According to Imam Raghib this word means to slice the ears or other organ or hair of an animal. During the times of ignorance people used to do these things as an offering or sacrifice. In the Quran

Surah An-Nisa (The women): سورۃ النِّسا 4:119

 وَلَأُضِلَّنَّهُمۡ وَلَأُمَنِّيَنَّهُمۡ وَلَأَمُرَنَّهُمۡ فَلَيُبَتِّڪُنَّ ءَاذَانَ ٱلۡأَنۡعَـٰمِ

“And I will mislead them, and I will entice them, and I will prompt them to slit the ears of cattle, and I will prompt them to alter the creation of God.”

1.7 ADDA (عَضَّ): To bite an animal with teeth, to bite. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Furqaan (The criterion): سورۃ الفُرْقان 25:27

وَيَوۡمَ يَعَضُّ ٱلظَّالِمُ عَلَىٰ يَدَيۡهِ يَقُولُ يَـٰلَيۡتَنِى ٱتَّخَذۡتُ مَعَ ٱلرَّسُولِ سَبِيلاً۬

On that Day, the wrongdoer will bite his hands, and say, “If only I had followed the way with the Messenger.

1.8 KHADADA (خَضَدَ): To cut or remove the thorns of a tree. To make thorn-less (dictionary Al Manjad). In the Quran

Surah Al-Waqiah (The Inevitable): سُوۡرَةُ الواقِعَة 56:28

فِى سِدۡرٍ۬ مَّخۡضُودٍ۬

 (They will be) among Lote-trees without thorns,

 

1.9 JAZZA (جَزَّ): The word actually means to cut or grind into tiny pieces. AL-JIZZ (الجِزّ) means a small piece of a chopped up thing, JUZAD (جُزاد) or JUZAZ (جُزاز) means a slice or a piece of gold, JAAZAT (جَازات) means  small bits and pieces of a chopped up thing (dictionary Al Manjad). In Quran this word is used for the same meanings.  فَجَعَلَهُمۡ جُذَٲذًا (21:58)  ( So he reduced them into pieces) and this word was started to mean cutting and breaking. In the Quran

Surah Hud (Hud): سورۃ ھود 11:108

 عَطَآءً غَيۡرَ مَجۡذُوذٍ۬

 a gift without break.

 

1.10 AQARA (عَقَرَ): Has the meaning of wounding in it. AL-KALB-AQOOR (الکلب العقور) means a biting dog. AQAR-AN-NAKHLA (عَقَرَالنَّخْلَۃ) means to uproot a date palm, cut it from its roots (dictionary Al Manjad). In the Quran

Surah Ash-Shams (The Sun): سُوۡرَةُ الشّمس 91:14

فَكَذَّبُوهُ فَعَقَرُوهَا

Then they rejected him (as a false prophet), and they hamstrung her.

Summary:

1.1  HASADA: To Cut a ripe crop with sickle

1.2 SARAMA: To cut bunch of fruits with a sharp tool.

1.3 QATA’A: To slice of something.

1.4 QATTA’A: To cut into pieces

1.5 BATARA: To cut an animal’s tail, to be childless

1.6 BATAKA: To slice or cut an ear

1.7 ADDA: To bite

1.9 JAZZA: To cut a hard thing into small pieces.
