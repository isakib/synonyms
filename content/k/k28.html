28 KOI [any, anyone, one]

Synonyms:  AHAD, MAN and MIN

28.1 AHAD (اَحَد): Means incomparable, extraordinary, one and peerless like قُلۡ هُوَ ٱللَّهُ أَحَدٌ (Say, “He is God, the One.) (112:1) and in numeric system it means one ‘1’. Like AHADA-ASHARA (اَحَدَعَشَرَ), (feminine IHDAA (اِحْدٰی)). And the third use of this word is to represent one element in a group, both in positive and negative means. And used for everything usually. For instance

Surah Yusuf (Joseph): سورۃ یُوسُف 12:78

 فَخُذۡ أَحَدَنَا مَڪَانَهُ ۥۤ‌ۖ

so take one of us 

And when used in a negative sense it will give the meaning of ‘anyone’. For instance

Surah Al-Haqqah (The reality): سورۃ الحَاقَّۃ 69:47

 فَمَا مِنكُم مِّنۡ أَحَدٍ عَنۡهُ حَـٰجِزِينَ

Nor could any of you withhold him (from Our wrath).

28.2 MAN (مَنْ) MIN (مِنْ): These two words sometime represent unknown units of a group. The only difference in that MAN (مَنْ) is used for any intelligent being and gives the meaning of ‘anyone’ or ‘any person’. Like

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:8

 وَمِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ مَن يَقُولُ ءَامَنَّا بِٱللَّهِ

Among the people are those who say, “We believe in God and in the Last Day,”

And MIN (مِنْ) is usually used for creations without intelligence. For example in the Quran

Surah Al-Isra (The Night Journey): سُوۡرَةُ الإسرَاء 17:44

 وَإِن مِّن شَىۡءٍ إِلَّا يُسَبِّحُ بِحَمۡدِهِۦ

There is not a thing that does not glorify Him with praise,

Summary:

28.1 AHAD: Means one of the groups. Usually used for all intelligent beings

28.2 MAN, MIN: MAN is used for one or many units of a group, usually used for intelligent beings. MIN is used for dumb creations.
