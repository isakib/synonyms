33 – Religion

 

The words دِين, شَرِيعَة and مِلَّة have been used in the Quran

 

33.1 DEEN (دِين): this term holds numerous meanings; four of these will be outlined here.  (1) It refers to the whole and complete sovereignty of Allah, (2) the complete enslavement and obedience /devotion of humans, (3) the law of recompense (reward and punishment) and (4) the power of enforcement of the law of recompense.  The term can come in one meaning or more than one, for example

 

39:3

Surat Az-Zumar (The Troops) - الزمر سورة

 أَلَا لِلَّهِ ٱلدِّينُ ٱلۡخَالِصُ‌ (﻿٣﻿)

Unquestionably, for Allah is the pure religion (3)

 

In this verse, the term holds the essence of all four of the above meanings.  دِين requires that Allah ordains some things for humans and forbids certain things and who ever fails to obey His commands is then recompensed accordingly.  The commands that have been enjoined from Adam (peace be upon him) to the prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) are the real religion.  For example associating partners with the Almighty, stealing, fornication, indecency, etc have always been prohibited while the certainty of the accounting that will take place in the hereafter, etc.  The submission to these commands is called Islam.

 

Surat 'Āli `Imrān (Family of Imran) - عمران آل سورة

 3:19

 إِنَّ ٱلدِّينَ عِندَ ٱللَّهِ ٱلۡإِسۡلَـٰمُ‌ۗ (﻿١٩﻿)

Indeed, the religion in the sight of Allah is Islam (19)

 

33.2 SHARI’AH (شَرِيعَة): linguistically شَرَعَ refers to specify a clear path.  شَرِيعَة refers to the commands that change according to the time and surrounding conditions.  For example, during the time of Adam (peace be upon him), the marriage of blood siblings was not prohibited, but such a bond was prohibited later on.  Prophet Yaqoob (Jacob, peace be upon him) was married to two blood sisters, this was also forbidden later on.  Similarly, during these times, prostrating to someone other than God because of their greatness was allowed but it was forbidden in the commands that came later on.  Likewise, earlier nations were not allowed anything from war booty while for the Muslims it is lawful to take from it.  The difference between شَرِيعَة and دِين is clarified by the Prophet (peace be upon him) in the following narration:

 

 (متفق عليه) وَاحِدٌ وَدِينُهُم شَتّىٰ اُمَّهَاتُهُم العَلَّاتِ اِخوَةُ اَلاَنبِياءُ

“The prophets are brothers (brothers which have the same father and different mothers), their mothers are different (referring to the شَرِيعَة) while their religion (دِين) is one. (Bukhari & Muslim)

In the Quran, Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat Ash-Shūraá (The Consultation) - الشورى سورة

42:13

 شَرَعَ لَكُم مِّنَ ٱلدِّينِ (﻿١٣﻿)

He has ordained for you of religion (13)

 

33.3 MILLAH (مِلَّة): Everything that is included in دِين and شَرِيعَة is revealed by Allah.  Along with conveying these commands, the prophet is the first to abide by them.  Gradually, an assembly of followers emerges who also abide by these commands and thus the Islamic society/system forms.  This system/society is called a مِلَّة.  مِلَّة does not refer to the commands but to the system where the prophet is the leader, the followers follow the prophet and the way of life is based on the دِين and شَرِيعَة.  It is compulsory for the followers to obey the leader.  مِلَّة is often translated as ‘religion’ while the religion of all the prophets is the same.  مِلَّة is used to point towards a special prophet.  For example:

 

Surat 'Āli `Imrān (Family of Imran) - عمران آل سورة

 3:95

فَٱتَّبِعُواْ مِلَّةَ إِبۡرَٲهِيمَ حَنِيفً۬ا (﻿٩٥﻿)

So follow the religion of Abraham, inclining toward truth (95)

 

Summary:

1.      دِين: the commands of Allah that have been the same from Adam (peace be upon him) to Muhammad (peace be upon him) and their obedience

2.      شَرِيعَة: the commands of Allah that change according to the time and conditions

3.      مِلَّة: the system that a prophet, in obedience with the commands of God, establishes amongst his followers
