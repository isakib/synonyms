Running-20

 

Synonyms are:

1)    سَرَعَ

2)    سَعٰى

3)    زَفَّ

4)    رَكَضَ

5)    جَمَعَ

6)    هَرَعَ

7)    نَسَلَ

8)    اَوْفضَ

9)    هَطَعَ

10)                       ضَبَحَ

11)                       سَبَقَ (اِسْتَبَقَ)

 

1)saraa سَرَعَ:to finish the task quickly that is meant to finish and not delaying it and doing it quickly Mufraddat - مفردات.and in pace saraa means fast pace only. To walk fast even though there is no danger  Fiqhul lughat - فقه اللغة -33 and  its opposite is  bataa means slow:

Allah says in Quran

يَوْمَ يَخْرُجُونَ مِنَ الْأَجْدَاثِ سِرَاعًا

70:43 (surah 70 marij verse 43)

translation: The Day whereon they will issue from their sepulchres in sudden haste.

 

2) saaa سَعٰى :the a gait that is not very fast like running nor slow like usual gait but in between both of them.saa is the pace at which one  walk in between  safa and marwa.

Allah says:

وَجَاءَ مِنْ أَقْصَى الْمَدِينَةِ رَجُلٌ يَسْعَىٰ

36:23 (surah 36 yaseen verse 23)

Translation: Then there came running, from the farthest part of the City.

3)zaaf زَفَّ: the normal  running gait. And as per the munjiid author running fast.

Allah says:

فَرَاغَ عَلَيْهِمْ ضَرْبًا بِالْيَمِينِفَأَقْبَلُوا إِلَيْهِ يَزِفُّونَ

37:93-94 (surah 37 as safaat verse 93-94)

Translation : Then did he turn upon them, striking (them) with the right hand. Then came (the worshippers) with hurried steps, and faced (him).

4)rakdza رَكَضَ: means to move heel  “رَكَضَ الرِّجُلُ دآبّتَهُ” he kick his animal with heel so that he can get ahead Maqaayeesul-lughat - مقائيس اللغة. To hit or squeeze horse with heels on its sides (mayb stomach or kidneys ).to gallop.

Allah says in quran:

فَلَمَّا أَحَسُّوا بَأْسَنَا إِذَا هُم مِّنْهَا يَرْكُضُونَ

21:12 (surah 21 al anbiyaa verse 12)

Translation: Yet, when they felt Our Punishment (coming), behold, they (tried to) flee from it.

5)jamaha جَمَعَ :to get out of control. Horse running very fast and get out of riders control Munjid - منجد

Allah says in quran:

لَوْ يَجِدُونَ مَلْجَأً أَوْ مَغَارَاتٍ أَوْ مُدَّخَلًا لَّوَلَّوْا إِلَيْهِ وَهُمْ يَجْمَحُونَ

9:57 (surah 9 at tawbaah verse 57)

Translation: If they could find a place to flee to, or caves, or a place of concealment, they would turn straightaway thereto, with an obstinate rush.

 

6)haraa هَرَعَ :to break forth. to run because of uncontrollable emotions. To run in Shivery manner. Fiqhul lughat - فقه اللغة  33 To rush or run out of restlessness .to become restless because of rage ,enervation , terror or  cold and run Miraatul Quraan – مراة القرا.

Allah says in Quran:

وَجَاءَهُ قَوْمُهُ يُهْرَعُونَ إِلَيْهِ

11:78 (surah 11 hud verse 78)

Translation: And his people came rushing towards him.

 

7) nasala   نَسَلَ: to run from height  toward lowland. As per the author of munjid dictionary it also means to walk fast and plunk. And according to ibn al faris it means to generate one thing from other slowlyMaqaayeesul-lughat - مقائيس اللغة But in the below verse this meaning cannot be applied.

 

حَتَّىٰ إِذَا فُتِحَتْ يَأْجُوجُ وَمَأْجُوجُ وَهُم مِّن كُلِّ حَدَبٍ يَنسِلُونَ

21:96 (surah 21 al anbiyaa verse 96)

Translation: Until the Gog and Magog (people) are let through (their barrier), and they swiftly swarm from every hill..

8)aoofazda اَوْفضَ :runnig fast.and alwafdah الوَفضَة means a leather quiver in which arrows are kept Munjid - منجد  and as per imam Raghib to run like a hunter run towards the trap. Mufraddat - مفردات

Allah says in Quran:

كَأَنَّهُمْ إِلَىٰ نُصُبٍ يُوفِضُونَ

70:43 (surah 70 an maariij verse 43)

Translation: as if they were rushing to a goal-post (fixed for them).

9)hataa هَطَعَ:to stare the thing in front , become obedient and dedicated and run Maqaayeesul-lughat - مقائيس اللغة.

Allah says in Quran:

مُّهْطِعِينَ إِلَى الدَّاعِ ۖ يَقُولُ الْكَافِرُونَ هَٰذَا يَوْمٌ عَسِرٌ

54:8 (surah  54 al qamar verse 8)

Translation: Hastening, with eyes transfixed, towards the Caller!- "Hard is this Day!", the Unbelievers will say.

 

10)dabhaa ضَبَحَ: horse gallop in a way that it starts gasping.

وَالْعَادِيَاتِ ضَبْحًا

100:1 (surah 100 al aadiyaat verse 1)

Transltion: By the (Steeds) that run, with panting (breath),

11)sabaqa سَبَقَ and istabaqa اِسْتَبَقَ .sabqa means taking precedence. Getting ahead in a competition. running. and istabqa means trying to overtake one another in a competition.

Allah says:

وَلَا يَحْسَبَنَّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا سَبَقُوا ۚ إِنَّهُمْ لَا يُعْجِزُونَ

8:59 (surah 8 al anfaal verse 59)

Translation: Let not the unbelievers think that they can get the better (of the godly): they will never frustrate (them).

In another place

وَاسْتَبَقَا الْبَابَ وَقَدَّتْ قَمِيصَهُ مِن دُبُرٍ

12:25 (surah 12 yousaf verse 25)

Translation: So they both raced each other to the door, and she tore his shirt from the back.

Summary:

1)saraa:to walk fast.

2)saa:the medium gait which is not very slow like walking not like running.

3)zaaf:to run with a normal gait.

4)rakaza:to gallop.

5)jamaa:to break the ropes(as if to break the boundaries) to get out of control.

6)haraa:to break forth. To run because of uncontrollable emotion

7)nasala:to run from a height  toward lowland.

8)wafada:to run fast and cautious like the way hunter runs towards the trap.

9)hataa:to focus the glare in front and run obediently towards.

10)Zabhaa:horse galloping so fast that it starts gasping.

11)sabqa or istabqa: to run for  getting  ahead of the competitor.
