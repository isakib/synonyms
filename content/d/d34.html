34 – To Give

 

The words اٰتٰى, اَعطٰى (عطو), اَدَاء (ادو ادى), دِيَة, , اَثَاب (ثوب), وَهَبَ, رَفَدَ, , اِلٰى دَفَعَ, هَدِيَّة and نَحَلَ have been used in the holy Quran

 

34.1 ATAA (اٰتٰى): to give; to give something to someone.  Used in common language.

 

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - البقرة سورة

 2:177

وَءَاتَى ٱلۡمَالَ عَلَىٰ حُبِّهِۦ (﻿١٧٧﻿)

And gives wealth, in spite of love for it (177)

 

34.2 A’ATAA (اَعطٰى): used in two meanings: (1) to give something to someone as a favor or to forgive.

 

Surat Ţāhā (Ta-Ha) - طه سورة

 20:50

 قَالَ رَبُّنَا ٱلَّذِىٓ أَعۡطَىٰ كُلَّ شَىۡءٍ خَلۡقَهُ ۥ ثُمَّ هَدَىٰ (﻿٥٠﻿)

He said, "Our Lord is He who gave each thing its form and then guided [it]." (50)

 

(2) اَعطٰى can also mean to obey, to serve.  Thus giving someone more than what one owes them for their obedience and services is also اَعطٰى.  To gift, to reward because of satisfaction with hard work or services are also considered اَعطٰى.

 

Surat Hūd (Hud) - هود سورة

 11:108

 وَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ سُعِدُواْ فَفِى ٱلۡجَنَّةِ خَـٰلِدِينَ فِيہَا مَا دَامَتِ ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٲتُ وَٱلۡأَرۡضُ إِلَّا مَا شَآءَ رَبُّكَ‌ۖ عَطَآءً غَيۡرَ مَجۡذُوذٍ۬ (﻿١٠٨﻿)

And as for those who were [destined to be] prosperous, they will be in Paradise, abiding therein as long as the heavens and the earth endure, except what your Lord should will - a bestowal uninterrupted (108).  

 

34.3 ADAA (اَدَاء): to give someone what is rightfully theirs and to give it all at once.  This term is usually used in situations where one is giving or taking such as in loans, trusts, etc.

 

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - البقرة سورة

2:283

 فَإِنۡ أَمِنَ بَعۡضُكُم بَعۡضً۬ا فَلۡيُؤَدِّ ٱلَّذِى ٱؤۡتُمِنَ أَمَـٰنَتَهُ ۥ (﻿٢٨٣﻿)

And if one of you entrusts another, then let him who is entrusted discharge his trust [faithfully] (283)

 

34.4 DIYAH (دِيَة): (ودى) ودٰى – يدى – دِيَةٌ this term is specifically used for giving blood money; the giving of money in compensation for life.

 

Surat An-Nisā' (The Women) - النساء سورة

4:92

 فَدِيَةٌ۬ مُّسَلَّمَةٌ إِلَىٰٓ أَهۡلِهِۦ (﻿٩٢﻿)

Then a compensation payment presented to his family (92)

 

34.5 ATHAAB (اَثَاب): comes for the payment of anything, whether paying back for a good thing or a bad.  Usually used for the reward of good works.  Like اَعطٰى, it includes the meaning of giving what is due or a bit extra.

 

Surat Al-Mā'idah (The Table Spread) - المائدة سورة

5:85

 فَأَثَـٰبَهُمُ ٱللَّهُ بِمَا قَالُواْ جَنَّـٰتٍ۬ تَجۡرِى مِن تَحۡتِهَا ٱلۡأَنۡهَـٰرُ (﻿٨٥﻿)

So Allah rewarded them for what they said with gardens [in Paradise] beneath which rivers flow (85)

 

34.6 WAHABA (وَهَبَ): To grant/bestow/endow (without having received any services and without the expectation of getting anything in return).

 

Surat Ash-Shūraá (The Consultation) - الشورى سورة

42:49

يَہَبُ لِمَن يَشَآءُ إِنَـٰثً۬ا وَيَهَبُ لِمَن يَشَآءُ ٱلذُّكُورَ (﻿٤٩﻿)

He gives to whom He wills female [children], and He gives to whom He wills males (49)

 

34.7 RADAFA (رَفَدَ):  to bestow and/or to assist.  The term includes two fundamental things: to give to someone needy and poor and to give or allocate a stipend/benefit.  رفاده refers to the fund that the Quraish had created for needy pilgrims.  اَرفَدَ refers to allocating a benefit so that he/she can take from a set amount.  رفد refers to a stipend; assistance.

 

Surat Hūd (Hud) - هود سورة

11:99

 وَأُتۡبِعُواْ فِى هَـٰذِهِۦ لَعۡنَةً۬ وَيَوۡمَ ٱلۡقِيَـٰمَةِ‌ۚ بِئۡسَ ٱلرِّفۡدُ ٱلۡمَرۡفُودُ (﻿٩٩﻿)

And they were followed in this [world] with a curse and on the Day of Resurrection. And wretched is the gift which is given (99)

 

34.8 DAFA’A -دَفَعَ (اِلٰى): دفع means to protect something from external dangers and attack and to ward such threats off.  When the term is directed towards اِلٰى, it means to give, or to give something back to its original possessor.

 

Surat An-Nisā' (The Women) - النساء سورة

4:6

 فَإِنۡ ءَانَسۡتُم مِّنۡہُمۡ رُشۡدً۬ا فَٱدۡفَعُوٓاْ إِلَيۡہِمۡ أَمۡوَٲلَهُمۡ‌ۖ (﻿٦﻿)

Then if you perceive in them sound judgement, release their property to them (6)

 

34.9 HADIIYAH (هَدِيَّة): refers to the gifts that are exchanged to uphold or enhance relationships.  مَهدٰى refers to the object in which a gift is presented.

 

Surat An-Naml (The Ant) - النمل سورة

27:36

 فَمَآ ءَاتَٮٰنِۦَ ٱللَّهُ خَيۡرٌ۬ مِّمَّآ ءَاتَٮٰكُم بَلۡ أَنتُم بِہَدِيَّتِكُمۡ تَفۡرَحُونَ (﻿٣٦﻿)

 But what Allah has given me is better than what He has given you. Rather, it is you who rejoice in your gift (36)

 

34.10 NAHALA (نَحَلَ):  to give something to someone.  المِراَة نَحَلَ refers to the give a woman her mahr (dower money).  نَحل refers to something that is given as a token of forgiveness.  اَلنَّحلَة and اَلنِّحلَة refers to something as a donation.  This is different from a gift, every gift can be considered a اَلنَّحلَة but not every اَلنَّحلَة can be considered a gift.

 

Surat An-Nisā' (The Women) - النساء سورة

4:4

 وَءَاتُواْ ٱلنِّسَآءَ صَدُقَـٰتِہِنَّ نِحۡلَةً۬‌ۚ (﻿٤﻿)

And give the women [upon marriage] their [bridal] gifts graciously (4)

 

Summary:

1.      اٰتٰى: to give, commonly used term

2.      اَعطٰى:  to give forgivingly, to give more than what is due as a payment of services

3.      اَدَاء: to give rights or in financial dealings

4.      دِيَة: to give blood money

5.      اَثَاب: to give more than what is required in the payment of anything

6.      وَهَبَ: to give without having acquired anything and to not expect anything or advantage in return

7.      رَفَدَ: to give a stipend, assistance or something like it to someone needy

8.      دَفَعَ (اِلٰى):  to return something to its owner

9.      هَدِيَّة: to give gifts as cordiality

10.  نَحَلَ: to give a gift pleasantly

34. Giving - Dena - (دينا)  

Synonyms:-

Ata (اٰتٰی), Aata (اَعْطَى), Ada (اَدَاء), Deyatun (دِيَة), Asaaba (اَثَاب), Wahaba (وَهَبَ), Rafada (رَفَدَ), DafaAa (Ila) ((اِلٰی) دَفَعَ), Hadeyyat (هَدِيِّة), Nahala (نَحَلَ)

34.1. Ata (اٰتٰی): To give someone something.

Quranic Example:-

“…and gives wealth, in spite of love for it…” (2:177)

34.2. Aata (اَعْطَى): Two meanings.

1). To give someone something as a favor to them. To something bestow upon someone (Mufraddat).

Quranic Example:-

“He said, “Our Lord is He who *gave* each thing its form and then guided

[it].”” (20:50)

2). To be loyal to someone and to serve them (Munjid). And therefore it

also means to give someone a reward greater than their service or hard work.

Quranic Example:-

“And as for those who were [destined to be] prosperous, they will be in

Paradise, abiding therein as long as the heavens and the earth endure,

except what your Lord should will – a bestowal uninterrupted.” (11:108)

34.3. Ada (اَدَاء): To give someone their due right in full. This word is usually used in “give & take” situations such as with a deposit, loan or a tribute.

Quranic Example:-

“And if one of you entrusts another, then let him who is entrusted

discharge his trust [faithfully] and let him fear Allah, his Lord.” (2:283)

34.4. Deyatun (دِيَة): Used to mean blood money but specifically

payment/ransom for someone who was unintentionally killed.

Quranic Example:-

“And whoever kills a believer by mistake – then the freeing of a believing

slave and a compensation payment presented to the deceased’s family [is required] unless they give [up their right as] charity.” (4:92)

34.5. Asaaba (اَثَابَ): A reward or return for any sort of act good or bad, although usually it is used for a return for good acts. Also this word contains some characteristics of 34.1.Aata (اَعْطَى) i.e. to give the return equal to or more than what the act deserves.

Quranic Example:-

“So Allah *rewarded* them for what they said with gardens [in Paradise]

beneath which rivers flow, wherein they abide eternally.” (5:85)

34.6. Wahaba (وَهَبَ):To give someone something not as a return and

without anything done by them to deserve it, and without any expectation of

receiving anything in return for it.

Quranic Example:-

“He gives to whom He wills female [children], and He gives to whom He wills males.” (42:49)

34.7. Rafada (رَفَدَ): Its meaning is based on two things; ‘to give’ and ‘to help’. It could mean like to give the poor something as a donation, or, the giving of or appointing of allowance to someone. Rafadah (رفادہ)

is the name of that fund that the Quraish used to keep for those hopeful for performing Pilgrimage (hajj) but were too impoverished to do so. And

Arfada (اَرْفَدَ) means to establish an amount of donation for someone

such that they keep taking their desired amount from that appointed amount (Mufraddat). And Rafd (رفد) means donation.

Quranic Example:-

“And they were followed in this [world] with a curse and on the Day of

Resurrection. And wretched is the gift which is given.” (11:99)

34.8. DafaAa (Ila) ((اِلٰی) دَفَعَ): DafaAa (دَفَعَ) means that for the protection of something keeping any external dangers and attacks away (Mufraddat).  And when it is used with Ila (اِلٰی) then that means to hand over someone their possession.

Quranic Example:-

“And test the orphans [in their abilities] until they reach marriageable

age. Then if you perceive in them sound judgement, release their property

to them.” (4:6)

34.9. Hadeyyat (هَدِيِّة): These are those gifts we give each other as a welcome or upon the pleasure of meeting them. And Mehda (مِهْدٰى) is the thing in which this gift is put in to give.

Quranic Example:-

“But what Allah has given me is better than what He has given you. Rather,

it is you who rejoice in your gift.” (27:36)

34.10. Nahala (نَحَلَ): To give someone something. Nehal Almira (نَحَلَ الْمِرْاة) is giving a bride her promissory gift from her in-laws at the time of marriage. And Nahal (نَحل) is compensation (Munjid). Therefore, Al'nahlat (الْنَّحْلَة) and Al'nihlat (الْنِّحْلَة) are both used for a compensation that is given without the hopes of getting something in return – that is the meaning of Tabran (تَبرعاً).Therefore, both the words are associated with Hiba (ہبہ) which is a gift. And any kind of Hiba or gift can be called Nihlat (نِحْلَة), but any kind of Nihlat cannot be called Hiba, because all compensations are not gifts but, all gifts are compensations, without demanding something in return.

Quranic Example:-

“And give the women [upon marriage] their [bridal] gifts graciously. (4:4)

Summary:-

Ata (اٰتٰی):  To give. Used commonly.

Aata (اَعْطَى): A reward for a service or hard work given greater that

what it deserves.

Ada (اَدَاءٌ): To give someone their due right in full.

Deyat (دِيَة): Payment of blood money.

Asaaba (اَثَابَ): Similar to Aata (اَعْطَى). To give a return for a

work more than what it deserves.

Wahab (وَهب): To give something not as a return or reward and without

expectation of receiving a return for it.

Rafada (رَفَدَ): To help the poor or destitute by giving them a donation.

DafaAa (Ila) ((اِلٰی) دَفَعَ): To hand over someone their possession.

Hadeyyat (هَدِيَّة): Those gifts we give each other as a welcome or upon

the pleasure of meeting them.

Nehlatan (نِحْلَةَ): A compensation given without hopes of getting

something in return.
