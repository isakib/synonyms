26. DHUNDLANA/DHUNDLA NAZAR AANA [blurry, faded, vague]

Synonyms: INKADARA, ABYAZZA, ‘AMAA, GHUMMA

 

26.1 INKADARA (اِنْكَدَرَ): Kadarun (كَدَرٌ) means dingy,muddy [opposite of Saffaun (صَفَاءٌ)]. Kudratun (كُدْرَةٌ) means the state of being not bright,vivid, or shining for a colour. Allah says in the Quran:

 

Surat At-Takwīr (The Overthrowing) - التكوير سورة

81:2

وَإِذَا النُّجُومُ انكَدَرَتْ

When the stars fall, losing their luster

 

26.2 ABYAZZA (اَبْيَضَّ) means whiten - make or become white [opposite of Sawad (سَوَاد) meaning blackness]. Biyaz-ul-‘ain (العين بياض) means eyes becoming white. Ibyazzat Aiyaanuhu (عَيْنُهُ اِبَيَضَّت) means his eyes turned white or he lost his sight as in Quran:

Surat Yūsuf (Joseph) - يوسف  سورة

12:84

وَابْيَضَّتْ عَيْنَاهُ مِنَ الْحُزْنِ فَهُوَ كَظِيمٌ

And he turned away from them and said, "Oh, my sorrow over Joseph," and his eyes became white from grief, for he was [of that] a suppressor.

 

26.3 ‘AMMA (عَمَّى)   : ‘Amia  (عَمِىَ) means blindness - state of being blind and ‘Amiyatil akhbaru an fulaanin  (فُلَانٍ عَنْ عَمِيَتِالْاَخْبَارُ) means the person is lost somewhere and no one knows his location. ‘Amal m’anaa (الْمَعْنٰى عَمَّى is to make vague; to hide. Allah says in the Quran:

 

Surat Fuşşilat (Explained in Detail) - فصلت سورة

41:44

وَالَّذِينَ لَا يُؤْمِنُونَ فِي آذَانِهِمْ وَقْرٌ وَهُوَ عَلَيْهِمْ عَمًى

And those who do not believe - in their ears is deafness, and it is upon them blindness.

 

26.4 GHUMMA (غُمَّةً) : To hide something and ghummaa (غُمّٰى) means darkness and dusty. Ghamam (غمام)  means clouds that cover the sunlight of the sun. Ghummatal amr (الْاَمْر غُمَّ)ةَ means complex or obscure matter. Allah says:

 

Surat Yūnus (Jonah) - يونس سورة

10:71

فَأَجْمِعُوا أَمْرَكُمْ وَشُرَكَاءَكُمْ ثُمَّ لَا يَكُنْ أَمْرُكُمْ عَلَيْكُمْ غُمَّةً ثُمَّ اقْضُوا إِلَيَّ وَلَا تُنظِرُونِ

So resolve upon your plan and [call upon] your associates. Then let not your plan be obscure to you. Then carry it out upon me and do not give me respite.

 

Summary:

26.1 INKADARA: Fading of the color

26.2 AMAL AMAR: Vague or hidden matter

26.3 ABAYAZZA ‘AINUHU: Eyes becoming white

26.4 GHUMMATUL AMR: complex or obscure matter
