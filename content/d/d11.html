11. Dushmani دشمنی (Rivalry – Enmity)

Also known as ‘aduww’ عدُوّ and ‘adawah’ عَدَاوَة, ‘baghdaa’ بَغضَاء and ‘shana’an’ شَنَان and ‘shaani’a’ شَانِيءَ

1.      Aduww عدُوّ: (aadaa) اعدَاء It refers so such a person who is a worse kind of enemy. One who thinks and schemes for evil, harm and loss, and feels joyful at suffering and misery. The antonym is ‘Siddique’ صدّيق .

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Araaf (The Heights)  سُوۡرَةُ الاٴعرَاف

(7:22)

 إِنَّ ٱلشَّيۡطَـٰنَ لَكُمَا عَدُوٌّ۬ مُّبِينٌ۬

“So he made them fall, through deception. And when they tasted of the tree, their private parts became apparent to them, and they began to fasten together over themselves from the leaves of Paradise. And their Lord called to them, "Did I not forbid you from that tree and tell you that Satan is to you a clear enemy?”

Adawah عَدَاوَة means enmity and antagonism. It is the first stage of rivalry. It means to be put off by someone’s victory. (Antonym: ‘wilayat’ ولَايت [guardianship])

2.      Baghdaa بَغضَاء: ‘Bughd’ بُغض (whose antonym is ‘Hubb’ حُبّ) means to be disgusted by or be hateful towards something or someone. This is the second stage of enmity. ‘Bughdaah’ بغضة means to aim to make someone contemptible, worthless and humiliated.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Maidah (The Table Spread) سُوۡرَةُ المَائدة

(5:64)

وَأَلۡقَيۡنَا بَيۡنَہُمُ ٱلۡعَدَٲوَةَ وَٱلۡبَغۡضَآءَ إِلَىٰ يَوۡمِ ٱلۡقِيَـٰمَةِ‌ۚ

“And the Jews say, "The hand of Allah is chained." Chained are their hands, and cursed are they for what they say. Rather, both His hands are extended; He spends however He wills. And that which has been revealed to you from your Lord will surely increase many of them in transgression and disbelief. And We have cast among them animosity and hatred until the Day of Resurrection. Every time they kindled the fire of war [against you], Allah extinguished it. And they strive throughout the land [causing] corruption, and Allah does not like corrupters.”

3.      Shaani’a شَانِيءَ: ‘Shanaa’ شَنَاءَ means to hold contempt, antagonism and malice. To hate. It is the root for ‘shanaa’an’ شَنَاءَن.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Maidah (The Table Spread) سُوۡرَةُ المَائدة

(5:8)

وَلَا يَجۡرِمَنَّڪُمۡ شَنَـَٔانُ قَوۡمٍ عَلَىٰٓ أَلَّا تَعۡدِلُواْ‌ۚ

“O you who have believed, be persistently standing firm for Allah , witnesses in justice, and do not let the hatred of a people prevent you from being just. Be just; that is nearer to righteousness. And fear Allah ; indeed, Allah is Acquainted with what you do.”

‘Shaani’ شَانِيء is a person who not only holds hatred and antagonism but is also vindictive; which means he holds contempt and malice concurrently. This is the worst kind of enemy. It is the third stage of enmity.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Kauther (The Abundance)  سُوۡرَةُ الکَوثَر 

(108:3)

إِنَّ شَانِئَكَ هُوَ ٱلۡأَبۡتَرُ

“Indeed, your enemy is the one cut off.”
