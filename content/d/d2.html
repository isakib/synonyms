Guard:

The words used for guard in Arabic are (i) خَزَنَه and (ii) مُصِيطِر

(i) خَزَنَه

This is the plural of خَازن. It means the treasurer or trustee, or the one who is responsible for collecting money or property. This word is also used for angels who are assigned duties in the Hellfire. Allah says in the Quran:

كُلَّمَا أُلْقِيَ فِيهَا فَوْجٌ سَأَلَهُمْ خَزَنَتُهَا أَلَمْ يَأْتِكُمْ نَذِيرٌ

Every time a group is cast therein, its keeper will ask: "Did no warner come to you?" (67:8)

(ii) مُصِيطِر

It is also spelled with a س so in Quran, a small س is written over this word to clarify that. Some mention the root as سطر and others mention the root of this word as سطِير.

سطِيرِ على means to overpower someone or become a guardian. سطِير is a custodian.

Allah says in the Quran:

فَذَكِّرْ إِنَّمَا أَنْتَ مُذَكِّرٌ لَسْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ بِمُصَيْطِرٍ

So remind them (O Muhammad (sallellahu alaihi wasallam)), you are only a one who reminds.You are not a dictator over them. (88:21-22)

Meaning that they are in no position to convince you of what they want.

Conclusion:

The same difference lies between خَازن and مُصِيطِر as it is in حفِيظ and رَقِيب.

خَازن is only a caretaker, while مُصِيطِر is a custodian and has responsibilities over the things he is taking care of.
