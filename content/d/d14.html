14. Dil Mein Baat Daalna دل میں بات ڈالنا (To Drop an Idea/Hint in the Heart)

Also known as ‘Wahi’ وَحىٌ, ‘Ilhaam’ اِلهَام, ‘Ilqaa’ اِلقَاء , ‘Waswaas’ وَسوَاس and ‘Hamazaat’ هَمَزَات in the Holy Quran.

1.      Wahi وَحىٌ: The lexical meaning is a strong sign. ‘Aohaa’ اَوحَى means to give a rapid sign about the unseen; hidden and unknown. There are many types of ‘Wahi’ وحى ; one of which deals with the inexpugnable and impregnable affairs.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Fussilat (Explained in Detail) سُوۡرَةُ فُصّلَت

(41:12)

 وَأَوۡحَىٰ فِى كُلِّ سَمَآءٍ أَمۡرَهَا‌ۚ

“And He completed them as seven heavens within two days and inspired in each heaven its command. And We adorned the nearest heaven with lamps and as protection. That is the determination of the Exalted in Might, the Knowing.”

In a different context, ‘wahi’ وحى has also been explained as an innate or natural guidance/intuition. For example, the instinct of a new born baby to dart towards the mother’s chest.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah An Nahl (The Bee) سُوۡرَةُ النّحل

(12:68)

وَأَوۡحَىٰ رَبُّكَ إِلَى ٱلنَّحۡلِ

“And your Lord inspired to the bee, "Take for yourself among the mountains, houses, and among the trees and [in] that which they construct.”

As though the guidance and intuition (wahi) وحى from Allah Taa’la comes to all creatures; mankind, animals and insects. Such intuition can come towards a non prophet as well.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Qasas (The Stories) سُوۡرَةُ القَصَص

(28:7)

وَأَوۡحَيۡنَآ إِلَىٰٓ أُمِّ مُوسَىٰٓ أَنۡ أَرۡضِعِيهِ‌ۖ

“And We inspired to the mother of Moses, "Suckle him; but when you fear for him, cast him into the river and do not fear and do not grieve. Indeed, We will return him to you and will make him [one] of the messengers."

In all of the above examples, the meaning of ‘wahi’ وحى has been used in its lexical context. In a similar connotation, a person also does ‘wahi’ وحى to another person.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Maryam (Mary) سُوۡرَةُ مَریَم

(19:11)

 فَأَوۡحَىٰٓ إِلَيۡہِمۡ أَن سَبِّحُواْ بُكۡرَةً۬ وَعَشِيًّ۬ا

“So he came out to his people from the prayer chamber and signaled to them to exalt [ Allah ] in the morning and afternoon.”

Even Satan (shaitaan) شيطان does ‘wahi’ وحى amongst each other.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Anaam (The Cattle) سُوۡرَةُ الاٴنعَام

(6:121)

 وَإِنَّ ٱلشَّيَـٰطِينَ لَيُوحُونَ إِلَىٰٓ أَوۡلِيَآٮِٕهِمۡ لِيُجَـٰدِلُوكُمۡ‌ۖ

“And do not eat of that upon which the name of Allah has not been mentioned, for indeed, it is grave disobedience. And indeed do the devils inspire their allies [among men] to dispute with you. And if you were to obey them, indeed, you would be associators [of others with Him].”

Therefore, ‘wahi’ وحى can be a guidance, a blessing or even sinister. The ‘wahi’ وحى that is guidance is always based on wellness, and the ‘wahi’ وحى that goes against the guidance is definitely the sinister whisperings. In the Islamic term, ‘wahi’ وحى refers to such knowledge, wisdom and insights of the unseen that Allah Taa’la sends onto his beloved creation (prophet) for the guidance of the mankind. Three of such scenarios have been discussed in the Holy Quran. (1) Allah Taa’la directly puts in the heart. (2) Allah Taa’la sends an angel to his messenger. (3) Communicate behind a veil. (Case in point Musa aleh assalam). Henceforth, the commonly used scenario from these is Allah Taa’la sends the guidance, the ‘wahi’ وحى through his angel (Gabriel aleh assalam) to his prophets and messengers who then verbalize it.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Baqarah (the Cow) سُوۡرَةُ البَقَرَة

(2:97)

قُلۡ مَن كَانَ عَدُوًّ۬ا لِّجِبۡرِيلَ فَإِنَّهُ ۥ نَزَّلَهُ ۥ عَلَىٰ قَلۡبِكَ بِإِذۡنِ ٱللَّهِ

“Say, "Whoever is an enemy to Gabriel - it is [none but] he who has brought the Qur'an down upon your heart, [O Muhammad], by permission of Allah , confirming that which was before it and as guidance and good tidings for the believers."

2.      Ilhaam اِلهَام:  It refers to the intuition/guidance that is induced in the heart by Allah taa’la or a wise and knowledgeable person. To provide insight, understanding and strengthen encouragement. Similar to ‘wahi’ وحى, ‘ilhaam’ اِلهَام can also come from Shaitaan شيطان, especially when it cannot be validated through any ayah or Islamic text. This is why, apart from the Sufi class, none of the scholars consider ‘ilhaam’ اِلهَام as debatable for evidence. The difference between ‘wahi’ وحى and ‘ilhaam’ اِلهَام is that one ‘ilhaam’ اِلهَام is applicable to the intellectual minded whereas ‘wahi’ وحى is common. Secondly, ‘ilhaam’ اِلهَام is associated with the notion of performing or not performing a particular task, whereas ‘wahi’ وحى constitutes enormity and vastness.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Ash Shams (The Sun) سُوۡرَةُ الشّمس

(91:8)

فَأَلۡهَمَهَا فُجُورَهَا وَتَقۡوَٮٰهَا

“And inspired it [with discernment of] its wickedness and its righteousness”

 

3.      Ilqaa اِلقَاء: The lexical meaning is ‘to put’. ‘Alqaa aala’ ألقَى عَلَى means to educate.

In the Holy Quran,

Surah Al Qamar (The Moon) سُوۡرَةُ القَمَر

(54:25)

أَءُلۡقِىَ ٱلذِّكۡرُ عَلَيۡهِ مِنۢ بَيۡنِنَا

“Has the message been sent down upon him from among us? Rather, he is an insolent liar."

‘Talaqa-ashaee min-hu’ تَلَقَى الشَئ مِنهُ means to learn; to receive education.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Baqarah (The Cow) سُوۡرَةُ البَقَرَة

(2:37)

فَتَلَقَّىٰٓ ءَادَمُ مِن رَّبِّهِۦ كَلِمَـٰتٍ۬

“Then Adam received from his Lord [some] words, and He accepted his repentance. Indeed, it is He who is the Accepting of repentance, the Merciful.”

As though ‘ilqaa’ اِلقَاء is the guidance/intuition put in a person’s heart that relates to education and the services of teaching and learning. Like ‘wahi’ وحى and ‘ilhaam’ اِلهَام, ‘ilqaa’ اِلقَاء can also come from Shaitaan. شيطان

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Haj (The Pilgrimage) سُوۡرَةُ الحَجّ

(22:52)

 فَيَنسَخُ ٱللَّهُ مَا يُلۡقِى ٱلشَّيۡطَـٰنُ

“And We did not send before you any messenger or prophet except that when he spoke [or recited], Satan threw into it [some misunderstanding]. But Allah abolishes that which Satan throws in; then Allah makes precise His verses. And Allah is Knowing and Wise.”

4.      Waswaas وَسوَاس:  From a medical perspective, it is a disease that weakens the mind due to domination or control. As a result, a person starts talking about nonsense things frivolously that were previously not in their heart and neither do these thoughts spring any benefit or wellness for anyone. ‘Wiswaas’ وِسوَاس refers to the disease of having doubts; unbeneficial or evil that drives its way into the heart. Shaitaan شيطان, or the act of Shaitaan شيطان to persuade to an evil deed or have evil thoughts set in a person’s heart. It also refers to the early stages of madness, as though ‘waswasa’ وسوسه places itself in all categories of evil thoughts that may arise in one’s heart or is put in by Shaitaan شيطان; and it is related only to Shaitaan شيطان.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah An Nas (The Mankind) سُوۡرَةُ النَّاس

(114:4-5)

مِن شَرِّ ٱلۡوَسۡوَاسِ ٱلۡخَنَّاسِ (﻿٤﻿) ٱلَّذِى يُوَسۡوِسُ فِى صُدُورِ ٱلنَّاسِ

“From the evil of the retreating whisperer - Who whispers [evil] into the breasts of mankind”

 

5.      Hamazaat هَمَزَات: ‘Hamaza yadullu Aala Zaghtin-wa Asr-in’ هَمَزَ يَدُلُّ عَلَى ضَغطٍ وَّ عَصرٍ which means to pinch, press, or nip. It refers to the act of poking a sharp object, like a wooden stick, on the back or the hind legs of an animal so that it may run faster. In Punjabi, a common term for this is ‘Chohnbarh Dena’ چونہبڑ دینا. It basically means that the Shaitaan شيطان not only puts an evil thought in a heart but also provokes and stimulates desire to pursue it. ‘Hamazaat’ هَمَزَات is also in relation with Shaitaan شيطان.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Muminun (The Believers) سُوۡرَةُ المؤمنون

(23:97)

وَقُل رَّبِّ أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنۡ هَمَزَٲتِ ٱلشَّيَـٰطِينِ

“And say, "My Lord, I seek refuge in You from the incitements of the devils,”

 

Conclusion:

(1)   Wahi وحى: Wahi وحى, Ilhaam اِلهَام and Ilqaa اِلقَاء can come from Allah Taa’la and Shaitaan شيطان as well; however waswasa وَسوَسَه and hamaz هَمَزَ are only in relation with Shaitaan شيطان.

(2)   Wahi وحى is related to learning, education, beliefs, rules, commands and the unseen. Ilhaam اِلهَام is only associated with the decision of performing or not performing a task. Ilqaa اِلقَاء is only related to education.

(3)   Waswasa وَسوَسَه : It only refers to the influx of evil thoughts in one’s heart, however hamaza هَمَزَ, other than the evil thought, also includes the element of persuasion from Shaitaan شيطان.

(4)   Every wahi وحى, ilhaam اِلهَام or ilqaa اِلقَاء that is in accordance with the Islamic law is a source of guidance, and if it is not, then it is sinister and coming from Shaitaan شيطان.
