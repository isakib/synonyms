15. Daleel دلیل (Evidence)

Also known as ‘daleel’ دَلِيل, ‘hujjah’ حُجَّة, ‘bayinnah’ بَيّنَة (bayyin) بيّن, ‘burhaan’ بُرهَان (burh wa burhn) بره و برهن and ‘sultaan’ سُلطَن in the Holy Quran.

1.      Daleel دَلِيل:  ‘Dalalat’ دَلَالَت and ‘daleel’ دَلِيل mean such guidance through which cognition of something and knowledge is obtained. Just a person comprehends that a living being is alive through its movement or a scripted/written vocabulary provides evidence of its meaning. Even in a regular conversation, the context in which a vocabulary is used, are evident of its comprehension. ‘Dalla’ دَلَّ means to express; to mention; to guide; to be a source of knowledge.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Furqan (The Criterian) سُوۡرَةُ الفُرقان

(25:45)

أَلَمۡ تَرَ إِلَىٰ رَبِّكَ كَيۡفَ مَدَّ ٱلظِّلَّ وَلَوۡ شَآءَ لَجَعَلَهُ ۥ سَاكِنً۬ا ثُمَّ جَعَلۡنَا ٱلشَّمۡسَ عَلَيۡهِ دَلِيلاً۬

“Have you not considered your Lord - how He extends the shadow, and if He willed, He could have made it stationary? Then We made the sun for it an indication.”

As though the growing of the length of shadows, the setting of the sun and the proportionality between them are evidence over each other; and the ability to assess the precise direction of the sun by the shortening or lengthening of its shadows. A similar determination can be made with timings. Also, the length and direction of a shadow can be determined by observing the direction of the sun.

2.      Hujjah حُجَّة: ‘Haajja’ حَاجَّ means to argue and to turn out dominant in the argument. ‘Almahajaah’ المحاجة is having every party reject the proposed argument of the other. ‘Hujjah’ حُجَّة is an argument having a group of peoples agree unanimously on something, and after coming to a conclusion, one party provides evidence to another. This evidence is called ‘Hujjah’ حُجَّة. For example, the disbelievers accepted the fact that their creator, master and sustainer is only Allah Taa’la so in relation to that Allah Taa’la says that why can supreme entity, that creates the human being from a drop by getting it through various processes and then brings it to death, not give birth to it again? Such an argument is called ‘Hujjah’. حُجَّة

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Anaam (The Cattle) سُوۡرَةُ الاٴنعَام

(6:83)

وَتِلۡكَ حُجَّتُنَآ ءَاتَيۡنَـٰهَآ إِبۡرَٲهِيمَ عَلَىٰ قَوۡمِهِۦ‌ۚ

“And that was Our [conclusive] argument which We gave Abraham against his people. We raise by degrees whom We will. Indeed, your Lord is Wise and Knowing.”

 

3.      Bayinnah بَيّنَة: It refers to clear and obvious/open dialogue; proof. Some interpretation of ‘Bayinnah’ بَيّنَة is also elicited through this hadeeth حديث of the Prophet (s.a.w). The Prophet (s.a.w) says: “Al-bayinnatu Aala-almudda-I wal-yameenu Aala-almuddaa Alaeh” اَلبَيّنَةُ عَلَى المُدَّعِي وَاليَميِنُ عَلَى المُدَّعَى عَلَيهِ (Bukhari). It means that it is the responsibility of the claimant to provide evidence and if he is not able to do so, then it is an adjuration on the defendant.

For example Zaid claims some money from Bakr to which Bakr denies. If Zaid carries a statement from Bakr proving Zaid’s claim or helps in proving Zaid’s claim then this statement is ‘Bayinnah’ بَيّنَة. Similarly, if Zaid presents a witness to prove his case then it is also called ‘Bayinnah’ بَيّنَة. ‘Bayinnah’ بَيّنَة is any strong argument making the opposing party dominated or speechless. The existence of the Holy Quran is ‘Bayinnah’ بَيّنَة itself, to that the disbelievers could not produce something similar even in a challenge. Evidently, the ayahs in the Holy Quran are ‘Bayinnah’ بَيّنَة as well.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Ankabut (The Spider) سُوۡرَةُ العَنکبوت

(29:49)

بَلۡ هُوَ ءَايَـٰتُۢ بَيِّنَـٰتٌ۬ فِى صُدُورِ ٱلَّذِينَ أُوتُواْ ٱلۡعِلۡمَ‌ۚ وَمَا يَجۡحَدُ بِـَٔايَـٰتِنَآ إِلَّا ٱلظَّـٰلِمُونَ

“Rather, the Qur'an is distinct verses [preserved] within the breasts of those who have been given knowledge. And none reject Our verses except the wrongdoers.”

In the above ayah, the use of the word ‘jahd’ جَحد clarifies the fact that they accepted these ayaahs as ‘Bayinnah’ بَيّنَة in their hearts but denied by their tongues.

 

4.      Burhaan بُرهَان : ‘Baraha بَرَة’ means white and bright/shiny. ‘Burhaan’ بُرهَان is such evidence or an argument that not proves truthfulness and brings clarity but also confirms the word of Allah Taa’la. According to some, ‘Burhaan’ بُرهَان is associated with the Persian word ‘Burraan’ بُرَّان, which means a sharp edged cutting sword. ‘Burhaan’ بُرهَان is such an argument that completely removes controversy.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Baqarah (The Cow) سُوۡرَةُ البَقَرَة

(2:111)

تِلۡكَ أَمَانِيُّهُمۡ‌ۗ قُلۡ هَاتُواْ بُرۡهَـٰنَڪُمۡ إِن ڪُنتُمۡ صَـٰدِقِينَ

“And they say, "None will enter Paradise except one who is a Jew or a Christian." That is [merely] their wishful thinking, Say, "Produce your proof, if you should be truthful."

In another location,

Surah As Qasas (The Stories) سُوۡرَةُ القَصَص

(28:32)

فَذَٲنِكَ بُرۡهَـٰنَانِ مِن رَّبِّكَ إِلَىٰ فِرۡعَوۡنَ وَمَلَإِيْهِۦۤ‌ۚ

“Insert your hand into the opening of your garment; it will come out white, without disease. And draw in your arm close to you [as prevention] from fear, for those are two proofs from your Lord to Pharaoh and his establishment. Indeed, they have been a people defiantly disobedient."

5.      Sultaan سُلطَان : Refers to domination, control and power. The royal decree. An authority letter. An argument that is verified by the divine revelation. A certification. ‘Sultaan سلطان, Salateen سلاطين’ means king. Its meanings are virtual not lexical, neither has the Holy Quran used this word in these contexts.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Araf (The Heights) سُوۡرَةُ الاٴعرَاف

 أَتُجَـٰدِلُونَنِى فِىٓ أَسۡمَآءٍ۬ سَمَّيۡتُمُوهَآ أَنتُمۡ وَءَابَآؤُكُم مَّا نَزَّلَ ٱللَّهُ بِہَا مِن سُلۡطَـٰنٍ۬‌ۚ

“[Hud] said, "Already have defilement and anger fallen upon you from your Lord. Do you dispute with me concerning [mere] names you have named them, you and your fathers, for which Allah has not sent down any authority? Then wait; indeed, I am with you among those who wait."

 

Conclusion:

(1)   Daleel دَلِيل: To take the guidance of one thing in order to verify another.

(2)   Hujjah حُجَّة: To provide evidence as a result or conclusion of something that is already agreed upon/confirmed.

(3)   Bayinnah بَيّنَة: Such evidence by which the claimant can prove the invalidity of the defendant.

(4)   Burhaan بُرهَان: A rational or duplicate argument that works best to negate a controversy.

(5)   Sultaan سلاطين: Such argument that is verified by the divine revelation. The royal decree.
