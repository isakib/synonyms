28. DHOOP [sun, heat, hotness]

Synonyms: SHAMS, ZUHAA, HARRA, HAROOR

 

28.1 SHAMS (شَمْس) : Sun and also sunlight. Shamsa Yoomuna  (يُوْمَنَا شَمْسَ) means to be sunny (day) and Shammasa means (شَمَّسَ) means to expose to the sun. Shams is used for the sunlight (any time of the day), but it is usually used to signify excessive heat of the sun.

Allah says in the Quran:

 

Surat Al-'Insān (The Man) -  الانسان سورة

76:13

لَا يَرَوْنَ فِيهَا شَمْسًا وَلَا زَمْهَرِيرًا

Reclining therein on raised thrones, they will see there neither the excessive heat of the sun

 

28.2 ZUHAA (ضُحٰى): Forenoon. It is also used to refer to the sun at the time of Chaasht .

Allah says in the Quran:

 

Surat An-Nāzi`āt (Those who drag forth) -  النازعات سورة

79:29

وَأَغْطَشَ لَيْلَهَا وَأَخْرَجَ ضُحَاهَا

Its night He covers with darkness, and its forenoon He brings out (with light)

 

28.3 HARRA (حَرَّ) : Harra and Hararat ( حَرَارَت ) means to be hot, heat. Since the temperature of the sun and sunlight determines the hotness of the atmosphere, so harra is also used to refer sun at the time of noon and hot weather. In Quran:

 

Surat At-Tawbah (The Repentance) - التوبة سورة

وَقَالُوا لَا تَنفِرُوا فِي الْحَرِّ ۗ

And said, 'Do not go forth in the heat.

 

28.4 HAROOR (حَرُوْر ): to be hot [opposite of zalla ( ظَلّ ) meaning shade]. Extreme heat due to the hot rays of the sun.

Allah says in the Quran:

 

Surat Fāţir (Originator) -  فاطر سورة

35:20-21

وَلَا الظُّلُمَاتُ وَلَا النُّورُ وَلَا الظِّلُّ وَلَا الْحَرُورُ

Nor are the darknesses and the light, Nor are the shade and the heat

 

Summary:

28.1 SHAMS: Common word used for sun

28.2 ZUHAA: Forenoon

28.3 HARRA: Hotness at the time of noon

28.4 HAROOR: Extreme heat due to the hot rays of the sun
