13. Dil دل (heart)

Also known as ‘qalb’ قَلب, ‘fuwaad’ فُوَأد (faad) فاد, ‘sadr’ صَدر and ‘nafs’ نَفس in the Holy Quran.

1.      Qalb قَلب: An important organ. A source of life and existence. For thinking, comprehending, wisdom and intellect, Allah Taa’la has addressed the heart. The actions that the modern medicine has associated with the mind, the Holy Quran has associated with the heart.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Araaf (The Heights) سُوۡرَةُ الاٴعرَاف

(7:179)

لَهُمۡ قُلُوبٌ۬ لَّا يَفۡقَهُونَ بِہَا

“And We have certainly created for Hell many of the jinn and mankind. They have hearts with which they do not understand, they have eyes with which they do not see, and they have ears with which they do not hear. Those are like livestock; rather, they are more astray. It is they who are the heedless.”

 

2.      Fuwaad فُوَأد: Some scholars have identified the difference between ‘qalb’ قَلب and ‘fuwaad’ فُوَأد to be similar as the difference between ‘ain’ عَين (eye) and ‘basr’ بصر (eyesight) or ‘uzn’ اُزن (ears) and ‘samie’ سمع (hearing). The evident ayah is presented below:

Surah Al Isra (The Night Journey) سُوۡرَةُ الإسرَاء

(17:36)

 إِنَّ ٱلسَّمۡعَ وَٱلۡبَصَرَ وَٱلۡفُؤَادَ كُلُّ أُوْلَـٰٓٮِٕكَ كَانَ عَنۡهُ مَسۡـُٔولاً۬

“And do not pursue that of which you have no knowledge. Indeed, the hearing, the sight and the heart - about all those [one] will be questioned.”        

However, this is not the truth since Allah Taa’la has directly and straight forwardly addressed intellect, wisdom and consciousness to the heart. Actually the heart, as an organ, has many parts just like a brain consists of many parts and each division carries various strengths. Similarly, the heart also specifies different actions and emotions to different parts. According to medical science, ‘fuwaad’ فُوَأد is the upper part of the heart that is attached to the stomach. ‘Waj’al-fuwaad’ وجع الفواد is referred to the pain in this area.

Fuwaad  فُوَأد (plural is Af’idaah اَفىئِدَة) is a derivative of Faad فأد. ‘Faad-ul’lahm’ فأد اللحم means to roast meat on fire. ‘Lahm un fa’idun’ لَحمٌ فَئِيدٌ refers to such meat that is roasted on fire. According to Ibn-e-Faaris ‘Al-faadu ya-dullu aala hamman shiddatu-ulhararah’ اَلفأدُ يَدُلُّ عَلَى حَمّاً وَّ شِدَّةُ الحَرَارَة which means ‘this word validates temperature and the degree of heat’. Therefore, anywhere a person’s emotions and the height of those emotions would be mentioned, this word would be used.

For example,

(a)    Surah Al Qasas (The Stories) سُوۡرَةُ القَصَص

(28:10)

وَأَصۡبَحَ فُؤَادُ أُمِّ مُوسَىٰ فَـٰرِغًا‌ۖ

“And the heart of Moses' mother became empty [of all else]. She was about to disclose [the matter concerning] him had We not bound fast her heart that she would be of the believers.”

(b)   Surah Ibrahim (Abraham) سُوۡرَةُ إبراهیم

 

(14:43)

 

مُهۡطِعِينَ مُقۡنِعِى رُءُوسِہِمۡ لَا يَرۡتَدُّ إِلَيۡہِمۡ طَرۡفُهُمۡ‌ۖ وَأَفۡـِٔدَتُہُمۡ هَوَآءٌ۬

“Racing ahead, their heads raised up, their glance does not come back to them, and their hearts are void.”

In addition, in contexts where ‘fuwaad’ فُوَأد is used to ask Allah, it would be associated with those actions that a person does in the influence of the passion of their emotions.

3.      Sadr صَدر: refers to the chest (sudoor) صُدُور; and it is the chest where the heart lies.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Haj (The Pilgrimage) سُوۡرَةُ الحَجّ

(22:46)

 وَلَـٰكِن تَعۡمَى ٱلۡقُلُوبُ ٱلَّتِى فِى ٱلصُّدُورِ

“So have they not traveled through the earth and have hearts by which to reason and ears by which to hear? For indeed, it is not eyes that are blinded, but blinded are the hearts which are within the breasts.”

Therefore, the meaning of ‘quloob’ قلوب is referred to by saying ‘fi-sudoor’ فِي الصُّدُور , for example:

Surah Yunus (Jonah) سُوۡرَةُ یُونس

(10:57)

شِفَآءٌ۬ لِّمَا فِى ٱلصُّدُورِ

“O mankind, there has to come to you instruction from your Lord and healing for what is in the breasts and guidance and mercy for the believers.”

Since the association of ‘sadr’ صَدر is locative, so the immensity or narrowness of the heart is described by using the word ‘sadr’ صَدر. For example,

(a)    Surah Al Hijr (The Rocky Tract) سُوۡرَةُ الحِجر

(15:97)

وَلَقَدۡ نَعۡلَمُ أَنَّكَ يَضِيقُ صَدۡرُكَ بِمَا يَقُولُونَ

“And We already know that your breast is constrained by what they say.”

 

(b)   Surah Ash Sharh (The Relief) سُوۡرَةُ الشَّرح

(94:1)

أَلَمۡ نَشۡرَحۡ لَكَ صَدۡرَكَ

“Did We not expand for you, [O Muhammad], your breast?”

                            

‘Sharah Sadr’ شرح صدر or ‘Ansharah Sadr’ انشراح صدر is an idiom. It refers to the feeling where a person senses an idea or a correct path at a complicated or complex situation. Then again, some sort of a vessel or a container is needed in order to hide or conceal something; therefore ‘sadr’ صَدر is also used in the context of a vessel that contains all the secrets, inner thoughts, whisperings etc. For example:

(a)    Surah Al Maeda (The Table Spread) سُوۡرَةُ المَائدة

(5:7)

 إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ عَلِيمُۢ بِذَاتِ ٱلصُّدُورِ

“And remember the favor of Allah upon you and His covenant with which He bound you when you said, "We hear and we obey"; and fear Allah . Indeed, Allah is Knowing of that within the breasts.”

 

(b)   Surah Ghafir (The Forgiver) سُوۡرَةُ غَافر

(40:19)

يَعۡلَمُ خَآٮِٕنَةَ ٱلۡأَعۡيُنِ وَمَا تُخۡفِى ٱلصُّدُورُ

“He knows that which deceives the eyes and what the breasts conceal.”

(c)    Surah An Nas (The Mankind) سُوۡرَةُ النَّاس

(114:5)

ٱلَّذِى يُوَسۡوِسُ فِى صُدُورِ ٱلنَّاسِ

“Who whispers [evil] into the breasts of mankind”

 

4.      Nafs نَفس: The spirit, life, existence (nufoos) نفوس and the origin and refuge of the desires and wishes. It refers to the heart that aspires and is delighted.

(1) If the context of the subject relates to keeping thoughts and secretive things concealed, then this quality is common with both ‘nufoos’ نفوس and ‘sudoor’ صدور.

Allah Taa’la says:                             

Surah Al Ahzab (The Combined Forces) سُوۡرَةُ الاٴحزَاب

(33:37)

 وَتُخۡفِى فِى نَفۡسِكَ مَا ٱللَّهُ مُبۡدِيهِ

“And [remember, O Muhammad], when you said to the one on whom Allah bestowed favor and you bestowed favor, "Keep your wife and fear Allah ," while you concealed within yourself that which Allah is to disclose. And you feared the people, while Allah has more right that you fear Him. So when Zayd had no longer any need for her, We married her to you in order that there not be upon the believers any discomfort concerning the wives of their adopted sons when they no longer have need of them. And ever is the command of Allah accomplished.”

(2) The association of wishes and desires is with ‘nafs’ نَفس whether the desires are good or evil.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah An Najm (The Star) سُوۡرَةُ النّجْم

(53:23)

 إِن يَتَّبِعُونَ إِلَّا ٱلظَّنَّ وَمَا تَهۡوَى ٱلۡأَنفُسُ‌ۖ

“They are not but [mere] names you have named them - you and your forefathers - for which Allah has sent down no authority. They follow not except assumption and what [their] souls desire, and there has already come to them from their Lord guidance.”

 

(3) The association of happiness is also related with ‘nafs’ نَفس.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah An Nisa (The Women) سُوۡرَةُ النِّسَاء

(4:4)

 فَإِن طِبۡنَ لَكُمۡ عَن شَىۡءٍ۬ مِّنۡهُ نَفۡسً۬ا فَكُلُوهُ هَنِيٓـًٔ۬ا مَّرِيٓـًٔ۬ا

“And give the women [upon marriage] their [bridal] gifts graciously. But if they give up willingly to you anything of it, then take it in satisfaction and ease.”

 

Conclusion:

(1)   Qalb قَلب: A source of rationality, Sense of awareness, understanding and wisdom.

(2)   Fuwaad فُوَأد: A center of the intensity of feelings and emotions.

(3)   Sadr صَدر: Locative. Vastness and narrowness. To keep thoughts in secret.

(4)   Nafs نَفس: A center of desires. It is used to refer to being delighted and to keep things secretive.

 دل – Heart

These words   ) صدر نفس قلب فؤاد (فاد are in Quran.

1.         قلب – a well-known organ of the body, the source of soul and life (قلوب) Allah addresses the heart for thinking, thought, intelligence, understanding.   All the things which modern science and medicine have related to the brain, the Quran has related to the heart.

 

In Surah Al-AAraaf:   (7:179):

 

لَهُمْ قُلُوبٌ لَّا يَفْقَهُونَ بِهَا

 

They have hearts with which they do not understand,

 

2.         فؤاد – some scholars have written that the same difference between ain  عينand basarبصر   or uzun  اذنand samaسمع  , is the same difference in قلب and فؤاد , the proof is in this Ayah below.

 

In Surah Al-Israa:   (17:36):

 

إِنَّ السَّمْعَ وَالْبَصَرَ وَالْفُؤَادَ كُلُّ أُولَٰئِكَ كَانَ عَنْهُ مَسْئُولًا

 

Indeed, the hearing, the sight and the heart - about all those [one] will be questioned.

 

But this is not the fact, because Allah has addressed the heart directly for intelligence, to understand, to think, to comprehend. The real fact is that this part of the body, the heart has many components, just as the brain has different components with different strengths.  In the same way the heart has special components related to special emotions, feelings and actions. According to the science of medicine فؤاد is the upper part of the heart which is above the opening of the stomach, and وجع الفؤاد  means pain of that particular part of the body, فؤاد plural افئدة is derived from فأد.  فأد اللحم means to roast meat on fire, and لحم فئيد  means roasted meat on fire. According to Ibn Faaris الفأد يدل على حمى وشدة الحرارة (ML) – this word indicates heat and severe heat, therefore whenever human’s experience strong emotions and its effects are mentioned then this word would be used.

 

In Surah Al-Qasas: (28:10):

 

وَأَصْبَحَ فُؤَادُ أُمِّ مُوسَىٰ فَارِغًا ۖ

 

And the heart of Moses' mother became empty [of all else].

In Surah Ibrahim ( 14;43)

 

مُهْطِعِينَ مُقْنِعِي رُءُوسِهِمْ لَا يَرْتَدُّ إِلَيْهِمْ طَرْفُهُمْ ۖ وَأَفْئِدَتُهُمْ هَوَاءٌ

 

Racing ahead, their heads raised up, their glance does not come back to them, and their hearts are void.

 

And the mentioning of when Allah will question the فؤاد , it is related to those deeds which have occurred due to strong emotions.

 

3.         صدر – means chest, (plural صدور) and the heart is in the chest.

 

In Surah Haj: (22:46):

 

وَلَٰكِن تَعْمَى الْقُلُوبُ الَّتِي فِي الصُّدُورِ

 

but blinded are the hearts which are within the breasts.

 

Therefore sometimes when the word صدور is used it means hearts, such as:

 

In Surah Yunus: (10:57):

 

وَشِفَاءٌ لِّمَا فِي الصُّدُورِ

 

and healing for what is in the breasts,

Now because the connection of صدر is to adverb of place, therefore whenever there is a need of mentioning the tightness or spaciousness of the heart then the word صدر is used.

 

In Surah Al-Hijr:  (15:97):

 

وَلَقَدْ نَعْلَمُ أَنَّكَ يَضِيقُ صَدْرُكَ بِمَا يَقُولُونَ

 

And We already know that your breast is constrained by what they say

In Surah As-Sarh: (94:1):

 

أَلَمْ نَشْرَحْ لَكَ صَدْرَكَ

 

Did We not expand for you, [O Muhammad], your breast?

 

And شرح صدر  or انشراح صدر is an idiom, when your has  heart has a right feeling about a complicated, difficult matter you call it شرح صدر  .  In order to hide something you need an adverb, therefore to hide a secret, a superstition, a thought, the word  صدر will be used.

 

In Surah Gafir: (40:19):

 

يَعْلَمُ خَائِنَةَ الْأَعْيُنِ وَمَا تُخْفِي الصُّدُورُ

 

He knows that which deceives the eyes and what the breasts conceal.

In Surah Maida: (5:7):

 

إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلِيمٌ بِذَاتِ الصُّدُورِ

 

Indeed, Allah is Knowing of that within the breasts.

 

In Surah Naas:  (114:5):

 

الَّذِي يُوَسْوِسُ فِي صُدُورِ النَّاسِ

 

Who whispers [evil] into the breasts of mankind.

 

4.         نفس – soul, life, heart, mind , plural نفوس – the source/ origin and refuge of desires. Meaning a heart that desires things and is happy.

 

a.         the connection of concealing thoughts and secrets, this attribute is shared in both  نفوس and صدور.

 

In Surah Ahzaab: (33:37):

 

وَتُخْفِي فِي نَفْسِكَ مَا اللَّهُ مُبْدِيهِ

 

While you concealed within yourself that which Allah is to disclose.

 

b.           the connection of desires is with  نفس either good or bad.

 

In Surah Najm:  (53:23):

 

إِن يَتَّبِعُونَ إِلَّا الظَّنَّ وَمَا تَهْوَى الْأَنفُسُ

 

They follow not except assumption and what [their] souls desire,

c.       the connection of happiness is with    نفس too.

 

In Surah Nisaa: (4:4):

 

فَإِن طِبْنَ لَكُمْ عَن شَيْءٍ مِّنْهُ نَفْسًا فَكُلُوهُ هَنِيئًا مَّرِيئًا

 

But if they give up willingly to you anything of it, then take it in satisfaction and ease.

 

 

Summary:

 

1.     قلب – the source origin of intelligence sense, comprehension.

2.     فؤاد – the centre of strong emotions

3.     صدر – adverb for tightness , spaciousness and to conceal matters

4.     نفس – the centre of desires, it is used for ‘to be happy’ and ‘to hide matters’
