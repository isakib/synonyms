Lethargy – Idleness 

In the Quran the words بَطَّا (بَطَاء), إِستَحسَرَ (حسر), وَنَي, وَهَنَ, كَسَلَhave been used.

1- كَسَلَ

Being unwell because of despair or lack or interest, feeling sluggish. 

وَإِذَا قَامُوٓاْ إِلَى ٱلصَّلَوٰةِ قَامُواْ كُسَالَىٰ 

“When they stand up to worship they perform it languidly “ 4/142

2- وَهَنَ

Being idle because of a physical ailment or because of low moods. 

وَلَا تَهِنُواْ فِى ٱبۡتِغَآءِ ٱلۡقَوۡمِ*ۖ 

“Relent not in pursuit of the enemy” 4/104

3- وَنَي

Means to err, negligence, idleness and not paying due attention in undertaking a talk.

ٱذۡهَبۡ أَنتَ وَأَخُوكَ بِـَٔايَـٰتِى وَلَا تَنِيَا فِى ذِكۡرِى 

“Go, thou and thy brother, with My tokens, and be not faint in remembrance of Me” 20/42

4- إِستَحسَرَ

حسر means to be tired and حَسَرَالبَصَرَmeans fatigue of eyes or weakness of sight. حَسِيرmeans fatigued, thus حسرmeans the idleness resulting from fatigue, being fed up.

وَمَنۡ عِندَهُۥ لَا يَسۡتَكۡبِرُونَ عَنۡ عِبَادَتِهِۦ وَلَا يَسۡتَحۡسِرُونَ 

“And those who dwell in His presence are not too proud to worship Him, nor do they weary” 31/19

5- بَطَّا

البَطَوءُ is to trundle along and إِبطَاءٌmeans one who is lazy and بَطَّاmeans to be extremely idle and lazy.

وَإِنَّ مِنكُمۡ لَمَن لَّيُبَطِّئَنَّ 

“Lo! among you there is he who loitereth” 4/73

Inference 

1- كَسَلَ: Laziness resulting from despair

2- وَهَنَ: Laziness resulting from physical or mental sickness

3- وَنَي: Laziness due to negligence

4- إِستَحسَرَ: Laziness due to fatigue

5- بَطَّا: To be lazy in nature or habit
