Straight 

Quran uses the words مُستَقِيم, سَوََيسَوَيّ, قَصَدand سَدِيد

1- مُستَقِيم

قَامَ علَيالاَمرmeans to keep up, to maintain, to stand ground and اَقَامالشَيmeans to erect or to stand something straight. مُستَقِيمis something straight as well as well balanced.

وَزِنُواْ بِٱلۡقِسۡطَاسِ ٱلۡمُسۡتَقِيمِ*ۚ 

“And weigh with a right balance” 17/35

Because if athe beam of a scale is not perfectly horizontal then the weights will be incorrect and here it means horizontal and straight. In Geometry, the shortest distance between two points is called displacement(straight line). So, a straight line is a way that is of course straight first of all and is not bending or imperfect. 

ٱهۡدِنَا ٱلصِّرَٲطَ ٱلۡمُسۡتَقِيمَ 

“Show us the straight path “ 1/4

2- سَوََيand سَوَيّ

Means to be correct. سَوَّيis correct and even and سَوِيis the same, healthy.

فَتَمَثَّلَ لَهَا بَشَرً۬ا سَوِيًّ۬ا 

“ It assumed for her the likeness of a perfect man” 19/17

and صِرََاطَاسَوِّيَاis also a path that is correct and straight and even without ups and downs and turns.

فَٱتَّبِعۡنِىٓ أَهۡدِكَ صِرَٲطً۬ا سَوِيًّ۬ا 

“So follow me, and I will lead thee on a right path” 19/43

سَوَاء is used in the meaning of even. And if this evenness is in the context of quantity or distance than وَسطis used and if equality is required in every imaginable aspect than سَوَاءis used, thus meaning converging point or in the absolute middle.

خُذُوهُ فَٱعۡتِلُوهُ إِلَىٰ سَوَآءِ ٱلۡجَحِيمِ 

“Take him and drag him to the midst of hell” 44/47

سَوَآءَالسَّبِيل means a path that goes through the middles leaving deviations and imperfections on the sides.

وَلَمَّا تَوَجَّهَ تِلۡقَآءَ مَدۡيَنَ قَالَ عَسَىٰ رَبِّىٓ أَن يَهۡدِيَنِى سَوَآءَ ٱلسَّبِيلِ 

“And when he turned his face toward Midian, he said: Peradventure my Lord will guide me in the right road” 

28/22 

3- قَصَدَ

It is the legitimate use of a thing. For example, if a person could take a bath using one bucket full of water and he uses just that, then this is قَصَدَbut if he uses more than the need then, this is thriftiness and if he uses less than the needed amount then this is miserliness. To consider this rule in all matters is called economics and if looked at pragmatically, being always lenient and being always strict doesn't get the job done but how lenient and how strict one should be is a matter for the jurisprudential system to define.

وَعَلَى ٱللَّهِ قَصۡدُ ٱلسَّبِيلِ وَمِنۡهَا جَآٮِٕرٌ۬*ۚ 

“And Allah's is the direction of the way, and some (roads) go not straight” 16/9

قَصَدَ doesn't exactly give the meaning of a middle path either, so , its generally used as aforementioned.
