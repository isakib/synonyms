To Improve – To Better 

For this the Quran uses, اَصلَحَ، زَكَّي

1- اَصلَحَ(Ant: اَفسَدَ)

To correct faults and flaws. It is commonly used in both material and non-physical meaning. 

كَفَّرَ عَنۡہُمۡ سَيِّـَٔاتِہِمۡ وَأَصۡلَحَ بَالَهُمۡ 

“He riddeth them of their ill-deeds and improveth their state” 47:2

And 

وَلَا تُفۡسِدُواْ فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ بَعۡدَ إِصۡلَـٰحِهَا 

“Work not confusion in the earth after the fair ordering” 7:56

2- زَكَّي(from زكو)

زكَي andزَكَّيmean the thriving of crops and زَكِيمَالَهُmeans to pay زَكَوَةand زَكَّينَفسَهُmeans to blow ones one trumpet or to brag. But in jurisprudence, the word تَزكِيةhas been used in the meaning of facilitating improvement and تَزكِيةنَفسmeans to clean one's self from bad morals and to thrive in good character.

قَدۡ أَفۡلَحَ مَن زَكَّٮٰهَا (﻿٩﻿) وَقَدۡ خَابَ مَن دَسَّٮٰهَا

“He is indeed successful who causeth it to grow, And he is indeed a failure who stunteth it.” 91:9-10

Inference 

إِصلاَح is used for correcting any kind of faults or errors but زَكَّيis used specifically for improvements in one's character and excelling in it.
