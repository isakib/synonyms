Journey – To Travel 

The glorious Quran uses the words ضَرَبَ فِي الاَرض, نَفَرَ, ظَعَنَ, سََاحَ(سِيح), سَفَرَ, رَحَلَfor this.

1- سَفَرَ

means to leave your place of residence to go somewhere far and is used in the meaning of travelling. Its a common used word. 

وَإِن كُنتُم مَّرۡضَىٰٓ أَوۡ عَلَىٰ سَفَرٍ 

“And if ye be ill, or on a journey” 4/43

2- سََاحَ

سَاحَ المَاءmeans water running freely and سَاحَةmeans a wide place. سَاحَةُالدَّارmeans a yard in a house , a wide space adjacent to the house and سََاحَmeans to walk about in a wide place, whether it is leisurely or for some other reason.

فَسِيحُواْ فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ أَرۡبَعَةَ أَشۡہُرٍ۬ 

“Travel freely in the land four months” 9/2

3- ظَعَنَ

Means to depart, to leave for a a destination or to emigrate. ظَعَانmeans a rope for tying a saddle on an animal and ظَعنةis a woman who often travels.

وَجَعَلَ لَكُم مِّن جُلُودِ ٱلۡأَنۡعَـٰمِ بُيُوتً۬ا تَسۡتَخِفُّونَهَا يَوۡمَ ظَعۡنِكُمۡ وَيَوۡمَ إِقَامَتِڪُمۡ*ۙ 

“And hath given you (also), of the hides of cattle, tent-houses which ye find light (to carry) on the day of migration and on the day of pitching camp” 16/80

4- نَفَرَ

النَّفِير is a group of less than 10 men, a battle party that is departing and النَّفِيرالعَامmeans when people rise against the enemy and نَفَرَ means to go on a venture or a war against the enemy.

فَٱنفِرُواْ ثُبَاتٍ أَوِ ٱنفِرُواْ جَمِيعً۬ا 

“Then advance the proven ones, or advance all together” 4/71

5- ضَرَبَ فِي الاَرض

Means to strike feet on the ground and is used proverbially for a journey and usually for a long and purposeful journey. 

وَءَاخَرُونَ يَضۡرِبُونَ فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ يَبۡتَغُونَ مِن فَضۡلِ ٱللَّهِ*ۙ 

“There are sick folk among you, while others travel in the land in search of Allah's bounty” 73/20

6- رَحَلَ

اَلرَّحَل means destiny, place of rest, saddle of a camel, a cushion used between the saddle and the body of a beast of burden, luggage that stays with the traveller. رَحَلَis to saddle a beast for riding, to climb on an animal, to march, to set off on a journey.

إِیلَـٰفِهِمۡ رِحۡلَةَ ٱلشِّتَآءِ وَٱلصَّيۡفِ 

“For their taming (We cause) the caravans to set forth in winter and summer” 106/2

Inference 

1- سَفَرَ: Common word for a journey

2- سََاحَ: A journey in numerous directions in the opens, also means tourism

3- ظَعَنَ: To emigrate

4- نَفَرَ: To set off for a venture

5- ضَرَبَفِيالاَرض: To make a purposeful, long journey

6- رَحَلَ: Marching off and everything related to it, including preparations
