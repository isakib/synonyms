6. SAANP [snake, serpent]

Synonyms: HAYYAH, JAAN, THAUBAAN

6.1 HAYYAH (حَیَّۃٌ): It’s a gender noun representing every kind of snake, and used for both the genders of snakes. A convoluted snake according to dictionary Al Manjad. HAWAYA (حَوَایَا) means intestines convoluted like a snake. TAHWI-AL-HAYYA (تحوی الحیّۃ) refers to convolution or coiling of a snake. HAAWI (حَاوِی) means a snake charmer (dictionary Al Manjad). Both the meanings seem appropriate because every snake convolutes. This is possibly why it’s called HAYYAH (حَیَّۃٌ). In the Quran

Surah Ta-Ha (Ta-ha): سورۃ طٰھٰ  20:19

قَالَ أَلۡقِهَا يَـٰمُوسَىٰ  فَأَلۡقَٮٰهَا فَإِذَا هِىَ حَيَّةٌ۬ تَسۡعَىٰ

He said, “Throw it, O Moses.”  So he threw it—thereupon it became a moving serpent.

6.2 JAANN (جَآنٌ): means Jinn, demon or a fairy, Gigantic and every jinn that is in form of a snake as mentioned in hadith. Moulana Shabbir Ahmed Usmani translates it as a thin white snake or stick serpent. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah An-Naml (The ant): سورۃ النَّمل 27:10

 فَلَمَّا رَءَاهَا تَہۡتَزُّ كَأَنَّہَا جَآنٌّ۬ وَلَّىٰ مُدۡبِرً۬ا وَلَمۡ يُعَقِّبۡ‌ۚ

But when he saw it quivering, as though it were a demon, he turned around not looking back.

6.3 THAUBAAN (ثَعْبَانٌ): means a huge snake, anaconda or boa. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:107

 فَأَلۡقَىٰ عَصَاهُ فَإِذَا هِىَ ثُعۡبَانٌ۬ مُّبِينٌ۬

 So he threw his staff, and it was an apparent serpent.

Allah has used the all these words HAYYAH (حَیَّۃٌ) JAANN (جَآنٌ) and THAUBAAN (ثَعْبَانٌ) in a single event. HAYYAH (حَیَّۃٌ) because it can refers to any kind of snake. JAANN (جَآنٌ) referring to the initial phase of transition of a stick into snake. THAUBAAN (ثَعْبَانٌ) as the later and final stages of transition. And all these events occurred instantaneously.

Summary:

6.1 HAYYAH: Any kind of snake

6.2 JAANN: Thin and long snake

6.3 THAUBAAN:  A huge snake for instance anaconda or boa.

Snake, Serpent

For a snake, the words حَيَةٌ (حَوِي), جَانّand ثُعبَانhave been used.

1- حَيَّةٌ

This is the general term use for snakes and it includes every kind of snake whether male or female and according to one dictionary this means a coiled up snake. حَوَايَانare the intestines that are coiled up like a snake. تحويالهيةmeans coiling up of a snake and حََاوِيis someone who chants on snakes. Both of the above mentioned meaning seem correct as almost all snakes coil up and most probably for this reason it is called حَيَّةٌ.

قَالَ أَلۡقِهَا يَـٰمُوسَىٰ فَأَلۡقَٮٰهَا فَإِذَا هِىَ حَيَّةٌ۬ تَسۡعَىٰ

“He said: Cast it down, O Moses! So he cast it down, and lo! it was a serpent, gliding ” 20/19-20

2- جَانّ

It means djinn, ghost, fairies, having a massive body and every djinn that takes the form of a snake as its mentioned in a Hadith as well. Molana Shabbir Ahmad Usmani has translated it as a slim white snake.

فَلَمَّا رَءَاهَا تَہۡتَزُّ كَأَنَّہَا جَآنٌّ۬ وَلَّىٰ مُدۡبِرً۬ا وَلَمۡ يُعَقِّبۡ*ۚ

“But when he saw it writhing as it were a demon, he turned to flee headlong” 27/10

3- ثُعبَان

ثُعبَانmeans a huge serpent and a good example would be a big Anaconda or a Boa Constrictor.

فَأَلۡقَىٰ عَصَاهُ فَإِذَا هِىَ ثُعۡبَانٌ۬ مُّبِينٌ۬

“Then he flung down his staff and lo! it was a serpent manifest” 7/107

Observed carefully, in the same incident Allah has used حَيَّةٌ, جَانّand ثُعبَان.

حَيَّةٌfor the reason that every snake is حَيَّةٌ.

جَانّis the state of the staff changing its form into a snake

ثُعبَانis the final form of the snake and all those events happened in rapid succession.

Inference

1- حَيَّةٌ: is for all the snakes in general

2- جَانّ: is for a long slender snake

3- ثُعبَان: is for a bull snake or a big serpent.
