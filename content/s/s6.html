Snake, Serpent

For a snake, the words حَيَةٌ (حَوِي), جَانّand ثُعبَانhave been used.

1- حَيَّةٌ

This is the general term use for snakes and it includes every kind of snake whether male or female and according to one dictionary this means a coiled up snake. حَوَايَانare the intestines that are coiled up like a snake. تحويالهيةmeans coiling up of a snake and حََاوِيis someone who chants on snakes. Both of the above mentioned meaning seem correct as almost all snakes coil up and most probably for this reason it is called حَيَّةٌ.

قَالَ أَلۡقِهَا يَـٰمُوسَىٰ فَأَلۡقَٮٰهَا فَإِذَا هِىَ حَيَّةٌ۬ تَسۡعَىٰ

“He said: Cast it down, O Moses! So he cast it down, and lo! it was a serpent, gliding ” 20/19-20

2- جَانّ

It means djinn, ghost, fairies, having a massive body and every djinn that takes the form of a snake as its mentioned in a Hadith as well. Molana Shabbir Ahmad Usmani has translated it as a slim white snake.

فَلَمَّا رَءَاهَا تَہۡتَزُّ كَأَنَّہَا جَآنٌّ۬ وَلَّىٰ مُدۡبِرً۬ا وَلَمۡ يُعَقِّبۡ*ۚ

“But when he saw it writhing as it were a demon, he turned to flee headlong” 27/10

3- ثُعبَان

ثُعبَانmeans a huge serpent and a good example would be a big Anaconda or a Boa Constrictor.

فَأَلۡقَىٰ عَصَاهُ فَإِذَا هِىَ ثُعۡبَانٌ۬ مُّبِينٌ۬

“Then he flung down his staff and lo! it was a serpent manifest” 7/107

Observed carefully, in the same incident Allah has used حَيَّةٌ, جَانّand ثُعبَان.

حَيَّةٌfor the reason that every snake is حَيَّةٌ.

جَانّis the state of the staff changing its form into a snake

ثُعبَانis the final form of the snake and all those events happened in rapid succession.

Inference

1- حَيَّةٌ: is for all the snakes in general

2- جَانّ: is for a long slender snake

3- ثُعبَان: is for a bull snake or a big serpent.
