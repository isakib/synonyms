To Rise 

In the Quran, the words اَقمَحَ , اَقنَعَand عَلاَhave been used for this.

1- اَقمَحَ

When a camel raises its head after drinking water but keeps looking down, then this is called اَقمَحَالبَعِيرand when a person want to look downward but for some reason cant bow[stiffness] his head then it is called اَقمَحَ ارَّجُل وَ غَضَّ بَصَرَهُ.

إِنَّا جَعَلۡنَا فِىٓ أَعۡنَـٰقِهِمۡ أَغۡلَـٰلاً۬ فَهِىَ إِلَى ٱلۡأَذۡقَانِ فَهُم مُّقۡمَحُونَ 

“Lo! We have put on their necks carcans reaching unto the chins, so that they are made stiff-necked “ 36/8

2- اَقنَعَ

Means to raise one's head or to raise voice. مقنعis the person who raises his head to take a glance.

مُهۡطِعِينَ مُقۡنِعِى رُءُوسِہِمۡ لَا يَرۡتَدُّ إِلَيۡہِمۡ طَرۡفُهُمۡ*ۖ وَأَفۡـِٔدَتُہُمۡ هَوَآءٌ۬ 

“As they come hurrying on in fear, their heads upraised, their gaze returning not to them, and their hearts as air “14/43

3- عَلاَ يَعلَو عُلُوّاً

To rise, ( عُلوAnt:سُفل). It is a common word but is normally used in a bad sense. It is used both in the material and intentive forms. In contrast عَلِيَand يَعلَيare used in a good sense.

إِنَّ فِرۡعَوۡنَ عَلَا فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ 

“Lo! Pharaoh exalted himself in the earth “28/4

Inference 

1- اَقمَحَ: To raise head but keep eyes down

2- اَقنَعَ: To raise head but look straight or up

3- عَلاَ: Used in the meaning of arrogance
