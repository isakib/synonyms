Tunnel 

Quran uses نَفَقَand سَرَبَfor this.

1- نَفَقَ

Is a path or a way which is open on both ends, a tunnel. Just like tunnels are made in mountains to allow trains and traffic to go through. نَيفَقُالسَّرَاوِيلmeans that tunnel like sewing in the top of some trousers (used mainly in Asian cultures), through which a piece of string or a belt is run through.

نَافِقَاءَ ايَربُوعmeans hole of a wild mouse and نَافَقَاليَربُوع وَنَفَقَmeans that the mouse entered the tunnel from one end and came out of the other.

فَإِنِ ٱسۡتَطَعۡتَ أَن تَبۡتَغِىَ نَفَقً۬ا فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ أَوۡ سُلَّمً۬ا فِى ٱلسَّمَآءِ 

“If thou canst, seek a way down into the earth or a ladder unto the sky” 6/35

2- سَرَبَ

Is a tunnel with only one opening or a burrow. سَرَبَmeans to continue going in and إِنسَرَبَتِالحَيَّةُإِلَيحُجرِهَاmeans the snake entering its burrow.

فَلَمَّا بَلَغَا مَجۡمَعَ بَيۡنِهِمَا نَسِيَا حُوتَهُمَا فَٱتَّخَذَ سَبِيلَهُ ۥ فِى ٱلۡبَحۡرِ سَرَبً۬ا 

“And when they reached the point where the two met, they forgot their fish, and it took its way into the waters, being free “ 18/61

Inference 

A tunnel with 2 openings is نَفَقَand a burrow with only one opening is سَرَبَ.
