Leader – Head 

For this the Quran uses, نَقِيب, اَءِمَّة, رَهَط, سَِيد(سود), مَلاَء

1- سَِيد

سَادَ means noble and decent, leader of a people, to be above in position and place. سَوَادَmeans a large group and سَيدis the head of this group. As the leader is expected to be decent, thus, any common decent man is also called سَِيد. (Pl: سَادَاتand سَادَت) and is used in a good sense.

وَقَالُواْ رَبَّنَآ إِنَّآ أَطَعۡنَا سَادَتَنَا وَكُبَرَآءَنَا فَأَضَلُّونَا ٱلسَّبِيلَ 

“And they say: Our Lord! Lo! we obeyed our princes and great men, and they misled us from the Way” 33/67

2- مَلاَء

مَلاَ means to fill and مَلاَءmeans a group of people who agree on something and is intimidating to sight and their leader displays arrogance in his actions and looks and commonly مَلاَmeans officers of the government, who, because of authority and them being imposed on the common man.

قَالَ ٱلۡمَلَأُ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱسۡتَكۡبَرُواْ مِن قَوۡمِهِۦ لَنُخۡرِجَنَّكَ يَـٰشُعَيۡبُ وَٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ مَعَكَ مِن قَرۡيَتِنَآ 

“The chieftains of his people, who were scornful, said: Surely we will drive thee out, O Shu'eyb, and those who believe with thee, from our township” 7/88

3- رَهَط

Means a tribe, a small group of people, less than ten in number and not having any women amongst them. Such group's leader is also called رَهَطand is usually used in a bad sense. رَهَطis also a piece of cloth or rag that a woman keeps on her private parts during menstruation. Their is also a proverb which says: هُوَ اذَلُّ مَنَ الرَهَطَwhich means that he is even lower than the rag of menstruation. When رَهَطis used quantitatively, then it means individuals. For example,عِشرُونَ رَهطاًmeaning 20 people.

وَكَانَ فِى ٱلۡمَدِينَةِ تِسۡعَةُ رَهۡطٍ۬ يُفۡسِدُونَ 

“And there were in the city nine persons who made mischief in the land” 27/48

4- اَءِمَّة

It is the plural of اِمَام. It means a person, book or a word that is followed regardless of whether it is true or not. In the form of an individual, it means a religious leader, a leader of salah.

وَكُلَّ شَىۡءٍ أَحۡصَيۡنَـٰهُ فِىٓ إِمَامٍ۬ مُّبِينٍ۬ 

“And their footprints. And all things We have kept in a clear Register” 36/12

And 

فَقَـٰتِلُوٓاْ أَٮِٕمَّةَ ٱلۡڪُفۡرِ*ۙ إِنَّهُمۡ لَآ أَيۡمَـٰنَ لَهُمۡ 

“Then fight the heads of disbelief - Lo! they have no binding oaths” 9/12

5- نَقِيب

It means to make a hole in the wall. نَاقَبَmeans means a person who is proud of his actions and نَقَبَ عَنِِ الَخبَارmeans to verify news. نَاقِبmeans a leader, governor who changes the condition of his people. It is used ina good sense.

وَلَقَدۡ أَخَذَ ٱللَّهُ مِيثَـٰقَ بَنِىٓ إِسۡرَٲٓءِيلَ وَبَعَثۡنَا مِنۡهُمُ ٱثۡنَىۡ عَشَرَ نَقِيبً۬ا*ۖ 

“Allah made a covenant of old with the Children of Israel and We raised among them twelve Chieftains” 5/12

Inference 

1- سَِيد: A leader of a large group (used in a positive meaning)

2- مَلاَء: Officers of government, imposed over common people

3- رَهَط: A small group, a troublesome group and their leader

4- اِمَام: A leader that is followed, whether good or bad

5- نَقِيب: A head, a governor.
