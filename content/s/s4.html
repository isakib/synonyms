4 SAMAAN

[Luggage, belongings, provision]

Synonyms: ARZ, MATAA’, ASAAS, RAHAL, WIA’A, JAHAZ, ZAAD(ZOD), ASLIHAAH, UDDAH, NA’AMA, REESH, BIDA’A, MA’OON(MA’AN), HIZR, MA’AISH,

4.1 arz(عَرَضَ): all those things which are not eternal. Meaning that everything from this world’s belongings are arz. When one wants to shed light on the non-permanence and fragility of this world and its belongings, then the word arz will be used, the material gains of this world and its belongings. Allah states that:

________________

Surat An-Nisa (Women) - سُوۡرَةُ النِّسَاء

4:94

وَلَا تَقُولُواْ لِمَنۡ أَلۡقَىٰٓ إِلَيۡڪُمُ ٱلسَّلَـٰمَ لَسۡتَ مُؤۡمِنً۬ا تَبۡتَغُونَ عَرَضَ ٱلۡحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنۡيَا فَعِندَ ٱللَّهِ مَغَانِمُ ڪَثِيرَةٌ۬‌ۚ

O ye who believe! When ye go forth (to fight) in the way of Allah, be careful to discriminate, and say not unto one who offereth you peace: "Thou art not a believer," seeking the chance profits of this life (so that ye may despoil him). With Allah are plenteous spoils. Even thus (as he now is) were ye before; but Allah hath since then been gracious unto you. Therefore take care to discriminate. Allah is ever Informed of what ye do.

________________

To shed light on this world’s belongings and benefitting from them, then this word is used. Allah states that:

________________

But Satan caused them to deflect therefrom and expelled them from the (happy) state in which they were; and We said: Fall down, one of you a foe unto the other! There shall be for you on earth a habitation and provision for a time.

4.3 asaas(أَثَـٰثً۬ا): asaas is the plural of asaasa but it is usually used as a plural such as the noted word asaas-ul-bait(أثاث البيت) meaning domestic equipment that is being used. Household equipment such as crockery, bed, clothes, furniture etc all these are included in asaas-ul-bait. Then this word got started to be used for useless and extra merchandise. And also used for shops and junkyards. Moreover, that animal or slave who is under the authority or personal use of someone is also included in asaas-ul-bait such as horse, cow, camel etc. Allah states that:

4.15 MA’AISH (مَعَائِش): (singular MAEESHAH مَعِیْشَۃ) Means articles of sustenance. AASH (عاش) means to live or exist. And any items which can be used as a source of nourishment like food, grains, water etc. will be known as MA’AISH(مَعَائِش). Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:10

وَلَقَدۡ مَكَّنَّـٰڪُمۡ فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ وَجَعَلۡنَا لَكُمۡ فِيہَا مَعَـٰيِشَ‌ۗ

We have established you firmly on earth, and made for you in it livelihood—

 

Summary:

4.1 ARD: Ephemerality of the world and worldly goods.

4.2 MATA’: Every item/good that can be benefitted from.

4.3 ASSAS: Household Goods.

4.4 RAHL: Luggage/Baggage for travelling

4.5 WIAA’: Luggage/Baggage that is enclosed or locked.

4.6 JAHAZ: Luggage that is packed together for travelling it can be RAHL or ZAAD

4.7 ZAAD: Food and perishable items for travel.

4.8 ASLIHAH: Weapons and arms.

4.9 UDDAH: Preparations for war, and all the items related to the preparation.

4.10 NI’MAH: Luxury items.

4.11 REESH: Haughty attire or related items.

4.12 BIDAA’AH: Merchantable goods, capital.

4.13 MA’OON: Items of daily use.

4.14 HIZR: Articles for self conservation.

4.15 MA’AISH: Articles of sustenance.



<<Alternate Translation>>

Luggage, Belongings

For this the words عَرَضَ, مَتَاع, اَثَاث, رَحَلَ, وِعَأ(وعِي), جَهَاز, زَاد (زود), اَسلِحة, عُدَّة , نَعمَة, رِيش, مَاعوُن (معن), مَعَا يشhave been used.

1- عَرَضَ

عَرَضَis everything which is not constant and everything in this world, with reference to this is عَرَضَ. When it is necessary to highlight inconstancy and fickleness of worldly things or possessions then عَرَضَis used. It also means that worldly gains and possession are flimsy and short lived.

وَلَا تَقُولُواْ لِمَنۡ أَلۡقَىٰٓ إِلَيۡڪُمُ ٱلسَّلَـٰمَ لَسۡتَ مُؤۡمِنً۬ا تَبۡتَغُونَ عَرَضَ ٱلۡحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنۡيَا فَعِندَ ٱللَّهِ مَغَانِمُ ڪَثِيرَةٌ۬*ۚ

“Say not unto one who offereth you peace: "Thou art not a believer," seeking the chance profits of this life. With Allah are plenteous spoils” 4/94

2- مَتَاع(Pl: اَمتِعَة)

مَتَعَmeans to benefit from something in the long run. Any useful commodity or any part of it which is useful and to enjoy worldly gains a possessions. When its necessary to highlight worldly possessions and gains they bring, this word is used.

وَلَكُمۡ فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ مُسۡتَقَرٌّ۬ وَمَتَـٰعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍ۬

“There shall be for you on earth a habitation and provision for a time” 2/36

3- اَثَاث

اَثَاثةis the plural and is most commonly used. اثَاث البَيتis a famous phrase meaning household items or property. Household essentials like clothing, furniture, culinary items, beds etc. are all classed as اثَاث البَيت. This phrase, afterwards, was also used for all kinds of other things which were not so useful and has also been used for scrapyards and shops which sell this kind of items. Also, a slave or an animal that belongs to someone and is in direct use of that person is also referred to as اثَاث البَيت. For example, horse, cow, camel etc.

وَكَمۡ أَهۡلَكۡنَا قَبۡلَهُم مِّن قَرۡنٍ هُمۡ أَحۡسَنُ أَثَـٰثً۬ا وَرِءۡيً۬ا

“How many a generation have We destroyed before them, who were more imposing in respect of gear and outward seeming” 19/74

4- رَحل

رَحَلَmeans to put a saddle on a camel, to set off, to ride and the personal luggage that one takes on a journey is also رَحَلَ. In a Hadith, the Prophet Muhammad (saw) says:

لاَ تَشَدَّ و ا اَلَرِّحََالَ إِلاَّ لِثَلاَثة

which can be roughly translated as, 'don't put a saddle on your camel for pilgrimage of anywhere apart from 3 mosques(Masjid Al-Haraam, Masjid An-Nabawi, Masjid Al-Aqsa)'

In the Quran it says:

جَعَلَ ٱلسِّقَايَةَ فِى رَحۡلِ أَخِيهِ

“He put the drinking-cup in his brother's saddlebag” 12/70

5- وِعَأ(sing: اوعِيَة)

وَعَيmeans to put something a bag and tie a knot on it for safe keeping. اَوعِيَةis that item which is put inside a container and locked.

ثُمَّ ٱسۡتَخۡرَجَهَا مِن وِعَآءِ أَخِيهِ*ۚ

“Then he produced it from his brother's bag ” 12/76

6- جَهَاز

جَهَّزَmeans to make ready, to load and to send. جَهَازis that luggage which a passenger makes ready before setting off. تَجهِيزis that which is prepared before burying a person and جَهِيزis that which parents give to their daughter, at the time of her wedding, to take with.

فَلَمَّا جَهَّزَهُم بِجَهَازِهِمۡ

“And when he provided them with their provision” 12/70

7- زََاد

It means extra, something spare and زََادis normally used in text to journeys. زَادرَاهmeans 'spending money' and food for the journey andالمزاد هmeaning a water skin which is used during travelling and المِزوَدmeans a food container and تَزَوَّدَmeans to take provisions for a journey.

وَتَزَوَّدُواْ فَإِنَّ خَيۡرَ ٱلزَّادِ ٱلتَّقۡوَىٰ*ۚ

“So make provision for yourselves (Hereafter); for the best provision is to ward off evil“ 2/197

8- اَسلِحَة(sing: سَلاَح)

This word is commonly associated with war and fighting and gives the meaning of arms, weapons and everything else that is used to fight with or to resist with.

فَلۡتَقُمۡ طَآٮِٕفَةٌ۬ مِّنۡہُم مَّعَكَ وَلۡيَأۡخُذُوٓاْ أَسۡلِحَتَہُمۡ

“Let only a party of them stand with thee (to worship) and let them take their arms” 4/102

9- عُدَّة(sing: عُدَد)

عَدَّmeans to make preparations or to make ready.اَعَدَّand اَعتَدَmean to keep ready for actions and عُدَّةmeans equipment with regards to readiness. In Quran this word has been used in context of readiness and preparedness for war. Hence, the meaning more broadly encompasses any sort of preparation and equipment.

وَلَوۡ أَرَادُواْ ٱلۡخُرُوجَ لَأَعَدُّواْ لَهُۥ عُدَّةً۬

“And if they had wished to go forth they would assuredly have made ready some equipment” 9/46

10- نَعمَة

نَعَمmeans prosperity and good fortune. نَعمَةmeans items that constitute luxury.

وَنَعۡمَةٍ۬ كَانُواْ فِيہَا فَـٰكِهِينَ

“And pleasant things wherein they took delight!“ 44/27

11- رِيشاً

رِيش الطَّأرmeans the wings and feathers of a bird. رَيََّاشis a person who puts bird feathers on arrows. Because bird feathers are like regalia so similarly the word رِيشاًis used for raiment worn by people.

يَـٰبَنِىٓ ءَادَمَ قَدۡ أَنزَلۡنَا عَلَيۡكُمۡ لِبَاسً۬ا يُوَٲرِى سَوۡءَٲتِكُمۡ وَرِيشً۬ا*ۖ

“Children of Adam! We have revealed unto you raiment to conceal your shame, and splendid vesture” 7/26

It is also used in the meaning of decoration and ornamentation.

12- بِضَاعَةً

Means a generous part of wealth that is put aside for business or commodities that are for sale. It is also used for the capitol that is set aside for trade.

قَالَ يَـٰبُشۡرَىٰ هَـٰذَا غُلَـٰمٌ۬*ۚ وَأَسَرُّوهُ بِضَـٰعَةً۬*ۚ

“He said: Good luck! Here is a youth. And they hid him as a treasure” 12/19

13- مَاعُون

اَلمَعَنَmeans useful belongings. مَاعُونis everything of use that is commonly used by everyone. For example, everyday used things like axe, pots, household items etc.

وَيَمۡنَعُونَ ٱلۡمَاعُونَ

“Yet refuse small kindnesses” 107/7

14- حِذر

حَذَرَmeans to stay vigilant and sharp and حِذرis all the equipment that is used for defence. So, every instrument used in defence during a war is حِذر.

وَلَا جُنَاحَ عَلَيۡڪُمۡ إِن كَانَ بِكُمۡ أَذً۬ى مِّن مَّطَرٍ أَوۡ كُنتُم مَّرۡضَىٰٓ أَن تَضَعُوٓاْ أَسۡلِحَتَكُمۡ*ۖ وَخُذُواْ حِذۡرَكُمۡ*ۗ

“It is no sin for you to lay aside your arms, if rain impedeth you or ye are sick. But take your precaution” 4/102

15- مَعَائِش(sing: مَعِيشَة)

عَاشmeans to stay alive and everything that is needed to stay alive for example, good, water etc. isمَعَائِش.

وَلَقَدۡ مَكَّنَّـٰڪُمۡ فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ وَجَعَلۡنَا لَكُمۡ فِيہَا مَعَـٰيِشَ*ۗ

“And We have given you (mankind) power in the earth, and appointed for you therein livelihood” 7/10

Inference

1- عَرض: Points towards fickleness of the world and the worldly wealth

2- مَتَاع: Everything that is useful in some way

3- اَثَاث: Household items and property

4- رَحل: Luggage that stays with the traveller throughout the journey

5- وِعَأ: Everything that is kept locked

6- جَهَاز: Whatever is prepared for the journey and it can be زَادand/or رَحل

7- زَاد: Food material for the journey

8- اَسلِحَة: Arms and weapons

9- عُدَّةَ: Preparation for war and fighting equipment

10- نَعمَة: Items that constitute luxury

11- رِِيش: Raiment and adornments

12- بِضَاعَة: Commodities or wealth for investment

13- مَاعُون: Everyday use household items

14- حِذر: Defensive equipment or gear

15- مَعَائِش: Necessities of life
