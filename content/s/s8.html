To Commit, To Entrust 

The words اَكفَلَ، كَفَّلَ، وَكَّلَ، دَفَعَ (إِلَي)، سَلَّمَ، فَوَضَّ، إِستَودَعَhave been used in the Quran.

1- اَكفَلَandكَفَّلَ

كَفَلَ means to be responsible for someone necessary expenditure and taking care, as in a guarantor. اَكفَلَandكَفَّلَmeans entrusting the above responsibility to someone else.

“ إِنَّ هَـٰذَآ أَخِى لَهُۥ تِسۡعٌ۬ وَتِسۡعُونَ نَعۡجَةً۬ وَلِىَ نَعۡجَةٌ۬ وَٲحِدَةٌ۬ فَقَالَ أَكۡفِلۡنِيہَا وَعَزَّنِى فِى ٱلۡخِطَابِ"

“Lo! this my brother hath ninety and nine ewes while I had one ewe; and he said: Entrust it to me, and he conquered me in speech” 38/23

On another occasion: 

فَتَقَبَّلَهَا رَبُّهَا بِقَبُولٍ حَسَنٍ۬ وَأَنۢبَتَهَا نَبَاتًا حَسَنً۬ا وَكَفَّلَهَا زَكَرِيَّا*ۖ 

“And her Lord accepted her with full acceptance and vouchsafed to her a goodly growth; and made Zachariah her guardian” 3/37 

2- وَكَّلَ

وَكَلَ means to have trust on someone in ones personal matters. وَكَّلَmeans to entrust someone without personal matter, to take someone as a trustee.

قُلۡ يَتَوَفَّٮٰكُم مَّلَكُ ٱلۡمَوۡتِ ٱلَّذِى وُكِّلَ بِكُمۡ 

“Say: The angel of death, who hath charge concerning you” 32/11 

3- إِلِي) دَفَعَ)

Means to return a trust. 

فَإِنۡ ءَانَسۡتُم مِّنۡہُمۡ رُشۡدً۬ا فَٱدۡفَعُوٓاْ إِلَيۡہِمۡ أَمۡوَٲلَهُمۡ*ۖ 

“Then, if ye find them of sound judgement, deliver over unto them their fortune” 4/6 

4- سَلَّمَ

سَلِمَ means to be delivered or be rescued from a calamity. سَلَّمَalso means saying السَّلاَمُعَلَيكُمor to rescue someone as سَلِمَ.

سَلَّمَهُ اَلشَّيءَفَتَسَلَمَّهُmeans to entrust to someone and to accept that trust. Hence, to relieve from adversity and entrust, سَلَّمَis used.

وَإِنۡ أَرَدتُّمۡ أَن تَسۡتَرۡضِعُوٓاْ أَوۡلَـٰدَكُمۡ فَلَا جُنَاحَ عَلَيۡكُمۡ إِذَا سَلَّمۡتُم مَّآ ءَاتَيۡتُم بِٱلۡمَعۡرُوفِ*ۗ 

“And if ye wish to give your children out to nurse, it is no sin for you, provide that ye pay what is due from you in kindness” 2/233 

On another occasion: 

وَدِيَةٌ۬ مُّسَلَّمَةٌ إِلَىٰٓ أَهۡلِهِۦۤ إِلَّآ أَن يَصَّدَّقُو 

“And pay the blood-money to the family of the slain, unless they remit it as a charity” 4/92 

5 - فَوَضَّ

(تَفوَيضا (اليه الَامر means to take and advocate or a regent or to give power of attorney.

وَأُفَوِّضُ أَمۡرِىٓ إِلَى ٱل 

“I confide my cause unto Allah” 40/44 

6- إِستَودَعَ

وَدَعَ means to let go and اَودَعَاَلشَّيَmeans to keep as a trust. وَدَّعَmeans to see off a traveller and إِستَودَعَفُلانَاشَيَاis to keep a trust with someone.

وَهُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنشَأَكُم مِّن نَّفۡسٍ۬ وَٲحِدَةٍ۬ فَمُسۡتَقَرٌّ۬ وَمُسۡتَوۡدَعٌ۬*ۗ 

“And He it is Who hath produced you from a single being, and (hath given you) a habitation and a repository” 6/99 

Jalandhary says: “You are kept alive in the world for a specified period of time and then buried in the earth and are entrusted to Allah” 

Inference 

1- كَفَّلَ: To entrust one with someone's upbringing

2- دَفَأَإِلَي: To return a trust to its owner

3- سَلَّمَ: To transfer something to someone else in order to escape adversity

4- وَكَّلَ: To trust someone with your matters

5- فَوَّضَ: To take a regent, to take an attorney

6- استودع: To entrust some with.
