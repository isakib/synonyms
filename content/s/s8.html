8- SUPURD KERNA, HAWALAY KERNA [to devolve, to commend]

Synonyms: AKFALA, KAFFALA, WAKKALA, DAFA’ (ILA), SALLAMA, FAWWADA, ISTAWDA’

8.1 AKFALA (اَکْفَلَ) and KAFFALA (کْفَّلَ): KAFALA (کْفَلَ) means liable to someone’s livelihood and protection. Guarantor. AKFALA (اَکْفَلَ) and KAFFALA (کْفَّلَ) means to commend the responsibilities of protection and grooming to someone, to assign a guarantor, Entrust someone else in charge (dictionary Al Manjad). In the Quran

Surah Saad (Saad): سورۃ ص 38:23

إِنَّ هَـٰذَآ أَخِى لَهُ ۥ تِسۡعٌ۬ وَتِسۡعُونَ نَعۡجَةً۬ وَلِىَ نَعۡجَةٌ۬ وَٲحِدَةٌ۬ فَقَالَ أَكۡفِلۡنِيہَا وَعَزَّنِى فِى ٱلۡخِطَابِ

“This brother of mine has ninety nine ewes, and I have one ewe, and he said, ‘Entrust it to me,’ and he pressured me with words.”

In Another reference:

Surah Al-e-Imran (The family of Imran): سورۃ آل عِمْرن 3:37

 فَتَقَبَّلَهَا رَبُّهَا بِقَبُولٍ حَسَنٍ۬ وَأَنۢبَتَهَا نَبَاتًا حَسَنً۬ا وَكَفَّلَهَا زَكَرِيَّا‌ۖ

Her Lord accepted her with a gracious reception, and brought her a beautiful upbringing, and entrusted her to the care of Zechariah.

8.2 WAKKALA (وَکَّلَ): WAKAL (وَکَل) means trust someone in ones’ affair. WAKKALA (وَکَّلَ) means to trust someone and put him in charge of the affair. To appoint a solicitor or representative  (dictionary Al Manjad). Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah As-Sajdah (The Prostration): سورۃ السَّجدَہ 32:11

 قُلۡ يَتَوَفَّٮٰكُم مَّلَكُ ٱلۡمَوۡتِ ٱلَّذِى وُكِّلَ بِكُمۡ

 Say, “The angel of death put in charge of you will reclaim you. Then to your Lord you will be returned.”

8.3 DAFA’ (ILA) (دَفَعَ (الٰی)): To return something back to its owner, to hand over, to devolve. Allah says in the Quran

Surah An-Nisa (The women): سورۃ النِّسا 4:6

فَإِنۡ ءَانَسۡتُم مِّنۡہُمۡ رُشۡدً۬ا فَٱدۡفَعُوٓاْ إِلَيۡہِمۡ أَمۡوَٲلَهُمۡ‌ۖ

 If you find them to be mature enough, hand over their properties to them.

8.4 SALLAMA (سَلَّمَ): SALIMA (سَلِمَ) means to get rid of a flaw or a calamity. SALLAMA (سَلَّمَ) means to say ASSALAMU ALAIKUM (اسلام علیکم) and also to save from a calamity. And SALLAMA-ILA-FULAN (سَلَّمَہ الٰی فُلاَن) means to commend something to other, and SALLAMA-HUSH-SHAI’A-FATASALLAMHU (سَلَّمَہُ الشَّیْءَفَتَسَلَّمَہُ) means to commend something to someone else and the other person accepting the entity (dictionary Al Manjad). In other words SALLAMA (سَلَّمَ) means to commend something to other in order to salvage from harm. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:233

وَإِنۡ أَرَدتُّمۡ أَن تَسۡتَرۡضِعُوٓاْ أَوۡلَـٰدَكُمۡ فَلَا جُنَاحَ عَلَيۡكُمۡ إِذَا سَلَّمۡتُم مَّآ ءَاتَيۡتُم بِٱلۡمَعۡرُوفِ‌ۗ

And if you wish to give your children out to nurse, it is no sin for you, provided that you pay what is due from you in kindness.

In another place in Holy Quran

Surah An-Nisa (The women): سورۃ النِّسا 4:92

 وَدِيَةٌ۬ مُّسَلَّمَةٌ إِلَىٰٓ أَهۡلِهِۦۤ إِلَّآ أَن يَصَّدَّقُواْ‌ۚ

And pay compensation to the victim’s family, unless they remit it as charity

8.5 FAWWAD (فَوَّض): TAFWEEDAN-ALAIH-AL-AMAR(تفویضًا الیہ الامر)  means to transfer the authority of some matter to other (dictionary Al Manjad). In the Quran

Surah Al-Ghafir (The forgiver): سورۃ غافِر 40:44

 وَأُفَوِّضُ أَمۡرِىٓ إِلَى ٱللَّهِۚ

So I commit my case to God

8.6 ISTAWDA’A (اِسْتَوْدَعَ): WADA’A (وَدَعَ) means to leave. And AWDA’-ASH-SHAI (اَوْدَعَ الشَّیْ) means to keep (someone’s) deposit. WADDA’A (وَدَّعَ) refers to bid farewell to a traveler. ISTAWDA’A-FALANA-SHIA (اِسْتَوْدَعَ فَالَانَّا شَیْٔاً) means to entrust something into other’s keeping (dictionary Al Manjad) in other words to commence something to other. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Ana’am (The cattle): سورۃ الانعام 6:98

وَهُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنشَأَكُم مِّن نَّفۡسٍ۬ وَٲحِدَةٍ۬ فَمُسۡتَقَرٌّ۬ وَمُسۡتَوۡدَعٌ۬‌ۗ

And it is He who produced you from a single person, then a repository, then a depository

Summary:

8.1 KAFFALA: Commend the responsibilities of grooming

8.2 WAKKALA: Trust someone and commend ones’ affair to that person

8.3 DAFA’A ILA: To devolve, to hand over

8.4 SALLAM: Commend something to other in order to salvage from harm

8.5 FAWWAD: Transfer authority to someone.

8.6 ISTAWDA’A: Entrust something into other’s keeping

To Commit, To Entrust 

The words اَكفَلَ، كَفَّلَ، وَكَّلَ، دَفَعَ (إِلَي)، سَلَّمَ، فَوَضَّ، إِستَودَعَhave been used in the Quran.

1- اَكفَلَandكَفَّلَ

كَفَلَ means to be responsible for someone necessary expenditure and taking care, as in a guarantor. اَكفَلَandكَفَّلَmeans entrusting the above responsibility to someone else.

“ إِنَّ هَـٰذَآ أَخِى لَهُۥ تِسۡعٌ۬ وَتِسۡعُونَ نَعۡجَةً۬ وَلِىَ نَعۡجَةٌ۬ وَٲحِدَةٌ۬ فَقَالَ أَكۡفِلۡنِيہَا وَعَزَّنِى فِى ٱلۡخِطَابِ"

“Lo! this my brother hath ninety and nine ewes while I had one ewe; and he said: Entrust it to me, and he conquered me in speech” 38/23

On another occasion: 

فَتَقَبَّلَهَا رَبُّهَا بِقَبُولٍ حَسَنٍ۬ وَأَنۢبَتَهَا نَبَاتًا حَسَنً۬ا وَكَفَّلَهَا زَكَرِيَّا*ۖ 

“And her Lord accepted her with full acceptance and vouchsafed to her a goodly growth; and made Zachariah her guardian” 3/37 

2- وَكَّلَ

وَكَلَ means to have trust on someone in ones personal matters. وَكَّلَmeans to entrust someone without personal matter, to take someone as a trustee.

قُلۡ يَتَوَفَّٮٰكُم مَّلَكُ ٱلۡمَوۡتِ ٱلَّذِى وُكِّلَ بِكُمۡ 

“Say: The angel of death, who hath charge concerning you” 32/11 

3- إِلِي) دَفَعَ)

Means to return a trust. 

فَإِنۡ ءَانَسۡتُم مِّنۡہُمۡ رُشۡدً۬ا فَٱدۡفَعُوٓاْ إِلَيۡہِمۡ أَمۡوَٲلَهُمۡ*ۖ 

“Then, if ye find them of sound judgement, deliver over unto them their fortune” 4/6 

4- سَلَّمَ

سَلِمَ means to be delivered or be rescued from a calamity. سَلَّمَalso means saying السَّلاَمُعَلَيكُمor to rescue someone as سَلِمَ.

سَلَّمَهُ اَلشَّيءَفَتَسَلَمَّهُmeans to entrust to someone and to accept that trust. Hence, to relieve from adversity and entrust, سَلَّمَis used.

وَإِنۡ أَرَدتُّمۡ أَن تَسۡتَرۡضِعُوٓاْ أَوۡلَـٰدَكُمۡ فَلَا جُنَاحَ عَلَيۡكُمۡ إِذَا سَلَّمۡتُم مَّآ ءَاتَيۡتُم بِٱلۡمَعۡرُوفِ*ۗ 

“And if ye wish to give your children out to nurse, it is no sin for you, provide that ye pay what is due from you in kindness” 2/233 

On another occasion: 

وَدِيَةٌ۬ مُّسَلَّمَةٌ إِلَىٰٓ أَهۡلِهِۦۤ إِلَّآ أَن يَصَّدَّقُو 

“And pay the blood-money to the family of the slain, unless they remit it as a charity” 4/92 

5 - فَوَضَّ

(تَفوَيضا (اليه الَامر means to take and advocate or a regent or to give power of attorney.

وَأُفَوِّضُ أَمۡرِىٓ إِلَى ٱل 

“I confide my cause unto Allah” 40/44 

6- إِستَودَعَ

وَدَعَ means to let go and اَودَعَاَلشَّيَmeans to keep as a trust. وَدَّعَmeans to see off a traveller and إِستَودَعَفُلانَاشَيَاis to keep a trust with someone.

وَهُوَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنشَأَكُم مِّن نَّفۡسٍ۬ وَٲحِدَةٍ۬ فَمُسۡتَقَرٌّ۬ وَمُسۡتَوۡدَعٌ۬*ۗ 

“And He it is Who hath produced you from a single being, and (hath given you) a habitation and a repository” 6/99 

Jalandhary says: “You are kept alive in the world for a specified period of time and then buried in the earth and are entrusted to Allah” 

Inference 

1- كَفَّلَ: To entrust one with someone's upbringing

2- دَفَأَإِلَي: To return a trust to its owner

3- سَلَّمَ: To transfer something to someone else in order to escape adversity

4- وَكَّلَ: To trust someone with your matters

5- فَوَّضَ: To take a regent, to take an attorney

6- استودع: To entrust some with.
