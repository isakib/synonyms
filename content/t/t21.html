Miskeen – Poverty

Quran has used the words Faqr, Maskana, A'elah, Imlaaq, Qatr, Ba'saa and Matrabah.

1- Faqr

It means poverty and Fiqrah means vertebra from the backbone and its plural is Fiqaar. Fiqrah is also used for severity that is back breaking. 'Faqrat hud-dahiyah' means that such a severe situation befall him that it left him back broken. So, Faqr means back breaking poverty.

“If they should be poor, Allah will enrich them from His bounty ” 24:32

2- Maskana

Miskeen is a person who barely has means for everyday survival.. According to another opinion, Miskeen is a person who is living in poverty but he doesn't own anything, meaning he doesn't even have a place to live. Linguists have always differed in their opinion over who is financially weaker, Faqir or Miskeen but in the opinion of the Quran, a Faqir is more deserving.

“Zakah expenditures are only for the poor and for the needy “ 9:60

In the above aayah, the occurance of the word Faqir before Miskeen is an argument in favour of this opinion and the following aayah shows that Miskeen can own.

“The boat belonged to the poor people who used to work at the river” 18:80

3- A'elah

A'elah or A'ol is everything that is a burden on a person. Ayal mean those people for whom one is responsible to spend on and A'elah is the poverty resulting from such responsibility. Allah says:

“And if you fear poverty than if Allah willed, He will make you rich with his bounty” 9:38

4- Imlaaq

Imlaaq doesn't mean poverty itself but it means the fear of loosing everything by spending all of it. It is said that 'Amla qadahru ma lahu' meaning the time made his riches slip away from him and Amlaq means the slipping away of the riches.

Allah says:

“And do not kill your offspring for fear of poverty” 17:31

The same meaning is implied in another aayah using 'Khashyata infaaq'.

5- Qatr

It has been used in the Quran in the meaning of stinginess. For example,

“But give them [a gift of] compensation - the wealthy according to his capability and the poor according to his capability - a provision according to what is acceptable“ 2:236

But the linguists say that it means spending on ones family with miserliness and it has been ussed so in Quran too.

“And [they are] those who, when they spend, do so not excessively or sparingly but are ever, between that, [justly] moderate” 25:67

6- Ba'saa

Bi'sa and Yaba'sa mean dire neediness and Ba'saa means war, calamity and hunger. Ba'saa is commonly used to depict adversity and its antonym is Sara'a which means prosperity and favour. Ba'saa also means a calamity that also brings along fear.

“And feed the miserable and the poor” 22:28

Similarly in 2:214, Ba'saa means advesity and Daraa means other afflictions.

7- Matrabah

Turab means dry soil. 'Tarib ar-rajul' (Tarban, Matraba) means a person who is so poor and worthless as dirt. It says in the Quran:

“Or feeding on a day of severe hunger or a needy person in misery” 90:14-16

Inference

1- Faqr: Severe and extreme poverty

2- Maskanat: Hardly making ends meet

3- A'elah: Buried under the burden of responsibility

4- Imlaaq: Fear of poverty

5- Qatr: Stinginess in spending on family

6- Ba'saa: Times of adversity

7- Matrabah: Being miserable because of poverty
