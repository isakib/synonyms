10. Taraashnaa تراشنا (To Trim/Cut/Carve)

Also known as ‘nahata’ نَحَتَ and ‘jaaba’ جَابَ (jwb جوب) in the Holy Quran.

1.      Nahata نَحَتَ: It refers to the peeling, abrading or carving of hard and solid objects such as wood, stone or metal etc. ‘Nuhataah’ نُحَاتَه refers to the clippings or the trimmed pieces.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah As Saffat (Those who set the Ranks) سُوۡرَةُ الصَّافات

(37:95)

أَتَعۡبُدُونَ مَا تَنۡحِتُونَ

“He said, "Do you worship that which you [yourselves] carve,”

It is evident that these idols were made out of stone, wood or metal. In another location, Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Araf (The Heights) سُوۡرَةُ الاٴعرَاف

(7:75)

 وَتَنۡحِتُونَ ٱلۡجِبَالَ بُيُوتً۬ا‌ۖ

“And remember when He made you successors after the 'Aad and settled you in the land, [and] you take for yourselves palaces from its plains and carve from the mountains, homes. Then remember the favors of Allah and do not commit abuse on the earth, spreading corruption."

2.      Jaaba جَابَs: To cut or crop. It also refers to making a hole or a slit/fissure and then carving and peeling it to accuracy. In addition, for a delicate and meticulous job, the word ‘nahata’ نَحَتَ is more appropriate, whereas for a heavy duty job, the word ‘jaaba’ جَابَ is suitable.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Fajr (The Dawn) سُوۡرَةُ الفَجر

(89:9)

وَثَمُودَ ٱلَّذِينَ جَابُواْ ٱلصَّخۡرَ بِٱلۡوَادِ

“And [with] Thamud, who carved out the rocks in the valley?”

Conclusion:

(1)   Nahata نَحَتَ: To carve and peel; commonly used for delicate tasks.

(2) Jaaba جَابَ: To cut, drill into, and peel. It is associated with heavy duty tasks.
