27. BADKAARI

[prostitution]

Synonyms: ZINA, BAGHAAE, SAAFAHA, FAHISHA

27.1 ZINA (زِنَا): is a popular word meaning the committing of sexual act of a man with a woman who is a stranger, or of a woman with a man other than her husband.

__________________________________

Surah Al-Isra' (The Journey by Night)

17:32

وَلاَ تَقْرَبُواْ الزِّنَى إِنَّهُ كَانَ فَاحِشَةً وَسَاء سَبِيلاً

And come not near to the unlawful sexual intercourse. Verily, it is a Fâhishah.

__________________________________

27.2 BAGHAAe (بَغَاء) : it means to go beyond limits, to disobey, to force. And بِغَآءً الاَمَةُ بَاغَتِ means a slave girl committing zina and بَغِيّ means a shameless woman who has committed zina. The word بَغَاء either specifies a slave girl who has committed zina or for a woman who is of a bad profession i.e. a prostitute, the woman who has become famous for committing zina. The Quran shows both these meanings:

_________________________________

Surah An-Nur (The Light)

24:33

وَلَا تُكْرِهُوا فَتَيَاتِكُمْ عَلَى الْبِغَاء

And force not your maids to prostitution

__________________________________

At another place the Jews say related to Maryam (AS)

Surah Maryam (Mary)

19:28

يَا أُخْتَ هَارُونَ مَا كَانَ أَبُوكِ امْرَأَ سَوْءٍ وَمَا كَانَتْ أُمُّكِ بَغِيًّا

"O sister (i.e. the like) of Hârûn (Aaron) ! Your father was not a man who used to commit adultery, nor your mother was an unchaste woman."

__________________________________

27.3 SAAFAHA (سَافَحَ) : سفح means to make blood or water flow and سَافَحَ means to commit zina. نِكَاح عَقْدِ بِلَا الماَء صَبَّ means to commit sexual intercourse without being married to each other. Therefore its difference with zina is that argument is found in this. And in the Holy Quran, it is promoted by using مُحْصِنِيْن with مُسَافِحِيْن

So سَافَحَ means to keep an unlawful relationship and مُسَافِح is the man and woman involved in this. The word آشنَا is also used nowadays to mean the same.

__________________________________

Surah An-Nisa' (The Women)

4:25

فَانكِحُوهُنَّ بِإِذْنِ أَهْلِهِنَّ وَآتُوهُنَّ أُجُورَهُنَّ بِالْمَعْرُوفِ مُحْصَنَاتٍ غَيْرَ مُسَافِحَاتٍ وَلاَ مُتَّخِذَاتِ أَخْدَانٍ

Wed them with the permission of their own folk (guardians, Auliyâ' or masters) and give them their Mahr according to what is reasonable; they (the above said captive and slave-girls) should be chaste, not adulterous, nor taking boy-friends.

__________________________________

27.4 FAHISHA (فَاحِشَة) : فحش means a noun or verb for someone who has crossed his limits in vice. And فَاحِشَة refers to nouns or verbs which bring a person close to zina, acts and talk of shamelessness. مُبَيّنَة فَحشَة is used for zina.

__________________________________

Surah An-Nisa' (The Women)

4:19

وَلاَ تَعْضُلُوهُنَّ لِتَذْهَبُواْ بِبَعْضِ مَا آتَيْتُمُوهُنَّ إِلاَّ أَن يَأْتِينَ بِفَاحِشَةٍمُّبَيِّنَةٍ

and you should not treat them with harshness, that you may take away part of the Mahr you have given them, unless they commit open illegal sexual intercourse

______________________________________

And the word FAAHISHA is used for such an act of adultery which is yet to be proven.

Surah An-Nisa' (The Women)

4:15

وَاللاَّتِي يَأْتِينَ الْفَاحِشَةَ مِن نِّسَآئِكُمْ فَاسْتَشْهِدُواْ عَلَيْهِنَّ أَرْبَعةً مِّنكُمْ

And those of your women who commit illegal sexual intercourse, take the evidence of four witnesses from amongst you against them

________________________________________

SUMMARY

ZINA : it is a popular word. A strange man and a strange woman having illegal relations.

BAGHAAE : A slave girl involved in zina or a prostitute.

SAFAHA : a strange man and woman involved in an illegal relationship.

FAHISHA : refers to the acts or the talk that bring a person close to zina, or the accusation of zina without any proof.
