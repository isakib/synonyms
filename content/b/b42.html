42. Basti بستی – Basti walay بستی والے (The Township – The Inhabitants of the Township)

Also known as ‘qaryaa’ قَريَة, ‘badu’ بَدو and ‘aaraab’ اَعرَاب in the Holy Quran.

1.      Qaryaa قَريَة: Any place where people would gather and populate the area, whether it is a small township, a village, a neighborhood or a city. ‘Um ul-quraa’ اُمُّ القُرَى means a main or central city or township. The city of Makkah has also been called ‘Um ul-quraa’ اُمُّ القُرَى and ‘qaryaa’ قَريَة means a township, the inhabitants of the township or the residents.

In the Holy Quran,

Surah Al Yusuf (Joseph) سُوۡرَةُ یُوسُف

(12:82)

وَسۡـَٔلِ ٱلۡقَرۡيَةَ ٱلَّتِى ڪُنَّا فِيہَا

“And ask the city in which we were and the caravan in which we came - and indeed, we are truthful,"

2.      Badu بَدو: means village; the rural or remote areas. ‘Badaa’ بَدَا means to be apparent and evident and ‘baduu’ بَدو means a place that does not have any tall buildings that, in turn, makes every feature eminently visible. The words ‘baadiya’ بَاديَة meaning desert, ‘baadi’ بَادى meaning nomads and ‘badaawi-un’ بَدَوِيٌ meaning country folk or rural folk, are derivatives of ‘Baduu’ بَدو.

In the Holy Quran,

Surah Al Yusuf (Joseph) سُوۡرَةُ یُوسُف

(12:100)

وَقَدۡ أَحۡسَنَ بِىٓ إِذۡ أَخۡرَجَنِى مِنَ ٱلسِّجۡنِ وَجَآءَ بِكُم مِّنَ ٱلۡبَدۡوِ

“And he raised his parents upon the throne, and they bowed to him in prostration. And he said, "O my father, this is the explanation of my vision of before. My Lord has made it reality. And He was certainly good to me when He took me out of prison and brought you [here] from bedouin life after Satan had induced [estrangement] between me and my brothers. Indeed, my Lord is Subtle in what He wills. Indeed, it is He who is the Knowing, the Wise.”

3.      Aaraab اَعرَاب: ‘Arabiyyun’ عَرَبِيٌ means a resident or inhabitant of the country Arab. ‘Aaraabi’ اَعرَابِي means a nomad or rural of Arab; a person who resides in the rural areas of Arab: a Bedouin. Since the people living in the rural are normally ignorant and uneducated, the word ‘aaraab’ اَعرَاب also comes in to define it.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah At –Tawbah (The Repentance) سُوۡرَةُ التّوبَة

(9:98)

وَمِنَ ٱلۡأَعۡرَابِ مَن يَتَّخِذُ مَا يُنفِقُ مَغۡرَمً۬ا

“And among the bedouins are some who consider what they spend as a loss and await for you turns of misfortune. Upon them will be a misfortune of evil. And Allah is Hearing and Knowing.”

 

Conclusion:

(1)   Qaryaa قَريَة: A township (village or city) and its inhabitants.

(2)   Baduu بَدو: Rural or remote areas.

(3)   Aaraab اَعرَاب: Bedouins and the uncouth.
