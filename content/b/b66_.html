66 – To Relate/Narrate

 

The words وَصَفَ , قَصَّ, ضَرَبَ, حَدَّثَ, بَيَّنَ, صَرَّفَ, فَصَّلَ and فَسَّرَ have been used in the holy Quran.

 

66.1 وَصَفَ: used with the appearance of something is described, whether the description is true or not; to paint a scene (linguistically).

 

Surat Yūsuf (Joseph) - يوسف سورة

12:18

 (﻿١٨﻿)تَصِفُونَ مَا عَلَىٰ ٱلۡمُسۡتَعَانُ وَٱللَّهُ جَمِيلٌ۬‌فَصَبۡرٌ۬ أَمۡرً۬ا‌ أَنفُسُكُمۡ لَكُمۡ سَوَّلَتۡ بَلۡ قَالَ

[Jacob] said, "Rather, your souls have enticed you to something, so patience is most fitting. And Allah is the one sought for help against that which you describe."(18)

_______________________________________

 

66.2 قَصَّ: literally to walk in someone’s footsteps, as in

 

Surat Al-Kahf (The Cave) -  الكهف سورة

18: 64

 (﻿٦٤﻿) قَصَصً۬ا ءَاثَارِهِمَا عَلَىٰٓ فَٱرۡتَدَّا

So they returned, following their footprints (64).

 

In this, قَصَّ means to follow step by step.  It also refers to narrating an incident which has been narrated in a people generation after generation, as Allah (the exalted) says:

Surat Yūsuf (Joseph) - يوسف  سورة

12:3

 نَحۡنُ نَقُصُّ عَلَيۡكَ أَحۡسَنَ ٱلۡقَصَصِ بِمَآ أَوۡحَيۡنَآ إِلَيۡكَ هَـٰذَا ٱلۡقُرۡءَانَ (﻿٣﻿)

We relate to you, [O Muhammad], the best of stories in what We have revealed to you of this Qur'an (3).

 

قَصَّ has also been used to narrate an ordinary event, as Allah (the exalted) says:

Surat Al-Qaşaş (The Stories) - القصص سورة

28:25

 (﻿٢٥﻿)ٱلظَّـٰلِمِينَ ٱلۡقَوۡمِ مِنَ نَجَوۡتَ تَخَفۡ‌ لَا قَالَ ٱلۡقَصَصَ عَلَيۡهِ وَقَصَّ جَآءَهُ  فَلَمَّا

So when he (Moses) came to him and related to him the story, he said, "Fear not. You have escaped from the wrongdoing people."(25)

______________________________________

 

66.3 ضَرَبَ (مَثَلاً): to relate something in order to understand another.  To relate an example or a saying.  Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) -  البقرة سورة

2:26

(﻿٢٦﻿)فَوۡقَهَا‌ فَمَا بَعُوضَةً۬ مَّا مَثَلاً۬ يَضۡرِبَ أَن يَسۡتَحۡىِۦۤ لَا ٱللَّهَ إِنَّ

Indeed, Allah is not timid to present an example - that of a mosquito or what is smaller than it (26)

_______________________________________

 

66.4 حَدَّثَ: حَدَثَ refers to the coming into existence of something that did not exist before.  حَدَّثَ means to relate something or some news which is unknown to the masses.  Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat Az-Zalzalah (The Earthquake) - الزلزلة سورة

99:3-4

 وَقَالَ ٱلۡإِنسَـٰنُ مَا لَهَا (﻿٣﻿) يَوۡمَٮِٕذٍ۬ تُحَدِّثُ أَخۡبَارَهَا (﻿٤﻿)

And man says, "What is [wrong] with it?"(3). That Day, it will report its news (4).

_______________________________________

 

66.5 بَيَّنَ: بيَان refers to eloquent speech and بيِّنَة refers to evidence or proof.  بَيَّنَ refers to present a case with proof.  According to Ibn Al-Gaaris, بَيَّنَ includes three things: افتراق (separation), بُعد, (to come after) and وضُوح (clarity); meaning for something to be clear and separate from another.  Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - البقرة سورة

2:187

(﻿١٨٧﻿) يَتَّقُونَ لَعَلَّهُمۡ لِلنَّاسِ ءَايَـٰتِهِۦ ٱللَّهُ يُبَيِّنُ كَذَٲلِكَ

Thus does Allah make clear His ordinances to the people that they may become righteous (187).

 

تَبيَّنَ comes in the meaning of being clear and separate in the verse below:

 

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - البقرة سورة

2:256

(﻿٢٥٦﻿) ٱلۡغَىِّ‌ۚ مِنَ ٱلرُّشۡدُ تَّبَيَّنَ قَد

The right course has become clear from the wrong (256).

______________________________________

 

66.6 صَرَّفَ: صرف refers to turning something from one condition to another and صَرَّفَ means to explain something repeatedly.  This means that one explains a matter in different ways so that its different aspects can be well understood. Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat Al-'An`ām (The Cattle) - الأنعام سورة

6:65

 (﻿٦٥﻿)يَفۡقَهُونَ لَعَلَّهُمۡ ٱلۡأَيَـٰتِ نُصَرِّفُ كَيۡفَ ٱنظُرۡ

Look how We diversify the signs that they might understand (65).

____________________________________

 

66.7 فَصَّلَ: فَصَلَ refers to separate one thing from another in such a way that there is space between them.  فَصَّلَ refers to explain something bit by bit in an orderly fashion. Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat Al-'Isrā' (The Night Journey) - الإسراء سورة

17:12

 (﻿١٢﻿)تَفۡصِيلاً۬ فَصَّلۡنَـٰهُ شَىۡءٍ۬ وَڪُلَّ

And everything We have set out in detail (12).

________________________________________

 

66.8 فَسَّرَ: الفسر refers to explain the meaning of something while فسر refers to quote and describe/explain something.  فَسَّرَ refers to uncover something, this is usually done in two ways: first to clarify the speech its self (اَي) and then to explain its meaning and what it is referring to (اَو).  It also includes explaining each word of a sentence by its self while keeping in mind the context.  Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat Al-Furqān (The Criterian) - الفرقان سورة

25:33

 وَلَا يَأۡتُونَكَ بِمَثَلٍ إِلَّا جِئۡنَـٰكَ بِٱلۡحَقِّ وَأَحۡسَنَ تَفۡسِيرًا (﻿٣٣﻿)

And they do not come to you with an argument except that We bring you the truth and the best explanation (33).

________________________________________

 

Summary:  

1.      وَصَفَ: to describe the physical appearance of something

2.      قَصَّ: to narrate a story or event

3.      ضَرَبَ (مَثَلاً): to cite an example or saying

4.      حَدَّثَ: to relate something that is not known to ordinary

5.      بَيَّنَ: to explain with evidence

6.      صَرَّفَ: to explain the different aspects of something

7.      فَصَّلَ: to explain something bit by bit and in an orderly fashion

فَسَّرَ: to explain the difficult points
