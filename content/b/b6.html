6. Cloud_ Badal_ (بادل)

Synonyms- Sahaab (سَحَاب), Gamaam (غَمَام), Aaridd (عَارِضْ), Mawseraat (مُعْصِرَات), Mozun (مُزْن), Sayyib (صَيِّب)

6.1. Sahaab (سَحَاب): (Sahub(سحب) means to drag or to pull. It is a word commonly used for cloud (Fiqhul lughat, page 254))

Sahaan is used for every kind of cloud, therefore the application of this word is for the clouds that the wind carries and spreads across the sky. Hence this is the introductory form of every cloud.

Quranic Example:-

“And it is Allah who sends the winds, and they stir the clouds…” (38: 9)

6.2. Gamaam (غَمَام): Gamma (غَمَّ) means to enwrap or take cover (Munjid), and Gamaam (غمام) is that cloud that thickens layer by layer and blocks the sunlight from reaching the Earth (Fiqhul lughat, page 254) meaning it becomes umbrageous over the Earth.

Quranic Example:-

“And We shaded you with clouds and sent down to you manna and quails…” (2: 57)

6.4. Aaridd (عَارِضْ): This is used for when the clouds start to sprinkle light rains (۴۵۴ ل ف).

Quranic Example:-

“And when they saw it as a cloud approaching their valleys, they said, “This is a cloud bringing us rain!” Rather, it is that for which you were impatient: a wind, within it a painful punishment,” (46: 24)

6.3. Mawseraat (مُعْصِرَات): Asar (عصر) means to squeeze out water from a wet clothe or to squeeze out the juice from fruits etc. ‘Asar’ is also used for squeezed out nectar (Maqaayeesul-lughat). Aasar-ul-Qaum (اعصرالقوم) means to pour rain on a nation (Munjid).Mawseraat (مُعْصِرَات) is the plural of Mawseratun (مُعْصِرَةٌ) which means clouds that are loaded with water and it’s about to rain, or rain is about to be squeezed out from them.

Qurani Example:-

“And sent down, from the rain clouds, pouring water” (78: 14)

6.4. Mozun (مُزْن):Mazana (مَزَنَ) means filling up. The moon that peeks through the clouds is called Abn-ul-mazanth (ابن المزنۃ)(Mufraddat). Shining bright white clouds are called Maz’n (مزن) (Mufraddat, page 255). Hail is called Habbul-muzn (الْمُزْن حَبُّ)(Munjid). Rainy clouds are called Mozun (مُزْن) (Munjid).

Quranic Example:-

“Is it you who bring it down from the clouds. Or is it We who bring it down?” (56:29)

6.5. Sayyib (صَيِّب): Thunderous clouds (page 255) Heavy rainfall (Mutahal arab) Any rainy cloud (Munjid).

Quranic Example:-

“Or [it is] like a rainstorm from the sky within which is darkness, thunder and lightning.” (2:19)

Summary

Sahaab (سَحَاب): Common word for clouds and the initial stages of rain.

Gamaam (غَمَام): When the cloud becomes heavy and turns grey.

Aaridd (عَارِضْ): A cloud that has begun to lightly shower droplets of rain.

Mawseraat (مُعْصِرَات): Heavy clouds full of water.

Mozun (مُزْن): White shining clouds.

Sayyib (صَيِّب): Thunderous clouds.
