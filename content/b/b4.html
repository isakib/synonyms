4. Talking _ “Baat Karna” _ (بات کرنا)

Synonyms- Kallam (كَلَّمَ), Hawara (حَاوَرَ) and Khatab (خاطب).

4.1. Kallam (كَلَّمَ): To chat, to have a conversation. It’s usually used for when a short conversation is taking place or for when the talk is such that the replies can given promptly. Also in at times it is not necessary that the conversation should be two way. It can also be one way.

Quranic Example:- 

“…and Allah will not speak to them or look at them on the Day of Resurrection...” (7:77)

4.2. Hawara (حَاوَرَ): According to one understanding, a related word Hawr (حور) contains two basic meanings 1. رجوح اور مراجعتہ الکلام (could not translate) 2. Returning something to one and then to the other (Maqaayeesul-lughat). Therefore Hawara (حَاوَرَ) means a conversation that takes place by just questioning and answering, and the answers need to be well thought out. Such conversations are then usually very long.

Quranic Example:- 

“His companion said to him while he was conversing with him, “Have you disbelieved in He who created you from dust and then from a sperm-drop and then proportioned you [as] a man?” (18:37)

4.3. Khatab (خَاطَب): This is also used to mean a conversation (Munjid) and also a sermon or a speech which is usually one way andKhateeb would be the speaker. Khateeb can also be the one who is talking with detailed explanation (Miraatul Quraan). Sometimes it so happens that the one listening makes a minor comment to which the speaker will have nothing to reply with.

Quranic Example:- 

Allah (SWT) says to Hazrat Noah (as)

“And construct the ship under Our observation and Our inspiration and do not address Me concerning those who have wronged.” (11: 37)

And in another place when Allah (SWT) talks about the attributes of His people: 

“…and when the ignorant address then [harshly], they say [words of] peace,” (25:63)

Summary 

4.1. Kallam (كَلَّمَ): Common word for conversation. Usually used for short conversations with prompt replies.

4.2. Hawara (حَاوَرَ): For long conversations that involve questioning and answering, and requires well thought out replies. Such conversations are elaborate and long.

4.3. Khatab (خَاطَب): Usually for a one sided conversation, or also for when the one being addressed can give a short reply.
