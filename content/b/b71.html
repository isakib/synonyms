71 – To be unfair

 

The words عَدَلَ, قَسَطَ, ظَلَمَ, حَافَ (حيف), عَال and ضَازَ (ضيز) have been used

 

For the words عَدَلَ and قَسَطَ see ‘to be fair’ because these are from the terms which can have opposite meanings.

________________________________________

 

71.3 ظَلَمَ: ظَلَمَ is basically used in two ways: (1) to illegitimately take something out of its genuine place and put it in the wrong place and (2) darkness (opposite of which is light).  ظُلمَت refers to darkness, its plural is ظُلُمآت while the plural of multitudes is ظلام.

 

If ظلم comes in the first meaning, its opposite is عدل.  Every wrongful act whether with reference to Allah (the exalted) or with reference to any of His creation is ظلم.  The use of the term in this context is very common.  Allah (the exalted) says in the holy Quran:

 

Surat Luqmān (Luqman) - لقمان سورة

31:13

 يَـٰبُنَىَّ لَا تُشۡرِكۡ بِٱللَّهِ‌ۖ إِنَّ ٱلشِّرۡكَ لَظُلۡمٌ عَظِيمٌ۬ (﻿١٣﻿)

"O my son, do not associate [anything] with Allah . Indeed, association [with him] is great injustice."(13)

 

And

 

Surat An-Nisā' (The Women) - النساء سورة

4:148

لَّا يُحِبُّ ٱللَّهُ ٱلۡجَهۡرَ بِٱلسُّوٓءِ مِنَ ٱلۡقَوۡلِ إِلَّا مَن ظُلِمَ‌ (﻿١٤٨﻿)

Allah does not like the public mention of evil except by one who has been wronged (148).

________________________________________

 

71.4 حَافَ: the root meaning of حَافَ is المَيل (to incline in any direction) and contextually it refers to take one side when making a decision and so make a biased decision.  حَافَ has also been used in the context of reducing something from its sides.  In the holy Quran Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat An-Nūr (The Light) - النور سورة

24:50

أَمۡ يَخَافُونَ أَن يَحِيفَ ٱللَّهُ عَلَيۡہِمۡ وَرَسُولُهُ (﻿٥٠﻿)

Or do they fear that Allah will be unjust to them, or His Messenger? (50)

________________________________________

 

71.5 عَال: means to be unjust; to move away from the right path.  العول is used for anything that is burdensome to a person and he/she gets buried under it.  It can refer to taking more than your rightful share and so doing injustice.  Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat An-Nisā' (The Women) - النساء سورة

4:3

 وَإِنۡ خِفۡتُمۡ أَلَّا تُقۡسِطُواْ فِى ٱلۡيَتَـٰمَىٰ فَٱنكِحُواْ مَا طَابَ لَكُم مِّنَ ٱلنِّسَآءِ مَثۡنَىٰ وَثُلَـٰثَ وَرُبَـٰعَ‌ۖ فَإِنۡ خِفۡتُمۡ أَلَّا تَعۡدِلُواْ فَوَٲحِدَةً أَوۡ مَا مَلَكَتۡ أَيۡمَـٰنُكُمۡ‌ۚ ذَٲلِكَ أَدۡنَىٰٓ أَلَّا تَعُولُواْ (﻿٣﻿)

And if you fear that you will not deal justly with the orphan girls, then marry those that please you of [other] women, two or three or four. But if you fear that you will not be just, then [marry only] one or those your right hand possesses. That is more suitable that you may not incline [to injustice](3).

________________________________________

 

71.6 ضَازَ: to be unjust, to not give someone what is rightfully theirs.  ضيز means ‘chosen’ and ضِيزَى قِسمَةٌ refers to distribute/divide unjustly, meaning that in determining one’s own rights and the rights of others one fails to adhere to the principles of justice/equity.  Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat An-Najm (The Star) - النجم سورة

53: 21-22

 أَلَكُمُ ٱلذَّكَرُ وَلَهُ ٱلۡأُنثَىٰ (﻿٢١﻿) تِلۡكَ إِذً۬ا قِسۡمَةٌ۬ ضِيزَىٰٓ (﻿٢٢﻿)

Is the male for you and for Him the female? (21) That, then, is an unjust division (22).
