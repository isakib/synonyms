24 BAD BAKHTI

[misery]

Synonyms : SHIQWA,NAHOOSAH,TAAIR,SHAOOM,HUSOOM.

 

24.1 SHIQWA (شِقْوَة): SHIQWA(شِقْوَة) and SHIQAWA(شِقَاوَة) both mean wretchedness. (opposite:S3ADA[سعادة]). And like S3ADA(سعادة) is a subjective issue, similarly, SHIQAWA(شِقَاوَة) and  SHIQWA(شِقْوَة) are also subjective issues. And SHAQI(شقي) is that person who is innately wretched or unlucky.  It is in the Quran that:

________________

Surat Al-Mu'minūn (The Believers) - سورة المؤمنون

23:106

قَالُوا رَبَّنَا غَلَبَتْ عَلَيْنَا شِقْوَتُنَا وَكُنَّا قَوْمًا ضَالِّينَ

They will say, "Our Lord, our wretchedness overcame us, and we were a people astray.

________________

 

24.2 NAHOOSAH (نَحُوْسَة): NUHAASA(نُحاس) also means copper and also that part of the fire, whose colour is like copper. NAHASA(نَحَسَ) means that ‘Inspite of being the spectrum of the sky, to become like the colour of copper’ and this is a commonly used proverb for adversity. And NAHASA(نحس) and NAHAWSAH(نحوسة) means hard and wretched times. ALLAH(SWT) says :

________________

Surat Fuşşilat (Explained in Detail) - سورة فصلت

41:16

فَأَرْسَلْنَا عَلَيْهِمْ رِيحًا صَرْصَرًا فِي أَيَّامٍ نَّحِسَاتٍ

So We sent upon them a screaming wind during days of misfortune.

________________

 

 

24.3 TAAIR (طَائِر): TAAR(طَارَ) means to flying of birds, etc. in the air and TAAIR(طَائِر) means ‘bird’. Since, seeing an omen in birds was a common custom, consequently, the word TAAIR(طَائِر) also started to be used to mean ‘to see an omen or auspiciousness’. This word is, generally, used in a negative meaning. ITTAYYIRA(اِطَّيَّرَ) and TATAYYARA(تَطَيَّرَ) mean to see an evil omen or inauspiciousness. In fact, the actual meaning of TAAIRA(طائر) is not misfortune but to see an omen for misfortune. It is in the Quran that:

________________

36:18

قَالُوا إِنَّا تَطَيَّرْنَا بِكُمْ

They said, "Indeed, we consider you a bad omen.

24.4 SHAOOM (شَأُوْم): AS-SHAMATAL MASHAMAH(الشَّأمَةَ الْمَشَّأَمَةَ) means adversity, scarcity and left side. And left side is also considered a sign of adversity. And shamath[misfortune], is actually used for a kind of inauspiciousness, that is anticipated because of our deeds. Shamathi aamaal[misery] is a popular word. And TASHAUM(تَشَاءُم) means to see an evil omen. ALLAH(SWT) says:

________________

Surat Al-Balad (The City) - سورة البلد

90:17-19

 أُولَٰئِكَ أَصْحَابُ الْمَيْمَنَةِ ثُمَّ كَانَ مِنَ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالصَّبْرِ وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالْمَرْحَمَة وَالَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا بِآيَاتِنَا هُمْ أَصْحَابُ الْمَشْأَمَةِ

And then being among those who believed and advised one another to patience and advised one another to compassion. Those are the companions of the right. But they who disbelieved in Our signs - those are the companions of the left.

________________

24.5 HUSOOM (حُسُوْم): Al-HASAMA(الحسم) means to chop a thing and to destroy all evidences of its existence. And HUSAAM(حُسَام) means sword. Then, HUSOOM(حسوم) also indicates such an adversity or affliction, which almost obliterates the person.  ALLAH(SWT) says:

________________

Surat Al-Ĥāqqah (The Reality) - سورة الحاقة

69:6,7

وَأَمَّا عَادٌ فَأُهْلِكُوا بِرِيحٍ صَرْصَرٍ عَاتِيَةٍ سَخَّرَهَا عَلَيْهِمْ سَبْعَ لَيَالٍ وَثَمَانِيَةَ أَيَّامٍ حُسُومًا فَتَرَى الْقَوْمَ فِيهَا صَرْعَىٰ كَأَنَّهُمْ أَعْجَازُ نَخْلٍ خَاوِيَةٍ

And as for 'Aad, they were destroyed by a screaming, violent wind. Which Allah imposed upon them for seven nights and eight days in succession, so you would see the people therein fallen as if they were hollow trunks of palm trees.

 

Summary:

24.1 SHIQWA: Wretchedness that is destined.

24.2 NAHOOSAH: Period of misery and hardship.

24.3 TAAIR: To see an omen of misfortune concerning something, inauspiciousness.

24.4 SHAOOM,SHAAMAH: Adversity that is anticpated as a consequence of one’s deeds.

24.5 HUSOOM:That adversity which obliterates.
