28 BADAL DENA

[to change]

Synonyms : BADDAL,HAWWAL,GHAIR,HARRAF or TAHARRAF,NAKKAR,DAAWAL.

 

28.1 BADDAL (بَدَّل) : BADALA(بَدَل)is a well-known word (i.e.) to substitute one thing in place of another and BADDALA(بدّل) means to add something in place of another. In fact the first thing is thrown away and the second thing comes into existence.  

________________

28.2 HAWWAL (حَوَّل) : The meaning of HAWALA(حول) acoording to Ibn Farsi is TAHARAK FI DAWR(تحرك فِي دور) i.e. to move in circles, and the area surrounding a thing is known as HAWALA(حول). HAWWAL(حَوَّل) means to take a place from its original place and keep it in another place. In fact, in HAWWAL(حَوَّل), the original thing is not lost, but only moves from its original place. HAROOF AL_KALAM(تحريف الكلام) is that, in which, some words of an unbroken composition are taken from one place and put in another place, without paying attention to the context and due to which,the meaning changes. Ism Nakeera(indefinite noun) and Ism Maroofa(definite noun) are famous words. And munkar means bad deeds and maroof means good deeds. 28.6 DAAWAL (دَاوَل): AD-DAWLAH WADDULAH(الدَّوْلَةَ وَالدُّوْلَة) means to circulate.  The antonym of DAALA(دَالَ) is DAARA(دَارَ). DAWR(دَوْر) and DAIRAH(دَوْر) means distress, adversity or sorrows of everyday life. DAWLAH(دَوْلَةَ) and DULAH(دَوْلَة) indicate the turning of bad days into fortunate days. And DAAWAL(دَاوَل) means the initial change of fortunate days or its alternation.

[ to change ]

Synonyms: BADDALA, HAWWALA, GHAYYAR, HARRAF and TAHARRAF, NAKKAR, DAWAL.

28.1 BADDALA (بَدَّلَ) : بَدَل is a popular word meaning one thing replaced by another, and بدّل means to substitute a thing with something else. Therefore the first thing is mafqud and the second thing is amojood *********************************.

28.2 HAWWALA (حَوَّلَ) : حول means according to ibn faaris to move in circles. ***********************. And حول means the surroundings of something. حَوَّلَ means to move something from its original place and place it somewhere else. Therefore in حوّل the thing is not maqdood, but it moves from its original place.

Surah Al-Fatir or Al-Mala'ikah (The Originator or The Angels)

35:43

فَهَلْ يَنظُرُونَ إِلَّا سُنَّتَ الْأَوَّلِينَ فَلَن تَجِدَ لِسُنَّتِ اللَّهِ تَبْدِيلًا وَلَن تَجِدَ لِسُنَّتِ اللَّهِ تَحْوِيلًا

Then, can they expect anything (else), but the Sunnah (way of dealing) of the peoples of old? So no change will you find in Allâh's way of dealing, and no turning off will you find in Allâh's way of dealing.

________________________________________

And God’s way of dealing is such that criminal nations are killed. In the ayah above, to make a “تبديل” in God’s way of dealing means that this can never happen that a criminal nation will get a reward instead of punishment. And “تحويل” means the crime is done by someone and the punishment is given to someone else.

28.3 GHAYYAR (غَيَّرَ) : غير means except someone else. ************************* and غيّر means change of condition. In غيّر nothingis maaqood and nor does it move from its place, rather the change comes in its condition or situation.
