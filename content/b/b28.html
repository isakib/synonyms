28 BADAL DENA

[ to change ]

Synonyms: BADDALA, HAWWALA, GHAYYAR, HARRAF and TAHARRAF, NAKKAR, DAWAL.

28.1 BADDALA (بَدَّلَ) : بَدَل is a popular word meaning one thing replaced by another, and بدّل means to substitute a thing with something else. Therefore the first thing is mafqud and the second thing is amojood *********************************.

28.2 HAWWALA (حَوَّلَ) : حول means according to ibn faaris to move in circles. ***********************. And حول means the surroundings of something. حَوَّلَ means to move something from its original place and place it somewhere else. Therefore in حوّل the thing is not maqdood, but it moves from its original place.

Surah Al-Fatir or Al-Mala'ikah (The Originator or The Angels)

35:43

فَهَلْ يَنظُرُونَ إِلَّا سُنَّتَ الْأَوَّلِينَ فَلَن تَجِدَ لِسُنَّتِ اللَّهِ تَبْدِيلًا وَلَن تَجِدَ لِسُنَّتِ اللَّهِ تَحْوِيلًا

Then, can they expect anything (else), but the Sunnah (way of dealing) of the peoples of old? So no change will you find in Allâh's way of dealing, and no turning off will you find in Allâh's way of dealing.

________________________________________

And God’s way of dealing is such that criminal nations are killed. In the ayah above, to make a “تبديل” in God’s way of dealing means that this can never happen that a criminal nation will get a reward instead of punishment. And “تحويل” means the crime is done by someone and the punishment is given to someone else.

28.3 GHAYYAR (غَيَّرَ) : غير means except someone else. ************************* and غيّر means change of condition. In غيّر nothingis maaqood and nor does it move from its place, rather the change comes in its condition or situation.

Surah Ar-Ra'd (The Thunder)

13:11

إِنَّ اللّهَ لاَ يُغَيِّرُ مَا بِقَوْمٍ حَتَّى يُغَيِّرُواْ مَا بِأَنْفُسِهِمْ

Verily! Allâh will not change the (good) condition of a people as long as they do not change their state (of goodness) themselves.

A poet has translated this ayah as follows :

“Allah has not changed the condition of this nation till today

That nation which does not think of changing itself.”
