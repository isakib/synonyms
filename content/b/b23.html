23   BAKHIL KARNA

 [To be miserly]

 

Synonyms : BAKHALA,AM’SAKA,AW’AA,3AK’DAA,AQNAA,DHAN’NA,SHAH’HA,GHALLA

 

23.1 BAKHALA(بَخِلَ) : means to refrain from spending one’s accumulated wealth even when it is necessary  . BAKHALA(بخل) is of two types a) to be miserly in fulfilling another person’s rights or in spending for Allah’s cause. b) not spend even on one’s necessary requirements. Both these types are detestable deeds and the word is common, which can be used on all types of misers. ALLAH(SWT) says

Summary:

23.1 BAKHALA:to spend less on necessary requirements for oneself and for others. This is the common word for being stingy.

23.2 AM’SAKA:To retain whatever is in one’s possession.

23.3 AW’3AA:To withhold and not allow it to decrease. Common for all types of things worthy of protection.

23.4 AK’DAA: Stop after spending little or to make an intention but not fulfil it.

23.5 AQNAA:To be stingy in looking after and spending on one’s family or to be stingy on oneself.

23.6 DHAN’NA:To be miserly in teaching one’s skilful expertise.

23.7 SHAH’HA:combination of intense greed and stinginess.

23.8 MAGLOOL ALAYD:A miserly person who does not give anything to others.
