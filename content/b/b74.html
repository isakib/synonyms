b74-No Doubt (beshak) pg 253

قَد، لاَجَرَمَ، اَنَّ،اِنَّ are the words that have been used for it in the Quran. These words are used for emphasis and give the meaning of “Indeed”, “No Doubt”, “Verily”.

1-2: اَنَّ،اِنَّ: These two words encompass the noun. اَنَّ comes as the main focus on the speech. Like :

قَدِيرٌ شَيْءٍ كُلِّ عَلَىٰاللَّهَ إِنَّ “Indeed, Allah is over all things competent” (2:20) . But اِنَّ: comes in the middle like: (قَالَ أَعْلَمُ أَنَّ اللَّهَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ) “ "I know that Allah is over all things competent.".(2:259).

This should be noted that after and words similar to it has to come in the middle of the speech. In Quran it comes like: (قَالَ إِنَّهُ يَقُولُ إِنَّهَا بَقَرَةٌ) “Musa said that no doubt your lord says that it is a cow. " (2:68). After عَلِمَ and شَهِدَ according to grammatical rules (اِنَّ) has to come in the middle of the speech (like the example is given above) but in one place in Quran after عَلِمَ and شَهِدَ the word (اِنَّ) has also come. It comes in Quran like this, (وَاللَّهُ يَعْلَمُ إِنَّكَ لَرَسُولُهُ وَاللَّهُ يَشْهَدُ إِنَّ الْمُنَافِقِينَ لَكَاذِبُونَ) “And ALLAH knows that you [Muhammad S.A.W] are His Messenger and ALLAH [also] testifies that hypocrites are liars.” (63:1)

3: (لاَ جَرَمَ) : Scholars of Tafseer have different opinions regarding its meaning. Most translators write its meaning “No Doubt” or “Indeed”. (means I ) write its meaning as “Oath of ALLAH”.

The word is used 3 times in Quran and all the times before (اَنَّ) (11:22 / 16:23 / 16:109) whose meaning is that it is used for more emphasis. Although if this meaning i.e “Oath of ALLAH” is accepted then it is also correct and only gives more emphasis. ALLAH says in the Quran (لَا جَرَمَ أَنَّهُمْ فِي الْآخِرَةِ هُمُ الْأَخْسَرُونَ) “No doubt, these are the very ones who will lose most in the Hereafter!”(Yunus 11:22) (also used in Ghafir 40:43).

4: (قَد) by entering into past tense gives the meaning of emphasis or certainity and moves it closer to present tense also. Meaning changes past tense to present tense. ALLAH says in the Quran (قَدْ سَمِعَ اللَّهُ قَوْلَ الَّتِي تُجَادِلُكَ فِي زَوْجِهَا) “No doubt ALLAH heard the speech of the one who [pointing to a women named Khola from Ansar.]”(Mujadilah 58:1).

SUMMARY:

i - (اِنَّ) comes as main focus on speech,

ii - (اَنَّ ) comes in the middle,

iii - (لاَجَرَمَ ) by coming before (اَنَّ ) gives more certainty or emphasis,

iv - (قَد) by encompassing past tense also gives the meaning of present tense.
