68 – To Sit

 

The words جَلَسَ, قَعَدَ and جَثَا (جثو) have been used in the holy Quran

 

68.1 جَلَسَ: it is the common term used for sitting, whether someone is sitting up after lying down or sitting down after standing.  جَلسٌ refers to hard or elevated land while جَلَسَ means to seat one’s self in a firm/rugged place.  The place of sitting is known as a مجلس, this term also refers to a group who are sitting together (plural being مجالس).  جلسه is مرّه إسم (noun of instance), it refers to the state of sitting or to sit once.

 

Allah (the exalted) states:

Surat Al-Mujādila (The Pleading Woman) - المجادلة سورة

58:11

 يَـٰٓأَيُّہَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓاْ إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمۡ تَفَسَّحُواْ فِى ٱلۡمَجَـٰلِسِ فَٱفۡسَحُواْ يَفۡسَحِ ٱللَّهُ لَكُمۡ‌ۖ ٌ۬ (﻿١١﻿)

O you who have believed, when you are told, "Space yourselves" in assemblies, then make space; Allah will make space for you (11).

68.2 قَعَدَ: this term is used in many different ways, for example:

 

1-) to sit down after having stood up.  قعده is also an مرّه إسم (noun of instance), in the meaning of sitting once, like the sitting between two prostrations in prayer.  It is used in the Quran when Allah (the exalted) says:

 

Surat Al-'An`ām (The Cattle) -  الأنعام سورة

6:68

(﻿٦٨﻿) ٱلظَّـٰلِمِينَ ٱلۡقَوۡمِ مَعَ ٱلذِّڪۡرَىٰ بَعۡدَ تَقۡعُدۡ فَلَا

Then do not remain (sit) after the reminder with the wrongdoing people (68).

 

2-) the word is also used in the meaning of ‘to remain sitting’ or to do nothing, to neglect one’s work.  جَلَسَ is not used in a similar fashion.  Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat At-Tawbah (The Repentance) -  التوبة سورة

 9:90

 وَجَآءَ ٱلۡمُعَذِّرُونَ مِنَ ٱلۡأَعۡرَابِ لِيُؤۡذَنَ لَهُمۡ وَقَعَدَ ٱلَّذِينَ كَذَبُواْ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ

And those with excuses among the bedouins came to be permitted [to remain], and they who had lied to Allah and His Messenger sat [at home] (90).

 

3-) it is also used in the meaning of ‘to stand’.  قَعَدَ is from الاضداد ذوي لغت (a term which can have opposite meanings).  قعودا can mean sitting or standing.  Likewise مَقعَد can refer to a place where one sits or a place where one stands.  Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat Al-Qamar (The Moon) -  القمر سورة

55:54-55

 إِنَّ ٱلۡمُتَّقِينَ فِى جَنَّـٰتٍ۬ وَنَہَرٍ۬ (﻿٥٤﻿)فِى مَقۡعَدِ صِدۡقٍ عِندَ مَلِيكٍ۬ مُّقۡتَدِرِۭ (﻿٥٥﻿)

Indeed, the righteous will be among gardens and rivers (54), in a seat of honor near a Sovereign, Perfect in Ability (55).

 

4-) قَعَدَ also comes in the meaning of ‘to be ready’ for some work.  The phrase لِلحَربِ قَعَدَ describes one who is ready to fight.  Allah (the exalted) states:

Surat At-Tawbah (The Repentance) -  التوبة سورة

9:5

‌(﻿٥﻿) مَرۡصَدٍ۬ڪُلَّ لَهُمۡ وَٱقۡعُدُواْ وَٱحۡصُرُوهُمۡ وَخُذُوهُمۡ وَجَدتُّمُوهُمۡ حَيۡثُ ٱلۡمُشۡرِكِينَ فَٱقۡتُلُواْ

Then kill the polytheists wherever you find them and capture them and besiege them and sit in wait for them at every place of ambush (5).

 

قعيده is used to describe a woman who is the caretaker and protector of the house and stays in it.  Allah (the exalted) says:

 

Surat An-Nūr (The Light) - النور سورة

24:60

 وَٱلۡقَوَٲعِدُ مِنَ ٱلنِّسَآءِ ٱلَّـٰتِى لَا يَرۡجُونَ نِكَاحً۬ا (﻿٦٠﻿)

And women of post-menstrual age who have no desire for marriage (60).

68.3 جَثَا يَجثُوا جَثواً  also comes in the forms of جَثَى يَجثِي جَثِيّاً.  The word refers to sitting on one’s knees, to sit on both knees.  Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat Al-Jāthiyah (The Crouching) -  الجاثية سورة

45:28

 وَتَرَىٰ كُلَّ أُمَّةٍ۬ جَاثِيَةً۬‌ۚ (﻿٢٨﻿)

And you will see every nation kneeling [from fear] (28).

________________________________________

 

Summary:

1.      جَلَسَ: to sit after lying down, standing or any other movement

2.      قَعَدَ: used in many different ways; to sit, to keep sitting, to neglect work, to be ready; can have opposite meanings

3.      جَثَا (جثو): to kneel
