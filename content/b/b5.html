5. Kingdom _ Badshahi _ (بادشاہی)

Synonyms- Sultaan (سُلْطان), Mulk (مُلْک) and Malakoot (مَلَکُوْت)

5.1. Sultaan (سُلْطان): This word comes in 3 meanings: 1. Authority; this meaning is implied many times in the Quran, 2. Empire or kingdom (سلطنت یا بادشاہی); this meaning is implied only once in the Quran, 3. King (بادشاہ) (ج سلاطین); this meaning is never implied in the Quran.

When Sultaan comes in the 2nd meaning of Empire or Kingdom it implies that territory that is under the subjugation or domination of a person or a king.

Quranic Example:- 

The disbelievers will say on the Day of Judgment:

“My wealth has not availed me. Gone for me is my authority.” (69: 28, 29)

5.2. Mulk (مُلّک): This word suits better to the meaning of Badshahi (بادشاہی) than Sultaan (سُلْطان). It means country. A particular piece of land that is limited by a natural boundary can be called a country and land in its entirety can also be called a country.

Quranic Example:- 

“Say (O Muhammad SAW): “O Allah! Possessor of the kingdom (مُلْکِ), You give the kingdom (مُلْکَ) to whim You will…” (3: 26)

Here the way the first Mulk is used is in the meaning of land in its entirety, as in all the land that exists, and the way it is used the second time is to mean a specific area.

The meaning of Mulk (مُلّک) can encompass ownership, kingship or possession, and the meaning of Malik (مَلِک), and the plurals for both come as Amlaak (اَمْلاَک) and Mulook (مُلُوک) (Munjid) and Malakoot (مَلَکوْت) comes in an even wider meaning encompassing the earth and the skies or the entire universe.

Quranic Example:- 

“So exalted is He in whose hand is the realm of all things, and to Him you will be returned.” (36: 83)

Summary 

When power or stateliness is implied Sultaan is used and when an area or acreage is implied Mulk is used.
