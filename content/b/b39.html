39. Baraa بڑا – Buzurg بزرگ (Senior – Elderly)

 

Also known as kabeer كبِير, akbar اكبر, azeem عظِيم, aazam اعظم, zul-jalal ذوَالجلاَل and majeed مجِيد.

 

1.      Kabeer كبِير and Akbar اكبر: Akbar basically carries the meaning of greatness. The antonym of Kabeer كبِير is Sagheer صغِير, and the antonym of Akbar اكبر is Asghar اصغر. These are comparative nouns; meaning anything compared to something smaller would be Kabeer كبِير, and the same thing in comparison with something bigger than itself would be Sagheer صغِير. Fundamentally, the word Kabeer كبِير is used for objects or bodies whether that object or body is greater in terms of age or volume/size or in any other physical manifestation. For example,

 

(a)    In terms of age:

Surah Yusuf (Joseph) سُوۡرَةُ یُوسُف

(12:80)

قَالَ ڪَبِيرُهُمۡ أَلَمۡ تَعۡلَمُوٓاْ أَنَّ أَبَاكُمۡ قَدۡ أَخَذَ عَلَيۡكُم مَّوۡثِقً۬ا مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ

“So when they had despaired of him, they secluded themselves in private consultation. The eldest of them said, "Do you not know that your father has taken upon you an oath by Allah and [that] before you failed in [your duty to] Joseph? So I will never leave [this] land until my father permits me or Allah decides for me, and He is the best of judges.”

 

(b)   In terms of size and volume:

Surah Al Anbiya (The Prophets) سُوۡرَةُ الاٴنبیَاء

(21:58)

فَجَعَلَهُمۡ جُذَٲذًا إِلَّا ڪَبِيرً۬ا لَّهُمۡ لَعَلَّهُمۡ إِلَيۡهِ يَرۡجِعُونَ

“So he made them into fragments, except a large one among them, that they might return to it [and question].”

However, in later chapters, the words Kabeer and Akbar have been used literally as well. For example,

Surah Al Baqarah (The Cow) سُوۡرَةُ البَقَرَة

(2:219)

 يَسۡـَٔلُونَكَ عَنِ ٱلۡخَمۡرِ وَٱلۡمَيۡسِرِ‌ۖ قُلۡ فِيهِمَآ إِثۡمٌ۬ ڪَبِيرٌ۬ وَمَنَـٰفِعُ لِلنَّاسِ وَإِثۡمُهُمَآ أَڪۡبَرُ مِن نَّفۡعِهِمَ

“They ask you about wine and gambling. Say, "In them is great sin and [yet, some] benefit for people. But their sin is greater than their benefit." And they ask you what they should spend. Say, "The excess [beyond needs]." Thus Allah makes clear to you the verses [of revelation] that you might give thought.”

The plural of Kabeer كبِير is Kubaraa كُبَرَاء and it is an elaborated noun which means great or greatest. It is used in both these meanings and its plural is akaabir اكَابر.

2.      Azeem عظِيم: (the antonym is Haqeer حقِير) and Aazam اعظم. The fundamental meaning of Azm عظم carries the degrees of strength and intensity in addition to greatness. These literal qualities are found together in some places and individually in others. Even a bone is called Azm عظم due to its strength and intensity. The plural of Azm عظم and Azeem عظِيم is Izaam عِظَام. The word Azeem عظِيم is also used in the cases of objects and things.

Aazam اعظم is an elaborated noun, which means ‘of greatness and seniority’ or ‘the greatest and the most senior/elderly’.

Now consider the following examples:

(a)    Surah Al Baqarah (The Cow) سُوۡرَةُ البَقَرَة

(2:7)

 وَلَهُمۡ عَذَابٌ عَظِيمٌ۬

“Allah has set a seal upon their hearts and upon their hearing, and over their vision is a veil. And for them is a great punishment.”

(b)   Surah Al Waqia (The Inevitable) سُوۡرَةُ الواقِعَة

(56:96)

فَسَبِّحۡ بِٱسۡمِ رَبِّكَ ٱلۡعَظِيمِ

“So exalt the name of your Lord, the Most Great.”

In a certain nation, many a people can be Kabeer كبِير and Akbar اكبر, but only one is Azeem عظِيم.

3.      Zul-jalal ذوَالجلاَل: Jalal جلاَل expresses the esteem, importance and honor in terms of greatness. Zul-jalal ذوَالجلاَل means the magnificence and distinction. Jaleel جلِيل means the one who creates the impressively magnificent creations and things; also deserves the title of qualifying as the majestic and the magnificent for creating the majestic creations.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Rahman (The Beneficent) سُوۡرَةُ الرَّحمٰن

تَبَـٰرَكَ ٱسۡمُ رَبِّكَ ذِى ٱلۡجَلَـٰلِ وَٱلۡإِكۡرَامِ

(55:78)

“Blessed is the name of your Lord, Owner of Majesty and Honor.”

4.      Majeed مجِيد: Majd مجد means to be great in terms of the magnitude of glory, splendor or loftiness. According to Ibn-e-Faris, it defines the one who has reached the boundaries of benevolence, reputation, character, nobility and modesty, and is also reputed great in the eyes of the people.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Burooj (The Mansions of the Stars) سُوۡرَةُ البُرُوج

(85:14-15)

وَهُوَ ٱلۡغَفُورُ ٱلۡوَدُودُ (﻿١٤﻿) ذُو ٱلۡعَرۡشِ ٱلۡمَجِيدُ

“And He is the Forgiving, the Affectionate, Honorable Owner of the Throne,”

 

Conclusion:

(1)   Kabeer كبِير: A common term used to describe ‘the great’. Normally qualifies for things and objects with the attribute of greatness.

(2) Azeem عظِيم: Describes the degree of greatness, but in addition to strength and intensity. Azeem is a higher degree than Akbar, the same way Haqeer is a lower degree than Asghar.

(3)  Zul-jalal ذوَالجلاَل: Describes seniority and greatness in magnificence, distinction and honor. Earns the praise and laudation in regard to the magnificent creations.

(4) Majeed مجِيد: To be grand and senior in terms of glory and splendor, and great in terms of command and domination.
