45. Buland kerna بلند کرنا (Ooncha kerna) اونچا کرنا To raise (To Heighten)

Also known as ‘Rafa’a’ رَفَعَ and ‘Ansha’a’ اَنشَاءَ.

1.      Rafa’a رَفَعَ: It means to rise. However, if this verb is used in the context of lifting something off the ground and raising it, then its antonym becomes ‘wada’a’ وَضَعَ. Furthermore, if the context is regarding the raise in voice, then the antonym becomes ‘Khafada’ خَفَضَ  .

Allah Taa’la says:

(i)                 For lifting/raising something:

Surah Al Baqarah (The Cow) سُوۡرَةُ البَقَرَة

(2:127)

وَإِذۡ يَرۡفَعُ إِبۡرَٲهِـۧمُ ٱلۡقَوَاعِدَ مِنَ ٱلۡبَيۡتِ وَإِسۡمَـٰعِيلُ

“And [mention] when Abraham was raising the foundations of the House and [with him] Ishmael, [saying], "Our Lord, accept [this] from us. Indeed You are the Hearing, the Knowing.”

(ii)               For raising one’s voice:

Surah Al Hujurat (The Rooms) سُوۡرَةُ الحُجرَات

(49:2)

يَـٰٓأَيُّہَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ لَا تَرۡفَعُوٓاْ أَصۡوَٲتَكُمۡ فَوۡقَ صَوۡتِ ٱلنَّبِىِّ

“O you who have believed, do not raise your voices above the voice of the Prophet or be loud to him in speech like the loudness of some of you to others, lest your deeds become worthless while you perceive not.”

2.      Ansha’a اَنشَاءَ: ‘Nasho’ نشو means for something to be lifted and elevated. It also means to raise someone and heighten them in their brought up. ‘Nashaah’ نشاته is used in the meaning of the lift, or the raise. In this context, the point of argument lies at the elevation or the raise. ‘Anshaa’ اَنشَاءَ means to create something, lift it, bring it up, raise it and elevate it.

In the Holy Quran,

Surah Al Rad (The Thunder) سُوۡرَةُ الرّعد

(13:12)

هُوَ ٱلَّذِى يُرِيڪُمُ ٱلۡبَرۡقَ خَوۡفً۬ا وَطَمَعً۬ا وَيُنشِئُ ٱلسَّحَابَ ٱلثِّقَالَ

“It is He who shows you lightening, [causing] fear and aspiration, and generates the heavy clouds.”

 

Conclusion:

(1)   Rafa’a رَفَعَ: To lift or elevate something from the ground, heighten it.

(2) Ansha’a اَنشَاءَ: To create something, raise it and elevate it in brought up.
