76. Futile/To be idle:

 

 

Faragha    فَرَغَ & atala  عَطَلَ are the words used in the Quran.

 

1.        Faragha فَرَغَ : means to be idle. Its opposite is shaghal شَغَل . Meaning, to be idle after finishing some work.

 

Allah says in the Quran: 94:7-8:  Surat Ash-Sharĥ (The Relief) - سورة الشرح

 

فَإِذَا فَرَغْتَ فَانصَبْ وَإِلَىٰ رَبِّكَ فَارْغَب

So when you have finished [your duties], then stand up [for worship]. And to your Lord direct [your] longing. 

 

2.        Atala  عَطَلَ: this means futility of a worker or a female without her ornaments (jewellery). & Attala   عَطَّلَ means to make someone futile or idle.

 

In the Quran: 81:4:  Surat At-Takwīr (The Overthrowing) - سورة التكوير

 

 

وَإِذَا الْعِشَارُ عُطِّلَتْ

 

And when full-term she-camels are neglected

 

 

Summary:

 

Faraghat  فَراغت : means the gap between finishing some work & starting another one. This idle period is occasional(present sometimes & absent at other times). Even if this phase of free period is there, it is temporary.

 & atala  عَطَلَ means  futility or sitting idle because of titubation ( nature??) اضطرارًا
