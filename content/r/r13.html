13 – To Place, Put

 

The words وَضَعَ and القىٰ have been used in the holy Quran

 

13.1 WADA’A (وَضَعَ):  its opposite is رَفَعَ – to pick up or raise up.  Means to carefully put something down.   رفيع is the opposite of وضيع which refers to a person who has no place in society.  حمل وضع refers to unload a burden, whether apparent or hidden.

 

Surat Ash-Sharĥ (The Relief) - الشرح سورة

94:2-3

 وَوَضَعۡنَا عَنكَ وِزۡرَكَ (﻿٢﻿) ٱلَّذِىٓ أَنقَضَ ظَهۡرَكَ (﻿٣﻿)

And We removed from you your burden (2). Which had weighed upon your back (3).

 

 

13: 2 ALQAA (القىٰ): means to put something, in the meaning of carefully and slowly placing something; does not include throwing.  Also includes the notion of placing something which is visible for others to see.

 

Surat An-Naĥl (The Bee) - النحل سورة

16:15

 وَأَلۡقَىٰ فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ رَوَٲسِىَ (﻿١٥﻿)

And He has cast into the earth firmly set mountains (15)

 

Summary:

1.      وَضَعَ: to place something whether visible or not

2.      القىٰ: to place something that is visible to others
