3- RAH-RAASTA [Way Path]

Qur’an has referred them by the words SIRAAT   (صراط )  TAREEQ   (طريق )  SABEEL (سبيل ) FAJH  (  فج ) IMAAM  (امام ) HUDA  (هدى ) NAJD  ( نجد ) AND SABAB   (سبب ).

Page-535

 

3.1   TAREEQ   (طريق ) : This word is used for all types of paths. This word is also used in both physical and spiritual terms. A physical example is this.

Surat Ţāhā (Ta-Ha) - سورة طه  20:77

  فَاضْرِبْ لَهُمْ طَرِيْقًا فِي الْبَحْرِ يَبَسًا    

And strike for them a dry path through the sea.

 

And Spiritual example is this.

Surat An-Nisā' (The Women) - سورة النساء 4:168:169

           اِلَّا طَرِيْقَ جَهَنَّمَ وَلَا لِيَهْدِيَهُمْ طَرِيْقًا            

Nor will He guide them to a path Except the path of Hell.

 

3.2   SIRAAT   (صراط ): SIRAAT   (صراط ) is called as long and sharp sword and the path that will be made to cross the Jahanam, which is normally called as PULLSIRAAT (پلصراط) It is described with same attribute that it will be sharper than sword and thinner than hair. Hence SIRAAT   (صراط ) is the path that is to be passed with extreme care and around which are many dangers. The way of crossing this path with extreme care is named as TAQWA: This word of SIRAAT   (صراط ) is used in both physical and spiritual terms. Its concept in literal terms is generally the path of guidance. And according to Abu-Hilaal, SIRAAT   (صراط ) is called as an easy path. Allah says:

Surat Al-Fātiĥah (The Opener) - سورة الفاتحة 1:4

  اِھْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَـقِيْمَ

  Guide us to the straight path.

 

  And an example of its usage in physical terms is this:

Surat Al-'A`rāf (The Heights) - سورة الأعراف 7:86

   وَلَا تَقْعُدُوْا بِكُلِّ صِرَاطٍ تُوْعِدُوْنَ وَتَصُدُّوْنَ عَنْ سَبِيْلِ اللّٰهِ  

And do not sit on every path, threatening and averting from the way of Allah those who believe in Him.

3.3   SABEEL (سبيل ): Every path on which one can walk easily. IBNESABEEL (ابن اسبیل) means traveller and ABARISABEEL (عابری سبیل) means passer and SABEEL (سبيل ) means wide road. ASBALATAREEQ (اسبل اطریق) means the route that has great traffic and ASBALADAMAAWALMATR (اسبل ادمع و المطر) means continuous flow of tears or continuous rain shower. Hence SABEEL (سبيل ) is called as the path that has the most traffic. Allah says to honey bee:

Surat An-Naĥl (The Bee) - سورة النحل 16:69

  فَاسْلُكِيْ سُبُلَ رَبِّكِ ذُلُلًا

And follow the ways of your Lord laid down [for you].

                                      Here Subul (سبل) denotes that natural guidance which Allah has installed in everything. And on which one can move easily and all move equally. This word is also used in both physical and spiritual terms. The above example is the spiritual form and an example of physical form is this: Allah says:

Surat Al-Ĥijr (The Rocky Tract) - سورة الحجر 15:76

  وَاِنَّهَا لَبِسَبِيْلٍ مُّقِيْمٍ

And indeed, those cities are [situated] on an established road.

Page-536

Now see that Allah has used 3 words TAREEQ   (طريق ) SABEEL (سبيل ) and SABEEL (سبيل ) in Qur’an for this “path of guidance”. TAREEQ   (طريق ) is in the sense that TAREEQ   (طريق ) is a common word that is used for any route. SIRAAT   (صراط ) is in the sense that it is encircled in the limits and restrictions of commands & prohibitions. And SABEEL (سبيل ) is in the sense that when a person starts walking on this path with determination then Shariat has taken account of all conveniences. And the number of people walking on it is also enough.

 

3.4   FAJH  (  فج ): FAJATUN (فجة ) is the opening between two mountains and FAJWA (فجوة ) is the field between two mountains and FAJAH (فج ) is that open path who connects two mountains after passing through this field. It is in Qur’an:

 

Surat Al-Ĥaj (The Pilgrimage) - سورة الحج 22:27

 وَاَذِّنْ فِي النَّاسِ بِالْحَجِّ يَاْتُوْكَ رِجَالًا وَّعَلٰي كُلِّ ضَامِرٍ يَّاْتِيْنَ مِنْ كُلِّ فَجٍّ عَمِيْقٍ           

 And proclaim to the people the Hajj [pilgrimage]; they will come to you on foot and on every lean camel; they will come from every distant pass.

                                 

3.5   IMAAM  (امام ): means leader and means highway, a wide and long road. It is in Qur’an

Surat Al-Ĥijr (The Rocky Tract) - سورة الحجر 15:79

  فَانْتَقَمْنَا مِنْهُمْ ۘ وَاِنَّهُمَا لَبِاِمَامٍ مُّبِيْنٍ  

So We took retribution from them, and indeed, both [cities] are on a clear highway.

 

3.6   NAJD  ( نجد ): means difficult pass and the path that climbs to the top altitude or comes down from the top. Allah says

Surat Al-Balad (The City) - سورة البلد 90:10:11

  فَلَا اقْتَحَمَ الْعَقَبَةَ وَهَدَيْنٰهُ النَّجْدَيْنِ

And have shown him the two ways But he has not broken through the difficult pass.

3.7   HUDA  (هدى ): means Guidance and Path. If a forgotten or lost person finds the right path then it will be called Huda:  It is in Qur’an

Surat Ţāhā (Ta-Ha) - سورة طه 20:10

  اِنِّىْٓ اٰنَسْتُ نَارًا لَّعَلِّيْٓ اٰتِيْكُمْ مِّنْهَا بِقَبَسٍ اَوْ اَجِدُ عَلَي  النَّارِ هُدًى  

I have perceived a fire; perhaps I can bring you a torch or find at the fire some guidance.

3.8   SABAB   (سبب ): means Source, rope, path. The path and the resources to pass it. Travel supplies  It is in Qur’an

Surat Al-Kahf (The Cave) - سورة الكهف 18:89

   ثُمَّ اَتْـبَـعَ سَبَبًا      

Then he followed a way.

 

(Surat-Momin) (True Believer) -  سورة مومن 40:36

  وَقَالَ فِرْعَوْنُ يٰهَامٰنُ ابْنِ لِيْ صَرْحًا لَّعَلِّيْٓ اَبْلُغُ الْاَسْبَابَ         

And Pharaoh said, "O Haman, construct for me a tower that I might reach the ways.

Page-537

 

Summary:

3.1          TAREEQ: Common word for path.

3.2          SIRAAT:  The path where one has to walk carefully.

3.3          SABEEL: The path where one can walk easily and there is high traffic.

3.4          FAJH: Wide path between two mountains.

3.5            IMAAM: highway, wide road.

3.6          NAJD: The path to climb and descend from a difficult pass.

3.7          HUDAA: If a lost forgotten person finds the right path.

3.8          SABAB: Path with supplies and resources.
