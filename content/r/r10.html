10 – Relatives

 

The words اَقرَبُونَ, نَسَبَ. صِهر, الاَرحَام اُولُو and اِلّ have been used in the holy Quran

 

10.1 AQRABUN (اَقرَبُونَ): قَرُبَ means to be near, to be close; this word can be used with reference to time, distance, status, relations and in any other context.  اَقرَبَ refers to being more close, more near.  The words اَقرَبُونَ, اَقرَبِين, ذَاقُربىٰ and القُربىٰ ذَوِي are used especially in the context of close relatives.  Close relatives are those for whom Allah has fixed a share in inheritance, such as children, parents, spouses and siblings.

 

Surat An-Nisā' (The Women) - النساء سورة

4:7

 لِّلرِّجَالِ نَصِيبٌ۬ مِّمَّا تَرَكَ ٱلۡوَٲلِدَانِ وَٱلۡأَقۡرَبُونَ (﻿٧﻿)

For men is a share of what the parents and close relatives leave (7)

 

10.2 NASAB (نَسَب):  يَنسُبُ نَسَبَ means to relate lineage or to get information about it.  اِستَنسَبَ is to relate lineage, to inquire about it.  نَسَب and اِنسَاب are in the meaning of relationship, relatedness. نَسَب can be used to refer only to relatives from the father’s side because of patrilineal descent rules.  According to Imam Ragib, نَسَب can be refer to any one side of a person’s relatives, for example, either the mother’s side (including maternal grandparents, aunts and uncles) or the father’s side (including paternal grandparents, aunts and uncles).

 

Surat Al-Mu'minūn (The Believers) - المؤمنون سورة

23:101

 فَإِذَا نُفِخَ فِى ٱلصُّورِ فَلَآ أَنسَابَ بَيۡنَهُمۡ يَوۡمَٮِٕذٍ۬ َ (﻿١٠١﻿)

So when the Horn is blown, no relationship will there be among them that Day (101)  

 

 

10.3 SIHR (صِهر): refers to in-laws or relatedness which arises because of marriage.  For the wife, the lineal relatives of the husband and for the husband the lineal relatives of the wife are in-laws.

 

Surat Al-Furqān (The Criterian) - الفرقان سورة

25:54

 وَهُوَ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ مِنَ ٱلۡمَآءِ بَشَرً۬ا فَجَعَلَهُ ۥ نَسَبً۬ا وَصِهۡرً۬ا‌ (﻿٥٤﻿)

And it is He who has created from water a human being and made him [a relative by] lineage and marriage (54)

 

10.4 ULUL ARHAAM (الاَرحَام اُولُو): literally it means relatives because of the womb; this encompasses all of the blood relatives from both the mother and father’s side.

 

Surat Al-'Anfāl (The Spoils of War) - الأنفال سورة

8:75

 وَأُوْلُواْ ٱلۡأَرۡحَامِ بَعۡضُہُمۡ أَوۡلَىٰ بِبَعۡضٍ۬ فِى كِتَـٰبِ ٱللَّهِ  (﻿٧٥﻿)

But those of [blood] relationship are more entitled [to inheritance] in the decree of Allah (75)

 

10.5 ILLA (اِلّ): often translated as nearness or relatedness but linguistically it refers to promises or contracts made to relatives.  اِلّ refers to an oath.  The phrase الرَّجُل اِلَّ means جَارَبِهِ - ‘he became his neighbor.

 

Surat At-Tawbah (The Repentance) - التوبة سورة

9:10

 لَا يَرۡقُبُونَ فِى مُؤۡمِنٍ إِلاًّ۬ وَلَا ذِمَّةً۬‌ (﻿١٠﻿)

They do not observe toward a believer any pact of kinship or covenant of protection (10)

 

Summary:

1.      اَقرَبُونَ: close relatives which have a share in inheritance

2.      نَسَب: relatives from the father’s side

3.      صِهر: in-laws

4.      الاَرحَام : relatives related to the womb, encompasses most relatives

5.      اِلّ: contracts made to relatives or neighbors
