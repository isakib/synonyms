8 – Rubbish, Trash

 

The words نَكِدَ, خَمطَ, دَاحِضَة, نَاقص, بَخس, خَبِيث, اَدنىٰ and مَزجٰة have been used in the holy Quran

 

8.1 NAKIDA (نَكِدَ): refers to something that lacks goodness or good qualities - الخير قليل.   According to Imam Ragib, it refers to anything that is attained by a seeker after great difficulty.  The phrase نكداء ناقة refers to a she-camel that gives very little milk and is also difficult to milk.  نَكِدَ means to prevent someone from attaining their needs, to deprive or to give less. الرجل نكد refers to a person who asks much and lacks goodness.  نَكِدَ is something which is attained after difficulty, is very little in quantity, and lacks goodness; something that is little and is rubbish.

 

Surat Al-'A`rāf (The Heights) -  الأعراف سورة

7:58

وَٱلَّذِى خَبُثَ لَا يَخۡرُجُ إِلَّا نَكِدً۬ا‌ۚ (﻿٥٨﻿)

 But that which is bad - nothing emerges except sparsely, with difficulty (58)

 

8.2 KHAMTA (خَمطَ): every sour or bitter thing; trees which bear little fruit; a tree which has long thorns.

 

Surat Saba' (Sheba) - سبإ سورة

34:16

وَبَدَّلۡنَـٰهُم بِجَنَّتَيۡہِمۡ جَنَّتَيۡنِ ذَوَاتَىۡ أُڪُلٍ خَمۡطٍ۬ وَأَثۡلٍ۬ وَشَىۡءٍ۬ مِّن سِدۡرٍ۬ قَلِيلٍ۬ (﻿١٦﻿)

We replaced their two [fields of] gardens with gardens of bitter fruit, tamarisks and something of sparse lote trees (16)

 

8.3 DAHIDAH (دَاحِضَة): دَحَضَ refers to fling one’s feet like an animal that is being slaughtered.  The phrase الحُجَّة دَحَضَ refers to an argument being nullified or proven false.  اَدحَضَ refers to making an argument invalid and دَحضَ refers to a slippery place.

 

Surat Ash-Shūraá (The Consultation) - الشورى سورة

42:16

 وَٱلَّذِينَ يُحَآجُّونَ فِى ٱللَّهِ مِنۢ بَعۡدِ مَا ٱسۡتُجِيبَ لَهُ ۥ حُجَّتُهُمۡ دَاحِضَةٌ عِندَ رَبِّہِمۡ (﻿١٦﻿)

And those who argue concerning Allah after He has been responded to - their argument is invalid with their Lord (16)

 

8.4 NAAQS (نَاقص): refers to something incomplete.  The phrase ناقصٌ درهمٌ refers to a dirham which is less weighty or deficient.  نقص means lacking and can also be used in place of loss.

 

Surat Al-'A`rāf (The Heights) -  الأعراف سورة

7:130

 وَلَقَدۡ أَخَذۡنَآ ءَالَ فِرۡعَوۡنَ بِٱلسِّنِينَ وَنَقۡصٍ۬ مِّنَ ٱلثَّمَرَٲتِ (﻿١٣٠﻿)

 And We certainly seized the people of Pharaoh with years of famine and a deficiency in fruits (130)

 

8.5 BAKHS (بَخس): refers to something deficient, of poor quality and inferior.

 

Surat Yūsuf (Joseph) - يوسف سورة

12:20

 وَشَرَوۡهُ بِثَمَنِۭ بَخۡسٍ۬ دَرَٲهِمَ مَعۡدُودَةٍ۬ َ (﻿٢٠﻿)

And they sold him for a reduced price - a few dirhams (20)

 

8.6 KHABEETH (خَبِيث): خَبُثَ refers to impure and filthy, something that is rubbish and useless.  Something that is disliked, faulty and soiled.  Its opposite is طيب - pure.

 

Surat An-Nisā' (The Women) - النساء سورة

4:2

وَلَا تَتَبَدَّلُواْ ٱلۡخَبِيثَ بِٱلطَّيِّبِ‌ۖ (﻿٢﻿)

And do not substitute the defective [of your own] for the good [of theirs] (2)

 

8.7 ADNAA (اَدنىٰ): its opposite is اَعلىٰ – high.  This word refers to anything that is lacking, low and deficient when compared to something else.        

 

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - البقرة سورة

2:61

قَالَ أَتَسۡتَبۡدِلُونَ ٱلَّذِى هُوَ أَدۡنَىٰ بِٱلَّذِى هُوَ خَيۡرٌ‌ (﻿٦١﻿)

 [Moses] said, "Would you exchange what is better for what is less? (61)

 

8.9 MAZJAAH (مَزجٰة): زَجَا and اَزجَىٰ refer to return, to shun or reject something while مُزجَىٰ

( feminine مزجاة) refers to something that is little in quantity and is trash.  So the word refers to something that is returned because no one is willing to accept it.

 

Surat Yūsuf (Joseph) - يوسف سورة

12:88

 قَالُواْ يَـٰٓأَيُّہَا ٱلۡعَزِيزُ مَسَّنَا وَأَهۡلَنَا ٱلضُّرُّ وَجِئۡنَا بِبِضَـٰعَةٍ۬ مُّزۡجَٮٰةٍ۬ فَأَوۡفِ لَنَا ٱلۡكَيۡلَ (﻿٨٨﻿)

So when they entered upon Joseph, they said, "O 'Azeez, adversity has touched us and our family, and we have come with goods poor in quality, but give us full measure (88)

 

Summary:

1.      نَكِدَ: something that is little in quantity, is difficult to attain and is discarded

2.      خَمطَ: something bitter, repugnant and distasteful

3.      دَاحِضَة: futile, indecent and false

4.      نَاقص: incomplete and defective

5.      بَخس:  of poor quality and is junk

6.      خَبِيث: dirty, impure or something impermissible

7.      اَدنىٰ: something that is deficient in comparison to something else

8.      مَزجٰة:  something so rejected that no one is willing to accept it
