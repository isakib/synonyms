12 – To Incline

 

The words رَغِبَ and تَنَافَسَ have been used in the holy Quran

 

12.1 RAGIBA (رَغِبَ): to incline/slant or to be inclined; to have the desire to attain something in one’s heart and to endeavor to attain it.

 

Surat Ash-Sharĥ (The Relief) - الشرح سورة

94:7-8

 فَإِذَا فَرَغۡتَ فَٱنصَبۡ (﻿٧﻿) وَإِلَىٰ رَبِّكَ فَٱرۡغَب (﻿٨﻿)

So when you have finished [your duties], then stand up [for worship] (7).  And to your Lord direct [your] longing (8).

 

12.2 TANAFUS (تَنَافَسَ): نَفِسَ means to envy while نَفَّسَ means to recommend something.  تنافس means to be proud mutually, to incline in competition.  To compete with esteem in inclining towards virtuous deeds.

 

Surat Al-Muţaffifīn (The Defrauding) - المطففين سورة

83: 25-6

 يُسۡقَوۡنَ مِن رَّحِيقٍ۬ مَّخۡتُومٍ (﻿٢٥﻿) خِتَـٰمُهُ ۥ مِسۡكٌ۬‌ۚ وَفِى ذَٲلِكَ فَلۡيَتَنَافَسِ ٱلۡمُتَنَـٰفِسُونَ (﻿٢٦﻿)

They will be given to drink [pure] wine [which was] sealed (25).  The last of it is musk. So for this let the competitors compete (26).

 

Summary:

رَغِبَ is commonly used for inclination/desire while تَنَافَسَ is used for competing in going towards virtuous deeds because of desire for them.
