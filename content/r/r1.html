1- RAAT [Night]

 

Qur’an has referred them by the words LAIL (ليل ) and BAYAAT (بيات ).

 

 

1.1   LAIL  (لیل ): night “opposite of Day” is a known word. The time from sunset till sunrise. And in Qur’an, there are many occasions where LAIL AND NIHAAR (ليل و نهار ) are used together. The night is divided into 12 times or 12 hours. These names of these times are written in order as: SHAFAQ (شفق ) GHASAK (غسق ) DUQA (دقة ) FAHMA (فحمة ) ZULFA (زلفة ) ZALUQA (زلقة ) BEHRA (بحرة ) SAHER (سحر ) FAJAR (فجر ) SUBAH (صبح ) SABAH (صباح ).

1.2   BAYATUN  (بیاتا ): BAAT(بات )  means to spend night, spending of night-time.  It is in Qur’an

Surat Al-Furqān (The Criterian) - سورة الفرقان 25:64

فوَالَّذِيْنَ يَبِيْتُوْنَ لِرَبِّهِمْ سُجَّدًا وَّقِيَامًا

And those who spend [part of] the night to their Lord prostrating and standing [in prayer].

And BAYATUN  (بیاتا ) means any time during the night, when sleeping, when dreaming. It is in Qur’an

 

Surat Yūnus (Jonah) - سورة يونس 10:50

 قُلْ اَرَءَيْتُمْ اِنْ اَتٰىكُمْ عَذَابُهٗ بَيَاتًا اَوْ نَهَارًا

 Say, "Have you considered: if His punishment should come to you by night or by day.
