9 – Rope

 

The words حَبل and سَبَب have been used in the holy book.

 

9.1 HABL (حَبل): [plural – حبال] means a rope or something that resembles it.  The phrase

الوريد حبل refers to the jugular vein.  الصيّاد حبالة refers to the rope used by a hunter.  This word is used commonly.

 

Surat Al-Masad (The Palm Fiber, Flame) - المسد سورة

111:5

 فِى جِيدِهَا حَبۡلٌ۬ مِّن مَّسَدِۭ (﻿٥﻿)

Around her neck is a rope of [twisted] fiber (5)

 

9.2 SABAB (سَبَب): a rope which is used to climb a tree, a rope which is used in travel, as a travel essential or as a tool to tie travel luggage.

 

Surat Al-Ĥaj (The Pilgrimage) - الحج سورة

22:15

فَلۡيَمۡدُدۡ بِسَبَبٍ إِلَى ٱلسَّمَآءِ ثُمَّ لۡيَقۡطَعۡ فَلۡيَنظُرۡ هَلۡ يُذۡهِبَنَّ كَيۡدُهُ ۥ مَا يَغِيظُ (﻿١٥﻿)

Let him extend a rope to the ceiling, then cut off [his breath], and let him see: will his effort remove that which enrages [him]? (15)

 

 

Summary:

                HABL (حَبل) is used for any kind of rope while SABAB (سَبَب) is used to describe a rope which is used for climbing or for a rope used in travel.
