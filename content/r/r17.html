17 ROKNA [to stop, to forbid]

Synonyms: MANA, NAHA, AWWAQA, ADAL, AMSAKA, SADDA, AHSARA, HADARA, AKAFA, SABBATA, KAFFA, ZAADA, WAZ’A, HABASA, HAJARA

17.1 MANA’A (مَنَعَ): basically has two meanings,

1. To be ungenerous (and withhold) (opposite of ATTA’ عطا ). MANNAA’ (مَنَّاع) means a miser.

2. To forbid someone (either verbally or physically) to do something. Become a hindrance in some work. To deprive of. or to stop (dictionary Al Manjad) .In the Quran:

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ 2:114

وَمَنۡ أَظۡلَمُ مِمَّن مَّنَعَ مَسَـٰجِدَ ٱللَّهِ أَن يُذۡكَرَ فِيہَا ٱسۡمُهُ

Who is more unjust than him who forbids the remembrance of God’s name in places of worship

Page – 550

17.2 NAHA (نَھٰی): mostly means to restrain from doing sinful activities. Allahﷻ says in the Quran

Surah An-Nazi'at (Those who drag forth): سورۃ النَّازعَات 79:40

وَأَمَّا مَنۡ خَافَ مَقَامَ رَبِّهِۦ وَنَهَى ٱلنَّفۡسَ عَنِ ٱلۡهَوَىٰ

But as for him who feared the Standing of his Lord, and restrained the self from desires.

17.3 AWWAQA (عَوَّقّ): AL-‘AWQA WAL-‘OOQ (اَلْعَوْق وَاْلعُوْق) means a devious person, a person who stops others from good deeds. (dictionary Al Manjad) AWWAQA (عَوّق) also refers to a person who stops people from performing good deeds. In the Quran

Suran Al-Ahzaab (The clans): سورۃ الأحزَاب 33:18

قَدۡ يَعۡلَمُ ٱللَّهُ ٱلۡمُعَوِّقِينَ مِنكُمۡ وَٱلۡقَآٮِٕلِينَ لِإِخۡوَٲنِهِمۡ هَلُمَّ إِلَيۡنَا*ۖ

God already knows the hinderers among you, and those who say to their brethren, “Come and join us.”

17.4 ‘ADALA (عَضَلَ): to create hindrance, to stop vehemently. ‘ADALAHU (عضلھ) means a ligament and ‘ADDALA (عَضَّلَ) means to cause obstruction in some objective (dictionary Al Manjad). Usually refers to keeping a man from marrying (a woman), to cause distress in order to get personal gains. In the Quran

Surah An-Nisa (The women): سورۃ النِّسا 4:19

وَلَا تَعۡضُلُوهُنَّ لِتَذۡهَبُواْ بِبَعۡضِ مَآ ءَاتَيۡتُمُوهُنَّ

And do not coerce them in order to take away some of what you had given them,

17.5 AMSAKA (اَمْسَك): means to adhere to something and protect it, generally used when a person has the possession of something and refuses to relinquish it. MUMISSK (مُمِسّك) also signifies a miser. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ 2:231

فَأَمۡسِكُوهُنَّ بِمَعۡرُوفٍ أَوۡ سَرِّحُوهُنَّ بِمَعۡرُوفٍ۬*ۚ

ٓAnd they have reached their term, either retain them amicably, or release them amicably.

17.6 SADDA (صَدَّ): means to bar or to stop, SADDA’AN (صَدَّعَنْ) means to forbid, to prevent someone leniently. According to Ibn-al-Faris “ المیل اِلٰی اَحَدِ ألْجَانِبیْنِ وَعَدَلَ عَنْھُ” and “صَدّ َبمعنئ منع عن قصدالشیء خاصۃ” translation: to stop someone from an objective or intent, in other words to prohibit someone leniently. In the Quran:

Surat An-Nahl (The Bee): سورۃ النّحل16:94

وَتَذُوقُواْ ٱلسُّوٓءَ بِمَا صَدَدتُّمۡ عَن سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ*ۖ

You taste misery because you hindered from God’s path,

17.7 AHSARA (اَحْصَرَ): is to circumvent or to surround, to narrow down a siege. HISAAR (حِصَارَ) means castle (or a fort). And HAASARA(حَاصَرَ) means to besiege (and blockade) (dictionary Al Manjad). Allah says in the Quran:

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ 2:273

لِلۡفُقَرَآءِ ٱلَّذِينَ أُحۡصِرُواْ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ لَا يَسۡتَطِيعُونَ ضَرۡبً۬ا فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ

It is for the poor; those who are restrained in the way of God, and unable to travel in the land.

Page 551

17.8 HADHARA (حَظَرَ): put a barricade around something in order to protect it. Pile up something and fortify it. In the Quran

Surah Al-Israa (The Night Journey): سورۃ الٕاسرَاء 17-20

وَمَا كَانَ عَطَآءُ رَبِّكَ مَحۡظُورًا

And the bounties of your Lord are not restricted.

17.9 AKAFA (عَکَفَ): means to pay attention to something with respect and to remain associated with it. To tie in or to be imprisoned. In the Quran

Surah Al-Fath (The victory): سورۃ الفَتْح 48:25

هُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُواْ وَصَدُّوڪُمۡ عَنِ ٱلۡمَسۡجِدِ ٱلۡحَرَامِ وَٱلۡهَدۡىَ مَعۡكُوفًا أَن يَبۡلُغَ مَحِلَّهُ

It is they who disbelieved, and barred you from the Sacred Mosque, and prevented the offering from reaching its destination.

17.10 KAFFA (کَفَّ ): KAFF(کَفّ) means a hand palm. And KAFFA (کَفَّ) means to obstruct an attack using ones’ palm; or to defend. Later on this word started to be used in reference to defending or impeding an attack. In the Quran

Surah Al-Maeda (The table spread): سورۃ المَائدہ 5:11

إِذۡ هَمَّ قَوۡمٌ أَن يَبۡسُطُوٓاْ إِلَيۡكُمۡ أَيۡدِيَهُمۡ فَكَفَّ أَيۡدِيَهُمۡ عَنڪُمۡ*ۖ

When certain people intended to extend their hands against you, and He restrained their hands from you.

17.11 THABBATA (ثَبَّطَ): to delay a matter and keep holding it up; or to be indolent. ATHBATA-AL-MARAD(اَثْبَطُ الْمَرْض) means a disease or malady that is contracted by a person and does not withdraw (dictionary Al Manjad). THABBATAL-MARD(ثْبَّطُ الْمَرْض) implies an illness that restrains a person (dictionary Al Manjad), thereby unable to perform any task, or due to indolence or delay. In the Quran Allah says

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:46

وَلَوۡ أَرَادُواْ ٱلۡخُرُوجَ لَأَعَدُّواْ لَهُ ۥ عُدَّةً۬ وَلَـٰكِن ڪَرِهَ ٱللَّهُ ٱنۢبِعَاثَهُمۡ فَثَبَّطَهُمۡ

Had they wanted to mobilize, they would have made preparations for it; but God disliked their participation, so he held them back,

17.12 ZAADA (ذَادَ): to outdistance or outstrip, to prevent from coming near or to hold back (dictionary Al Manjad). ZAWWADA-‘AN-HASBIHI (ذَوَّدَعَنْ حَسْبِھِ ) when a person supports and defends (his posessions). Allah Says in the Quran

Surah Al Qasas (The stories): سورۃ القَصَص 28:23

وَوَجَدَ مِن دُونِهِمُ ٱمۡرَأَتَيۡنِ تَذُودَانِ*ۖ

And besides them he found two women who were keeping back (their flocks).

17.13 WAZA’ (وَزَعَ): means to forbid or stop. WAZA’-AL-JAISH (وَزَعَ الْجَیش) means to organise an army into groups. AL-AWAZA’ (الاوزع) means groups, it has no singular. ALWAZA’TUH (الْوَزْعَۃُ) (plural WAAZI’U وَازِعُ) means helpers (supporters) of a ruler (dictionary Al Manjad). So WAZA’ (وَزَعَ) means to forbid someone in maintaining order and regulation. In the Quran:

Surah An-Naml (The ant): سورۃ النَّمل 27:17

وَحُشِرَ لِسُلَيۡمَـٰنَ جُنُودُهُ ۥ مِنَ ٱلۡجِنِّ وَٱلۡإِنسِ وَٱلطَّيۡرِ فَهُمۡ يُوزَعُونَ

To the service of Solomon were mobilized his troops of sprites, and men, and birds—all held in strict order.

PAGE 552

17.14 HABASA (حَبَسَ): To enslave, or guard something stringently or to forbid (dictionary Al Manjad). To stop someone from getting up. Allah says in the Quran.

Surah Al-Maeda (The table spread): سورۃ المَائدہ 5:106

تَحۡبِسُونَهُمَا مِنۢ بَعۡدِ ٱلصَّلَوٰةِ

detain them both after prayer,

17.15 HIJRAN (حِجْرًا): HAJARA (حَجَرَ) means a stone and HIJARAN-MAHJOORAN (حِجْرًامَھْجُوْرًا) is an idiom that means a strong obstruction that cannot be cleared. During the times of ignorance, whenever a dreaded or feared person was confronted one would say HIJARANMAHJOORAN (حِجْرًامَھْجُوْرًا) (we seek refuge from you) and on hearing these words the enemy would bring no harm to the person. This idiom is also used in the Quran:

Surah Al-Furqaan (The criterion): سورۃ الفُرْقان 25:22

يَوۡمَ يَرَوۡنَ ٱلۡمَلَـٰٓٮِٕكَةَ لَا بُشۡرَىٰ يَوۡمَٮِٕذٍ۬ لِّلۡمُجۡرِمِينَ وَيَقُولُونَ حِجۡرً۬ا مَّحۡجُورً۬ا

On the Day when they see the angels—there will be no good news for sinners on that Day; and they will say, “A protective refuge.”

Summary:

17.1 MANA’: to stop verbally or physically.

17.2 NAHAA: restrain from doing sinful activities.

17.3 AWWAQA: to stop from good deeds.

17.4 ADALA: refrain from something in order to gain some personal benefit.

17.5 AMSAKA: refusing to relinquish something in possession.

17.6 SADDA: leniently (and gradually) stop someone from an objective or intent.

17.7 AHSARA: refrain by besieging and constricting.

17.8 HADARA: barricade

17.9 AKAFA: refrain with respect.

17.10 KAFFA: Defend oneself or impede an attack.

17.11 THABBATA: stopping due to indolence or delay.

17.12 ZAADA: outdistance or outstrip, to hold back.

17.13 WAZAA’: forbid someone in maintain order and regulation.

17.14 HABASA: stop someone from getting up or leaving.

17.15 HAJARAN-MAHJOORAN: an idiom, invocation to avoid harm.
