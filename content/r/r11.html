11 – Approval; Satisfaction

 

The words رِضوَان, مَرضَاة and وَجه have been used in the holy Quran

 

11.1 RIDWAAN (رِضوَان); MARDAAH ( مَرضَاة):  رَضِيَ is commonly used for happiness or pleasure, from it the words رِضوَان, رَضوَان and  مَرضَاة are derived.  These terms are infinitive nouns used in the meaning of approval, satisfaction and liking.

 

Surat Al-Mā'idah (The Table Spread) - المائدة سورة

5:2

يَبۡتَغُونَ فَضۡلاً۬ مِّن رَّبِّہِمۡ وَرِضۡوَٲنً۬ا‌ۚ (﻿٢﻿)

[violate the safety of] those coming to the Sacred House seeking bounty from their Lord and [His] approval (2)

 

And in another place the Almighty says:

 

Surat At-Taĥrīm (The Prohibtiion) - التحريم سورة

66:1

يَـٰٓأَيُّہَا ٱلنَّبِىُّ لِمَ تُحَرِّمُ مَآ أَحَلَّ ٱللَّهُ لَكَ‌ۖ تَبۡتَغِى مَرۡضَاتَ أَزۡوَٲجِكَ‌ۚ (﻿١﻿)

O Prophet, why do you prohibit [yourself from] what Allah has made lawful for you, seeking the approval of your wives? (1)

 

11.2 WAJH (وَجه):  literally the face/countenance, direction or front side or the side that comes first, used for approval.  The phrase النَّهَار وَجهُ refers to the first part of the day.  Turning the face away from another is a sign of disapproval while turning one’s face towards another is a sign of satisfaction and attentiveness.  To seek approval is to seek ‘the face’; this can be done by doing what leads to satisfaction and avoiding what leads to dissatisfaction.

 

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - البقرة سورة

2: 272

 وَمَا تُنفِقُونَ إِلَّا ٱبۡتِغَآءَ وَجۡهِ ٱللَّهِ‌ۚ (﻿٢٧٢﻿)

And you do not spend except seeking the countenance of Allah (272)

 

Summary:

رِضوَان and مَرضَاة are used only for approval while وَجه also includes attention along with approval.
