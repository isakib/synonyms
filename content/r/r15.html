15 : LIGHT _ LUSTRE _ LIGHTEN,ILLUMINATE

for light following words are used :

انار (light) , اضاء (lighting), نار (flame , light), جلى ,تجلى (glorification) , وهج (flame, blaze , heat , glare ) اشرق (sunrise, rdiate ,shine) اسفر (brighten, uncover) ,ابصر(sight, vision, eyes)

1انار : نور in sense of light which irradiate the things ,diffusing light which help in seeing things .

نور light is used in both corporeal and incorporeal meanings

as Allah says in quran (sorah az-zummar # 22)

what is he whose heart Allah has open for islam so that he is in light from his lord.

(in this verse noor means both righteous path and enlightened heart)

there are three types of نور light :

1 light of bright irradiated things: like sun , moon, stars, lamp, which is essential to see substantial (material) things and without this light human beings cannot see the things around

2 eyesight : in absence of eyesight the light of bright irradiated things is of no use

3 light of revelation (inspiration) وحى or faith : in absence of this light man cannot get the benifits of guidence

as man cant see material things without eyes similarly teachings of Allah does'nt work on blind hearted person

Surah Ash-Shams (The Sun): سورۃ الشمس 91:3

وَٱلنَّہَارِ إِذَا جَلَّٮٰهَا

By the Day as it shows up (the Sun´s) glory

And JALWAH (جَلْوَۃ) refers to when a woman dresses up and presents herself to the husband. The word ISTAJLA (اِسْتَجْلٰی) means when a bride adorns herself and reveals to her husband. AL AJLA (الاجلی) means bright, (as in) someone with a bright and lovely face. TAJJALA (تجلّٰی) means manifestation of something bright and pleasing. In the Quran

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:143

فَلَمَّا تَجَلَّىٰ رَبُّهُ ۥ لِلۡجَبَلِ جَعَلَهُ ۥ دَڪًّ۬ا

But when his Lord manifested Himself to the mountain, He turned it into dust,

15.5 WAHHAJA (وھَّجَ): AL WAHJU (اَلْوَھْجُ)means blazing fire, kindling of the sun or fire. Radiance and heat. In the Quran

Surah An-Naba: سورۃ النّبَإ 78:12

وَجَعَلۡنَا سِرَاجً۬ا وَهَّاجً۬ا وَبَنَيۡنَا فَوۡقَكُمۡ سَبۡعً۬ا شِدَادً۬ا

And built above you seven strong ones? And placed a blazing lamp?

15.6 ASHRAQA (اَشْرَقَ): AL ISHRAAQ (الاشْرَاق) is a known word that means daybreak. And ASHRAQATIL SHAMSU (اَشْرَقَتِ الشَّمْسُ) means when the sun completely rises after the dawn. In the Quran

Surah Az-Zumar (The groups): سورۃ الزُّمَر 39:69

وَأَشۡرَقَتِ ٱلۡأَرۡضُ بِنُورِ رَبِّہَا

And the earth will shine with the Light of its Lord

15.7 ASFARA (اَسْفَرَ): SAFARA (سَفَرَ) means to reveal the face (of a woman), or to unveil. SAFARA-AN-AL-WAJH (سَفَرَعَنِ الْوَجْہ) means revealing of the face. ASFAR-AL-WAJH(اَسْفَرَالْوَجْہ) means the brightness or beauty of the face. ASFAR-AS-SUBH (اَسْفَرَالصُّبْح) means the lightening up of dawn or the morning aurora. In the Quran

Surah Al-Muddaththir (The Cloaked One): سورۃ المدَّثّر 74:33

وَٱلَّيۡلِ إِذۡ أَدۡبَرَ وَٱلصُّبۡحِ إِذَآ أَسۡفَرَ

And the night as it retreats. And the morning as it lights up.

Another reference in Quran

Surah Abasa (He frowned): سورۃعَبَسَ80:38

وُجُوهٌ۬ يَوۡمَٮِٕذٍ۬ مُّسۡفِرَةٌ۬ ضَاحِكَةٌ۬ مُّسۡتَبۡشِرَةٌ۬

Faces on that Day will be radiant. Laughing and rejoicing.

15.8 ABSARA (اَبْصَرَ): BASARA (بَصَرَ) mean to see (and to have insight). And MUBSIR (مُبْصِر) means to illuminate to an extent that everything is clearly visible. In the Quran

Surah Yunus (Jonas): سورۃ یُونس 10:67

هُوَ ٱلَّذِى جَعَلَ لَكُمُ ٱلَّيۡلَ لِتَسۡڪُنُواْ فِيهِ وَٱلنَّهَارَ مُبۡصِرًا*ۚ

It is He who made the night for your rest, and the daylight for visibility.

Summary:

15.3 NAAR: greater aspect of warmth and heat and lesser of light

15.4 JALLA: appearance of something pleasing/beautiful.

15.5 WHAJ: light that is fervid and hot.
