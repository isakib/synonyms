4 WAQT [Time]

Synonyms: WAQT, MEEQAAT, HEEN, HEENAIZN, AAN, AANAFA, AJL

4.1 WAQT (وقت): Famous word for time. ALWAQT (الوقت) means ZAMANUM-MALUM (زَمَانٌ مَّعْلُوْمٌ) (known time), (plural AUKAAT (اوقات)) According to Imam Raghib The word refers to the last limit of the appointed time for a task. Allah says in the Quran

Page 876

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:187

قُلْ إِنَّمَا عِلْمُهَا عِندَ اللَّهِ وَلَٰكِنَّ أَكْثَرَ النَّاسِ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ

Say, "Its knowledge is only with my Lord. None will reveal its time except Him.

 

And MEEQAAT (میقات) is the noun of place or time, Used both for time and place. (plural AWAQIYAT (وقت)). In the ayah below this word is used for time.

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:142

وَوَاعَدْنَا مُوسَىٰ ثَلَاثِينَ لَيْلَةً وَأَتْمَمْنَاهَا بِعَشْرٍ فَتَمَّ مِيقَاتُ رَبِّهِ أَرْبَعِينَ لَيْلَةً

And We made an appointment with Moses for thirty nights and perfected them by [the addition of] ten; so the term of his Lord was completed as forty nights.

 

And in the Following ayah the word MEEQAAT (میقات) is used for both place and time, meaning the time and place is specified.

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:143

وَلَمَّا جَاءَ مُوسَىٰ لِمِيقَاتِنَا وَكَلَّمَهُ رَبُّهُ

And when Moses arrived at Our appointed time and his Lord spoke to him,

 

4.2 HEEN (حِیْن): Is that time at which some news reaches or one gets something. It’s a hidden time or place, an unspecified time (Plural AHYAN (احیان)). And AHYANAN (اَحْیَانًا) means time to time; sometimes. In the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:36

وَلَكُمْ فِي الْأَرْضِ مُسْتَقَرٌّ وَمَتَاعٌ إِلَىٰ حِينٍ

you will have upon the earth a place of settlement and provision for a time."

 

When HEEN (حین) is followed by IZ (اذ) it becomes HEENAIZN (حین), then it refers to a certain time, or ‘this time’. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Waqiah (The Inevitable): سُوۡرَةُ الواقِعَة 56:84

وَأَنتُمْ حِينَئِذٍ تَنظُرُونَ

And you are at that time looking on –

 

4.3 AAN (اٰن): AAN-YAANI (اٰن یانی) means the time is here. Something reaching its peak or its maximum strength.  Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Hadeed (The Iron): سورة الحديد 57:16

أَلَمْ يَأْنِ لِلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا أَن تَخْشَعَ قُلُوبُهُمْ لِذِكْرِ اللَّهِ

Has the time not come for those who have believed that their hearts should become humbly submissive at the remembrance of Allah

 

And AAN (اٰن) means Time (Plural AANAA’ (اٰناء) means times). This word is accompanied by an AL (ال). ALAAN (الئٰن) means current time, this time, now. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Anfal (The Spoils of War): سُوۡرَةُ الاٴنفَال 8:66

الْآنَ خَفَّفَ اللَّهُ عَنكُمْ وَعَلِمَ أَنَّ فِيكُمْ ضَعْفًا

Now, Allah has lightened [the hardship] for you, and He knows that among you is weakness.

 

4.3 AANAFA (انِفًا): ANF (اَنْف) means a nose, the highest part of something or its start. ANF-AL-JABL (انف الجبل) means the peak of mountain. And ANF-AL-HAYAH (انف الحیۃ) means edge of beard. ASTANF-ASHAI (استانف الشئ) means to hold the edge of start of something or begin it. ASTANF-AL-AMR (اسانف الامر) means to restart a task anew (dictionary al Manjad). And ANIFA (اٰنِفا) means right now. At the start of this matter or task; A few moments ago (dictionary al Manjad). Allah says in the Quran      

Surah Muhammad (Muhammad): سُوۡرَةُ محَمَّد 47:16

قَالُوا لِلَّذِينَ أُوتُوا الْعِلْمَ مَاذَا قَالَ آنِفًا

 

they say to those who have received knowledge: "What has he said just now?

4.5 AJL (اجل): AJILA (اَجِلَ) means to delay. AAJL (اٰجل) and AAJLAH (اٰجلہ) happening with delay, Hereafter (dictionary al Manjad). Opposite of AAJL (اٰجل) is AA’JL (عاجل) both of these words are famous words used in the payment of HAQ-MAHR.  AA’JL (عاجل) means something paid immediately and AAJL (اٰجل) means a debt or credit. AJAL (اَجَل) means a term or time period, death (dictionary al Manjad). Thus the AJAL (اجل) refers to the mid time from the current time to the specified time or sometimes till the death. Its use is quite elaborate.  Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Isra (The Night Journey): سُوۡرَةُ الإسرَاء 17:99

وَجَعَلَ لَهُمْ أَجَلًا لَّا رَيْبَ فِيهِ

And He has appointed for them a term, about which there is no doubt.

 

In this Ayah the word AJAL (اجل) can be translated as time, or term or specific time period even death, all of these words fit into it.

Summary:

4.1 WAQT, MEEQAAT: The decided time or place.

4.2 HEEN: An unspecified time.

4.3 AAN and ALAAN: Now, current time.

4.4 AANAFA: Right now, a few moments back, at start of conversation.

4.5 AJL: The mid of the time between specified time and current time.
