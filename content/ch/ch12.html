12-Walk

Words used are ‘masaa’(مثی), ‘dabba’( دب), ‘intalaqa’( انطلق), ‘salaka’( سلک), ‘saraba’( سرب), ‘saraa’( سری), ‘da’ab’( داب), ‘mazaa’( مظی), ‘naqqab’( نقب) ‘saara’( سار, ‘sair’( سیر)), ‘qassa’( قص), ‘rajila’( رجل, ‘rijalan’( رجالا)).

1) ‘masaa’(مثی) :walking from one place to another intentionally, step by step, moving and walking with normal speed. Generally , this word is used for walking. Quran says: And they say, "What is this messenger that eats food and walks in the markets?  الْأَسْوَاقِ فِي وَيَمْشِي الطَّعَامَ يَأْكُلُ الرَّسُولِ هَـٰذَا  مَالِ وَقَالُوا [25:7]

2) ‘dabba’( دب) : It includes walking with slow and normal speed, creeping, crawling like snake, or children walking with the support of hands and legs. And everything walking and moving on earth. Quran says: And there is no creature on earth but that upon Allah is its provision, and He knows . رِزْقُهَا اللَّهِ عَلَى إِلَّا الْأَرْضِ فِي دَابَّةٍ مِن وَمَا[11:6]

3) ‘intalaqa’( انطلق) ‘talaqa’ means releasing from any boundation. And ‘intalaqa’ means walking after pause. Standing up, to leave, to leave after getting release from prison, starting talk after being quiet. For example: 1)So they set out, until when they had embarked on the ship. السَّفِينَةِ فِي رَكِبَا إِذَا حَتَّىٰ فَانطَلَقَا [18:71]

                                            2)(Moses said)And my tongue will not be fluent, so send for Aaron. هَارُونَ إِلَىٰ فَأَرْسِلْ لِسَانِي يَنطَلِقُ وَلَا [26:13]

4) ‘salaka’( سلک) : used for two meanings, 1)following a path without deviating ,following and leading a path. Quran says:

[o bee] Then eat from all the fruits and follow the ways of your Lord laid down [for you]."  ذُلُلًا رَبِّكِ سُبُلَ فَاسْلُكِي الثَّمَرَاتِ كُلِّ مِن كُلِي ثُمَّ [16:69]

   2) الشئ فی  نفوذالشئ means influencing one thing into another, to enter, to make enter. Quran says:

 Insert your hand into the opening of your garment; it will come out white, without disease. رِ سُوءٍ غَيْ مِنْ بَيْضَاءَ تَخْرُجْ جَيْبِكَ فِي يَدَكَ اسْلُكْ [28:32]

 

5)- ‘saraba’( سرب):means entering towards low ground or precipitousnes, and sarab means whole of wild animals. A pit, bottom sash, prison. It is said that , “snake entered into its hole”. Quran says: and it took its course into the sea, slipping away.  فَاتَّخَذَ سَبِيلَهُ فِي الْبَحْرِ سَرَبًا [18:61]

6)- ‘saraa’( سری) (یسری) means walking at night, travelling at night, and الساریتہ means a small group or crowd travelling at night. In Quran this word is meant for moving of night itself rather than travelling at night. For example: And [by] the night when it passes . وَاللَّيْلِ إِذَا يَسْرِ[89:4]

7)- ‘da’ab’( داب) trying to accomplish any task with hard work continuously in a regular manner, without any break or pause. Quran says:

And He subjected for you the sun and the moon, continuous [in orbit]  ۖ  وَسَخَّرَ لَكُمُ الشَّمْسَ وَالْقَمَرَ دَائِبَيْنِ[14:33], at another place,

[Joseph] said, "You will plant for seven years consecutively. تَزْرَعُونَ سَبْعَ سِنِينَ دَأَبًا[12:47]

8)- ‘mazaa’( مظی) means to pass, to go, being free. It is used in the context of passing of time and era. And maazi is meant for passed era or provide the meaning for something which is passing or passed by. ‘mazaa ma mazaa’(مضی ما مظی) means bygones be bygones or what happened has happened. Quran says: فَ

1-And We destroyed greater than them in [striking] power, and the example of the former peoples has preceded.

فَأَهْلَكْنَا أَشَدَّ مِنْهُم بَطْشًا وَمَضَىٰ مَثَلُ الْأَوَّلِينَ[43:8]

2-         "I will not cease [traveling] until I reach the junction of the two seas or continue for a long period."   حَتَّىٰ أَبْلُغَ مَجْمَعَ الْبَحْرَيْنِ أَوْ أَمْضِيَ حُقُبًا[18:60]

Here ‘mazaaa’ is used in the context of era, he will not cease until periods passes away.

9)- ‘naqqab’( نقب) : ‘naqaba’ means 1)making hole in wall or leather, 2) researching news. And  ‘naqqaba’ means navigating or to patrol. Entering a country, researching and investigating the situation. Quran says:

They  had explored throughout the lands. Is there any place of escape? مَّحِيصٍ مِن  هَلْ الْبِلَادِ فِي فَنَقَّبُوا [50:36]

 

 

10) ‘saara’( سار, ‘sair’( سیر)) means walking, roaming,  can be for any purpose. A person who is walking is termed as ‘saayur’ and a crowd walking in group is termed as ‘sayyarah’. Quran says:

And when Moses had completed the term and was traveling with his family. بِأَهْلِهِ وَسَارَ الْأَجَلَ مُوسَى قَضَىٰ فَلَمَّا [28:29]

11) ‘qassa’( قص): means to following, walking behind or following signs. For example:

 And she said to his sister, "Follow him"; so she watched him from a distance while they perceived not.

 يَشْعُرُونَ لَا وَهُمْ جُنُبٍ عَن بِهِ فَبَصُرَتْ  ۖ قُصِّيهِ لِأُخْتِهِ  وَقَالَتْ [28:11]

12)-‘rajila’( رجل, ‘rijalan’( رجالا)): means walking on foot, and is used when there are some boarded vehicles also in race. Quran says: they will come to you on foot and on every lean camel. ضَامِرٍ  كُلِّ وَعَلَىٰ رِجَالًا يَأْتُوكَ [22:27]. At another place: And if you fear [an enemy, then pray] on foot or riding.  رُكْبَانًا أَوْ فَرِجَالًا[2:239]

Conclusion:

1)            ‘masaa’(مثی): walking with normal speed, used generally.

2)            ‘dabba’( دب):walking slightly slower than ‘masaa’.

3)            ‘intalaqa’( انطلق):to leave, standing up to leave.

4)            ‘salaka’( سلک):following a path.

5)            ‘saraba’( سرب):crawling toward precipitousness/low ground, entering into hole.

6)            ‘saraa’( سری):walking at night or movement of night itself.

7)            ‘da’ab’( داب): walking on established or fixed path.

8)            ‘mazaa’( مظی): passing of time or period, or to move on with time.

9)            ‘naqqab’( نقب):means to patrol.

10)    ‘saara’( سار, ‘sair’( سیر)):walking, roaming, travelling.

11)    ‘qassa’( قص):to follow someone/walking behind.

12)    ‘rajila’( رجل, ‘rijalan’( رجالا)):walking on foot ,along with some boarded people.
