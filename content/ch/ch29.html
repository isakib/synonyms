29 CHEENNA or CHEEN LENA

[to snatch]

 

 

SYNONYMS: SALABA, GHASABA, NEEL, and KHATAFA

 

29.1 SALABA (سَلْب) : to take something from someone by force. It means to seize or to pick something, even if the one who is taking is weak.

Refer to 22:73

_______________________________________

29.2 GHASABA (غَصَبَ) : to snatch something with cruelty, oppression, forcefully, or by deception. However, there is a different word for snatching something away by using deception, which is غبن. Therefore, for the word غَصَبَ the meaning stated by Imam Raghib is correct i.e. a strong person forcefully taking over something from a weak person by cruelty and oppression.

Refer to 18:80

_______________________________________

29.3 NAALA (ناَلَ) : refers to everything that a human can catch with his hand, and he wants it too, or that thing which is within his reach. It refers to taking hold of something or to obtain something.

Refer to 33:25

 

Then an extension developed in the meaning of this word, and it was also used for a wish being fulfilled, or just fulfillment.

Refer to 2:124

On the other hand another extension developed, because of which this word was also used for the meaning of snatching.

Refer to 9:120

________________________________________

29.4 KHATIFA (خَطِفَ) : fast speed or to fly and swoop down on something in speed, suddenly.

Refer to 2:20

_______________________________________

SUMMARY

SALABA: to take something forcefully from someone else. common.

GHASABA: a strong person capturing something of a weak person.

NEEL: to snatch something which someone wants and it is within his reach.

KHATAFA: fast speed, and to rapidly grab and take something away.
