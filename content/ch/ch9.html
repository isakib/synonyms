9- Ascend /Climb(upward)( چڑھنا) - page 414, 415

Note: to watch rising Sun and planets.

Words used are ‘araja’(عرج), ‘Raqiya’(رقی), ‘sa’ada’(صعد), ‘zahara’(ظہر), ‘rahaqa’(رھق) and ‘tasawwara’(تسور).

1)- araja’(عرج) Meaning of ‘araja’ has two senses, inclination/tilt and height. ‘araja’ means hobbling and ‘a’araj’(اعرج) means lame. And ‘a’araja fis-salama’(ااسلم فی عرج) means to climb ladder or stair. Quran says:

And [even] if We opened to them a gate from the heaven and they continued therein to ascend, They would say, "Our eyes have only been dazzled.أَبْصَارُنَا سُكِّرَتْ إِنَّمَا لَقَالُوايَعْرُجُونَ فِيهِ فَظَلُّوا السَّمَاءِ مِّنَ بَابًا عَلَيْهِم فَتَحْنَا وَلَوْ [15:14].

2)-Raqiya’(رقی) means doing magic as well as climbing height. Such that one should do according to his/her power. To climb sedately with slow speed. It is said that ‘ irqi a’la zil’ika ’ (ظلعک علی ارق) means ascend as per ur ability. Do not burden yourself more than your power. It is said that, ‘ raqyatu fil-ilm wash-sharf.’  (وااشرف ااعلم فی رقیت), I did progress in knowledge and honor. Therefore its use is general. Quran says:

And [even then], we will not believe in your ascension until you bring down to us a book we may read. ۗ نَّقْرَؤُهُ كِتَابًا عَلَيْنَا تُنَزِّلَ حَتَّىٰلِرُقِيِّكَ نُّؤْمِنَ وَلَن السَّمَاءِ فِي تَرْقَىٰأَوْ[17:93]

3)-In the context of ‘sa’ada’(صعد) basically there are two meanings, 1)height and 2) hardwork or labor. Means to reach a high place with difficulty.

And ‘su’ood’ (صعود) is called height and valley(گھاٹی) and also the difficult way to it.(Scene( منجد)) Something ascending upward with difficulty like smoke. And (مصعدالکہر بایر ) is known as electric lift. And ‘as’ad’(اصعد) and ‘sa’ad’ (صعد) means to climb upward with problem and difficulty. Quran says:

He makes his breast tight and constricted as though he were climbing into the sky.  السَّمَاءِ فِي يَصَّعَّدُ كَأَنَّمَا حَرَجًا ضَيِّقًا صَدْرَهُ يَجْعَلْ [6:125].

Note: all the tree word are derived from ‘nazala’(نزل).

4)- ‘zahara’(ظہر) means to be clear ,apparent. to be dominant. Cilmb and to reach the height, it can be stairs or a wall etc which is climbed through stairs or without stairs. For example:

So Gog and Magog were unable to pass over it, nor were they able [to effect] in it any penetration. نَقْبًالَهُ اسْتَطَاعُوا وَمَا يَظْهَرُوهُ أَن اسْطَاعُوا فَمَا [18:97]

At another place:

We would have made for those who disbelieve in the Most Merciful - for their houses - ceilings and stairways of silver upon which to mount  يَظْهَرُونَ عَلَيْهَا وَمَعَارِجَ فِضَّةٍ مِّن سُقُفًا لِبُيُوتِهِمْ [43:33]

5)- ‘rahaqa’(رھق)  means one thing getting over another and hiding it or covering it. It has a reflex quality. رہقہ الامر means a matter hides it forcefully or oppressly i.e; covered it. Quran says:

And [other] faces, that Day, will have upon them dust. Blackness will cover them.قَتَرَةٌتَرْهَقُهَا  غَبَرَةٌ عَلَيْهَا يَوْمَئِذٍ وَوُجُوهٌ [80:41]

6)- ‘tasawwara’(تسور) soor(سور)means city shelter or such a high wall which is impossible to climb over and enter into it. And sara (سار) means to climb or crossing over a wall. It also means wearing Doughnut.(siwar, sawar, aswar etc are derivatives of doughnut). for example:

And has there come to you the news of the adversaries, when they climbed over the wall of [his] prayer chamber -الْمِحْرَابَ تَسَوَّرُوا إِذْ الْخَصْمِ نَبَأُ أَتَاكَ وَهَلْ [38:21]

Conclusion:1) ‘araja’(عرج) for climbing with pauses.

2) ‘Raqiya’(رقی) for climbing according to power.

3) ‘sa’ada’(صعد) for climbing upward with problem.

4) ‘zahara’(ظہر) for reaching the height.

5) ‘rahaqa’(رھق)   for getting over one thing over another and covering it.

6) ‘tasawwara’(تسور) for climbing wall or crossing over it.
