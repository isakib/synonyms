7-Lamp(چراغ) - page 413

Words used are ‘misbah’(مصباح) and ‘siraj’(سراج).

1- sabah(صبح) means bright and shining. Sabah-Ash-sha’ar(الشعر صبح) means shimmering sand. Sabah-al-hadid(الحدید صبح) means illumination of iron. Sabiyah(صبیاح) and Sabhan( صبحان) are used in terms of beautiful. Ibn faris writes that this word(sabah) is basically used for red colour. That is why morning (subah) and lamp(misbah) are named accordingly due to their redness. For example: The example of His light is like a niche within which is a lamp,   ۚ مِصْبَاحٌ فِيهَا كَمِشْكَاةٍ نُورِهِ مَثَلُ [24:35]

then the word misbah is again used for bright stars. For example:

And We have certainly beautified the nearest heaven with stars بِمَصَابِيحَالدُّنْيَا السَّمَاءَ زَيَّنَّا وَلَقَدْ [67:5].

2-Siraj(سراج) Ibn Faris says that the word ‘siraj’ indicates beauty, elegance or decoration and grace. And siraj is given its name due to its light and elegance. trapping of horse is also called its ‘zeen’ due to the same reason.it is said that وجہہ سراج , means he/she beautified his/her face. شعرہ سراج, means he/she managed(combed) his/her hairs.

Quran says:

Blessed is He who has placed in the sky great stars and placed therein a [burning] lamp and luminous moon. امُّنِيرًوَقَمَرًا سِرَاجًا فِيهَا وَجَعَلَ بُرُوجًا السَّمَاءِ فِي جَعَلَ الَّذِي تَبَارَكَ [25:61].

Ibn Al-faris sahb referencing ‘maqayis illalaghta’ that the Quran does not justify the information given above. Quran addressed the Sun as ‘siraj, (اوَهَّاجً سِرَاجًا[78:13]) although its light seems reedish but not whitish. Similarly Quran addressed stars as ‘masabiyah’ whose light seems to be white and not reddish. Therefore conclusion is:

Conclusion: Siraj indicates red light and misbah indicates white light. (Wallah Aalam)
