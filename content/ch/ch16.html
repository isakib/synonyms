16- To Shine/ Brightness

Words used are ‘Bazag’a’(بزغ), ‘Ashraqa’(اشرق( شرق)), ‘saqaba’( ثقب), ‘sana’( سنا(سنو)), ‘duriyyun’( دری).

1)             ‘Bazag’a’(بزغ): means rising of sun when its light is scattering. Used for the sun, moon, stars and other bodies while they are rising. And ‘nujoomun bavazig’un’ is meant for rising stars.

           Quran says: And when he saw the sun rising, he said, "This is my lord. رَبِّي  هَـٰذَا قَالَ بَازِغَةً الشَّمْسَ رَأَى فَلَمَّا[6:78]

2)             ‘Ashraqa’(اشرق( شرق)): ‘sharaq’ means scattering or spreading of light completely. And ‘ashraqa’ means to make something shine. Throwing light on it such that it shines out completely.

           For example: And the earth will shine with the light of its Lord. رَبِّهَا بِنُورِ الْأَرْضُ وَأَشْرَقَتِ [39:69]

3)             ‘saqaba’( ثقب): means lighting of fire or burning of flame, shimmering of star. ‘saqub’ means being reddish like that of fire, penetrating of light completely and being through something. ‘saqab’ means to make hole in something. And ‘saaqib’ means shiny to such an extent that can penetrate through anything on which it falls. Hence ‘saqab’ is means for intensity and reddishness like that of flame. Quran says: Except one who snatches [some words] by theft, but they are pursued by a burning flame, piercing [in brightness]. ثَاقِبٌ شِهَابٌ فَأَتْبَعَهُ الْخَطْفَةَ خَطِفَ مَنْ إِلَّا[37:10]

4)             ‘sana’( سنا(سنو)): means flashing of lightening. Light that can take away the eyesight.

 Quran says:  The flash of its[of clouds] lightening almost takes away the eyesight. بِالْأَبْصَارِ هَبُ يَذْ بَرْقِهِ سَنَا يَكَادُ[24:43]

5)           ‘duriyyun’( دری): ‘darra’ is meant for brightness and excess. ‘saifun duriyyun’ means shining or glowing sword.

‘darrun’ and ‘adwari’ means camel producing huge milk. The word ‘midraran’ has the same concept and ‘dur’ means shining pearl. Sometimes both the contexts are found together. For example, a very shining star that is intense as well as glowing. Quran says: as if it were a pearly [white] star lit. دُرِّيٌّ كَوْكَبٌ كَأَنَّهَا[24:35]

Conclusion:

1)             ‘Bazag’a’(بزغ): means scattering of light while rising of heavenly bodies.

2)           ‘Ashraqa’(اشرق( شرق)): means falling of light on something and make it glow.

3)           ‘saqaba’( ثقب): penetration of high intense light.

4)           ‘sana’( سنا(سنو)): light which effect eyesight.

5)           ‘duriyyun’( دری): high glowing.
