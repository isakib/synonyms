 Allah intends for you ease and does not intend for you hardship . الْعُسْرَ  بِكُمُ يُرِيدُ وَلَا الْيُسْرَ بِكُمُ اللَّهُ يُرِيدُ[2:185].

Similarly,at another place:

And Allah wants no injustice for [His] servants. لِّلْعِبَادِ ظُلْمًا يُرِيدُ اللَّهُ وَمَا [40:31].

Although,in actual Hardness, hardship and injustice are found among people due to their fortune.Regarding human it indicates such desires and plans which are the subject of destiny by Allah.for example:

And you do not will except that Allah wills - Lord of the worlds. الْعَالَمِينَ رَبُّ اللَّهُ يَشَاءَ أَن إِلَّا تَشَاءُونَ وَمَا[81:29].

However the human mind is free from it, he can be against the fortune decided by Allah. Like he wants to escape from death which is against this fortune. So it cannot happen. For example:

They want to extinguish the light of Allah with their mouths, but Allah will perfect His light, although the disbelievers dislike it.

 الْكَافِرُونَ كَرِهَ وَلَوْ نُورِهِ وَاللَّهُ بِأَفْوَاهِهِمْ اللَّهِ نُورَ لِيُطْفِئُوا يُرِيدُونَ .[61:8]

The thing which strongly differentiate fortune from the plans is that plans can be shifted forward or backward but fortune is fixed. It cannot be shifted. [40-فقل]

3)-Ishtahaa(اشتہی)- means to attract towards something which he likes naturally, like hunger, free will, covetousness etc. According to Mr. Manjad it means to wish for pleasant things, having no any interference of plans. For example:

And you will have therein whatever your souls desire, and you will have therein whatever you request [or wish]. 

وَلَكُمْ فِيهَا مَا تَشْتَهِي أَنفُسُكُمْ وَلَكُمْ فِيهَا مَا تَدَّعُونَ [41:31]

4)- bagaa(بغی):Wish(to have a strong desire of something)- wishing to cross the limits of moderations in the desire of getting something. This can be appreciable like obeying beyond one’s responsibilities and duties and unacceptable as well like attracting towards false against truth.

 for example: They said, "O our father, what [more] could we desire? This is our merchandise returned to us.

 هَـٰذِهِ بِضَاعَتُنَا رُدَّتْ إِلَيْنَا ۖيقَالُوا يَا أَبَانَا مَا نَبْغِ[12:65]

Hence recovery of money was their right which they got.

 

 <<page 412>>

It also means to keep trying to get something or to demand something i.e; trying hard more than the possible.

For example: But only seeking the countenance of his Lord, Most High. الْأَعْلَىٰ رَبِّهِ وَجْهِ ابْتِغَاءَ إِلَّا [92:20]

5-‘Raghiba’(رغب ): means to wish something heartedly. And ‘Marghoob’(مرغوب) means pleasing. Hence it may or may not be according to the predefined fortune. for example:

Perhaps our Lord will substitute for us [one] better than it. Indeed, we are toward our Lord desirous."

 عَسَىٰ رَبُّنَا أَن يُبْدِلَنَا خَيْرًا مِّنْهَا إِنَّا إِلَىٰ رَبِّنَا رَاغِبُونَ[68:32].

Conclusion:1) Sha’aa(شاء):such plans and desires which are the subjects of destiny by Allah. And it does not happened before or after the defined time.

2)Araada(ارادا): It is general, can or can not be according to destiny decided by Allah. It can be shifted backward or forward.

3) Ishtahaa(اشتہی) wishing for pleasant things.

4) bagaa(بغی):Crossing the limits of moderations to get something.

5) ‘Raghiba’(رغب ): to desire something heartedly, may or may not be according to the fortune.
