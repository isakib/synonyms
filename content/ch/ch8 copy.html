8-Pasture(چرانا) - page 413, 414

The words used in the holy Quran are ‘Ra’ayaa’(رعی) and  asaam(اسام).

1-)Ra’aa(رعی) means to feed an animal and to take care of it and protect it. And Ra’ai(راعی) means shepherd. And Mara’a(مرعی) means grass as well as grassland, to save something and remove the  causes of external dangers(صنداھمال) and animals without shepherd. For example: Eat [therefrom] and pasture your livestock. أَنْعَامَكُمْ كُلُوا وَارْعَوْا [20:54].

The word is originated from teachings and protection therefore it can also be related to human beings. King is ruler(care taker(راعی)) and public is commoner(رعیت).

2-) Asaama(اسام): Sawm(سوم)basically it has two meanings. 1)Demand and 2) Departure. Means to leave or go off for something, like camels went towards grassland for grazing. And ‘Asaama’ means to take for pasturing.

For example:and from it is foliage in which you pasture [animals].  وَمِنْهُ شَجَرٌ فِيهِ تُسِيمُونَ[16:10].

Conclusion: Ra’aa(رعی) means to protect and pasture and Asaama(اسام) means to take for pasturing.
