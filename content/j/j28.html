Kaththabooka = who denies

Kaththab: to give lie/deny/denial

For ‘to give lie /to deny’, words ‘Kaththab’ and ‘Abtala’ have been used in the Quran.

1: Kaththab:meaning to give lie or to declare something as lie/to deny is a well-known word.

Allah(swt) says in the Quran 3:184:

Then if they cry lies to/deny you, [O Muhammad] - so were messengers denied before you, who brought clear proofs and written ordinances and the enlightening Scripture.

2: Abtala: derived from ‘Batil’ meaning untrue or un-sturdy/fragile. Just like ‘Haq’ in a way is taken in the meaning of ‘truth’ similarly ‘ batil’ is taken in a way, in the meaning of ‘lie/falsehood’. And‘Abtala’ means to prove something wrong/false according to the events and circumstances.

Allah(swt) says in the Quran 8:8 :

That He should establish the truth and abolish falsehood, even if the criminals disliked it.

Conclusion:

‘Kaththab’ is used only to declare something as false/lie where as

‘Abtala’ is used to prove something wrong/false/lie with arguments, experience and introspection.
