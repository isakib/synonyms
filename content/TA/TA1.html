1-Taaqat (Strength)


Synonyms in the Quran: Taaqata (tauq), Quwwa (Quww), Mirrah(Marr), Rukn, Mahul

1.1-Taaqata(طاقة): It is a well recognized word and its usage is common. Literally, the strength and power required for a certain action.
Allah(swt) says in the Quran:

Surah Al-Baqarah-The Cow(2:286)
رَبَّنَا وَلَا تُحَمِّلْنَا مَا لَا طَاقَةَ لَنَا بِهِ ۖ
Our Lord, and burden us not with that which we have no ability to bear.


1.2-Quwwah(قُوَّة):Force. The potential and ability that is found in a thing. Antonym: weak
Mentioned in the Quran:

Surah Qasas-The Stories (28:76)
إِنَّ مَفَاتِحَهُ لَتَنُوءُ بِالْعُصْبَةِ أُولِي الْقُوَّةِ
We gave him of treasures whose keys would burden a band of strong men;


1.3-Mirrah(مِرَّة)(Marra): Meaning i)Passing by ii)To be acrimonious or iii)To divide firmly. And mirrah meaning with intensity. The perennial condition of a thing. Strength. Steadfastness.Intensity and resolve. And Zumarrah(ذومرة) is an idiom meaning strong and powerful. Energetic. Very forceful. And Mareer meaning a braided rope. Allah (swt) says:

Surah An-Najm-The Star (53:5-6)
عَلَّمَهُ شَدِيدُ الْقُوَىٰ
ذُو مِرَّةٍ فَاسْتَوَىٰ
Taught to him by one intense in strength -
One of soundness. And he rose to [his] true form


1.4-Rukn(رُكن): Something's Rukn is it's strongest direction. And meaning pillar. Fortress and foundation are also used.
Allah (swt) says:

Surah Hud (11:80)
قَالَ لَوْ أَنَّ لِي بِكُمْ قُوَّةً أَوْ آوِي إِلَىٰ رُكْنٍ شَدِيدٍ
He said, "If only I had against you some power or could take refuge in a strong support."


1.5-Mahula(مَحُلَ): Meaning to scheme against someone with power and strictness. Therefore, the meaning of Mahl may encompass Quwwah and Heelah. That is, to tighten one's grip upon a person via scheming. And Mahl meaning betrayal. Duplicity. Enmity. Intensity. Drought. Extreme hunger. Allah(swt) says:

Surah Ar-Ra'd-The Thunder(13:13)
وَهُمْ يُجَادِلُونَ فِي اللَّهِ وَهُوَ شَدِيدُ الْمِحَالِ
...they dispute about Allah ; and He is severe in assault.

Summary:
1) Taaqat: Common word. The amount of strength required for an act.
2) Quwwah: The capacity and ability present in something.
3) Marrah: Power, intensity and resolve.
4) Rukn: The strongest direction of something
5) Mahl: Power and excuses
