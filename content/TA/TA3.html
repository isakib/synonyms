3-Tareeqa(way)-Dastoor(norms)


Synonyms: Tareeqa, Sunnah, Ummah, Shari'ah, Minhaj, Mansak, Shakilah, Ma'aroof and Khuluq

3.1:Tareeqa(طَرِيقَة)-Meaning habit, condition, religion. Therefore all the sects and religions that humans adopt to do some work.
It is mentioned in the Quran:

Surah Taha (20:63):
قَالُوا إِنْ هَٰذَانِ لَسَاحِرَانِ يُرِيدَانِ أَن يُخْرِجَاكُم مِّنْ أَرْضِكُم بِسِحْرِهِمَا وَيَذْهَبَا بِطَرِيقَتِكُمُ الْمُثْلَىٰ
They said, "Indeed, these are two magicians who want to drive you out of your land with their magic and do away with your most exemplary way.

And in another place:

Surah Taha (20:104):
نَّحْنُ أَعْلَمُ بِمَا يَقُولُونَ إِذْ يَقُولُ أَمْثَلُهُمْ طَرِيقَةً إِن لَّبِثْتُمْ إِلَّا يَوْمًا
We are most knowing of what they say when the best of them in manner will say, "You remained not but one day."

Therefore in sharaee context, tareeqa refers to those rituals and habits that people have raised to the level of religion.


3.2:Sunnah(سُنَّة): Sanna(سَنَّ) meaning the advent of something and its continuation in a correct manner. Used for both things that are being done because they are an order and those that are being done because of inclination. For example 'mun sanna sunnah'(مَن سَنَّ سُنَّة) someone established something (way, ritual, norm). When used in reference to Allah Ta'ala then it will mean God's Law (which is usually referred to as nature's law).
Allah(swt) says in the Quran:

Surah Fatir-Originator(35:43):
فَهَلْ يَنظُرُونَ إِلَّا سُنَّتَ الْأَوَّلِينَ ۚ فَلَن تَجِدَ لِسُنَّتِ اللَّهِ تَبْدِيلًا
... Then do they await except the way of the former peoples? But you will never find in the way of Allah any change,

And when only the word Sunnat is used, it usually means the way of the Prophet (saw)

3.3:Ummah(اُمَّة): A group of people having a shared set of beliefs and opinions/outlooks. It also includes any belief and outlook that people are agreed upon, whether it is wrong or right.
Allah(swt) says in the Quran:

Surah Az-Zukhruf-The Ornaments of Gold(43:23):
إِنَّا وَجَدْنَا آبَاءَنَا عَلَىٰ أُمَّةٍ وَإِنَّا عَلَىٰ آثَارِهِم مُّقْتَدُونَ
"Indeed, we found our fathers upon a religion, and we are, in their footsteps, following."



3.4-Sharia'ah(شَرِيعَة): Shara'a(شرع)meaning for something to be brought or raised into being apparent. Shara'-al-ba'aeeroo o'onuqahoo(شَرَعَ البَعِيرُ عُنُقَهُ)- the camel raised his neck in such a way that it became more noticeable. And shara'a-al-qaum(شرع اللقوم) meaning to make the law for a nation. And sharia'a(شَرِيعَة) meaning Islamic law. God's orders. Order and shari'at-e-Islamia are used for Islamic laws. And Shari'ah meaning the clear and designated path. But this applies only to God's orders.
We think that Imam Raghib's explanation or meaning is the more correct view that is the word Shari'at means God's orders rather than Islamic law because the Prophet(saw) said that the Deen of all messengers and Prophets was one but the Shari'at kept changing (according to the requirements of that time). Deen is similar to a father whereas shari'at is like a mother. The Prophet(saw) said all the Messengers have a common father but different mothers. Therefore, the Deen is based on certain constant dictates of God that have been the same since the beginning of Creation such as belief in the unseen, belief in Allah, His angels and His books and laws of reward and punishment etc. Prayers, fasting, and Zakat's rulings and laws of punishments and orders of Shari'at is exemplified by the number of prayers, their rakaat and way of offering them. The shari'at for all messengers and prophets was different but the Deen was one.
Allah (swt) says:

Surah al-Jathiya-The Crouching(45:18)
ثُمَّ جَعَلْنَاكَ عَلَىٰ شَرِيعَةٍ مِّنَ الْأَمْرِ
Then We put you, [O Muhammad], on an ordained way

This Ayat makes it clear that Prophet(saw) was given a separate shari'at.



3.5-Minhaj(مِنهَاج): Nahj al amr waltareeq(نهج الامروالطريق) meaning for a work or way to be apparent. And minhaj meaning clear way of acts that is the way of enacting shara'ee orders. And this way is told to the messengers by Allah Himself as is made obvious by Sahih Hadith that Jibrael (as) came to the Prophet(saw) for two days and instructed him in the 5 prayers. The first day during optimum times and the second day during the late hours. And told him that prayers can also be offered between these two extremes of times. This is minhaj. Therefore, minhaj is a treatise and explanation of shari'at.
Allah(swt) says:

Surah Al-Maidah-The Table Spread (5:48)
كُلٍّ جَعَلْنَا مِنكُمْ شِرْعَةً وَمِنْهَاجًا ۚ
To each of you We prescribed a law and a method.



3.6-Munsaka(مَنسَكَ): Nasaka(نَسَكَ) meaning to become an ascetic. To become a dervish. And nasaka lillahi(نَسَكَ لِلَّهِ) meaning to sacrifice for Allah. Munsaka has been derived from minhaj and it only refers to those ways that are related to worship. As mentioned in the Quran:

Surah Al-Haj (22:67)
لِّكُلِّ أُمَّةٍ جَعَلْنَا مَنسَكًا هُمْ نَاسِكُوهُ ۖ
For every religion We have appointed rites which they perform.

The word Manasak is usually limited to the rulings and performance of Hajj. Manasik-e-Hajj meaning the locations, methods and manners for the acts of Hajj. And nasak(نسك) refers to the sacrifice offered during Hajj. (For details, refer to 'Qurbani')
In the Quran:

Surah Al-Baqrah-The Cow (2:196)
فَمَن كَانَ مِنكُم مَّرِيضًا أَوْ بِهِ أَذًى مِّن رَّأْسِهِ فَفِدْيَةٌ مِّن صِيَامٍ أَوْ صَدَقَةٍ أَوْ نُسُكٍ ۚ
And whoever among you is ill or has an ailment of the head [making shaving necessary must offer] a ransom of fasting [three days] or charity or sacrifice.



3.7-Shaakilah(شَاكِلَه): Shakala(شَكَلَ) meaning to be ambiguous. And Shakul-ad-daayah(شَكُلَ الدَّايَة) meaning sheltering an animal and an-nas-u ashkal-u-wa-ullaafun (اَلنَّاسُ اَشكَالٌ وَّالَّافٌ) meaning people being similar to each other with fondness in the sense that there are two aspects in the meaning of shakal. One being similar in apparence while shaakilah refers to a specific way of dealing with others to inspire and encourage them in doing the same. As the Holy Prophet (SAW) said: Kullum-muyassarul-limaa-khuliqa-lah(كُلٌّ مُّيَسَّرٌ لِّمَا خُلِقَ لَهُ) . You can understand this with the example of a man who takes care of the needy be giving sadaqah or charity while giving it in a way that it encourages others not to show off but without having any ill intent. Now, both these methods are correct and allowed although the act is similar or exactly the same but the way of performing them is unique in their own respect depending on their preference and execution. This is shakilah.
Allah (swt) says in the Quran:

Surah Al-Isra-The Night Journey (17:84)
قُلْ كُلٌّ يَعْمَلُ عَلَىٰ شَاكِلَتِهِ فَرَبُّكُمْ أَعْلَمُ بِمَنْ هُوَ أَهْدَىٰ سَبِيلًا
Say, "Each works according to his manner, but your Lord is most knowing of who is best guided in way."



3.8-Ma'aroof(مَعرُوف): Urf(عُرف) meaning recognizable and ma'aroof refers to a way that easily blends in with the norms of the society, customs and traditions in the sense that it is perceived to be good, independent of whether it is supported or backed by the shari'ah or not. Like treating elders with respect and calling them 'sir' or using 'jee' even though there is no such obligation by the shari'ah but such acts are good, meaning ma'aroof.
Allah (swt) says in the Quran:

Surah Al-Baqarah-The Cow (2:241)
وَلِلْمُطَلَّقَاتِ مَتَاعٌ بِالْمَعْرُوفِ ۖ
And for divorced women is a provision according to what is acceptable -



3.9-Khuluq(خُلُق): Khalq khilqat(خَلق خِلقَت) refers to the outer appearance that can be perceived by the human eye while khulq(خُلق) refers to the personality or hidden traits of a person like habits and weaknesses. Khulq or Khuluq meaning preference, weakness or an act or way that has become a habit.
Allah (swt) says in the Quran:

Surah Ash-Shu'ara-The Poets (26:137)
إِنْ هَٰذَا إِلَّا خُلُقُ الْأَوَّلِينَ
This is not but the custom of the former peoples,



Summary:
1) Tareeqah: Those traditions and rituals that people start believing to be part of religion
2) Sunnah: The laws of God that are established in the universe.
3) Ummah: The similar views or beliefs of a group of people.
4) Shari'ah: Those laws of God that kept changing due to different requirements and times.
5) Minhaj: The way to enact God's laws. Details and explanation.
6) Mansak: The rules of worship especially Hajj.
7) Shakilatih: The actions of an individual due to his personal inclinations
8) Ma'aroof: Such rituals and customs that are liked in a society and they are not contrary to Islamic law.
9) Khuluq: Those habits or ways that have become a part of an individual.
