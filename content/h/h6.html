6 HALAKAT                

[Destruction, Death]

Synonyms: TABBA(تب), TA’SA(تعس), QUTIL(قتل), SABARA(ثبر), AULA(أولى), WAILUN(ويل) (These words are used in a state of anger and annoyance)

6.1 tabba(تب): meaning to die, break, cut, to cut; used in all these senses. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Lahab - سُوۡرَةُ لهب

111:1

تَبَّتۡ يَدَآ أَبِى لَهَبٍ۬ وَتَبَّ

The power of Abu Lahab will perish, and he will perish.

________________

6.2 ta’sa (تعس): meaning to fall after stumbling and not able to stand up again, to die after falling in a pit. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Muhammad - سُوۡرَةُ محَمَّد

47:8

وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُواْ فَتَعۡسً۬ا لَّهُمۡ

And those who disbelieve, perdition is for them, and He will make their actions vain.

________________

6.3 qutila (قتل): qatala(قتل) meaning to kill someone, to separate the soul from the body and qutila(قتل) means to be killed, to die. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Al-Mudathir (The Cloaked One) - سُوۡرَةُ المدَّثِّر

74:19-20

ثُمَّ قُتِلَ كَيۡفَ قَدَّرَ فَقُتِلَ كَيۡفَ قَدَّرَ

(Self-)destroyed is he, how he planned! (19) Again (self-)destroyed is he, how he planned!

________________

6.4 sabara (ثبر): meaning to be killed or aggravating of a wound and suboor(ثبور) meaning death. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Al-Furqan (The Standard) - سُوۡرَةُ الفُرقان

25:14

لَّا تَدۡعُواْ ٱلۡيَوۡمَ ثُبُورً۬ا وَٲحِدً۬ا وَٱدۡعُواْ ثُبُورً۬ا ڪَثِيرً۬ا

Pray not that day for one destruction, but pray for many destructions!

________________

6.5 aula (أولى): if it ends in ب, it means worthy, appropriate, most deserving. And if it ends in ل, it means death, destruction and damage, sadness. This phrase is used for such one who has reached his death so that he may be warned. Also, this word is used for disagreement, so that the person may try to avoid it by pondering over the end result. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Al-Qiyama (Resurrection) - سُوۡرَةُ القِیَامَة

75:31-34

أَوۡلَىٰ لَكَ فَأَوۡلَىٰ  ثُمَّ ذَهَبَ إِلَىٰٓ أَهۡلِهِۦ يَتَمَطَّىٰٓ وَلَـٰكِن كَذَّبَ وَتَوَلَّىٰ فَلَا صَدَّقَ وَلَا صَلَّىٰ

For he neither trusted, nor prayed. (31) But he denied and flouted. (32) Then went he to his folk with glee. (33) Nearer unto thee and nearer

________________
