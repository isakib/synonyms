1 HATH [Hand]

Synonyms: YADD, YAMEEN, SHIMAAL, DIRAA’

1.1YADD (ید): Means a hand. The actually word is YADDI (یدی), but the ‘ی’ was dropped. The double of it is YADAAN (یَدَان) and YADAIN (یَدَیْن), and plural is AIEDI (اَیْدِی). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:79

فَوَيْلٌ لَّهُم مِّمَّا كَتَبَتْ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَوَيْلٌ لَّهُم مِّمَّا يَكْسِبُونَ

Woe to them for what their hands have written and woe to them for what they earn.

 

And because hand is the most vital part of body when it comes to work and strength, this word also symbolizes strength, possession and ownership. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:237

أَن يَعْفُونَ أَوْ يَعْفُوَ الَّذِي بِيَدِهِ عُقْدَةُ النِّكَاحِ

unless they forego the right, or the one in whose hand is the marriage contract foregoes it۔

And BAIEN-YADEE (بین یدی) and BAIEN-AIDEE (بین ایدی) is used for previous and front or present metaphorically. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Mujadila (The Pleading Women) سُوۡرَةُ المجَادلة 58:12

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا نَاجَيْتُمُ الرَّسُولَ فَقَدِّمُوا بَيْنَ يَدَيْ نَجْوَاكُمْ صَدَقَةً

O you who have believed, when you [wish to] privately consult the Messenger, present before your consultation a charity.

 

In another place in Holy Quran

Surah Yaseen (Yaseen): سورۃ ٰیٰس 36:9

وَجَعَلْنَا مِن بَيْنِ أَيْدِيهِمْ سَدًّا وَمِنْ خَلْفِهِمْ سَدًّا

And We have put a bar in front of them and a bar behind them,

 

Also in Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:65

فَقُلْنَا لَهُمْ كُونُوا قِرَدَةً خَاسِئِينَ فَجَعَلْنَاهَا نَكَالًا لِّمَا بَيْنَ يَدَيْهَا وَمَا خَلْفَهَا

We said to them: "Be you monkeys, despised and rejected." So We made this punishment an example to their own and to succeeding generations

1.2 YAMEEN (یَمِیْن): (Plural AIEMAAN (اَیْمَان)) means the right hand. For instance in Quran

Surah Ta-Ha (Ta-ha):سورۃ طٰھٰ  20:17

وَمَا تِلْكَ بِيَمِينِكَ يَا مُوسَىٰ

And what is that in your right hand, O Moses?"

 

And also the right side (Opposite SHIMAAL (شمال) (left)). For instance in the Quran

Surah Saba' (Sheba) - سورة سبإ 34:15

لَقَدْ كَانَ لِسَبَإٍ فِي مَسْكَنِهِمْ آيَةٌ ۖ جَنَّتَانِ عَن يَمِينٍ وَشِمَالٍ ۖ

There was for [the tribe of] Saba' in their dwelling place a sign: two [fields of] gardens on the right and on the left.

 

The way YADD (ید) is used for strength and possession, YAMEEN (یَمِیْن) is used for a wider range of meanings because in power and skill right hand is better than left hand. MULK-YAMEEN (ملک یمین) refers to that thing which is under complete ownership and authority. Metaphorically it refers to a male or female slave. For instance in the Quran

Surah An-Nisa (The women): سورۃ النِّسا 4:24

وَالْمُحْصَنَاتُ مِنَ النِّسَاءِ إِلَّا مَا مَلَكَتْ أَيْمَانُكُمْ

Also (forbidden are) women already married, except those (captives and slaves) whom your right hands possess.

 

Apart from this, Arab used to put or strike their right hand on the hand of the addressed person to strengthen an oath, thus this word started to be used for a vow or oath. YAMEEN (یَمِیْن) is that swearing that is done to strengthen a vow. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:13

أَلَا تُقَاتِلُونَ قَوْمًا نَّكَثُوا أَيْمَانَهُمْ

Would you not fight a people who broke their oaths

 

1.3 SHIMAAL (شِمَال): Means left, left hand, or left side (Opposite YAMEEN (یَمِیْن)). The way YAMEEN (یَمِیْن) refers to fortune and good luck, in the same way SHIMAAL (شِمَال) refers to bad luck (dictionary al Manjad). (Plural SHMAAIL (شمائل)). In the Quran

Surah Al-Haqqah (The reality): سورۃ الحَاقَّۃ 69:25

وَأَمَّا مَنْ أُوتِيَ كِتَابَهُ بِشِمَالِهِ فَيَقُولُ يَا لَيْتَنِي لَمْ أُوتَ كِتَابِيَهْ

But as for he who is given his record in his left hand, he will say, "Oh, I wish I had not been given my record

 

Another place in the Quran

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:17

ثُمَّ لَآتِيَنَّهُم مِّن بَيْنِ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمِنْ خَلْفِهِمْ وَعَنْ أَيْمَانِهِمْ وَعَن شَمَائِلِهِمْ

Then I will come to them from before them and from behind them and on their right and on their left,

1.4 DIRAA’ (ذِرَاع): Means hand. Span between elbow till the middle finger. DARAA’-ATH-THAUB (ذَرَعَ الثَوَبَ) means to measure a cloth with DIRAA’ (ذراع) and DARUHU-KADA (ذَرْعُہُ کَذَا) means this is the length or measurement. And DIRAA’ (ذراع) is a tool for measurement whose length is between fifty (50) to seventy (70) centimeters (dictionary al Manjad). Almost twenty (20) to twenty-seven (27) inches. The reason for the difference in measurement is that in the earlier ages the heights of humans used to be tall which have reduced now. Certain translators take DIRAA’ (ذِرَاع) as a yard.

Surah Al-Haqqah (The reality): سورۃ الحَاقَّۃ 69:32

ثُمَّ فِي سِلْسِلَةٍ ذَرْعُهَا سَبْعُونَ ذِرَاعًا فَاسْلُكُوهُ

Then into a chain whose length is seventy cubits insert him."

 

Summary:

1.1YADD: Hand, power or possession.

1.2 YAMEEN: Right hand, complete ownership and authority.

1.3 SHIMAAL: Left hand, left side.

1.4 DIRAA’: Hand and a tool for measuring cubits.
