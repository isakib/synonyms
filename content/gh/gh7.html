7 GHAM

[sorrow]

Synonyms: GHAM(غم), HUZN(حزن), BASSA(بت)

7.1 gham(غم): The literal meaning of gham(غم) is to conceal and hide. Ghumma(غمي) meaning dust and darkness and ghumaam(غمام) meaning those clouds that conceal sunlight. And gham(غم) means distress and suffering.  Allah states that:

________________

Surat Al-E-Imran(Family of Imran) - سُوۡرَةُ آل عِمرَان

3:154

ثُمَّ أَنزَلَ عَلَيۡكُم مِّنۢ بَعۡدِ ٱلۡغَمِّ أَمَنَةً۬ نُّعَاسً۬ا يَغۡشَىٰ طَآٮِٕفَةً۬ مِّنكُمۡ‌ۖ

Then, after grief, He sent down security for you. As slumber did it overcome a party of you, while (the other) party, who were anxious on their own account, thought wrongly of Allah, the thought of ignorance. They said: Have we any part in the cause? Say (O Muhammad): The cause belongeth wholly to Allah. They hide within themselves (a thought) which they reveal not unto thee, saying: Had we had any part in the cause we should not have been slain here. Say: Even though ye had been in your houses, those appointed to be slain would have gone forth to the places where they were to lie. (All this hath been) in order that Allah might try what is in your breasts and prove what is in your hearts. Allah is Aware of what is hidden in the breasts (of men)

________________

7.2 huzn (حزن): means sadness. To feels distressed with regard to something and this depicts the restless nature of human beings. When nothing can be done about distress, it takes the form of sadness. And huzn(حزن) is also used for difficulties. Therefore, that kind of sadness which is because of some difficulty is called huzn(حزن).  Allah states that:

________________

Surat Yusuf - سُوۡرَةُ یُوسُف

12:84

وَٱبۡيَضَّتۡ عَيۡنَاهُ مِنَ ٱلۡحُزۡنِ

And he turned away from them and said: Alas, my grief for Joseph! And his eyes were whitened with the sorrow that he was suppressing.

________________

7.3 bassa (بت): means extreme sadness (شدت الحزن) that kind of sorrow which eats a man from within, a long period of grief and sorrow. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Yusuf - سُوۡرَةُ یُوسُف

12:86

إِنَّمَآ أَشۡكُواْ بَثِّى وَحُزۡنِىٓ إِلَى ٱللَّهِ

He said: I expose my distress and anguish only unto Allah, and I know from Allah that which ye know not.

________________

Summary:

1.GHAM(غم): a common word used for distress and sorrow.

2. HUZN(حزن): that kind of sadness whose source is some kind of difficulty.

3. BASSA(بت): extreme sorrow.
