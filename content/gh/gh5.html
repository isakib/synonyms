5 GHUSSA

[Anger]

Synonyms: SAKHATA(سخط), GHAIZ(غيظ), GHAZABA(غضب), HARD(حرد)

5.1 sakhata(سخط): meaning to do something unwillingly and sakht(سخط) means dislike. This is the first level of anger and this is always associated with elders towards younger ones. One can say سخط الأمير علي الحاجب but not سخط الحاجب علي الأمير. Allah states that:

________________

Surat Ai-E-Imran (Family of Imran) - سُوۡرَةُ آل عِمرَان

3:162

أَفَمَنِ ٱتَّبَعَ رِضۡوَٲنَ ٱللَّهِ كَمَنۢ بَآءَ بِسَخَطٍ۬ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ

Is one who followeth the pleasure of Allah as one who hath earned condemnation from Allah, whose habitation is the Fire, a hapless journey's end?

________________

5.2 ghaiz (غيظ): such type of displeasure which increases a man’s blood circulation and this is the second level of anger.  But in this case, there is no act of revenge. And ghaiz(غيظ) can also be associated with a person’s own self. Allah states that:

________________

Surat Al-E-Imran (Family of Imran) - سُوۡرَةُ آل عِمرَان

3:134

وَٱلۡڪَـٰظِمِينَ ٱلۡغَيۡظَ وَٱلۡعَافِينَ عَنِ ٱلنَّاسِ‌ۗ

Those who spend (of that which Allah hath given them) in ease and in adversity, those who control their wrath and are forgiving toward mankind; Allah loveth the good.

________________

5.3 ghazaba(غضب): this word is common to the people of lexical jurisprudence which is used for all types of anger. But this definition does not appear to be correct. Our beloved Prophet PBUH has defined ghazaba(غضب) as:  

________________

اتقوا من الغضب فانه جمرة توقدفى قلب ابن أدم ألم تروا الى انتفاخ اوداجه و جمرة عينىىه

save yourself from ghazaba() as it is that flame of fire which lights in the hearts of the sons of Adam. Don’t you see that such a person’s veins swell and his eyes become red?

________________

And obviously, this situation arises when a person becomes so full of anger that he wants to take revenge. And Ibn-alFaris has defined its meaning as severe displeasure. And others have defined ghazaba(غضب) as to hold grudge and be furious and ghazaba(غضب) always arises for some other person meaning  that it could be of young towards elders or elders towards young. Allah states that:

________________

Surat Ta-Ha - سُوۡرَةُ طٰه

20:81

وَمَن يَحۡلِلۡ عَلَيۡهِ غَضَبِى فَقَدۡ هَوَىٰ

Eat of the good things wherewith We have provided you, and transgress not in respect thereof lest My wrath come upon you: and he on whom My wrath cometh, he is lost indeed.

________________

5.4 hard(حرد): harid(حرد) means to be angry on someone and harada(حرد) means to forbid. Hard(حرد) means to attack someone or investigate after being overwhelmed with anger. Harad(حرد) actually is not the name of a situation of anger but that of such acts of revenge and vengeance which a person does after being overwhelmed with anger. And according to Abu-Halal, harad(حرد) means to payback in insanity in a state of anger. Allah states that:      

________________

Surat Al-Qalam (The Pen) - سُوۡرَةُ القَلَم

68:25

وَغَدَوۡاْ عَلَىٰ حَرۡدٍ۬ قَـٰدِرِينَ

They went betimes, strong in (this) purpose

________________

Summary:

1.SAKHT(سخط): Just displeasure, dislike. First level, of elders towards young.

2. GHAIZ(غيظ): when blood circulation increases. Second level; and ghaiz can also come at one’s own self.

3. GHAZABA(غضب): when a person takes revenge, overwhelmed with anger regardless of young or old. This is not associated with one’s self but towards others.

4. HARAD(حرد): The state of revenge because of severe anger, to approach quickly and to do something in an altered state of mind.
