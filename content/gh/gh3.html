3 GHALIB ANA, HONA, KERNA

[To dominate, to prevail]

Synonyms: ZAHARA(ظَهَر), AZZA(عَزّ), GHALABA(غلب), ISTA’LA(استقلي), QAHARA(قهر)

3.1 zahara(ظَهَر): There are two main points in the meaning of zahara (i) to become visible, to come in front, to become noticeable and (ii)strength. Then this word is also used in the material and semantic sense. It is stated:

Corruption doth appear on land and sea because of (the evil) which men's hands have done, that He may make them taste a part of that which they have done, in order that they may return.

________________

3.4 ista’la (استعلي): ala ya’lo(على يعلو) means to elevate. And ista’la(استعلي) means wanting to be elevated, the desire to become dominant and prevailing. Ta’alli(تعلي) means to boast. The words ta’alli(تعلي) and ista’la(استعلي) are usually used in the negative sense. And ala(علا) and ya’lo(يعلو) used in positive as well as negative sense. And aliya(علي) and ya’la(يعلي) are used in positive sense. Allah states that:

3.5 qahara (قهر): There are two main points: (i)to prevail (ii)to ridicule the submissive, to repress him. Then this word is also used separately for these meanings. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Al-Araf (The Heights) - سُوۡرَةُ الاٴعرَاف

7:127

قَالَ سَنُقَتِّلُ أَبۡنَآءَهُمۡ وَنَسۡتَحۡىِۦ نِسَآءَهُمۡ وَإِنَّا فَوۡقَهُمۡ قَـٰهِرُونَ

The chiefs of Pharaoh's people said: (O King), wilt thou suffer Moses and his people to make mischief in the land, and flout thee and thy gods? He said: We will slay their sons and spare their women, for lo! we are in power over them.

In this ayat, the word qahar(قهر) is giving both the meanings. And in the following ayat, it is only giving the meaning of repressing. Allah states that:

Surat Ad-Dhuha (The Morning Hours) - سُوۡرَةُ الِضُّحىٰ

93:9-10

فَأَمَّا ٱلۡيَتِيمَ فَلَا تَقۡهَرۡۖ وَأَمَّا ٱلسَّآٮِٕلَ فَلَا تَنۡہَرۡ

Therefore the orphan oppress not. Therefor the beggar drive not away.

________________

Summary:

1. ZAHARA(ظَهَر): meaning to come forward with force, to become evident, an upcoming force.

2. AZZA(عَزّ): meaning to be dominant

3. GHALABA(غلب): to become dominant by force.

4. ISTA’LA(استعلي): to have the hungry desire of becoming dominant.

5. QAHAR(قهر): to become dominant and ridicule, to repress the weak.
