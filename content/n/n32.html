32 NAIK – NAIK BAKHT [Pious, Fortunate]

Synonyms: SALEH, ABRAR, BARARAH, RASHEED, SAEED, MUTTAQEEN, RABBANI.

32.1 SALEH (صَالِح): SALAH (صَلَح) means to rectify a problem; its opposite is FASADA (فَسَدَ) ۔SALEH (صالح) means one who polishes himself. One who rectifies his soul. One who takes up virtue and leaves the vice and is working in rectifying himself. In the Quran

Surah Al-Kahf (The Cave): سورۃ الکھف 18:82

وَكَانَ أَبُوهُمَا صَالِحًا

 and their father had been righteous.

In another place in the Quran

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:56

وَلَا تُفْسِدُوا فِي الْأَرْضِ بَعْدَ إِصْلَاحِهَا

And cause not corruption upon the earth after its reformation

32.2 ABRAR (ابْرَار): (Singular BAR (بار)) BIRR (بِرّ) means to do good deeds on a large scale, BIRR (بِرّ) is not Virtue but quality to always be indulged in doing virtuous acts. (Opposite ITHM) Allah says in the Quran

Surah Ale- Imran (The family of Imran): سورۃ آل عِمْرن 3:92

لَن تَنَالُوا الْبِرَّ حَتَّىٰ تُنفِقُوا مِمَّا تُحِبُّونَ

 You will not attain virtuous conduct until you give of what you cherish.

BIRRA (بِرَّ) and BARR (بَارّ) is that person who is always ready to do good deeds. And when given a chance he does it. HAJJ-E-MABROORAS (حج مبروراس) is that Hajj which is done correctly according to the sunnah of Prophet Muhammad (S.A.A.W). And no mistake is made while doing it. In the Quran

Surah Maryam (Mary): سُوۡرَةُ مَریَم 19:14

وَبَرًّا بِوَالِدَيْهِ وَلَمْ يَكُن جَبَّارًا عَصِيًّا

And dutiful towards his parents, and he was neither an arrogant nor disobedient (to Allah or to his parents).

32.3 BARARA (بَرَرَةٌ): Plural of BARRA (بَرَّ) and is more prolific then BAARR (بَارّ), and has more exaggeration in it. In the Quran

Surah Abasa (He frowned): سورۃعَبَسَ 80:15-16

بِأَيْدِي سَفَرَةٍكِرَامٍ بَرَرَةٍ

(Written) by the hands of scribes- Honourable and Pious and Just.

32.4 RASHEED (رَشِیْد): RUSHD (رُشْد) means a straight path. To walk on the right path or keep following a straight road. And its opposite is GHAEE (غَیّ). Means to take up a wrong path. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:256

قَد تَّبَيَّنَ الرُّشْدُ مِنَ الْغَيِّ

Verily, the Right Path has become distinct from the wrong path.

And RASHEED (رَشِیْد) means one who is virtuous who has good habits and ways. A pious and reasonable person. In the Quran

Surah Hud (Hud): سورۃ ھود 11:78

فَاتَّقُوا اللَّهَ وَلَا تُخْزُونِ فِي ضَيْفِي ۖ أَلَيْسَ مِنكُمْ رَجُلٌ رَّشِيدٌ

So fear God, and do not embarrass me before my guests. Is there not one reasonable man among you?”

32.5 SAEED (سَعِیْد): A person who is pious by nature. A prosperous person and its opposite is SHAQEE (شَقِیّ) which means an unfortunate person. SA’ADA (سَعَدَ) means good luck. And God’s help by creating appropriate conditions for doing virtue. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Hud (Hud): سورۃ ھود 11:105

يَوْمَ يَأْتِ لَا تَكَلَّمُ نَفْسٌ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِ ۚ فَمِنْهُمْ شَقِيٌّ وَسَعِيدٌ

The Day it comes no soul will speak except by His permission. And among them will be the wretched and the prosperous.

32.6 MUTTAQEEN (مُتَّقِیْنَ) or MUTTAQOON (مُتَّقُونَ): (Singular MUTTAQI (متقی)) WAQQA (وَقٰی) means to save something from a dangerous thing and ITTAQAA (اِتَّقٰی) - TAQWAA (تقوٰی) (Opposite UDWAAN (عدوان)) means to abstain from evil deeds due to fear of God and his wrath. MUTAAQI (مُتَّقِیْ) means a God fearing and continent person, who restrains from forbidden things because of the fear of Allah (dictionary al Manjad). In the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:177

ۗ أُولَٰئِكَ الَّذِينَ صَدَقُوا ۖ وَأُولَٰئِكَ هُمُ الْمُتَّقُونَ

These are the sincere; these are the pious.

32.7  RABBANI (رَبَّانِی): This word is heavily debated by Imam Raghib. According to some scholars it means those who nurtures of knowledge.  Some relate it to the word RABB (رَبّ) as JISMANI (جِسْمَانِیٌّ) is related to JISM (جِسْمٌ) similarly RABBANI is related to RABB means a person devoted to Allah only. A Muslim monk.  According to manjad it means both a great scholar and a person devoted to Allah or a pious person. The second meaning seems more correct because in the Quran the word AHBAAR أَحْبَار (Scholar) comes separately with RABBANIYOON وَالرَّبَّانِيُّونَ  (5:44). And also the word RIBBIYYOON رِبِّيُّونَ is also used in Quran with same meaning (3:146). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Maeda (The table spread): سورۃ المَائدہ 5:63

لَوْلَا يَنْهَاهُمُ الرَّبَّانِيُّونَ وَالْأَحْبَارُ عَن قَوْلِهِمُ الْإِثْمَ وَأَكْلِهِمُ السُّحْتَ

Why do the rabbis and religious scholars not forbid them from saying what is sinful and devouring what is unlawful

Summary:

32.1 SALEH: One who rectifies himself. And also every kind of vice.

32.2 ABRAR: (BAR) One who is always ready to do good deeds. One who does a lot of good deeds.

32.3 BARARAH: (BARR) more exaggerated then BAR.

32.4 RASHEED: One with good habits and ways, pious.

32.5 SAEED: Pious by nature. Fortunate.

32.6 MUTTAQEEN: One who abstains from bad deeds due to fear of God, continent person.

32.7 RABBANI: A person devoted to Allah. A saint.
