12 NAZAR-O-NIYAZ [votive, fulfill a vow, offering]

Synonyms: NAZAR, QURBAN

12.1 NAZAR (نَذَر): (Plural NAZOOR (نذور)) means to make some nonobligatory thing an obligation upon oneself. NAZARA (نَذَرَ) means to make a votive, to fulfill a vow. Usually it is based on some conditional event but the offering of such votive is an obligation. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Hajj  (The Pilgrimage): سُوۡرَةُ الحَجّ 22:29

ثُمَّ لْيَقْضُوا تَفَثَهُمْ وَلْيُوفُوا نُذُورَهُمْ

Then let them end their untidiness and fulfill their vows

12.2 QURBAN (قُرْبَان): QURBAN-AL-MALK (قربان الملك) means friend of a king. QURBAN (قُرْبَان) refers to anything through which nearness of Allah is attained that can be sacrifice or anything (dictionary al Manjad). All these kind of offerings for anyone other than Allah is forbidden because it is type of worship but infidels offers votives to other things which they might consider as   honorable. In the Quran

Surah Al-Maeda (The table spread): سورۃ المَائدہ 5:27

إِذْ قَرَّبَا قُرْبَانًا فَتُقُبِّلَ مِنْ أَحَدِهِمَا وَلَمْ يُتَقَبَّلْ مِنَ الْآخَرِ

 when they both offered a sacrifice [to Allah ], and it was accepted from one of them but was not accepted from the other.

Summary:

12.1 NAZAR: A votive that is made obligatory by oneself.

12.2 QURBAN: A votive that is offered for pleasing Allah.
