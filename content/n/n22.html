22 NIKALNA

[To exit]

Synonyms: KHARAJA(خرج), BARAZA(برز), NAFARA(نفر), GHA’ZA(غزى), ZAHAQA(زهق), NAFAZA(نفذ), SALLALA(سلل), LAWAZA(لوازا), DAFAQA(دفق), SHARAQA(شرق), TALAA’(طلع)

22.1 kharaja(خرج): to exit, to come outside (ant:dakhala(دخل)), it is a famous word and its use is also common. It is stated in the Quran:

22.5 zahaqa (زهق): to run off, zahaq-an-nafs(زهق  النفس) meaning the exiting of soul from the body. Zahaq-al-batil(زهق الباطل) means the escaping of evil and zahiq(زاهق) meaning disheartened, discouraged, the one coming for a battle and running after failing. Also, zahaq(زهق) is a part of the set of opposites. Zahiq(زاهق) means a fat animal as well as a very weak and thin animal. Hence, zahaq(زهق) means to make some run off after defeating them as well as making them run off after weakening them. Allah states that:

22.11 talaa’ (طلع): the rising of ordinary planets and it also includes the sun. Thus, the word talaa’(طلع) is common whereas sharaqa(شرق) is only used with the rising of the sun. The girls of Bani Najaar used to sing a song when the Prophet PBUH would come, its first line was tala-al-badru-a’laina(طلع البدر علينا). In the Quran, the word talaa’(طلع) has not been used for the rising of planets, although it has been associated with fajr(dusk) (or with the sun) and the light of fajr is much less than the sun. Summary:

1. KHARAJA(خرج): to exit, use is common.

2. BARAZA(برز): to exit to the ground, to come in front.

3. NAFARA(نفر): to set off on war or mission.

4. GHAZA(غزى): this word is specific to setting off on jihad.

5. ZAHAQA(زهق): to run off after getting disheartened and discouraged.

6. NAFAZA(نفذ): to pass through.

7. SALLALA(سلل): to slip off.

8. LAAZ(لاذ): to set off in search of concealment.

9. DAFAQA(دفق): to move forward with force and strength, to set off with a jump.

10. SHARAQA(شرق): rising of sun.

11. TALAA’(طلع): rising of planets and stars(including the sun). It is common.
