25 NIGEHBAAN

[guardian]

Synonyms: HAAFIZ(حافظ), HAFEEZ(حفيظ), RAQEEB(رقيب), MUQEET(مقيت)( قوة), HARAS(حرس), MUHAIMIN(مهيمن)

25.1 haafiz(حافظ): ahfaz(احفظ) (ant:azaa’(اضاع)) meaning to save something from wastage and destruction, to protect. To protect something from outside dangers. Haafiz(حافظ) is a pronoun meaning the protector and hafeez(حفيظ) has the aspect of addition i.e. the protect who protects at all times. Allah states that:

Hence, raqeeb(رقيب) is that kind of protector who is cautious himself at all times, and whoever has a raqeeb(رقيب), there is nothing hidden between them. It is stated in the Quran:

25.3 muqeet(مقيت): quwwa(قوة) qaata(قات) meaning to provide sustenance, provision, to be a custodian, and aaqat(اقات) meaning to have power, to protect, to provide provision and muqeet(مقيت) means the people of power, guardian and custodian. Thus muqeet(مقيت) is such a guardian who is self sufficient. According to Ibn-al-Faris, three things are included in muqeet: (i) to have power on something (ii) protection (iii) generous thus muqeet(مقيت) is that powerful being who has the complete power to protect you. 25.5 muhaimin(مهيمن): according to Ibn-al-Faris and some other scholars, define this as the one who provides peace and haiman-at-tairu a’la farakhihi(هيمن الطائر على فراخه) means the bird spread it feathers on her child. Muhaimin(مهيمن) is that one who (i) gives peace to someone from fear (ii) doesn’t let the rights of someone be neglected. This word has been used in the Quran twice and came in both these meanings. Summary:

1. HAAFIZ(حافظ): the one who saves something from destruction.

2. RAQEEB(رقيب): that protector who himself remain cautious as well as keeps an eye on all activities of the other thing.

3. MUQEET(مقيت): that protector who has complete power on protection;

4. HARAS(حرس): guard, watchman, the one who arrests, protection, era.

5. MUHAIMIN(مهيمن): the one who protects after offering refuge.
