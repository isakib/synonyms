16 LAINA [to take]

Synonyms: AKHAZA, TALAQQA, HAAUM

16.1 AKHAZA (اَخَذ): To acquire something, to take under ownership, to hold in hands. And it is used both semantically and receptively. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Ale- Imran (The family of Imran): سورۃ آل عِمْرن 3:81

وَإِذْ أَخَذَ اللَّهُ مِيثَاقَ النَّبِيِّينَ

And [recall, O People of the Scripture], when Allah took the covenant of the prophets,

In another place in the Quran

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:154

وَلَمَّا سَكَتَ عَن مُّوسَى الْغَضَبُ أَخَذَ الْأَلْوَاحَ

And when the anger subsided in Moses, he took up the tablets;

16.2 TALAQQA (تَلَقّٰی): LAQIYA (لَقِیَ) means to meet, to gather, TALQQA-AS-SHAI (تلقی الشئ) means to greet someone, to meet (dictionary al Manjad). Thus TALQQA (تَلَقّٰی) means to take something by visiting or meeting.

Surah Al-Anbiya (the prophets): سورۃ الانَبیاء 21:103

لَا يَحْزُنُهُمُ الْفَزَعُ الْأَكْبَرُ وَتَتَلَقَّاهُمُ الْمَلَائِكَةُ

The greatest alarm will not grieve them, and the Angels will receive them:

16.3 HAAUM (ھَاءُمْ): HAU’ (ھوء) is a verbal noun which means KHUZ (خُذْ) meaning ‘take’ or ‘hold’ and only the verbal side is used. And its conjugation is  ھَاءُمْ ھَاءُمَا ھَاءُمُوْا   . According to dictionary al Manjad the word HA (ھَا) means ‘take’ and HAL-KITAAB (ھَا الْکِتَاب) means ‘take the book’. HAA’ (ھَاءُ) (HAU’ (ھوء)) also means the same. HAA’ YA RAJUL (ھاء یا رجل) means ‘O man! Take this book’ (dictionary al Manjad). Thus this word refers to calling someone and telling him to take something. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Haqqah (The reality): سورۃ الحَاقَّۃ 69:19

فَأَمَّا مَنْ أُوتِيَ كِتَابَهُ بِيَمِينِهِ فَيَقُولُ هَاؤُمُ اقْرَءُوا كِتَابِيَهْ

Then, as for him who is given his record in his right hand, he will say: Take, read my book!

Summary:

16.1 AKHAZA: To take something, to hold it. Commonly used.

16.2 TALAQQA: To go near to something and take it.

16.3 HAAUM: To call someone and ask him to take something.
