1 LAATHI

[Staff, stick]

Synonyms: A’SA(عصا)(A’SU)( عصو), MINSA’TA(منساءة )(NISA’)( نساء)

1.1 a’sa(عصا): meaning the staff of a shepherd, a baton. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Al-Araf (The Heights) - سُوۡرَةُ الاٴعرَاف

7:107

فَأَلۡقَىٰ عَصَاهُ فَإِذَا هِىَ ثُعۡبَانٌ۬ مُّبِينٌ۬

Then he flung down his staff and lo! it was a serpent manifest;

________________

1.2 minsa’ta (منساءة): the stick of a patient or elderly. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Saba  - سُوۡرَةُ سَبَإ

34:14

مَا دَلَّهُمۡ عَلَىٰ مَوۡتِهِۦۤ إِلَّا دَآبَّةُ ٱلۡأَرۡضِ تَأۡڪُلُ مِنسَأَتَهُۖ ۥ

And when We decreed death for him, nothing showed his death to them save a creeping creature of the earth which gnawed away his staff. And when he fell the jinn saw clearly how, if they had known the Unseen, they would not have continued in despised toil.

________________

And that type of stick, one end of which is twisted is called mihjan(محجن). The ends of a’sa(عصا) and minsa’ta(منساءة) are not twisted. A’sa(عصا) is a thick and strong stick and minsa’ta(منساءة) is a relatively thin and fine looking stick.

For greed(lalach)( لالچ), see ‘tama rakhna’(طمع ركهنا).
