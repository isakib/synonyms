How to use QuranSynonyms

QuranSynonyms.com is the first effort in the English language in getting the synonyms of the Quran and compiling their meanings onto one site, so we can see why Allah (azza wa jal) spoke to us one word over another in the Qur’an, and what subtlety was being implied in the Quranic word which was chosen.

QuranSynonyms relation to Picturesque Arabic:

Arabic is an extremely visual language, so every word and sentence should be imagined as a picture. This is how you will understand the Quran the best. Every word will show you an image in your mind, and we hope to bring these images to life in your imagination inshaa Allah to inspire your relationship with Allah’s book in new and awesome ways.

Your job will be to visually look at the meanings of words and the examples we have given with them to imagine the image which Allah is portraying in your mind. We will now tell you how you can do this.

QuranSynonyms is layed out in the following structure:

1 – Quran Ayah (Arabic).

2 – A simple translation of the meaning of that ayah.

3 – The Meanings of the words in that ayah in-depth.

4 – Antonyms – the opposite meaning of the word (of  step 3)

5 – Synonyms – words which are similar to the words (of  step 3).

3 - The indepth Meanings: of the words in the Quran has not been focused in the English language before, when this is infact the most miraculous aspect of the Quranic language! QuranSynonyms  wants to show you the word meaning in every ayah, and the picture associated with it so the image comes to life in your mind.

4 – Antonyms: To enhance your understanding of the meaning of the word, Antonyms have been added because ‘words are known by their opposites.’ You can only know the color white because you have seen the colour black, the same goes for Arabic words. We want you to see the black and the white, so you can get a clear overview of why Allah chose one word especially.

(Example:  Fasaad generally means Corruption, so what is its antonym? It is SulH which generally means ‘to make Peace.’ But we go beyond this, the root meaning of SulH is actually ‘to Connect two broken separated things/people together.’ With knowledge of the Antonym, we’ve figured that Fasaad (Corruption) is mainly to break people and things apart.)

5 – Synonyms:  Words in Arabic are different shades of meaning. Since every word is a visual graphic picture, you wonder why one word is chosen especially compared to other words similar to it.

Example: Flowing water - there are different types of flowing of water, some being violent while others being light and dribbling, Arabic has a word for each of these concepts. We show you the comparison between all these Synonym words so you can see why Allah described and chose this word specifically.
