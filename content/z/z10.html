10 – Adornment

 

The words زِينَة, زُخرُف, رِيش, زُهرَة and جَمَال have been used in the holy Quran

 

10.1 ZEENAH (زِينَة): means to decorate, embellish, ornament; is commonly used.  Used with both tangible and intangible things as well as with reference to physical and external adornment.

 

An example of external adornment:

 

Surat Al-Qaşaş (The Stories) - القصص سورة

28:79

 فَخَرَجَ عَلَىٰ قَوۡمِهِۦ فِى زِينَتِهِۦ‌ۖ (﻿٧٩﻿)

So he came out before his people in his adornment (79)

 

An example of physical adornment:

 

Surat Al-'A`rāf (The Heights) - لأعرافا سورة

7:32

 قُلۡ مَنۡ حَرَّمَ زِينَةَ ٱللَّهِ ٱلَّتِىٓ أَخۡرَجَ لِعِبَادِهِۦ (﻿٣٢﻿)

Say, "Who has forbidden the adornment of Allah which He has produced for His servants (32)

 

An example of intangible adornment:

 

Surat Al-'An`ām (The Cattle) - الأنعام سورة

6:43

 وَزَيَّنَ لَهُمُ ٱلشَّيۡطَـٰنُ مَا ڪَانُواْ يَعۡمَلُونَ (﻿٤٣﻿)

And Satan made attractive to them that which they were doing (43)

 

10.2 ZUKHRUF (زُخرُف): can mean (1) apparent ornamentation; beautification that is attained through fabrication/coating.  The phrase الكلام رخرف refers to adorning a statement with lies. For example, Allah (the exalted) states in the holy Quran:

 

Surat Al-'An`ām (The Cattle) - الأنعام سورة

6:112

يُوحِى بَعۡضُهُمۡ إِلَىٰ بَعۡضٍ۬ زُخۡرُفَ ٱلۡقَوۡلِ غُرُورً۬ا‌ۚ (﻿١١٢﻿)

Inspiring to one another decorative speech in delusion (112)

 

 

(2) The term can also refer to the different forms and colors of plants.

 

Surat Yūnus (Jonah) - يونس سورة

10:24

حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَآ أَخَذَتِ ٱلۡأَرۡضُ زُخۡرُفَهَا (﻿٢٤﻿)

Until, when the earth has taken on its adornment (24)

 

10.3 REESHA (رِيش): الطاءر ريش refers to the feathers of birds, especially those of eagles.  رَيَّاش is one who decorates arrows with the feathers of birds.  Because the feathers of a bird are its clothing, this term began to be used metaphorically for luxurious attire – beautiful clothing.

 

Surat Al-'A`rāf (The Heights) - الأعراف سورة

7:26

يَـٰبَنِىٓ ءَادَمَ قَدۡ أَنزَلۡنَا عَلَيۡكُمۡ لِبَاسً۬ا يُوَٲرِى سَوۡءَٲتِكُمۡ وَرِيشً۬ا‌ۖ (﻿٢٦﻿)

O children of Adam, We have bestowed upon you clothing to conceal your private parts and as adornment (26)

 

10.4 ZAHRAH (زُهرَة): apparent attractiveness, eye candy, gloss and glitter, something lustrous.  The phrase الدنيا زهرة refers to the apparent luster and splendor of the worldly life.  The term is usually used in the context of this unsubstantial world, its attractiveness and sparkle.

 

Surat Ţāhā (Ta-Ha) - طه سورة

20:131

 وَلَا تَمُدَّنَّ عَيۡنَيۡكَ إِلَىٰ مَا مَتَّعۡنَا بِهِۦۤ أَزۡوَٲجً۬ا مِّنۡہُمۡ زَهۡرَةَ ٱلۡحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنۡيَا (﻿١٣١﻿)

And do not extend your eyes toward that by which We have given enjoyment to [some] categories of them, [its being but] the splendor of worldly life (131)

 

10.5 JAMAL (جَمَال): can refer to (1) the apparent/outer beauty of something, (2) qualities related to one’s character or (3) well decorated surroundings/environment.  In the Quran, the term has been used to describe goodness of deeds, character and apparent actions as well as observable beauty.  Originally the term referred only to internal beauty.

 

Surat An-Naĥl (The Bee) - النحل سورة

16:6

 وَلَكُمۡ فِيهَا جَمَالٌ حِينَ تُرِيحُونَ وَحِينَ تَسۡرَحُونَ (﻿٦﻿)

And for you in them is [the enjoyment of] beauty when you bring them in [for the evening] and when you send them out [to pasture] (6)

 

Summary:

1.      زِينَة: common term for adornment and decoration

2.      زُخرُف: adornment attained through coating

3.      رِيش: adornment attained through luxurious attire and beautification

4.      زُهرَة: used for the luster and charm of the worldly life

5.      جَمَال: used for apparent beauty, beauty of character and beautification of the environment
