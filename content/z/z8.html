8 – To Give Life

 

The words اَحيَا, بَعَثَ and اَنشَرَ have been used in the holy Quran

 

8.1 AHYAA (اَحيَا): حَيِيَ means to be alive or to stay alive.  اَحيَا (opposite – اَمَاتَ) refers to make something dead alive.  This term is commonly used.

 

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - البقرة سورة

2:73

 كَذَٲلِكَ يُحۡىِ ٱللَّهُ ٱلۡمَوۡتَىٰ وَيُرِيڪُمۡ ءَايَـٰتِهِۦ (﻿٧٣﻿)

Thus does Allah bring the dead to life, and He shows you His signs (73)

 

8.2 BA’AATHA (بَعَثَ): contains two essential meanings – to raise and to send alone.  If the term is being used in the context of the dead laying in their graves than it means to give them life, raise them and make them go toward the ground of Mehshar (the gathering ground on the Day of Judgment).

 

Surat Al-Ĥaj (The Pilgrimage) - الحج سورة

22:7

 وَأَنَّ ٱلسَّاعَةَ ءَاتِيَةٌ۬ لَّا رَيۡبَ فِيہَا وَأَنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَبۡعَثُ مَن فِى ٱلۡقُبُورِ (﻿٧﻿)

And [that they may know] that the Hour is coming - no doubt about it - and that Allah will resurrect those in the graves.

 

8.3 ANSHARA (اَنشَرَ): originally means to spread (opposite – طوي).  The phrase نشورا الميّت نشر refers to the dead being given life again.  النُّشُور اِلَيهِ includes the meanings of becoming alive, rising and spreading; it refers to being given life, spreading and going towards Allah.  اَنشَرَ means to give life and to spread in the ground of Mehshar (the gathering ground on the Day of Judgment).

 

Surat `Abasa (He Frowned) - عبس سورة

80:21-22

 ثُمَّ أَمَاتَهُ ۥ فَأَقۡبَرَهُ ۥ (﻿٢١﻿) ثُمَّ إِذَا شَآءَ أَنشَرَهُ ۥ (﻿٢٢﻿)

Then He causes his death and provides a grave for him (21). Then when He wills, He will resurrect him (22).

 

Summary:

1.      اَحيَا: commonly used term, to give life to anything dead

2.      بَعَثَ: to give life to the dead laying in the graves and to raise them

3.      اَنشَرَ: to give life, to raise and spread
