4 ZAMANA [time period, epoch]

Synonyms: DAHR, ASR, QARN, HUQBA, RAIB-AL-MANOON

4.1 DAHR (دَھْر): The time period since the creation of the universe till the end of it. According to Ibn-Al-Faris, DAHARA(دَھَرَ) has the essence of domination, and DAHR (دَھْر) is used to for time because it is faced (experienced) by everyone and dominates them. The Prophet said “لَا تَسُبُّواالدَّھْرَفَاِنَّ اﷲَ ھُوَالدَّھْر” translation: Do not revile time for Allah is time. DAHIR (دھری) means an atheist. Allah says in Quran

Surah Al-Insan (The Man): سورۃ الانْسان 76:1

هَلۡ أَتَىٰ عَلَى ٱلۡإِنسَـٰنِ حِينٌ۬ مِّنَ ٱلدَّهۡرِ لَمۡ يَكُن شَيۡـًٔ۬ا مَّذۡكُورًا

Has there come upon man a period of time when he was nothing to be mentioned?

4.2 ‘ASR (عَصْر): Last part of the day, Afternoon or Time. The time-period of days and nights until they last. Rather it should be the time when man came into being till the day of resurrection because Allah takes ‘Asr as a witness. And if the man did not exist then how could this be a witness? Allah Knows best. In Quran

Surah Al Asr (The Time): سورۃ العصر 103:1

وَٱلۡعَصۡرِ إِنَّ ٱلۡإِنسَـٰنَ لَفِى خُسۡرٍ

By time. The human being is in loss.

Certainly days and nights were made long before the creation of man. According to the above mentioned definition the plural of ‘ASR(عَصْر) and DAHR(دَھْر) do not exist, but according to dictionary, the plural of DAHR(دَھْر) is DUHOOR(دُھُوْر), and of ‘ASR (عَصْر) is USOOR(عُصُوْر). This is because the common meaning of DHAR (دَھْر) and ASR (عَصْر) is taken as an extended time period.

4.3 QARN (قَرْنْ): a century, people belonging to a same era, people of same historic period. Therefore QARN (قَرْنْ) can relate to a certain age or time period and also to the people of that age. Like QARYAH (قَرْیَۃ) (plural QAROON (قرون)) means a village and the people belonging to it. In Quran this word is mostly used for people of an age. For instance

Surah Ta-Ha (Ta-ha): سورۃ طٰھٰ 20:51

قَالَ فَمَا بَالُ ٱلۡقُرُونِ ٱلۡأُولَىٰ

(Pharaoh) said: "What then is the condition of previous generations?"

4.4 HUQBAH (حُقْبَۃ): period of eighty (80) years or more, long period of time, unspecified time period. Its plural is both HUQUB (حُقْبَ) and AHQAB (احْقَابَ) meaning centuries. The natives say that the word is taken from HAQEEBAH(حقیبۃ) which means the leather tote for carrying luggage that a rider fastens to his saddle - thus it’s not a part of time rather adverb of time and place. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Kahf (The Cave): سورۃ الکھف 18:60

حَتَّىٰٓ أَبۡلُغَ مَجۡمَعَ ٱلۡبَحۡرَيۡنِ أَوۡ أَمۡضِىَ حُقُبً۬ا

until I reach the junction of the two rivers, even if it takes me years.”

4.5 RAIB-AL-MANOON(رَیْبَ اْلمَنُوْن) : an idiom which means the alterations of time, Usually refers to accidents or calamity of time in a negative way; e.g. when someone goes through a difficult phase for a time or longer. In the Quran:

Surah At-Tur (The Mount): سورۃ الطُّور 52:30

أَمۡ يَقُولُونَ شَاعِرٌ۬ نَّتَرَبَّصُ بِهِۦ رَيۡبَ ٱلۡمَنُونِ

Or do they say, “A poet—we await for him a calamity of time”?

Summary:

4.1 DAHR: time from the creation of universe to the end of it.

4.2 ASR: time from creation of mankind till the day of resurrection.

4.3 QARN: Era or People of that era

4.4 HUQBAH: A long time period. Eighty years or more

4.5 RAIB-AL-MANOON : accidents of time. (see GARDISH-E-AYYAM)
