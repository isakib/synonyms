9 – To be Alive, stay Alive, keep Alive

 

The words حَيِيَ (حَيَّ), عَاشَ and اِستِحيَاءَ have been used in the holy Quran

 

9.1 HAIYA (حَيِيَ): means to be alive, to stay alive.  The term حَيٰوة means life, it is used in a wide variety of contexts, it is well established that animals have life but even inanimate objects show traces of life.  Allah (the All Mighty), His angels and the jinn are all alive.  This term is commonly used.  حَيٌّ refers to anything that is alive.

 

Surat Al-Mu'minūn (The Believers) - المؤمنون سورة

23:37

 إِنۡ هِىَ إِلَّا حَيَاتُنَا ٱلدُّنۡيَا نَمُوتُ وَنَحۡيَا وَمَا نَحۡنُ بِمَبۡعُوثِينَ (﻿٣٧﻿)

Life is not but our worldly life - we die and live, but we will not be resurrected (37)

 

9.2 A’AASHA (عَاشَ): means to stay alive.  عَيش refers to the kind of life that is found in animals, in other words beings that subsist because of food and drink.  This term is thus more specific than حَيِيَ.  مَعِيشَت (plural – مَعَائِش) refers to the things necessary for existence.

 

Surat Al-'A`rāf (The Heights) - الأعراف سورة

7:10

 وَلَقَدۡ مَكَّنَّـٰڪُمۡ فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ وَجَعَلۡنَا لَكُمۡ فِيہَا مَعَـٰيِشَ‌ (﻿١٠﻿)

And We have certainly established you upon the earth and made for you therein ways of livelihood (10).

 

9.3 ISTIHYAA (اِستِحيَاءَ): حَيِيَ has the infinitives: حيوة – life and حَيَاء – modesty.  اِستِحيَاءَ means to be/seek modesty, to keep alive or to let something live.  The meaning of ‘letting something live’ is implied when in comparison something else is being made to die.

 

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - البقرة سورة

2:49

 يُذَبِّحُونَ أَبۡنَآءَكُمۡ وَيَسۡتَحۡيُونَ نِسَآءَكُمۡ‌ۚ (﻿٤٩﻿)

Slaughtering your [newborn] sons and keeping your females alive (49).

 

Summary:

1.      حَيِيَ: the life of every living thing

2.      عَاشَ: the life of every such living being which is dependent upon food and drink for life

3.      اِستِحيَاءَ: to let someone else live
