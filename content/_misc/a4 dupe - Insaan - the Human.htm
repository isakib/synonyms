Word: الْإِنسَانَ:Meaning:Human.
The words used for it in the Quran are:
Ins and its derivatives like Insaan,insiyyaN, naas,unaasi etc. Besides these, words used in the Quran are Aadam and Bashar.

i.In Ghareeb ul Quran many meanings of Ins are given. Ibn e
Qutaiba writes that Insaan is called 'Ins' because he is apparent
and can be perceived with the eye.He quotes the following verse
from the Quran as the reason :
I have perceived a fire28:29

In this sense the opposite of Ins is Jinn,which means hidden
and cannot be perceived with the eye.Because of this it is
conceivable that Allah SWT has called these two creations
'thaqalaan' ,and have been used opposite each other.Allah SWT
says:
And I did not create the jinn and mankind except to worship Me.51:56.


ii.Ibn e Abbas(ra) narrates that Insaan is called 'Insaan' or
(InsiyyaN) because he forgot what he had promised/committed
to,hence the root of Insaan or Insiyyan is 'Nasi'(to forget).

iii. Imam Raghib(rh.a) says it is derived from 'Uns'(familiar).That is,
a creation which is attached to one another,and has been created
to live together in the society.Who are familiar with and know
each other. As such, its opposite would be Wahsh(savage,alien).
(Mf).
iv. Ibn al Faaris has submitted these two: (1)To be apparent.
(2) Anything different from savage and barbaric ways.(M-L)
'Ins' is a generic noun,that is,'Ins' can be used for one man or the
whole of mankind.The same is the case for the word 'Insaan',but
usually this word is used for the all mankind.The singular for
this is Insiyu.The Quran talks about it :
And if you see from among humanity anyone, say, 'Indeed, I have vowed to the Most Merciful abstention, so I will not speak today to [any] man.' "(19:26)


The plural for it are:Naas,Unaas,and Unasyyi.
The word Naas is used in general terms.If it is said "O mankind",then it would mean that all humans from the present and future are being addressed.
Allah SWT says:
O mankind, worship your Lord, who created you (2:21)

1.Unaas: A group of people who differ from each other because they
are divided by tribes or for some other reason.Allah SWT
says:
and every people knew its watering place.(2:60)
And a very big group of people is called Unasyyi.Such as:
We created of numerous livestock and men.(25:49)

2. Aadam:the root for this is adam which basically means
compatibility and tenderness/flexibility(M-L),and
'adaam' is used for everything that is compatible and
tender(Mnjd).The evidence for this is what the Prophet
SAW said to Mugheerah bin She'bah(RA) before
proposing to a woman for nikah:
Look at her, because it is more fitting that love and compatibility be established between you(Tirmizi,Nisaee).

Many other reasons are cited,but the most likely is the one cited above,as it is also supported by the Hadith.
Aadam:is the name of the the father of all humans Aadam (AS),but it can also be applied to people in general(Mnjd).So when it is said 'Children of Aadam',it means all human beings.
So,where ever Aadam or Bani Aadam (Children of Aadam) are mentioned in the Quran,it is in connection with human history.For example;creation of Aadam from clay,Covenant of Alast,prostration of the angels,his being the Best of Creations,shaitan misleading Aadam (AS) and Bani Aadam and his dialogue with Allah SWT etc etc.'


3.Bashar: basically means for something to appear in all its beauty.
(M-L)Whereas Uns means to just appear.The top layer
of the human skin is called Al basharah. As mentioned
in the Quran:
Blackening the skins.(74:29)
And its plural are BasharuN andAbshaaruN, and humans are called 'bashar' because, compared to other animals, their skin is nearly free of any wool,fur and hair and is clearly visible.(Mf)
The word bashar is used for singular,plural masculine and feminine,but its tasniyah (for two) is Basharain. As mentioned in the Quran:
"Should we believe two men like ourselves..."(23-47)
The word bashar is used when the aim is to discuss a human being from various aspects,for example; natural,material,physical formation,biological desires and weaknesses.This is evident from the verse given above.
In contrast of bashar is the word Malak (angel) which is free from all such aspects:material, that is the apparent physical form and biological desires.
The non believers always had one objection,that how can we believe in a bashar /human who is born and dies,eats,drinks,walks about in the market place and who satisfies all his needs just like us.What is superior about Him that He should be believed in.It would have been another matter if it had been an angel,free from all needs/desires.
Given below are the verses in which Allah SWT has replied to these objections:
And what prevented the people from believing when guidance came to them except that they said, "Has Allah sent a human messenger?"(17:94)
Say, "If there were upon the earth angels walking securely, We would have sent down to them from the heaven an angel [as a] messenger."(17:9).

Conclusion:
Ins and its derivatives are used whenever humans' social aspects are discussed .When they are discussed from a historical point of view;Aadam and Bani Aadam are used,and Bashar when he is being discussed from the aspect of his natural or biological needs.
