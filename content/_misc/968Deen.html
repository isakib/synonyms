Deen (Daanah, Yadeenuh, Deen)

Meaning Subjugated him, desired his servitude, demeaned/disgraced him. "Daan-ur-Rajul" means Honoured him, obeyed him, disobeyed him. (meem-qaaf-munjad).

So, "deen" may reflect two exactly opposite meanings, the absolute rulership or sovereignty as well as the absolute allegiance, slavery and humiliation. Allah, The Majestic says:

أَلَا لِلَّهِ الدِّينُ الْخَالِصُ

"Surely, the religion (i.e. the worship and the obedience) is for Allâh only." [39:3]

Another meaning of it is that the absolute soveregnty is for ALLAH alone. As Allah, The Exhalted says:

وَقَاتِلُوهُمْ حَتَّىٰ لَا تَكُونَ فِتْنَةٌ وَيَكُونَ الدِّينُ لِلَّهِ

"And fight them until there is no more Fitnah (disbelief and worshipping of others along with Allâh) and (all and every kind of) Lordship is for Allâh (Alone). " [2:174]

Moreover, "deen" could also mean the laws related to penology. Allah The Most High says:

مَا كَانَ لِيَأْخُذَ أَخَاهُ فِي دِينِ الْمَلِكِ

"He could not take his brother by the law of the king (as a slave)." [12:74] 

And it also means the Power of law enforcement. As He says:

فَلَوْلَا إِنْ كُنْتُمْ غَيْرَ مَدِينِينَ تَرْجِعُونَهَا إِنْ كُنْتُمْ صَادِقِينَ

"Then why do you not, if you are exempt from the reckoning and recompense (punishment) Bring back the soul (to its body), if you are truthful?" [56:87]

a)ALLAH's complete and absolute authority

c) law of reward and punishment

punishment

Surat Az-Zumar (The Troops)[39:3]

أَلَا لِلَّهِ الدِّينُ الْخَالِصُ ۚ

Unquestionably, for Allah is the pure religion

in above ayah this word represents all the four meanings.now DEEN requires that ALLAH command his slaves for some thing ,to forbid something and if someone disobey to punish him . so all those commandments from ADAM(AS) to MUHAMMAD(PBUH) remains unchanged,this is real DEEN,for e.g. Shirk(polytheism),Unjustified Killing,Theft,Fornication/Adultery, abstinence from Shamelessness and the reckoning of judgement day.and submitting and obeying these commandments of ALLAH is ISLAM

إِنَّ الدِّينَ عِندَ اللَّهِ الْإِسْلَامُ ۗ

Indeed, the religion in the sight of Allah is Islam

2 SHARIAH( شريعة):SHARAA(شَرعَ) literaly meaning to set out clear path and shariah meaning those commandments which varies as per the need and requirements of the time for e.g. It was allowed between children of ADAM to mary brother and sister that was a temporary command that was made unlawful in later shariah. likewise at that time prostration of respect was lawful which was made unlawful later on.In former shariahs it was unlawful to enjoy spoils of war but it is allowed for Muslim Ummah.The point is there are numerous example.

Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) himself explained the difference between DEEN and Shariah in his own words as

الَنبياء اِخوةإلعلّات امّهاتهم شتّي و دينهم واحد

All messengers are ALLATI(all brothers whose father are one but different mothers) brothers .as there mothers (SHARIAH) are different and their DEEN(father) is one.

شَرَعَ لَكُم مِّنَ الدِّينِ

He has ordained for you of religion

3 MILLAT(مِلّت):in DEEN and Shariah everything is from ALLAH and inscribed in the Divinely Revealed Book.First of all the messenger have to bound himself to act upon these commandments. then by preaching and propagating these commandments he has to establish a team of followers and bound them as well of these commandments and thus establish Islamic system this system is MILLAT.so MILLAT is not the sayings and commandments but it is the system in which It's mandatory to obey AMEER(Leader) by his followers (JAMAAH) whose consititution is ALLAH's commandment (DEEN+SHARIAH)and this system includes messenger,leader,follower,consitution

translators often translate MILLAT as DEEN.but DEEN is the same of all messengers whereas MILLAT is specific to a particular messenger

Summary

1 DEEN:those commandments by ALLAH which remains unchanged from ADAM(as) to prophet Muhammad (PBUH) and to obey them

2 SHARIAH:those commandments by ALLAH which varies as per the time and era
