3rd word:

‘Daykhana’ (to see)

Forms:ى رَا (رءى) بَصَرَ نَظَرَ بَصُرَ انَسَ زَار
Info: reey, nazara, basara, basora,aanasaa and zaar are synonyms.
All of these words are related to the actions of the heart. These words do not mean just to see physically with the eye rather they mean, “to perceive with the heart and the intellect.”

  • رَاى (to perceive in depth/ to see) It can refer to physically perceiving with the eyes or reflecting upon something with the intellect. It is also used commonly for considering or contemplating. The reflection can take place while the person is awake or while they are dreaming. Meaning final and concrete understanding after careful consideration.
    • To see with the eyes (Ar-Ra’d 13:4)
    • Reflecting/ considering (Al-Ma’un 107:1)
  • نَظَرَ (to cast a glance at/ to look) To make the effort to see regardless of whether you are actually able to see the object or not. It is used in both contexts.
    • To actually see (At-Tawbah 9:137)
    • To focus upon or aim for (An-Naml 27:33)

نَظَرَ is generally used for seeing with the eyes.

  • بَصَرَ is used for perception and understanding. It means ‘to discern/recognize with the heart’ and it is very rarely used for just looking with the eyes (7:198). In this ayat all three synonyms رَاىَ, نَظَرَ and بَصَرَ are used and their different connotations are clear. رَاىَ means to see/consider, نَظَرَ is used to glance with the physical eyes andبَصَرَ is used to perceive and recognize with the heart.

The word بَصُرَ is even more specific in its meaning. It implies an idea that occurred to him and did not occur to anyone else. (Al-‘Araf 20:96)

  • انسَ does not mean to consider, neither does it mean to look, nor does it mean to recognize with the heart. It means to be familiar with something or to recognize it by its characteristics. Imam Raghib said that ‘uns panaa’ (urdu) means ‘to become familiar with.’ However, in the context of the language it is usually translated as ‘looking/seeing’, meaning to look from far away or observe keenly and recognize. (Al-‘Araf 20:10)(An-Nisa’ 4:6)
  • زَارَ: زَارَة means to travel to meet someone. It also means to come to see something. ‘ziyarat karna’ (urdu) and زُوْر meaning the upper part of the chest. زُرْتُ فَلَاناً means I turned my chest towards him or turned my chest to meet his (indicating ‘met with’) (At-Takathur 102: 1-2).


Summary:

·          

    • رَاى : to consider/reflect upon something
    • نَظَرَ : (general usage) to look at/ to glance
    • بَصُرَ: to discern/ recognize with the heart
    • انَسَ: to figure out/ to get
    • زَارَ : to travel to see/meet
