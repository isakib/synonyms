64 – To Sear

 

شَوَى and حَنَذ have been used to refer to searing/browning

 

64.1 شَوَى: Refers to searing meat on fire. Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat Al-Kahf (The Cave) - الكهف سورة

18:29

(﻿٢٩﻿)ٱلۡوُجُوهَ‌ يَشۡوِى كَٱلۡمُهۡلِ بِمَآءٍ۬ يُغَاثُواْ يَسۡتَغِيثُواْ وَإِن

And if they call for relief, they will be relieved with water like murky oil, which scalds [their] faces (29).

____________________________________

 

64.2 حَنَذ: الحَنَذة refers to extreme heat and حَنيذ can refer to hot water or seared meat.  الشَمس حَنَذَهُ refers to a sunburn. حنذ actually refers to use heat to drive out viscosity and juices. For example, Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat Hūd (Hud) -  هود سورة

11:69

(﻿٦٩﻿)حَنِيذٍ۬بِعِجۡلٍ جَآءَ أَن لَبِثَ فَمَا

And he [Abraham] did not delay in bringing [them] a roasted calf (69).

________________________________________

 

Summary:

1.      شَوَى: to sear or cook meat (or something else) on fire

2.      حَنَذ: to use any type of heat to drive out the juices in meat (or something else).  As is related about Abraham (peace be upon him) that skinned the calf and placed in between two extremely hot rocks to cook it and make it edible.
