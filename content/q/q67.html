67 – Son or Daughter

 

The words ولد, وليد, مولود, إبن, بنت have been used

 

67.1 ولد, وليد and مولود: every child that is begotten by a mother is a ولد, وليد, and مولود.  These three words are used both in masculine and feminine tenses.  Linguistically the feminine of وليد is وليده, but it is not used in the Quran.  If there is any difference in these three words it is that the word ولد is applicable to any age.  An old man would be considered a ولد of his parents in the same way that he was considered a ولد when he was born.  The term وليد is used for a child from birth till puberty.  As Allah (the exalted) says:

 

Surat Ash-Shu`arā' (The Poets) - الشعراء سورة

26:18

 قَالَ أَلَمۡ نُرَبِّكَ فِينَا وَلِيدً۬اَ (﻿١٨﻿)

[Pharaoh] said, "Did we not raise you among us as a child (18)

 

The term مولود is usually directed more towards the father than the mother.  مولودله is used to refer to the father while مولود to a son. It is usually used with younger children. For example, Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat Luqmān (Luqman) - لقمان سورة

31:33

 وَلَا مَوۡلُودٌ هُوَ جَازٍ عَن وَالِدِهِۦ شَيۡـًٔا‌ۚ ُ (﻿٣٣﻿)

Nor will a son avail his father at all (33).

67.2 إبن and بنت: إبن is used for a son, the plural being ابنَاء and بنون while بنت for daughter plural being بنَات.  Like اَبّ and اُمّ, these words is not limited to describing biological offspring only but are used in many other contexts.  For example, آدم بني (the children of Adam), بنو إسمَعيل (the children of Israel) or the names of tribes which are often based on an ancestor from the past.

 

The second major difference between ولد and إبن is that the words إبن and بنت are used to show a relation or lineage.  For example, مريم بن عيسَى (Jesus son of Mary), عمرَان بنت مريم (Mary daughter of Imran), اِخوَانِهنَّ بَنيِ (the sons of their brothers), الاَخ بنَات (a brother’s daughters), etc.  ولد is not used in this manner.

 

The third difference between the two is that the terms إبن and بنت (and also اَبّ and اُمّ) have been used to describe كنية (nickname).  A nickname is often based on a minute resemblance or relation.  For example, تُرَاب ابو (the father of dust, nickname of Ali, may Allah be pleased with him), هريره بوا (the father of a kitten, nickname of famous companion of the Messenger, peace be upon him), الخبَائث ام (the mother of all evils, refers to alcohol), الَامرَاض ام (the mother of all diseases, refers to constipation), الوقت إبن (built by time), السبيل إبن (refers to a traveler),  إبن الادهم, الكرم بنت (refers to grapes), etc.  ولد and its derivatives are not used in such a way.
