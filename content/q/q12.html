12. QASAM TORNA [to break an oath]

Synonyms: NAQATHA, NAQADA, HANITHA

PAGE 683

12.1 NAQATHA (نَکَثَ): popular word for breaking an oath. To break an oath of commitment, allegiance, to break a promise (dictionary Al Manjad).  Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:13

أَلَا تُقَـٰتِلُونَ قَوۡمً۬ا نَّكَثُوٓاْ أَيۡمَـٰنَهُمۡ

 Will you not fight a people who violated their oaths?

 

12.2 NAQADA (نَقَضَ): NAQADA (نَقَضَ) means destruction. NAQDA-AL-BANA’ (نَقْضَ الْبَنَاء) means to demolish a building. NAQAD-AL-JABL (نَقَضَ الجَبْل) means to untie the knots of a rope. NAQAD-AL-AHD-WAL-AMR (نقض العھد والامر) means to ruin an oath after solidity, with lame excuses. NAQAD-AMAN means to disrupt peace and to spread mayhem (dictionary Al Manjad).  The word NAQADA (نَقَضَ) relates to wider meanings than NAQATHA (نَکَثَ). (Also see TORNA (to break)). Allah says in the Quran

Surat An-Nahl (The Bee): سورۃ النّحل16:91

 وَلَا تَنقُضُواْ ٱلۡأَيۡمَـٰنَ بَعۡدَ تَوۡڪِيدِهَا

and do not break your oaths after ratifying them

 

12.3 HANITHA (حَنِثَ): HINTH (حَنْث) means a false or fake oath, sin or disobey. HINTHA-FIL-YAMEEN (حَنِثَ فِی الْیَمِیْن) means to break a pledge or vow. To vow to do something and break it. Allah Says in the Holy Quran

Surah Saad (Saad): سورۃ ص 38:44

 وَخُذۡ بِيَدِكَ ضِغۡثً۬ا فَٱضۡرِب بِّهِۦ وَلَا تَحۡنَثۡ‌ۗ

 “Take with your hand a bundle, and strike with it, and do not break your oath.”

Summary:

12.1 NAQATHA: common word for breaking a vow.

12.2 NAQADA: ruin and oath after solidity, with lame excuses.

12.3 HANITHA: falsify an oath, to vow to do something and break the vow.
