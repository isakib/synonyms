Qadam (feet)

1. قَدَم (Qadam): foot or to step forward. Therefore, it is not only foot but the whole shin and the feet, famous body parts.

· اقدام: plural i.e. feet

Allah SWT says in Surah Nahl (16:94): فَتَزِلَّقَدَمٌبَعْدَثُبُوتِهَا “resulting in the slip of a foot after it was firmly planted.”

2. خُطُوَة (Khoot’wata): distance between the two feet. It means to cross the limits. To move forward.

· خُطُوَات: plural i.e. Steps, moving forward or progressing or the complete way.

· خُطُوَاتلَازِمَه: necessary steps

Allah SWT says in Surah Al-Baqarah (2:168): وَلاَتَتَّبِعُواْخُطُوَتِالشَّيْطَـنِ “and follow not the footsteps of Shaytan (Satan).”

3. اَثَر (Asaar): landmarks, signs, remnants for example, from a historical site.

· اثار: impressions left by footsteps.

Allah SWT says in Surah Kahf (18:64): قَالَذَلِكَمَاكُنَّانَبْغِفَارْتَدَّاعَلَىءَاثَارِهِمَاقَصَصًا“Musa AS said (to his companion Yusha AS): "That is what we have been seeking.'' So they went back retracing their footsteps.”

Summary: 

1. قَدَم: distance between two feet, famous body parts or limbs and to step forward, to walk

2. خُطُوَة: distance between the two feet

3. اَثَر: impression of a footstep or foot
