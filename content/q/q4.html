Qabool kerna (to accept)

1. قَبِلَ (Qabila): To happily accept something. Used only for actions.

· قَبُوْلاً: To be accepting of how one acts or behaves.

Allah SWT says in Surah Nuur (24:4): وَلاَتَقْبَلُواْلَهُمْشَهَادَةًأَبَداً “and never accept their testimony.”

2. قَبِلَ, تَقَبَّل (Taqabul and Qabila): both have similar meanings.

· تَقَبَّل: to accept payment for something.

· قَبِل: this is used more commonly.

Allah SWT says in Surah Al-Imraan (3:35): إِذْقَالَتِامْرَأَتُعِمْرَنَرَبِّإِنِّينَذَرْتُلَكَمَافِيبَطْنِيمُحَرَّرًافَتَقَبَّلْمِنِّي“(Remember) when the wife of `Imran said: "O my Lord! I have vowed to You what is in my womb to be dedicated for Your services, so accept this from me.”

3. اَجَاب, اِسْتِجَاب (Ajaab and Istijaab): both are used for acceptance of prayer. Means to not only accept the prayer, but to console the person as well.

Allah SWT says in Surah Naml (27:62): أَمَّنيُجِيبُالْمُضْطَرَّإِذَادَعَاهُ“Is not He Who responds to the distressed one, when he calls on Him.”

Also in Surah Mu’min He says (40:60): وَقَالَرَبُّكُـمْادْعُونِىأَسْتَجِبْلَكُمْ “And your Lord said: "Call upon Me, I will answer you.”

Summary:

1. قَبِلَ: to happily accept something. Used only for actions.

2. تَقَبَّل: It is derived from قَبِلَ . Means to accept payment for something.

3. اِسْتِجَاب: to not only accept the prayer, but to console the person as well.
