Qaafla (caravan)

1. سَيَّارَة (siyaara’ta): Anyone or anything that continuously stays in motion. This motion can be rotational for example, in the case of planets, or translational for example, a car. In common language, this means a group of people or a convoy that are travelling together.

· سَارَ: to walk or move around.

· سَيَّارَات: the planets of the universe.

Allah SWT says in Surah Yusuf (Jacob) (12:19): وَجَاءَتْسَيَّارَةٌفَأَرْسَلُواْوَارِدَهُمْفَأَدْلَىدَلْوَهُ“And there came a caravan of travelers and they sent their water-drawer, and he let down his bucket (into the well).”

2. عِيْر: A caravan that carries food. People and animals are all included in the caravan. Among linguists it doesn’t matter if this caravan has camels, horses or donkeys, they are all part of it. Hence, the Humeri tribe is also described by this term.

Allah SWT says in the Surah Yusuf (12:70): ثُمَّأَذَّنَمُؤَذِّنٌأَيَّتُهَاالْعِيرُإِنَّكُمْلَسَارِقُونَ “Then a crier cried: "O you (in) the caravan! Surely, you are thieves!”

3. رَكْب: A caravan riding on horses or ostriches.

· رَكَبَ: to ride on

· رِكَاب: the vehicle on which a rider rides. It can be an animal or modern day cars.

· رَاكِب: rider. In common usage this word has been designated for someone who is riding a high tide. In other words who thinks highly of him or herself and is probably in a seventh sky. Literally, a caravan riding on ostriches.

· رُكْبَان, رَكْب,رُكُوْب: These are all plural of رَاكِب or rider.

· ركْبُ: a convoy that is riding on horses or camels.

Allah SWT says in Surah Anfaal (8:42): إِذْأَنتُمبِالْعُدْوَةِالدُّنْيَاوَهُمبِالْعُدْوَةِالْقُصْوَىوَالرَّكْبُأَسْفَلَمِنكُمْ“(And remember) when you (the Muslim army) were on the near side of the valley (Medina), and they on the farther side, and the caravan on the ground lower than you.”

Summary:

1. سَيَّارَة: people travelling together either on foot or riding on animals/vehicles. This is common usage.

2. عِيْر: A convoy that carries food or crops.

3. رَكْب: A caravan riding on horses or ostriches.
