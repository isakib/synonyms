Qabar (grave)

1. قَبْر (Qabar): grave

· قبُور: Plural i.e. graves

· مقبره: A tomb. Grave of a famous person

· مقابر: Plural of tombs

Allah SWT says in Surah Tawbah (9:84): وَلاَتُصَلِّعَلَىأَحَدٍمِّنْهُممَّاتَأَبَداًوَلاَتَقُمْعَلَىقَبْرِهِ “And never (O Muhammad) pray (funeral prayer) for any of them (hypocrites) who dies, nor stand at his grave.”

2. مَرقْد (Marqad): Bedroom or wherever one sleeps. In the Qur’an this word is used metaphorically because disbelievers will compare the hardships of graveto those of the Day of Judgment and associate the former as easier compared to the latter.

· رَقَدَ: To sleep lightly for a long time. Slumber.

Allah SWT says in Surah Ya’seen (36:52): قَالُواْيوَيْلَنَامَنبَعَثَنَامِنمَّرْقَدِنَا“They (disbelievers) will say: "Woe to us! Who has raised us up from our place of sleep?”

3. جَدَث (Jada’as): A body which has no remains left or are not identifiable.

· جدث :Linguists have not distinguished this word from qabar. But, in the Quran even though qabar is used more commonly, جدث refers to a grave that cannot be identified as it has no remnants. Besides, many a times, somebody is devoured by an animal or a human is drowned in the ocean where sea animals eat the body. In Hindu and other cultures, the bodies are cremated so there is no chance that it can be identified and are just part of the larger system. So, for those cases جدث is used. In the Quran, this word is used twice and both the times it is used when there have been nothing left of the graves and thus, they cannot be identified.

· اجداث: plural of جدث

Allah SWT says in Surah Ma’aarij (70:43): يَوْمَيَخْرُجُونَمِنَالاٌّجْدَاثِسِرَاعاًكَأَنَّهُمْإِلَىنُصُبٍيُوفِضُونَ“The Day when they will come out of the graves quickly as a (hunter) runs towards the caged hunted game.”

This Ayah (verse) mentions Day of Judgment, when there would be no remnants of the graves would be left around. This second verse paints a similar picture:

Allah SWT says in Surah Ya’seen (36:51): وَنُفِخَفِىالصُّورِفَإِذَاهُممِّنَالاٌّجْدَاثِإِلَىرَبِّهِمْيَنسِلُونَ“And the Trumpet will be blown (second time) and behold from the graves they will come out quickly to their Lord.”

Summary:

1. قَبْر: this is common usage. Used for a body of which may or may not have any remains left.

2. مَرقْد: used for the grave as a metaphor.

3. جَدَث: a body which has no remains left or are not identifiable.
