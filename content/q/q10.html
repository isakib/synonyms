10- QURBANI KA JANWAR [The Animal for Sacrifice]

 

Quran-Kareem has used the words like BUDN (بدن), NUSUK (نسك), HADA (هدى), and QALAID (قلائد).

 

10.1 BUDAN (بدن): Plural of BUDNAU (بدنه). BADAN (بدن) is called the body of every living being provided that its blood has not dried. BADANA (بدن) and BADAN (بدن) means to become fat. And BUDN (بدن) means camels for sacrifice that are taken at Makah to slaughter. And the sacrificial camels are called BUDN (بدن) due to the relevance of their huge fat bodies.  Allah says:

 

Page-680

Surat Al-Ĥaj (The Pilgrimage) - سورة الحج 22:36

 

وَالْبُدْنَ جَعَلْنٰهَا لَكُمْ مِّنْ شَعَاۗىِٕرِ اللّٰهِ

And the camels and cattle We have appointed for you as among the symbols of Allah.

 

10.2 NUSUK (نسك): NASAK (نسك) advocates about pray and closeness to GOD. NASIK (ناسيك) means chaste and NASEEKA (نسیکه) is called that animal for sacrifice that is done for drawing closer to GOD. And NUSUK (نسك) is the plural of NASEEKA (نسیکه). Then this word has generally connected to HAJJ. MANASIK-E-HAJJ (مناسک حج) i.e. rules and regulations of HAJJ. And also those sites where these commands are performed. Similarly NUSUK (نسك) refers to those sacrificial animals that are slaughtered at the site of MANA (منى) during AYAAM-E-TASHREEQ (ایام تشریق). Allah says:

 

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - سورة البقرة 2:196

 

فَمَنْ كَانَ مِنْكُمْ مَّرِيْضًا اَوْ بِهٖٓ اَذًى مِّنْ رَّاْسِهٖ فَفِدْيَةٌ مِّنْ صِيَامٍ اَوْ صَدَقَةٍ اَوْ نُسُك

And whoever among you is ill or has an ailment of the head [making shaving necessary must offer] a ransom of fasting [three days] or charity or sacrifice.

 

10.3 HADA (هدى): Plural of HADIATUN (هدية) that sacrificial animal that is sent to BAIT-ALLAH-SHARIF (بیت الله شریف) whether it is camel or cow or sheep and whether it is male or female. Allah says:

 

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - سورة البقرة 2:196

 

وَلَا تَحْلِقُوْا رُءُوْسَكُمْ حَتّٰى يَبْلُغَ الْهَدْيُ مَحِلَّه

And do not shave your heads until the sacrificial animal has reached its place of slaughter.

 

10.4 QALAID (قلائد): Singular QALADATA (قلادة). QALADA (قلد) means to put necklace in neck and QALADATA (قلادة) means necklace. And QALADATA (قلادة) is also called that sacrificial animal whose neck is fitted with a necklace or strap as a mark. Whether it is related to HAJJ (حج) or fulfilling a vow.  Allah says:

 

Surat Al-Mā'idah (The Table Spread) - سورة المائدة 5:2

 

يٰٓاَيُّھَا الَّذِيْنَ اٰمَنُوْا لَا تُحِلُّوْا شَعَاۗىِٕرَ اللّٰهِ وَلَا الشَّهْرَ الْحَرَامَ وَلَا الْهَدْيَ وَلَا الْقَلَاۗىِٕدَ

O you who have believed, do not violate the rites of Allah or [the sanctity of] the sacred month or [neglect the marking of] the sacrificial animals and garlanding [them].

 

Summary:

10.1 BUDN: The sacrificial camels that are slaughtered during HAJJ.

10.2 HUDA: Those sacrificial animals which are taken by HAJI for HAJJ.

10.3 NUSUK: All types of those animals that are sacrificed at MNA.

10.4 QALAID: Those sacrificial animals whose necks are fitted with strap.
