11. QASAM UTHANA [to swear, oath]

The words WA (وَ), TA (تَ), and LA (لَ), are used to give the meaning of swearing in native Arabic language. These words are either used as a habit or a catch phrase or sometimes to emphasize on something. If these words are related to ALLAH, it means to provide a testimony for something one swears upon. For example, Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al Asr (The Time): سورۃ العصر 103:1

وَٱلۡعَصۡرِ إِنَّ ٱلۡإِنسَـٰنَ لَفِى خُسۡرٍ

By (the Token of) Time (through the ages), Verily Man is in loss,

 

TA (تَ) is specifically used to swear upon Allah, as said by Hazrat Ibraheem:

Surah Al-Anbiya (the prophets): سورۃ الانَبیاء 21:57

 وَتَٱللَّهِ لَأَڪِيدَنَّ أَصۡنَـٰمَكُم

And by Allah, I have a plan for your idols -

 

LA (لَ) is not used in Quran for swearing.

Synonyms:  QASAMA, YAMEEN, HALF, ALAI’AH, EELAA’

11.1 QASAMA (قَسَمَ): QASAMA (قَسَمَ) means to distribute or dispense and also to swear, QASM (قَسْم) means a suspicion in heart that later turns into certainty (dictionary Al Manjad).  QUSAAMAT (قَُسَامَت) are those oaths that were undertaken on blood by the suspicious tribes with the saints (dictionary Al Manjad). Thus QASAM (قَسَمَ) word is used to remove the suspicion in something and mostly used in case of a conflict. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Waqiah (The Inevitable): سُوۡرَةُ الواقِعَة 56:76

وَإِنَّهُ ۥ لَقَسَمٌ۬ لَّوۡ تَعۡلَمُونَ عَظِيمٌ

It is an oath, if you only knew, that is tremendous.

 

Another reference from the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:21

 وَقَاسَمَهُمَآ إِنِّى لَكُمَا لَمِنَ ٱلنَّـٰصِحِينَ

And he swore to them, “I am a sincere advisor to you.”

 

11.2 YAMEEN (یَمِیْن): To exempt from blame. Prophet Muhammad (S.A.A.W) said

 الْبَيِّنَةَ عَلَى الْمُدَّعِي وَالْيَمِينَ عَلَى الْمُدَّعٰی عَلَیْہ

 The burden of proof is on the claimant, and the taking of an oath is an obligation upon him who denies the claim

And YAMEEN (یَمِیْن) also refers to the right hand. When Native Arabs used to strengthen an oath, then the pledger would put his right hand on the hand of the pledgee. Therefore the word YAMEEN (یَمِیْن) started to be used to give the meaning of taking an oath. Plural AEMAAN (اَیْمَان). The word YAMEEN (یَمِیْن) is borrowed from word oath.

Sometimes in order to emphasize on something, Native Arabs used to swear upon, and pledge oath by placing hand which had no significance in reality. These oaths were called frivolous by Allah and could be forgiven. An oath that has significance in reality and importance are called YAMEEN-MUNAQIDAH (یَمِیْن مُنْعَقِدَۃ) and one can be held responsible for them. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:225

 لَّا يُؤَاخِذُكُمُ ٱللَّهُ بِٱللَّغۡوِ فِىٓ أَيۡمَـٰنِكُمۡ وَلَـٰكِن يُؤَاخِذُكُم بِمَا كَسَبَتۡ قُلُوبُكُمۡ‌ۗ

 God does not hold you responsible for your unintentional oaths, but He holds you responsible for your intentions.

 

11.3 HALF (حَلْف): HALAFA (حَلَفَ) means to be steadfast on something. Oath of loyalty. To stand resolutely to a pledge. Haleef tribes were those tribes which would pledge to help each other in peace or war. Thus HALF (حَلْف) referred to dissolving previous hostility and differences and to establish peace and loyalty amongst each other. In the Quran

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:42

 وَسَيَحۡلِفُونَ بِٱللَّهِ لَوِ ٱسۡتَطَعۡنَا لَخَرَجۡنَا مَعَكُمۡ

Still they swear by God: “Had we been able, we would have marched out with you.

 

11.4 ALAI’AH (اَلِیَّۃ): ALA (اَلَا) (YA’LU (یَاْلُوْا), ALUWWA (اَلْوَّا)) comprises of two meanings, 1. to delay or show delinquency in some matter. For instance

Surah Ale- Imran (The family of Imran): سورۃ آل عِمْرن 3:118

 لَا يَأۡلُونَكُمۡ خَبَالاً۬

who never cease to wish you harm.

 

2. to swear or pledge and oath, ALAI’AHTAN (اَلِیَّۃً) means a swear or oath. An oath whose pledger has to face suffering and delinquency. According to Ibn-Al-Faris an oath to prevent or disrupt a righteous matter. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Noor (The light): سورۃ النور 24:22

وَلَا يَأۡتَلِ أُوْلُواْ ٱلۡفَضۡلِ مِنكُمۡ وَٱلسَّعَةِ أَن يُؤۡتُوٓاْ أُوْلِى ٱلۡقُرۡبَىٰ وَٱلۡمَسَـٰكِينَ

 And let not those who possess dignity and ease among you swear not to give to the near of kin and to the needy,

11.5 EELAA’ (اِیْلَا): (ALA (اٰلٰی), YU’LU (یُؤْلُوْا), EELA’AN (اِیْلَاءً)): to stay away from wife, to oath to refrain from intimacy and sexual activates. During the times of ignorance people used to upset and distress their wives due to such oaths. Shariah has proclaimed the maximum tenure for this oath to be four months in order to curb this absurdity.  Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:226

 لِّلَّذِينَ يُؤۡلُونَ مِن نِّسَآٮِٕهِمۡ تَرَبُّصُ أَرۡبَعَةِ أَشۡہُرٍ۬‌ۖ

Those who vow abstinence from their wives must wait for four months

Summary:

11.1 QASAM: For removing suspicion

11.2 YAMEEN: Exempt from blame, strengthen an oath

11.3 HALAF: Oaths of peace and loyalty amongst each other

11.4 ALAI’AH: Oath to prevent or disrupt a righteous matter

11.5 EELAA’: Oath to refrain from intimacy with wife
